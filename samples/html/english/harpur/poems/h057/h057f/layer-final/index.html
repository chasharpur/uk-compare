[THE BUSH FIRE]

[...] The white heat seething from its spouty flows, Would, with a dread crash, from the hill-top trees (Massing aloft over the gleamy dark That shut in under a high hanging steep) Swoop flaming, like an unsphered group of stars Torn an disfeatured in their ruinous fall, And run together,—a swift dropping mass Of luminous points and flaring limbs that met All in one fiery train, back streaming,—till It shattered as it struck the blackened vale Into an upward rushing cloud of sparks And igneous dust! Or down the flickery glades Ghastfully glaring, huge dry-mouldered gums Stood, ’mid their living kindred, barked with fire! Boles, branches,—all! like ominous ghosts of trees,— The Wood’s first fathers wrapt in burning shrouds, Come from the past, within the Whiteman’s pale, To typify its doom. Such was the prospect! Illuminated cities were but jests Compared with it for splendor. But enough! Where are the words to paint the million shapes And unimaginable freaks of Fire, When holding thus its monster carnival In the primeval Forest all night long?
