[Pope]

2.

His Rape of the Lock.

What silver peals of music here are rung! What pearls of fancy in what order strung On golden threads of verse that fuse upon the tongue! How smiling is the satire, and the wit How forceable, and yet how exquesite! What gay creations too enlarge the theme, And “bask and whiten” in the Poet’s dream! As silken blossoms waving on their sprays, All dewy, glint in the moon’s mystic rays; As silver mist-shreds on the mountains born Flit in the first white glances of the morn.

Most finished lay! like, in its skillful sheen, The bridalmantle of some Eastern queen, All starred with gems, and richly figured o’er With golden symbols teaching Love’s bright lore: Even such a work as might employ the nice And nimble-handed Girls of Paradise.
