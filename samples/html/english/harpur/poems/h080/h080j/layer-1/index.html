[THE CREEK OF THE FOUR GRAVES]

[...] Bestrewn with leaves, and withered spraylets, stript By the loud wintry-wingēd gales that roamed Those solitudes, from the old trees which there Moaned the same leafy dirges that had caught The heed of dying Ages: these were all; And the place was long by travellers called The Creek of the Four Graves. Such was the Tale Egremont told us of the wild old times.
