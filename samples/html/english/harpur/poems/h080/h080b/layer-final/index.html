BY CHARLES HARPUR, ESQ.

[...] I will add, for the sake of further contrast, a passage from a poem of my own. But the reader must not mistake me in this. I do so purely in the spirit of critical disquisition for the benefit of the popular taste. 108a

[THE CREEK OF THE FOUR GRAVES]

[...] ————the Universe appeared Coiled in vast rest;—only that one long Cloud Crept, as with scrawling legs, across the sky, And also, that the multitudinous Stars, In their bright companies, high over all, Looked thoughtfully awake. And now the Moon, Up from behind an eastern hill, was seen Ascending slow, till level with its cone; Whereof the solid bulk seemed then to grow Half saturate with her radiance; whilst the trees That fringed its outline,—their huge statures dwarfed By distance into brambles, and yet all In delicate definement, looked to swell Out of her very face in dark relief, As sculptured from her substance.
