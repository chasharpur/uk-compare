84c

HER PERFECTION.

I never saw but one of many fair Amongst dear womankind, in whom, to me, Some inexpressible deficiency Lurked not, to leave my admiration bare And fruitless; so that my affections ne’er Might find abiding place, though fond and free; And cheated thus of its felicity, My heart grow wild with passionate despair. But one at length, a late-found charmer, came Over my lonely spirit—as a strain Of music, fann’d into a vocal flame, Floats o’er the troubled waters of the main— So perfect to my thought, that for sweet shame, Did she love, I could not but love again.
