Rosa’s Perfection.

Many of Womankind most sweet and fair Had I beheld; and yet in each, to me, Ere long some winter-like infirmity, some wrong Appeared, to strip my growing fancy bare Of budding love; wherefore that wanderer ne’er Of its rich fruit might the glad mother be: Till,—as if wronged of its felicity,— My heart grew wild with passionate despair! 41 But One at length, so soul-sweet in her sweetness, O’er my lone spirit breathed,—even as a strain Of music o’er the melancholy main;— So pure of life; so white in her completeness; That for fond shame and mere desire of meetness, Did she love, I could not but love again.
