[RECORDS OF A POET’S LOVE XI]

Her image haunts me! Lo! I muse at even, And straight it gathers from the gloom, to make My soul its mirror; which (as some still lake Holds pictured in its depths the face of heaven) Through the hushed night retains it: when ’tis given To take a warmer presence and incline A glowing cheek burning with love to mine, Saying—”The heart for which thou long hast striven With looks so fancy-pale, I grant thee now; And if for ruth, yet more for love’s sweet sake, My lips shall seal this promise on thy brow.” Thus blest in sleep—oh! who would care to wake, When the cold real from his belief must shake Such vows, like blossoms from a shattered bough?
