[TO THE MOON]

For ages shall have ceas’d to glow;— Shall into worthless dust decay;— Which, as th’invading plough-share drills The unrember’d burial=ground, Each wind, may, o’er a hundred hills, Scatter around;— That, in the mid=night heavens, thou Shalt hang thy ‘unfaded lamp, and smile serene as now!

10.

Nay, more than this;—could even those, The Edenites, who sojourn’d here Ere Noah’s tilted ark arose, Or Nimrod chas’d the bounding deer;— Whereever sepulchred, could they, As in the day of gen’ral doom, Shake, for an hour, Death’s bonds away, Into their tomb Prying, with midnight glance, might find Thee, as they dying left thee, watchful Moon! behind.

11.

But shall I have these thoughts, and not A profiting experience know? Seeing that, wither, change, and rot, Must all of earth, thy lamp below; Should I not turn with this sole aim:— T’abjure, in deed, in thought, control, All that might dim th’aspiring flame— Th’essential Soul, I feel within; and which must be A living thing when thou art blank’d eternally?

1836.
