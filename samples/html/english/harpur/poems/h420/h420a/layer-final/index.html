HER EYES.

As lustrous as the noontide heavens are The eyes of my beloved--and yet serene And shadowy for delight as evening’s scene; And fairy fancy, like a win’d star, Moves in their glowing depths, and lures afar The gazer’s soul—far from the present mean, To were the spirit of Old Romance is seen Pluming with love the iron front of War. Bright eyes before, in the world’s ways, with glance Ethereal, have made my Being one With passion, but such charm before had none My sense of the ideal to enhance! And works this witching power by fate or chance? Resolve me, Love, for my wild heart is gone!
