VI.

Love in the Long Ago.

Many and many a day hath flown Since once, face to face, stood we, Gramachree, Under the shadowy wild fig tree, While the suns of the past in the red west shone; And yet how my distant heart is thrilled Like a harp that is swept by the breeze alone, Whenever I’m thinking of thee, Gramachree! And my head with a dim wild stir is filled, Like the sound remote of a sooming sea.

‘Tis a fond feeling of happier days, (When once face to face, stood we, Gramachree, Under the shadowy wild fig tree, Close hid from the low sun’s lengthening rays) That thrills and sweetens within my breast Like a breeze that afar from its Eden strays, Whenever I’m thinking of thee, Gramachree! And this dim brain-stir is the wild unrest Of a moaning and homeless memory.

Ah! that the beautiful Spirit of Love, That once, when a-met were we, Gramachree, Under the shadowy wild fig tree, Did brood as it were in the branches above Impassioning all our sympathies, Should be wandering now like the arkless dove— Or then have been, only to be, Gramachree, Thus re-felt in the stir of Thought’s mystic breeze, And the shell-like murmurs of Memory!
