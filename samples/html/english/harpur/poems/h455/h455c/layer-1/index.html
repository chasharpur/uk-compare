A Regret. Uncopied

When the Father who toiled for our welfare is dead, And the Mother who bore us is laid in her grave, What would we not give if we never had said Or done in our frowardness, aught that e’er gave Those hearts that once loved us, as none can again, One pulse of disquiet—one moment of pain?

We loved them—but ah! in a measure that fell How short of the one they deserved on our part! We cherished their wisdom and honored them well, But the motive, though truthful, was cold,—and we start With a feeling of loss and a failure of peace, From which Time’s long tract is our only release.
