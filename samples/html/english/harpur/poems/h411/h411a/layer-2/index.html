A Blank Verse Paraphrase of the 51st. Psalm

O God, according to thy loving kindness, Have mercy on me; and blot out for good My manifold transgressions! For are not Thy tender mercies in their multitude As the pure stars of heaven? Wash my heart In the abundant waters of thy grace, And cleanse me from my sin. For I confess All my iniquity, and my fault is still Before me, like a dark and terrible cloud.

Yet against thee—thee only, have I sinned! For in thy holy sight, all things alike Were holly, didst thou will no difference. Hence all they judgments are as light, wherewith No possible shadow of a doubt can mix.

But Lord, thou knowest, that in iniquity My very limbs were shapen; that in sin She who conceived me warmed me into life. And yet, in witness to my truth of heart, Thy wisdom, like a ray of living light, Can purge my inward darkness. Purge me then, O Lord, in justice, and my life shall be Clean as a garment! Wash me in thy great Compassion, and behold, even as a man By nature sinful, I shall stand before thee Whiter than snow! So shall thy justice turn My spots to beauty, and thy mercy make My broken bones exceedingly to rejoice.

Hide then thy face from my transgressions! blot My sins away forever! Yea, create A clean heart in my being, and renew Within me a right spirit. Cast me not Out from thy glorious presence; nor withdraw Thy holy spirit from my paths of life. So shall the joy of thy salvation rest Upon me, and thy grace uphold me still; So shall my simple neighbour learn thy ways, 579 And sinners be converted unto thee.

And finally, deliver me, O God, From all blood guiltiness: wherefore my tongue Shall sing aloud of thy great righteousness. And to this end, open my lips, that I With eloquent speech may show forth all thy praise. For thou desirest not sacrifice; the blood Of blatant harts; else would I give it thee: but these, A broken spirit and a contrite heart, Are all the sacrifices I offer thee, of a God, And these, I know, thou never will despise.
