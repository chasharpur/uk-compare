XXV.

THE EGOTISM OF POETS.

Though Bards have egotism, never din them About it, as a failing, since their ”I” Is representative of that within them Which should be published, and not mutely die; That model with which, bravely urged, must win them A memory splendid as a sunset sky! Thus what in others were indeed a failing, In them is but self-royalty prevailing.
