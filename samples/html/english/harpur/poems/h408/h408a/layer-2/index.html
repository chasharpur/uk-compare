Psalm LVII.

O God, most merciful and just, In thee alone I build my trust! Yea, in the shadow of thy wings My soul, in its petitionings, Hath always sheltered,—first and last,— Till each calamity were past.

And now again, O God most high, To thee alone for help I cry! For thou from heaven wilt send to save From that reproach, which, like the grave, Would swallow up my life indeed, But for thy sovereign help in need.

Girt am I as with lions!—torn By foes on fire with hate and scorn! Whose teeth are spears, whose arrowy words Are shot from tongues as sharp as swords! And yet, ev’n in their midst, I praise My helper,—God, in all his ways!

My soul is bowed with grief—but yet My guided feet have scaped their net! And in the pit they digged for me Themselves shall stumble, bodily! While, still with a heart more fixed, I sing Of heaven and earth’s all-righteous King!

Awake my harp!—awake, and raise A morning song of public praise, To Him whose mercies wide expand,— Wide as the skies,—o’er every land! 588 —A morning song, whose praiseful lore Shall sound up heavenward evermore!
