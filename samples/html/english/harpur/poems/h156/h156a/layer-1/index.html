Stanzas Good Night &c

To speak the words my lips are slow— And yet Good Night to each by name, Frank, Harry, Fred and Tome—I go A kindlier creature than I came: Yes, even I, a stricken deer, Whom worldly weal might ne’er befal, Have known one cup of comfort here— Good Night, and God be with you all.

Note the Song of “Good Night”

This song was composed to commemorate a drinking bout I once had with some friends, during which a song of just a similar turn of words in the concluding lines of the stanzas was sung by one of them, and made a great impression upon my memory. That carouse was the most kindly and harmonious [unclear] affair of the kind that I ever participated in – and I wish therefore it had been their last.

I once saw a mounted Policeman on race course – who kept expressing himself greatly disatisfied with the feats of the horses though it was acknowledged on all other hands that they ran splendaciously”. “Why”, asked one of his comrades, “are you so displeased with the horses? – capitally – first rate! – I can’t deny that, mate: but somehow or other, I want to see them go faster.

Even thus I find it with myself in the matter of drink. I cannot enjoy it at a moderate pace; I am disatisfied and restless, like the Policeman, even under a rapid degree of excitement, and want to see every thing go faster – and faster still. Hence my peculiar peril. Se[l]f respectably, like a prudent profess ... I cannot. To dram it, like a tink... me. ... sleep over it, would bloat me ...
