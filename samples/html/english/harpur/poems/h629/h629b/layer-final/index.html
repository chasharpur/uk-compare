AUSTRALIAN LYRICS.—No. 3.

TO THE LYRE OF AUSTRALIA.

Wild Lyre of Australia, will none ravish thee From the charm mutt’ring savage’s hand unrefined Yes; such thy sole master no longer shall be— No longer thy lay be but wrote on the wind. And though he that now strings thee be unequal ever To evince in his verse all thy rapture of tone, Yet—yet should his country smile on the endeavour: Of that her young poet’s ambitious alone.

Wild Lyre of Australia, in song could I vie With the strength of a Burns, of a Byron the fire, Oh, then I would raise thee in glory on high, As the guardian of beauty, of valour the sire: And might I but hope that one song I may waken, As a voice in the gale that drives over the glade, Should ride, when my country her empire hath taken, On the flood of her ages, I’d count me repaid.

With her green forests round me, above her blue sky, I lap in thy measures some national dream; And I find that the notes, though unstudied, are high, When the glory of future Australia’s the theme! And e’er should thy lay, to her sons and her daughters, Be the voice of the land where the evergreen grows— Be to souls like the strong wind which swelleth the waters, To hearts like the spring breeze that opens the rose.

Charles Harpur.
