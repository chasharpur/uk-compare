137th. Psalm

By Babylon’s strange streams we sate, (The enslaver standing near the slave) And over Judah’s fall’n estate We wept into the passing wave.

Upon the willows there that grew Our once glad sounding harps were hung, That but the wild wind as th blew Might grieve their wailful chords among.

38

But they who held us captive—they Who wasted us with daily wrongs, To make them mirth, did asking say, Come sing us one of Zion’s songs.

How can to us the gift belong To strike the harp with fettered hand? How can we sing a joyful song, As captives, in a foreign land?

And, O Jerusalem, should we let Thy memory pass on Time’s grey wing, May our right hands at once forget Their mastery o’er the sounding string.

Yea, let our tongues to music dumb Be all, as in a voiceless dream, Till to thy courts again we come And thy deliverence be the theme.

Then, Edom, thou shalt get thy wound For saying, on her evil day, Raize all her beauty to the ground And captive drive her sons away!

And lo! yet drearer visions rise! The day is fixed—the hour is known,— When, Babelon, thy fateful skies Shall rain their fiery rain down!

And then shall the avenger hurl Thy sucklings gainst the stones—for thus In fallen Judah, ’mid the whirl Of slaughter, didst thou unto us!
