NOON IN THE FOREST AT MIDSUMMER.

Not a bird disturbs the air, There is quiet everywhere; Over plains and over woods, What a mighty stillness broods!

Only there’s a drowsy humming From yon Summer rill-side coming: ‘Tis the dragon-hornet,—see! All bedaubed gorgeously With crimson, splendid to behold! Dusted o’er with mealy gold;— Only there’s a droning, where Yon bright beetle gleams the air, And, rising in the sunshine higher, Seems sharded as with gems on fire!

Every other thing is still, Save the ever-wakeful rill, Whose cool murmur only throws A cooler comfort round Repose; Or some ripple in the sea Of leaves, when, intermittently, Summer in her forest bower, Turning from the noontide hour, Heaves a slumb’rous breath, ere she Once more slumbers peacefully.

Oh, ’tis easeful here to lie Hidden from Noon’s scorching eye, In this grassy cool recess, Musing thus of Quietness!

Charles Harpur.
