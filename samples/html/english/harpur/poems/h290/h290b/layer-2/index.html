Noon in the Forest at Midsummer.

Not a bird disturbes the air, There is quiet everywhere; Over plains and over woods What a mighty stillness broods.

Only there’s a drowsy humming From yon still Lagoon side coming: ‘Tis the dragon-hornet—see All bedaubed resplendently With crimson—each spot (coasted round By a tawny copper ground) Like a shapeless seal Dusted o’er with golden meal. Only there’s a droning, where Yon bright beetle gleams the air, Gleams it in its droning flight With a slanting track of light, Till, rising in the sunshine higher, Its wing-

shells

flame, like gems on fire.

Every other thing is still, Save some ever-wakeful rill, Whose cool murmur only throws A cooler comfort round Repose; 26 Or some ripple in the sea Of leaves, and boughs when, intermittingly, Drows’d Summer, in her forest bower, Turning with the noontide hour, Heaves a slumbrous breath ere she Once more slumbers peacefully.

Oh, ’tis easeful here to lie Hidden from Noon’s scorching eye, In this grassy cool recess, Musing thus of Quietness.
