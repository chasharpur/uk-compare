To the Great Comet of 1843.

Thy purpose, heavenly Stranger, who may know But Him who linked thee to the starry whole? We see those journeyest amid the worlds, And that is all the lore our age can boast Of thy celestial moods: for of the birth Of Motion, save as thy primordial step In God’s creative power, mankind even yet, With all their vaunted science, may but guess And dimly speculate as did of old, Egypt’s dark Seers, observatoried high On those star-pointing piles that crumbling stand Out amid voiceless wastes, as in the dim And desolate chambers of a ‘doubly dead’ Though monumental Past;—or, following these, The shepherd Magi of the mystic East; And this too with a faith less raised than theirs, And cumbered more with crude material helps. That, shining the wild wings of Wonder, glue x Our tempers down into the mire of Time— And tend to making even when they deaden least, Our senses final, and their objects, to us. [unclear]

Wherefore, in this our darkness, be it ours More to Muse of thee, in thy swift career, As of a meet though wandering symphony Amid the sounding harmonies that trace The aulic marches of Almighty Truth— The planetary voices of Creation, Singing together, in their sun-led choirs, As through unmeasurable voids they roll, The God-taught anthem of eternal Order?
