To the Great Comet of 1843.

Thy purpose, heavenly Stranger, who may know But Him who linked thee to the starry whole? We see those journeyest amid the worlds, And that is all the lore our age can boast Of thy celestial moods: for of the birth Of Motion, save as as thy primordial step In God’s creative mank, mankind even yet, With all their vaunted science, may but guess And dimly speculate as did they of old, Egypt’s dark Seers, observatoried high On those star-pointing piles that crumbling stand Out amid voiceless wastes, as in a dim dead Though monumental Past;—or, following these, The shepherd Magi of the mystic East; And this too with a faith less raised than theirs, And cumbered more with crude material helps. That, shining the wild wings of Wonder, glue Our spirits [unclear] into the mire of time Making our senses and their objects final.

Wherefore, in this our darkness, be it ours More to Muse of thee, in its swift career, As of a meet though wandering symphony Amid the sounding harmonies the Soul— The aulic marches of Almighty Truth— The planetary voices of Creation, Singing together, in their sun-led choirs, As through unmeasurable voids they wheel, The mighty anthem of eternal Order?
