2D

III

To my second born, Charles Chidley Harpur.

Methought my first born, Washington[,] had filled My heart, with his fair lineaments, so nearly, That by no other child it might be thrilled So deeply, or constrained to love so dearly. For, as in a framed miniature, we see An only semblance fondly set apart, So once his image might be said to be A sole child-portrait spread through all my heart. But now, my little Charley, thou canst start A pictured spirit there as well as he; Not one below the other or above, But equal occupants together moving Its inmost folds—not straitened, but through Love Enlarging the capacity of loving.
