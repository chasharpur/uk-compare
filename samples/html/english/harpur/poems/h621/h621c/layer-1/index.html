XLIX.

To My Second Born, Charles Chidley Harpur.

I thought my first born, Washington, had filled My heart with his fair lineaments so nearly, That by no other child it might be thrilled So deeply, or constrained to love so dearly. For, as in a framed miniature we see An only semblance fondly set apart, His image once might well be said to be A sole child-portrait spread through all my heart. But now, my little Charley, thou canst start A pictured spirit there as well as he; Not one below the other or above, But equal occupants together moving Its inmost folds—not straitened, but through Love Enlarging the capacity of loving.
