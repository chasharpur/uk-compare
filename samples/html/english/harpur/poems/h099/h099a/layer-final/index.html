5d

IV.

Dr. Johnson.

A Man-lump of Learning, in scholarly garments,[*] Who thought all live languages absolute varmints! But his Latin to polish, his English came pat in, (He was full to the throat, was the Doctor, of Latin). And so he rubbed down, with much erudite pother, The bark of the one with the rust of the other; And saw in the twain, when in tumid verse blended, A classical English poetic style splendid. He had wit, with scant measure of nature or mirth in it, And a great solemn soul, with a great deal of earth in it.

N.B.—I think I must mean (in that line at the head there) By the epithet “scholarly,” garments quite threadbare
