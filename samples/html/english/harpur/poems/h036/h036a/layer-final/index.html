THE TERRORS OF BEAUTY.

Thy fairy feet, thy sylphic waist, And those twin innocents, as they Fill up like doves whose nestward haste Had flurried them; those lips that play Apart so clovenly and rich; Those pinky cheeks, and, thou sweet Witch, Those eyes the loveliest in the world, And that fair forehead overcurled With tresses in their darkness bright, Like darkness of a starry night;— All fill me with such sweet dismay, That still, for looking though the more In love,—for Love I’ve less to say Than when I looked before.
