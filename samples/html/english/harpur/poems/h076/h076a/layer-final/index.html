A Contrast.

God help the Poor!—I saw a Widow stand Amid her Orphans three, on whom she gazed Even as one with desperate woe amazed; For they did ask for bread and her wan hand Had none to give! In supplication bland, At last tow’rd heaven her wild eyes were raised: While still those foodless Children, hunger-crazed, Her upturned face in asking misery scann’d! But mark the Contrast:—a few roods away, Music pealed up, and Groups rich clad and gay, Were revelling in a civic hall of mirth! Oh, ye expounders of the Almighty Mind, Say, could its perfect goodness have designed That Waste and Want neighbour thus Earth?
