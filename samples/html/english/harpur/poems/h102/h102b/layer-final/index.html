A DREAM OF THE ORIENT.

With a splendid Eastern bride Like a houri at my side, And music round us swelling; ‘Mid odours of so rare a steam That unembodied souls they seem, Dwell I, through a radiant dream, In an Orient dwelling.

In the pleasure court we lie Near a fountain flashing high, On a gorgeous pillow; The column’d waters mount the air, To hang in bright cascadence there, Like the sprays, ornate and rare, Of a crystal willow.

And fair faces, darkly bright, Like the countenance of Night In her starry glory: All ministrant, around us throng, And breathe their beauty into song, Or in tones, as rich, prolong Some wild rich-worded story.

Till through many voices, one Like a gush of gold doth run: “Why should kindred sever? Our life is a perpetual feast Of being, from all care released, Sunny souls are for the East— Dwell with us for ever!”
