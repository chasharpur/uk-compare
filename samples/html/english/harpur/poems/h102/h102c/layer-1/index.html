Lyrics. By Charles Harpur.

A Dream of the Orient.

With a splendid Eastern bride Like a houri at my side, And music round us swelling ‘Mid odours of so rare a steam That unembodied souls they seem, Dwell I, through a radiant dream, In a rich Orient dwelling.

Near a fountain flashing high In the pleasure-court we lie, Each on a gorgeous pillow: The columned water, mounting, breaks In outward curves and falling flakes, Till the whole a picture makes As of a crystal willow.

And fair faces, darkly bright, Like the countenance of Night Seen in her starry glory All ministrant around us throng And breathes their pathos into song, 28 Or in words, as rich, prolong Some wild rich-worded story.

Till through many voices, one Like a gush of gold doth run: “Why—why should kindred sever? Our life is a perpetual feast Of being, from all care released, Sunny souls are for the East— Then dwell with us for ever!”
