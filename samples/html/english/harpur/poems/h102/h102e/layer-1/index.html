IX.

A Dream of the Orient.

With a splendid Eastern bride Like a houri at my side, And music round us swelling, ‘Mid odors of so rare a steam That passing Peri souls they seem, Dwell I, through a radiant dream, In a rich Orient dwelling.

Near a fountain flashing high In the pleasure-court we lie, Each on a gorgeous pillow: The columned water, mounting, breaks In outward curves and falling flakes, Till the whole a picture makes As of a crystal willow.

Outward, gallaried walls extend, Pierced with arcs and aisles that bend On wreathen pillars slender; While in every vista—lo, Such clouds of blazoned banners glow, As in very semblance show A constant sunset splendor.
