The Tee-tree Grove.

Whene’er of one green Spot afar In loneliness of heart I dream, 642 Sweet recollections—sweet as are Love’s first fond sighs, thence breathing seem: For there a joyous Youth doth gaze Entranced on his first blooming Love— Seen o’er the waste of Manhood’s days ‘Mid the trembling sprays—Of the Tee-tree Grove.

My Hopes have perished like the grass— The World’s waves waft me where they may— But never from my mind can pass The evenings of each happy day; When that dear Girl with melting eyes, And blushing cheeks, confessed her love— Breaking her words with soul-sweet sighs ‘Neath the leafy skies Of the Tee-tree Grove.

The wild-birds sang amid the Trees, And the wild-bee went murmuring by; And Heaven, far purpling over these, Was imaged in the River nigh: But oh! of all this rich delight The crowning charm was Human Love, ‘Tween two young hearts there glowing bright In the checkered light—Of the Tee-tree Grove.

And since those passion-happy days I’ve wandered ‘neath a cruel doom— Have pined in the World’s beaten ways Without a Friend—without a Home! Yet, whatso else through Time might fail, Where’er my wayward feet might rove, Still Joy was mine,—so sweet though pale,— In Memory’s Tale—Of the Tee-tree Grove.
