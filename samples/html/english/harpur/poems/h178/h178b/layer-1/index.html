How comes it?

How comes it, that the special panders To olden Systems, every one, Are things more fearful than wild ganders Scar[e]d by a gun, Of aught that gainst their scheme’s meanders May chance to run?

650

How comes it, that all thrond Powers shun The bold enquirers of the free As they were charg[e]d artillery; And that our breath, when at such weather, Makes Mother Churches sweat like butter Before the sun?

It “comes by cause”, as sage Polonious Would say:—they’re fearful, being erroneous. For Truth alone is brave to fight Her battle with the sword of light. She—only she, hath for her dower This bright inherency of power: To rise through all, unhurt, unwrested, And shine the more the more she’s tested.
