How comes it?

How comes it, that the special panders To olden Systems, every one, Are things more fearful than wild ganders Scar[e]d by a gun, Of aught that gainst their scheme’s meanders May chance to run?

650

How comes it, that all thrond Powers shun The bold enquirers of the free As they were charg[e]d artillery? That even our breath, when such weather, Makes Mother Churches sweat like butter Before the sun? And musty moralists cry out As though their causes were about To be undone?

It “comes by cause”, as sage Polonious Would say:—they’re fearful, being erroneous. For Truth is brave, and loves to fight Her battle with the sword of light. Conscious of having for her dower This bright impunity,—the power To rise through all, unhurt, unwrested, And shine the more the more she’s tested.
