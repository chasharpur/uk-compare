II

[A LYRICAL LOVE STORY 3:4]

The Past is flowing through my thoughts— Flowing like a sea; With all its billows dancing bright Over what?—an under-might Of darkling loss and misery!

Thus it floweth through my thoughts— Floweth like a sea; While of worn Hope I ask alway, Like a dying cast-away: What can earth’s future be to me?

What can it be to me, indeed, But a drearier scene? Or at the very best, sans doubt, But a stale, stale lengthening out Of what the gainless Past hath been?
