Dawn in the Forest.

It is the Morning Star, arising slow Out of yon hill’s dark bulk, as born of it. Then glides she forth into the dim sky, leaving A whiteness in her wake, that whitens still As she ascends, till all the gloomy woods Are touched along their multiformal outlines 104 By a faint gleaming azure, that instils, With a soft clarifying sequence, down Through their dense fleeces of dark-heaping leaves And branches, as away beyond them, now, The dayspring strengthens momently, and spreads Into the morning tide. Then all the trees 104 Eastward disposed,—against the brightening sky Clearly defined to their minutest sprays,— Stand in unspeakable beauty. Moist with dew, 102 And glinting all with a dim silverness, The leaves curve out, or tremblingly depend, From the remoter tracery of the boughs; While interfused through all, the clearing light Keeps steeping all in a diviner glow— Diviner every moment. x for beyond The ruddy face of Morning more and more Is breaking into smiles, to fill the world To overflowing with the joy of brightness. 106 And the great soul of Man! which hence is raised To feel the presence of that greater Soul Which made all Nature; and of which it is Itself an effluence, however far Projected, and divorced by tract of time, Even as a Sunbeam’s fountain is the Sun, Whether it hit the Earth, or glance away Into Infinitude, shooting on forever!
