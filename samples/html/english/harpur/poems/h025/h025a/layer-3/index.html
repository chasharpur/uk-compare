FRAGMENT.

Since thou art dead!—since thou art dead, My Brother, though I see The blue sky bending o’er my head So Full of good showered bounteously; Though scenes of love be round me spread; Though o’er the hills, as once with thee, I wend, me still, with venturous tread, Where through dark woods broad rivers fret And lighten onward to the sea: 98 All, all unchanged, unchanging,—yet How different is the world to me!

You will understand the following fragment: and tell me what you may think of it.

The light as with a living robe Doth clothe the world when night is o’er; The sun with his great golden globe Doth crown the mountain as of yore; The moon and stars o’er watch Earth 99 As I have seen them from my birth: But O thou Light and Sun and Moon, And o ye stars so bright and boon, 100 How great and glorious things you be I feel as I have felt before, What you have all been once to me You never can be more!
