IV.

Love Budded.

The mimosas are blooming, For Summer is coming, I felt her warm breath in the forest to day; 282 Where the river is streaming, And Nature lies dreaming Of new love and beauty,—come, dearest, away!

Where, as infancy clinging, The woodvine is ringing All wildly the boughs of the kerrejong tree, There, like Love’s first feeling, Its joy half concealing, Already the blue-bell is blooming for thee.

His gentle mate wooing, The wood dove is cooing Where tall oaks o’ershadow the path we will take; Come forth then,—well knowing How greatly all glowing And beautiful beings please more for thy sake.

We will wander joy-drinking, Until the sun, sinking, Shall set all the west with his glory a-blaze; When homeward returning With poesy burning, I’ll mint from those splendors a song in thy praise.
