SONNETS DEDICATED TO AUSTRALIAN SENATORS.

NO. XIII.

THE MAN WHO DREAMT A MIGHTY DREAM.

What brought yon broad brimm’d Statesman o’er the sea? I ll tell thee. Once as snug abed he lay, Planning, himthought he heard a something say, Go to Australia R————, and be Whate’r thou wilt! No sooner shalt thou fling Thy Cashy Fortunes on her shores, than Trade Shall make thee mighty in the Land, till made A Senator; and thence, a “Shepherd King”! One morning shortly after, scarcely drest, (So great his haste) unwash’d too, and unkempt, He bundled hitherward; nor yet hath guest That night-cloak’d Something might be he who best With hopes of sway, and cent-per cent, can tempt Even Quakers.—But what funny things are dreamt!

BILL ORR.
