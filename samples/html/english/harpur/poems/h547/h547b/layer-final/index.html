XII.

The Man who dreamt a mighty Dream.

“———in dreaming, The clouds, methought, would open, and show riches Ready to drop upon me; that, when I waked, I cryed to dream again.”

Caliban.

What brought yon broad-brimm’d Statesman o’er the sea? I’ll tell thee. Once as snug a-bed he lay Dreaming, himthought he heard a Something say— Go to Australia, Robinson, and be 192b Whate’er thee wilt. No sooner shalt thee fling Thy cashy fortunes on her shores, than Trade Shall make thee mighty in the Land; till made A Senator—and thence, a “Shepherd King!— One morning shortly after, scarcely drest (So great his haste), unwasht too and unkempt, He bundled hitherward; nor yet hath guest That night-cloaked Something might be Him who best With hopes of sway, and cent-per-cent, can tempt Even Quakers—But what funny things are dreamt.
