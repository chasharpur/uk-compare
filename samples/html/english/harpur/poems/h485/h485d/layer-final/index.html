8

A Royal Child of Destiny

If never by the arms of reason Truth’s last battle may be won, But aye the work of mutual slaughter Men must do as men have done, For Class, for Faction, or for One: Then still to keep the road of progress, Let our chosen Leader be Some bold and matchless Freedom-queller— Some self-raised self-crowned man-compeller, In whom twere base then not to see A royal Child of Destiny.

And if we cannot quit with Tyrants, But by doom must vassals be; Nor bundle, once for all, to heaven, Or elsewhere, that false decree Heraldic aristocracy! Then,—still to keep our right road open, Let us bend at once our knee To some unparagoned Freedom-queller,— Some self-raised self-crowned man-compeller, In whom twere base then not to see A royal Child of Destiny.
