NAPOLEON, THE CHILD OF DESTINY, THE ONLY LEGITIMATE KING.

36

If never by the arms of Reason Truth’s last battle may be won, But aye the work of mutual slaughter Men must do, as men have done, For class, for Faction, or for One: Then,—still to keep the road of progress, Let our chosen Leader be Some bold and matchless Freedom-queller, Some self-raised, self-crowned man-compeller, In whom ‘twere folly not to see The great-souled Child of Destiny!

37c

And if we cannot quit with Monarchs, But by doom must subjects be; Nor bundle, once for all, to heaven Or to hell, the Thing we see Rag-Labell’d “Aristocracy!” Then,—still to keep one right road open, Let us turn, and bend the knee To some unparagoned Freedom-queller, Some self-raised, self-crowned, man-compeller, Like the Child of Destiny.
