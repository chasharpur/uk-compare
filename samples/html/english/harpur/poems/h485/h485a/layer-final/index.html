II.

NAPOLEON, THE CHILD OF DESTINY, THE ONLY LEGITIMATE KING.

If never by the arms of Reason Truth’s great battle may be won, But aye the work of mutual slaughter Men must do, as men have done, For a Faction or for one: Then.—still to keep the road of progress, Let our chosen Leader be Some bold and matchless Freedom-queller, Some self-raised, self crowned man compeller, Like the Child of Destiny.

And if we cannot down with Monarchs, But by doom must subjects be; Nor bundle, once for all, to heaven Or to hell, the Thing we see Labell’d “Austocracy!” Then,—still to keep one right road open, Let us turn, and bend the knee To some glorious Freedom-queller, Some self-raised, self-crowned, man-compeller, Like the Child of Destiny.

Note.—Have I no musical countryman? Is there no bold, free-spirited, republicanised Australian who can compose good music? If Australia has such a son, let him at once clap the wings of melody to these songs of mine, and others that I will write for him, and thereby waft them over the land of their inspiration. Aided thus far, they will fulfil of themselves all the rest of their mission;—it is their own work. And then let the fools and pretenders in Macquarie-street make what laws they please. Let them calve all sorts of John Bullish constitu- tions too, for a time, and half a time.

C. H.
