Poems in early Life by Charles Harpur

Vanished Away

Heavens face was starred as now, When homeward, long ago, I stood: Blest.—haveing won my Dora’s vow To love me next to God! And as homeward then I went Upon that happy far night, Up into the firmament My glowing spirit rayed, and lent Even to the star-light.

Years are gone—and Dora now Is gone, too, under the green sod: No! gone to Paradise with her vow To love me next to God! And as here aloft I gaze As on that happy far night, Heaven’s face is all ablaze, And up my spirit, yearning, rays— But above the Star-light.
