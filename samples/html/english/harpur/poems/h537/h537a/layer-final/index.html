NO. II.

A WINDY SUBJECT.

The Devil, astride on a Brickfielder, drew Suddenly up, just as the sand had beat Into the doors of half Macquarie-street, And turning with a grin to Belial, who Is ‘counted Hell’s best punster,—asked, “Can you Now tell me what s more windy than the wind?” Belial, who sat a little space behind Whistling, was mute at this, look’d posed,—then blew His nose; and from the question just was sliding, With—”Sire, I never joke when out a riding——’ When, luckily, he mark d a furious stir Pass throngh our ”Patriots,” and, with a sneer, Pointing to one of them, replied—”’Tis clear The Thing more windy must be WINDY—ER!”
