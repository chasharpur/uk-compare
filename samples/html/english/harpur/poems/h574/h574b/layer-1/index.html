III.

An Australian Night Scene.

Let us go forth, Belovëd! for the Night Is Beautiful: th maize-embattle fields, And orchard-bosomed homes, and zoning all, The old and solemn woods Float in a silver sea Poured from the fountains of the silent Moon, Who lingering full orbed on her hill-top Seems on some giant head A casgue of figured gold. On Earth’s uplifted head.

Heaven’s joyous multitudes are crowding forth, To hail their coming Queen, and then to fade In stellar music round her pathway pale: But over all beneath Sage Silence reigns supreme; Save when, at solemn intervals, there hangs Within the viewless tresses of the breeze, An accent of the prone And distant-gleaming brook.

Then let us forth, Beloved! So, our Walk Expects thee. On its bough-enwoven ares, And trellised borders, wreathed with vines, the dews In constellations wait The reign of thy blue eyes— Twin moons of Love! presiding nightly there Over a little yet an ample world Of pure and gentle joy! A World! and all our own.
