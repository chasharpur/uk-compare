STANZAS.

“The gayest, happiest attitude of Things.”

This World how bright! Or when its Day with sunny beams is boon, Or when its Night Is robed with stars, and crowned with the moon.

How fair and sweet! With many a flowery Bank and shady Grove, Where young hearts meet To melt into each other’s dream of Love.

How glad and true! With Mothers yearning o’er their cradled joy; And Fathers who Amid their boys put on again the Boy.

How frank and brave! As the abode of some who forth would throng And die to save Whate’er is manly from the thralls of Wrong.

How wise and rich! Temples possessing by the Muses blest; And Graves in which Sages and patriots, bards and martyrs, rest.

Here then, my Soul, Pause, and forbear on things less bright to seize! Why search the scroll Of Fate aught further?—be thy peace with These.
