13

The Hand of God.

If with a philosophic quest Creation be surveyed. The Hand of God must be confest In all that he has made.

We see it through the simplest means Attaining subtlest ends, Where nothing wrongly supervenes, And nothing vainly tends.

We see it dimly, it may be, But there it is—the line That ever points to unity In manifold design.

As when a living leaf we place Between us and the sun, All through it, with a vein-like trace, The life is seen to run.

All ribbing with preordained skill From one continuous spine It runs—but into union still Through every branching line
