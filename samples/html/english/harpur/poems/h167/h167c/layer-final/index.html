Lyrics. By Charles Harpur.

The Hand of God.

When with a philosophic quest Creation is surveyed, 97 The Hand of God must be confest In all that he hath made.

We see it through the simplest means Attaining subtlest ends, Where nothing wrongly supervenes, And nothing vainly tends.

We see it,—dimly, it may be, But there it is—the line That ever points to unity In manifold design.

As when a living leaf we place Between us and the sun, All through it, with a vein-like trace, The life is seen to run:

Seen ribbing from one central spine In modes so surely fit, As still to be, through every line In final union knit.Yea, 98

Yea, thus, when with a heedful quest Creation is surveyed, The Hand of God must be confest In all that he hath made.
