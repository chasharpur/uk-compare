XVII.

Rosa and the Evening Star.

The stars were lit in heaven, and we did rove, Together in the cool and shadowless haze Of early night, at their wild lights to gaze, Ere yet the Moon, whose rim was now above An eastern hill, should dim them. Soon did move In both strange sympathies, as though their rays Were memories of bright eyes that in the days Of the still Past, looked heavenward in their love. Distinguishingly then our thoughts did run From some rare planet to one yet more rare: But fixed at length, by mute consent, on one That burned in the deep west beyond compare: And thence we deemed that when the day was done, The spirit of our joy resided there.
