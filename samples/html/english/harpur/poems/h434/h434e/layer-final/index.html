IX.

[RECORDS OF A POET’S LOVE XVII]

Night was new throned in heaven, and we did rove— Nora and I—in the cool shadowless haze, At the wild lustre of the stars to gaze. Ere yet the moon’s red rim had showed above The pine trees. For in both our souls did move The same poetic fancy—That their rays Were richer for all those who from the ways Of Man’s long past had looked at them in love. And when our glances through their midst had run From orb to orb of all that seemed most fair, To fix at last, with mutual heed, on one That gloried in the West beyond compare, We farther deemed, than when the day was done The Spirit of our joy glowed mansioned there.
