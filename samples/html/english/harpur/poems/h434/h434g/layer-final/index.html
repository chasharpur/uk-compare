A Love-Fancy.

Night was new throned in heaven, and we did rove Together in the cool and shadowless haze That thickened round, at the wild stars to gaze Ere yet the moon’s red rim had showed above The pine trees. For in both our souls did move The same poetic fancy—That their rays Were richer for all those who from the ways Of Man’s long past had looked at them in love. And when our glances through their midst had run From orb to orb of all that seemed most fair To fix at last, with mutual heed, on one That gloried in the West beyond compare, We farther deemed, that when the day was done The spirit of our joy glowed mansioned there.
