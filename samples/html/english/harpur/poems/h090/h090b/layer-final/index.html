The Death of Sisera.

When the prophetess Debora ruled in God’s land, And Sisera died under Jael’s white hand, His Mother looked forth at the close of the day, When the roar of the battle had melted away:

And she cried, Still his chariot tarries afar! Are its wheels clogged about with the slaughter of war? And her Damsels made answer, Awhile yet they stay To trample the fallën, dividing the prey!

Day shut—and the stars that had doomed him to death Rushed out while to listen she pent in her breath: But the sound of his chariot over the plain, Like a far roll of thunder, came never again!
