Journeying Away.

Once, did I only stand in thought beside The grave of one who for his Country died; Or on a spot made holy by the vow Of tuneful Love, though of an ancient day, My very life would thrill—and am I now Journeying away From that fraternal interest which cast Around me then the feeling of the Past? I know not—but no longer thrills my brow Even when, as erst, I voice dame Memory’s lay: Bad progress is it, if from that I’m now Journeying away. x

x Though our early admiration of whatever is glorious and beautiful in the humanity of the past, may ever remain intact within [unclear] us, it soon ceases to act upon our feelings so exquisitely as at first, or rather our feelings soon cease to react so thrillingly under it—and these lines are the expression of this sensational 43 decay. I speak in them of the effect that even the imaginary pr oximity of the grave of a Hampden (for instance) had once upon my being; but this Sense of the Past manifested itself still more singularly. But I can now muse o’er such matters with marvellous composure. Is the Bard dying in my bosom?
