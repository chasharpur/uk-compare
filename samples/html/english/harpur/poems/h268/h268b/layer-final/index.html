Gramachree.

She said her heart was only mine, And so to hear her say, Would make mine own to bound for joy Ev’n like a fawn in play; But now her scorn that once glad heart Is wounding all the more! O Gramachree! O all to me! Core of my bosom’s core!

And all the lyric feeling I In Passion’s morning dream, Poured from my soul around her like An Eden-circling stream, 295 Returned upon its source, o’erwhelms The life it buoyed before! O Gramachree! O all to me! Core of my bosom’s core!

I know how sweet a spirit, Love With morning smile can start, To make earth’s rudest region seem A heaven for the heart! But why should aught so glad e’er make Life’s after woes the more! O Gramachree! O all to me! Core of my bosom’s core!

The hunted roe, when reached his rock, May bid his anguish cease; The plundered dove can flee away And be again at peace; But I, where’er I fly, from grief Can shelter find no more! O Gramachree! O all to me! Core of my bosom’s core!
