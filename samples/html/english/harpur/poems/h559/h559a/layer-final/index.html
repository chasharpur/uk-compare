LITERARY REGISTER.

EARLY SUMMER.

Chasing cold Winter’s dull sadness, Summer is come in her gladness! The Sky how serene! And the Hills how green! And see, my Mary, ‘Neath the vigorous beams Of the Sun, how the Streams Dazzle abroad! e’en as Spencer’s dreams Wandering blaze through the Realms of Faery.

The echoing Valleys and Rocks, How they ring with the life of the flocks! And how sportful the noise Of the Gambolling Herds! And hark to those little Boys Gleeing like birds, As they roll o’er the grass in their fun! As they roll o’er the grass in their fun!

And the Flowers,—they all but rejoice Aloud in their blooming delight! And the sprout-bright Trees Seem every one, With the Vines amid these That tress down on the sight, As their features dilate in the glad-coming breeze, A laughing with all their might! A laughing with all their might!

And the Birds, as the’d never have done, Are carolling over their happy employ! And the Bees are abroad in their murmurous mirth Wherever a balm-breathing blossom has birth, To plunder but not to destroy! And the beautiful Lizards, how deftly they run Over the pathway! and see, in the sun, How the Grasshoppers beat their wings for joy! How the Grasshoppers beat their wings for joy!

CHARLES HARPUR.
