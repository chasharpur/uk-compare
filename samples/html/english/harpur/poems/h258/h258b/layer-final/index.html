Love Budding.

I sang her bright songs,—not of splendor and fashion, But of Love when his nectar he sips; And I looked all the while fond appeals to the passion, And my tongue too kept making fond trips; Till I passion grew secretly big at her heart— Till I saw it flash forth in her glances, and part Like a ripe double cherry her lips.

Then gently I drew her yet nearer unto me, When she made—oh, the aptest of trips! For it brought us so close that her presence thrill’d thro’ me,— Did that luckiest, aptest of slips! Waking such a wild fondness, I could not but kiss Her ruby rich mouth all a-tremble with bliss! And her heart came away with my lips.
