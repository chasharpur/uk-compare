III.

Josephine.

The suns of many years have rolled away; And at the extremity of a small cove Of lake-like quietude, whose skiey depths Reflect with trembling truth the smallest clouds That fret the dome of heaven, and all its shores, Rock-roughened, but with wild vines overlaced, Or where a level space occurs, with canes Embattled, or with orange groves all bright, With golden globes,—their fruitage;—at its head Where the small ripple just is heard to lasp At regular intervals along a strip 601 Of white beach, crescent shaped, whose waveward edge Is fringed with rustling reeds; a sloping dell Profuse of every plant and spicy shrub Known to the Indian Isles, invites our steps With a full promise of delight:—invites, To disappoint, if melancholy thoughts, Given instead, upon the hapless fate Of one sweet Being, who, in the dim past, Beneath the bright blue heavëns whereon we then upturn our eyes so tearfully, was wrecked /In body in those waters—but in soul/ In depths unfathomable of her own Most precious love,—be held a disappointment. For there, ‘twixt two old trees whose boughs o’erhead Depend in mingled tresses, and wherein Birds of incredible beauty love to build Their downy nests, and every passing air, Panting, and laden like a bee, is caught, And its gentle struggle for escape, Compelled to shake its balmy plunder down;— There, gleaming in the broken rays, we find A low white Tomb, and on its lid inscribed A name, familiar through that Summer Isle’s Oldest and sweetest ditties—Josephine.

Note. The above Fragment was written as a conclusion for a Poem to be founded on a story in one of Marriatt’s Novels. I at first intended to write the piece in blank verse; but yielding to the opinion of a friend who thought that an irregularly rhymed dress after the fashion then so much in vogue, was better adapted to the subject, I attempted such a version of the intended conclusion, of which the Fragment immediately following is all that I have thought worthy of preservation, and it chiefly for the sake of the last two lines.
