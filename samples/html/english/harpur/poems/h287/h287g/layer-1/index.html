Memory’s Genesis.

How few with Memory musing mute, (However hopefuly resolute) Can view the backward scope where first Their youth rejoiced—for ever crossed— And mourn not, as did Adam erst, The Eden they have lost! Nor feel, alas! with it compared, The Present but a lengthening wild Whereon young Passion never fared, Young Beauty never smiled!

Yet ’tis a melancholy pleasure To sit by lonely Memory’s side, And list Regret’s wild lyre remeasure The story of our youthful pride! Hours recalling, ah! how rife With emotions lavished wide Through the Garden of our Life Ere all its spring-time roses died; And (like those splendors, when the sun Remits, in rising, from adorning: A robe of glory for the Morning) Fancy’s Elysiums, one by one, Had faded, as the toilsome day came more on.

Yes! tis a melancholy sweet: And thus with Memory, more and more, List we her firstborn’s lyre repeat In dreamy measures wildly meet, Life’s earliest tale; that to the core Retempered by such generous lore, Our sometime hardened hearts may learn 614 Even through its childish faiths, may yearn With some sad love tow’rds Hate itself, and treat With god-like pity what they scorned before.
