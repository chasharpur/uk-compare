Memory’s Genesis.

How few with Memory musing mute, (However hopefully resolute) Can view the backward scope where first Their youth rejoiced—for ever crossed— And mourn not, as did Adam erst, The Eden they have lost! Nor feel, alas! with it compared, The Present but a lengthening wild Whereon young Passion never fared, Young Beauty never smiled!

Yet ’tis a melancholy pleasure To sit by lonely Memory’s side, And list Regret’s wild lyre remeasure The story of our youthful pride! Hours recalling, ah! how rife With emotions lavished wide 56 Through the Garden of our life Ere all its spring-time roses died; And like cloud-splendours, when the sun Remits in rising, more and more, From wearing for his morning Bride A robe with glories blazoned o’er, Fancy Elysiums one by one, Had faded as our toilsome noon came on.

Yes sweetly sad; ’tis sadly sweet: And thus with Memory, more and more, List we her first-born’s lyre repeat In dreamy measures wildly meet, Life’s earliest tale; that to the core Retempered by such generous lore, Our else world hardened hearts may learn Even through their childish faiths, to yearn With some sad love tow’rds Hate itself, and pour The balm of Pity alone on what they scorned before.
