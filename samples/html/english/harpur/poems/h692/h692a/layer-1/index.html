Women as Glorifyers.

Insensate as the roarings of a sea Are the loud praises of the common herd Of mortals,—that unleavened lump, that yields To grey Oblivion such a waste of life; So little does it know aright, the scope And nature of the Noble and the True: And yet the sanction of this herd is that Which with a Woman is the list of glory! The hubbub of applause, the fashion, fame And honors foll[ow]ing a great achievement, Is what she alon can accredit,— The worth and virtue (silent it may be) That are its essence:—these are nought to her.
