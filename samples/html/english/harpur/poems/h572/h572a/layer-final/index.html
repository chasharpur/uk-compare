X.

The Honesty of Herohood.

Straightforwardness, in act and word and thought, No matter whither the effect may tend, Is the heroic element, full fraught Of Manhood,—and of Truth’s effectual Friend.

Note. No habitually crafty,—no sinuously designing man can ever be intrinsically 85 wise or great;—can ever be a hero in the unvulgar and rational sense of the term. Much of the reason wherefore is obvious. By a bias of heart and intellect, either innate or superinduced by education, such a man will always prefer the crooked to the straight; the specious to the truthful; the subtleties of art to the simplicities of nature.

And this crafty bias, renders at length, even our mere perceptiveness over-cunning and self-deceptions /so to speak/. Hence the profoundest mistakers of moral causes are the super-subtle of both sexes; they are always disposed to dive deeper after the principles of our conduct than there is any occasion for. The cork that floats on the surface, and the pebble that sinks below, are equally sought for by such gentry in the mud at the bottom.

Simple directness of nature is even in Things, as well as in Men, the prime characteristic of real greatness—of effectual truthfulness. Whatever is circuitous, no matter how it may be pretexed by expediency, is always more or less fraudulent— or merely specious at the best. Hence no System of religion can be the true one—can be wholly of God, which sanctions, or even permits, but one pious fraud:— one subterfuge, either for its maintenance or propagation. Nor can the man be other than an impostor, who adheres to such a System for a moment after he has become aware of the fact, that it does sanction, or even countenance, any dishonesty of the kind.
