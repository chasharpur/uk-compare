XXXVII.

The Wrongs of Poland. X

Since every drop of wrong-shed blood that cries Out of the lapse of ages, at the throne Of God is heard, still present; since the lies Of fraudulent Power are mortal, and the groan Of patriot grief smites not as against stone At the world’s heart, though hard; and since the sighs Of exiled Love roofed only by the skies, Must weary Mercy with perpetual moan: And Since all Right, though earth-born doth belong 267 To heaven as well, bowed Poland yet shall cast Far off the thraldom she hath borne so long; Calling retributive thunders from the Past To urge her cause, and swell the trumpet blast Blown forth by Freedom trampling prostrate Wrong!
