“Farewell.”

We love, and thence are blest in plenteous measure; Or making a true friendship, we rejoice In the peculiar fitness of our choice;— Then blow the blasts of Fate, and all our pleasure Dies with a word—Farewell! Alas! what tears Dimming the eyes of beauty, day by day Follow its sound? what sorrows, making grey The head of manhood even more than years? Pale wraiths of dreams that perished in the past— Of hopes that in our better days we knew; Grey memories of joys too bright to last, And of the dead—the early loved—the true— Even as we muse of it, as through a spell, Come shadowing round us with that word—Farewell!
