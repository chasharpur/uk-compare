Miscellaneous Sonnets.

“Adiew.”

We love, and we are blest in over measure; We give our hearts to Friendship, and rejoice In the peculiar fitness of our choice; Then blow the blasts of Fate—and all our pleasure Dies in one word—”Adiew!” And exquisite tears Dimming the eyes of pining Beauty, still Attend its utterance, with such pangs of will As wither Manhood even more than years! What shapes of Joys that faded in the Past— Of Dreams that in our better days we knew; 646 What memories of Hopes too bright to last— Of absent Friends;—the dead—the changed—the true— Even while we write it, from afar and fast, Throng shadowy round that little word—”Adiew.”
