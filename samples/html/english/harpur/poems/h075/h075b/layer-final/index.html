POEMS BY CHARLES HARPUR.

CONTEMPORARY PRAISE.

No hero is the Man who draws His breath of life through others’ lungs, Nor is he wise who deems applause But valid, when on others’ tongues.

For the heroic soul, if not All satisfied, is yet sustained By its own sense of strength in what By strength may be attained.

And knowing this, and knowing too How fondly men will praise amiss, The judgment of our subtlest view Should aye, methinks, be this:

When those familiar with thy fame, As watchers of its rising rays, Dare not to censure thee for shame, And yet withhold their praise;

Accept the omen, and begin To feel more confident and free, As nigh the being all ’tis in Thy destiny to be:

For thou hast come too near them then; They feel thy overshadowing might, And with the claims of lesser men Would shut thee from their sight.
