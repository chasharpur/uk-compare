Humanly Creative or Artistic Truth.

Beauty in the mind’s youth, Is but a simple uncompounded birth Of open, obvious Truth— Both rustic dwellers of the common earth. But when abroad the younger Spirit roams, Creatively to view All nature,—then the child itself becomes Truth’s mother too.

Note. Whatever has an enduring beauty in Literature and Art, must answer to some want of our moral nature, or bear upon some common phase of our mental constitution; and must become therefore, with the moral and intellectual progression of the race, 111 more and more the Truth: because, in the next place, the human mind not only acts according to the reception of its matter, but it grows and m[u]scularises also, in proportion to its activity, and in those directions most in which it is the most active. Hence it modifies, beautifies, enlarges, in conformity with and according to its own artistic creativeness. Hence too, in the process of time, not only does the mind become enlarged in its capacity for the perception of Truth and Beauty, but it becomes fruitful as well, to their progressive and proportionable enlargement.
