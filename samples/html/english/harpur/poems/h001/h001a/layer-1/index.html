Poems by Charles Harpur

I

Abed el Kadah.

Abed el Kadah the magnificent! The wealthiest of his people! So for long Men called him;—but, alas! a hideous change Had darkened o’er the fortunes of the man. Wind, water, fire, with all their accidents, Warred on the garners of his hoarded wealth, His harvests fell with blight; of murrain died His cattle; and the hinds that erst had served So rich a master with obsequious heed, Fled from him now,—and from the mighty woe That like the shadow of some terrible cloud Had settled on his home, and even his name,— As from the wrath of Heaven. And when at length, Of all forsaken, in the deadly dark He stood alone with Ruin and Dismay, Thus, in his soul, with scrupulous piety, He moralised a matter long foregone.

“Once on a time when supplicating heaven For mercy, notwithstanding many sins, And much that asked long-suffering divine, I raised my hand against a venomous fly That stung me on the forehead as I bowed, And crushed it! Woe is me! at such a time To slay that creature, though so base and brute, Was in the moral import of the act (As now I see it in the light of thought In all the dread significance wherewith It pointed to the heart’s habitual state) 542 Most flagrant, as most ignorant of God, The merciful Father of all sentient life: A sin including many sins; and thence Of mystic magnitude enough to draw Behind it, scorpian-like, the deadly curse Which hath so surely followed me and mine!

“What though the Prophet hat nowhere hath declared Aught like it reprobate, or of a kind To incur God’s signal wrath, and thence evoke The storms of Fate;—yet, as at such a time Committed—yea, by one bowed down in prayer For mercy from the merc! in heaven The impious fierceness of the act itself Should have been, even to my natural mind, So patent and pronounced, as to have kept The flames of anger, though provoked indeed, Yet innocent of life!”

In darkness, thus, He moralised that matter long foregone: To show this only,—that his life had been, Like pious [unclear]‘s, and that he had indeed But little to accuse himself withal. And to make this, too, patent x Whose rage had whelmed his worldly weal, and still Bent on his head, were missioned [unclear] Not against him and his, but had their path’s scope In the world’s structure—even that wherein He and his fortunes happened then to be.
