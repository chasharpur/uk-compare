POEMS BY CHARLES HARPUR.

I.

Cora.

Of Cora with the shining hair Would mournful Memory sing, Of how she came when flowers were fair, To leave us with the Spring.

That day whereon she first drew breath, And that whereon she died, Amid the pangs of Birth and Death Went blooming as a bride.

The Spring it came, the Spring it went, Nine times it went and came, Till with her all its beauty blent, And breathed her gentle name.

Her fairness only might be thought, Or told if hearts could speak, So subtle were the hues that wrought Within her lucid cheek;

And with a many-branching charm, About her neck as well, Went like the veins of light that warm A lily of the dell.

Her burnished locks, so bright they shone, Were precious to behold, And fell in their profusion down Like bursting sheaves of gold:

Bright garments of a spirit bright, That even in the shroud Were like the sunset’s golden light Within a lifeless cloud!

Her full-orbed eyes of heaven-deep blue Befringed with glossy jet, Were richly tender as a dew- Anointed violet.

A

nd for her hair’s abundant dower Her eyes more sweetly ray’d, As sunshine in a shady bower Is lovelier for the shade.

B

ut with the Spring we saw depart Those eyes, those tresses curled, And Summer, dying in Love’s heart, To Winter left the world!

I

n vain the dews of heaven are shed Where blight hath been before; So vainly weep we o’er the Dead, Yet only weep the more.

A

h! they are ever most undone Who best conceive delight; And even the Angels of the Sun Behind them leave the night.

O

r should the bright time of her birth And death instruct our pain, That like the Spring, though not on earth, Our Joy shall bloom again?
