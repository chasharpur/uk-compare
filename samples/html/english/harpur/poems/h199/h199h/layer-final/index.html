II.

[INTELLECTUAL GREATNESS]

His mind alone is great, who (though but one) Doth venerate of present things or past The absolute only—as the liege of none Save God and Truth. Who awed not by this vast And shadowy scheme of life, but anchored fast In Love, and sitting central, like the sun, So gives his mental beams to pierce and run Through all its secrets while his days may last. And thus progressive, little faith ha[t]h he For mysteries—till, sounding them, he hear The gathered tones of their stirred depths agree With that religious harmony severe, Which ever anthems, to his spirit’s ear, The hallowing presence of the Deity.

Euroma, September, 1866.
