VI.

Intellectual Greatness. ☞

His mind alone is great, who (though but one) Venerates only, of present things or past, What he believeth good,— the liege of none 28 Save God and Truth. Who awed not by this vast And shadowy scheme of life, but anchored fast In love, and sitting central, like the sun, So gives his mental beams to pierce and run Through all its secrets while his days may last. And thus progressive, little faith hath he For mysteries, till sounding them, he hear The gathered tones of their stirred depths agree With that religious harmony severe, Which to his spirit’s esoteric ear Anthems the presence of the Deity.
