Companion Pieces.

XIII.

Her Presence

Why tower my spirits, and what means this wild Disturbance at my heart?—this dreamy chase Of possible joys that glow like stars in space? Now feel I even to all things reconciled, For all seem one in spirit. Rudely uppiled Brown hills, grow beautiful; a novel grace Exalts the moorland’s once uncared-for face; The river that, like a pure mind beguiled, Grows purer for its errors, and the trees That fringe its margin with a dusky shade, Seem robed in fairy wonder: and are these Thus raised, to me, because with me surveyed By one sweet soul whom well they seem to please,— Here, at my side;—an almost stranger Maid?
