Records of a Poet’s Love.

I. ☞

Why tower my spirits, and what means this wild Disturbance at my heart?—this dreamy chase Of possible joys, glowing like stars in space? Now feel I ev’n to all things reconciled, For all seem one in spirit. Rudely up-piled Brown hills, grow beautiful; a novel grace Exalts the moorland’s dull and fruitless face; The river that, like a pure mind beguiled, Grows purer for its errors, and the trees That fringe its marges with a dusky shade, Seem robed in fairy wonder: and are these Raised thus, to me, because with me surveyed By one sweet soul whom well they seem to please, Here standing at my side;—an almost stranger Maid?
