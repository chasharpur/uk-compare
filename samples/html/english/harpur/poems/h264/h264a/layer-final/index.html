A Bower by Moonlight.

It is steeped rich in moonlight, this summer Alcove,— A fairer was never the temple of Love. ‘Tis roofed by the boughs of the greenest-leaved trees, And fenced with all bushes beloved by the breeze For their beauty of blossoms and the odour that swells like a spirit of song from their eloquent bells: And Jessamine hyacinths amaranths rare, Those balmy souled daughters of Summer, are there; And the dew-drops besprinkle the rose in its pride Like tears on the cheek of a too-happy Bride.
