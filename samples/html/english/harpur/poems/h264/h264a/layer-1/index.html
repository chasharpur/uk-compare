A Bower by Moonlight.

Come see thee by moonlight our chosen Alcove,— A fairer was never the temple of Love. ‘Tis roofed by the boughs of the greenest-leaved trees, And fenced with all bushes beloved by the breeze For their beauty of blossoms and the odours that part From them, like the sweetness of song from the heart: And violets, bluebells, and hyacinths rare, Those balmy souled daughters of Summer, are there; And the dew-drops besprinkle the rose in its pride Like gems on the brow of a blushing young Bride.
