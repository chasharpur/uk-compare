The Bower by Moonlight.

It is steeped rich in moonlight, yon Summer alcove And a fairer was never the Temple of Love It is roofed by the boughs of the greenest leaved trees And draped with those runners that most by the breeze Are beloved by the odor so sweet, that upswells Out of still shady places and dim forest dells, Like a spirit of song from their fair-rung bells, And mingled with pansies, wild amaranths rare, Those balmy-souled daughters of Flora, are there, With blooms that empurple the light, and the gay And lady like lily in richest array; While dewdrops besprinkle the rose in its pride Like tears on the cheek of a too happy Bride.

The leaf-glancing boughs of the trees over-doming Now seem in wild dance to the pipe of the roaming Night breeze, as aloft in the waves of its flow That as suddenly come and as suddenly go, With a clash and a clasp as in merry despite, They mass into shadow or quiver in light; When, cut by their motion, the slanting moonbeam Falls lifted, like coin, on the floor of the stream That goes murmuring by—a continuous theme Of lowly and mellow delight, that is heard As were in a dream by sweet fairy-spells stirred. Or at times, the breeze streaming in lengthened escape, Holds open the sprays for some steadier shape Or the radiance to spread ore the stream for a fleet Flying moment—no more—in a glittering sheet: And then too bright fragments of Night’s ripest blue Releiving the leaf-work come transciently thorough, 36 And broad stars seem glowing as seen among these Like apples of fire in the tops of the trees.

It is steeped rich in moonlight this Summer alcove, It is heapy with beauty around and above, While like a queen gem encased by the whole Is the Spirit of Love—its impassioning Soul.
