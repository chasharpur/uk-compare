A Bower by Moonlight.

It is steeped rich in moonlight, yon Summer alcove— A fairer was never the temple of Love. It is roofed by the boughs of the greenest leafed trees, And draped with those runners that most by the breeze Are beloved for the odour so sweet that upswells Out of still shady places and dim forest dells, Like a spirit of song from their fairy-rung bells; And jassamines, hyacinths, amaranths rare, Those balmy souled daughters of Flora, are there, To engolden the light—and there too, is the gay, And lady-like lily in richest array, And dew-drops be-sprinkle the rose in its pride, Like tears on the cheek of a too-happy Bride.

The leaf-glancing boughs of the trees overdoming Now seem in wild dance to the pipe of the roaming Night breeze, as aloft in the waves of its flow That suddenly come and as suddenly go, With a clash and a clasp, as in merry despite, 416 They mass into shadow or quiver in light; When, cut by their motion, the slanting moon beam Falls sifted, like corn, on the floor of the stream That murmurs thereby—a continuous theme Of mellow and lowly delight that is heard As it were in a dream by sweet fairy-spells stirred;— Or, at times, the breeze straining in lengthened escape, Holds open the sprays for some steadier shape Of the radiance to spread o’er that stream for a fleet Flying moment—no more—in a glittering sheet; And then too bright fragments of night’s ripest blue Relieving the leaf-work come transciently through, And broad stars seem glowing as seen amid these Like apples of fire in the tops of the trees.

It is steeped rich in moonlight, this Summer alcove, It is heapy with beauty around and above, While like a Queen-gem thus enchased by the whole Is the Spirit of Love—its impassioning Soul!
