Rhymes, Humourous Poems, Epigrams &c.

The Great Gun of the Australian-Crimean-War-Fund, brought suggestively to a queer Test.

Squire Sham, with many duties lying Under his very feet unfound, After a deal of internal sighing To think of other Shams outvying So great a Sham as he—(’twas trying!) Thumped down Fifty Pound! Several more, with Mort the thrifty, Gave five times Fifty! One Bank down thundered Twice that—Five Hundred! But to its swelling store Sir Daniel Cooper flung a Thousand! And had a beggar flung a Louse and Nothing (not a nit even) more! I wonder which might be proved to be The greater patriot—Dan or he: Since by the test of numbers nice, The knight were possibly a man Far richer even in Thousands than Any one beggar is in Lice?
