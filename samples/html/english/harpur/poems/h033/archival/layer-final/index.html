THE BATTLE OF THE WINDS.

Tune—”Judy O’Flannigan.”

A mighty pother and dust, Of late was raised in Sydney, About a Corporate Bill, Or something of that kidney. Such a row Was never in these dominions; Nor yet, I vow, Such difference of opinions.

The first and furious gust Was blown by Boreas-Venty, Who roared in the index so high, The welkin he had rent nigh.

His puffs were a rigmarole, Or everything jumbled together, To make folks blind as a mole, Or whirl their minds hither and thither.

But up rose Zephyrus-Bland, More mildly treated the matter, The subject he had in hand Dismissed soon was in a clatter.

His speech you might compare Unto an anodyne potion, Or mixture made with care Of a detergent lotion.

Lo Eurus Will—soon arise, On one leg he can stand, sir, With it kick the bill to the skies, Quite out of council and land, sir.

So have you seen on one peg, A goose stand, and cry “quack!” sir, But if he should spurn with that leg, On his beam-ends he soon would come whack, sir.

Now Notus My-dear-man blew hard In Boreas’ teeth such a rattler, The natives amazed were and stared At such a hardy tough battler.

The winds all at once grew so strong, And raised round his head a great racket; But he minded the noise of the throng No more than a tar an old jacket.

The trade winds long slumbering now blew, And steadily kept without veering; The captain and pilots well knew What course the ship should be steering.

Poor in wealth, rich in sense, like the man By wisdom who saved his city: If not remembered each one It will be a very great pity.
