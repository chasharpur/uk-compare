XIX.

Louis Kossuth (in 1850.) X

Thou Man of moral majesty and might, Now that thy Country, bound and bleeding, cries For help in vain, and regnant Austria’s spite Can thy truth with diplomatic lies; Not to despair alone bespeakes thee wise— Bespeaks thee true, thou pleader for the right! For Liberty, an essence born of light, May swoon—may slumber—but she never dies! Thy sacred purpose evermore shall swell The Magyar’s heart till through war-trump given In triumph! thy vast eloquence so well Doth clothe it, and project it up to heaven,— 254 Storming God’s peace, as the still sky is riven By some great mountain in the Land of Tell!
