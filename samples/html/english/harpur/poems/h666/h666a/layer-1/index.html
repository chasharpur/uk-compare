The Voice of the Oak

Who hath laid him underneath An old oak tree by a lonely stream? He hath heard a Spirit breathe Wilder than a Wizard’s charm.

Up in its dusky foliage, tressing Like the hair of a giant’s head, Mournful things beyond our guessing Day and night are utterëd

Yea even when the waveless air Only stirs the lightest leaf, Still that voice keeps moaning there Its mysteries of grief

Even as, there did ever dwell T fallen Spirit Hath taught the very tree to dream Of an eternity of despair:
