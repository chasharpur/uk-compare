THE BOY’S EVENING SONG.

O Sister! see the great red Sun Is half behind the western hills— And the tree-shades seem trying all How far they can stretch!

And all the birds that live afar, Are soaring off in twos and threes; And woodward, from the Farms below, The mouse-hawk comes.

And there, against the very sky, A merry flight of parrots see, As in the Sun’s last upward glance They gleam like fire!

Those birds, they must be happy things, My Sister! and I would that we Together so might glide within The calm cool air.

But yet there is enough for joy Upon the grassy ground for us— Those bloomy vines—those flowery banks— Those fine tall trees!

And hark! the Brook between the stones Is playing like to Father’s flute— I love it! though it ever plays The same old tune.

And now I see its upper pool’s Big gleaming eye, that always seems Up looking, in its placid joy, At Heaven’s blue round.

And there the herbs are ever fresh, And bunched all through with flowers—O come And see me, how I’ll roll adown The soft green bank!

And let us call our Neighbour’s son, The gentle curly-headed Boy That brought us grapes and peaches once, And all kinds of fruit.
