Odes and Lyrics.

Compensation.

A sylphic form and angel face, A young life void of sin— The outward lineaments and grace Of purity within: Such was my spirit’s visioned joy, When I was a dreaming Boy.

A figure rich in pleasant health, A warm heart and a good— The summer attributes and wealth Of thorough Womanhood: Such was my spirit’s yearned-for truth, When I was a lonely Youth.

And in my Manhood it may be, I have not chanced to find Even one who realised to me The Lady of my mind: Yet therefore feel I not as crost— Nothing but a Dream is lost.

While in the power that dreamt, there lives A love-creating ray, Worth all that Fortune ever gives Or ever takes away: Inspiring oft a genial rhyme Even ’mid the snows of Time.
