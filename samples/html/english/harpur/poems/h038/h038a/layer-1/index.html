I question whether the hero of the following draught would not have been more agreeable to a sound and mature feminine taste,—or rather to the natural preference, apart from the artistic appreciation, of such a taste,—if so much of the lion-like appendage proper to a face masculine had been attributed, as would have exhibited him more like Milton’s Michael, whose

“——starry helm unbuckled, showed him prime In manhood, where youth ended.”

A Youth of the Utopian Era.

He stood before his Bride in passion’s purple light, Great Nature’s perfect Child, a Vision boldly bright! High honor sits enthroned on his majestic front, While in the eyes below,—each one a christal font, His being Love embathes, till with their fancy fraught, He springeth from their depths, and lightens out in thought. His sunny hair, like thine, unshorn Apollo, spreads, Ruddy and smooth his cheek as Jove-loved, Gannymed’s, And such his swelling h[unclear] shape, so graceful and so strong, 697 As theirs who witched the daughters when the world was young; When men, all hunters swift, all masterless and bold, On bounded, each a pledge of Adam’s perfe mould. Gentle and true his heart, though free as ocean’s wave, And gentle too his mind;—inevitably brave, He hath no scorn for men, save such as bend the knee To Tyranny or Vice in willing slavery. Complete in mind and form, even such a manly birth As erst our mothers bore, ere Nimrod scourged the earth, And through the unequal toil imposed by gold accurst,— The only bond that Truth and Wisdom cannot burst, The rule of kings and priests, and city-nurtured shame, Man from the imaged God degenerate became.
