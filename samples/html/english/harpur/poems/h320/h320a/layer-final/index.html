5c

II.

Characteristics.

Friend A.

Were he only in earnest as much as he seems; Were his world-part in tune with the music he dreams; If not great, he were lovely—perhaps he were both! As it is, I could probe him—but ah! I am loth.

Friend B.

Were his frame like his mind, he were terribly tall! For this is as large as the other is small: Yet, methinks, such an halo is round its totality, As makes it look larger than ’tis in reality.

Friend C.

A dilettante, deep in law and politics, Versed in all sorts of journalists jolly tricks; Profoundly far gone in œsthetic latitudes, And quite at home in philosophic platitudes. One of his tastes is (all his tastes are healthy ones) To toady great men that are also wealthy ones; If very rich, to hug them till they glow, he’s fain; But if he finds them poor—he lets them go again.
