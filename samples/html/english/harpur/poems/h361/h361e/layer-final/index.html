[ON THE SONNET]

Why slight the Sonnet? Though one thoughted, it May show us,—as by the minutest star The world’s bright order is evinced most far,— Through how much truth may pierce, with mastery fit, One subtle, searching beam of mother wit Shot from a Poet’s ultra brain! Besides the value of no Thing on earth Is in its bulk—but, purely in its grain: As Man’s small body doth more soul engirth Than doth behemoth’s, big as it may be; Or as the tiniest pearl’s of greater worth Than any boulder washed up by the sea. Wherefore, I say, to slight a thing of mind (So long as it is happy in its kind) Because, by well-set verbal steps, to climb it— Or, with our critic watch in hand, to time it— May not ask at least an hour, Is just as tasteless as it were if we Should look with scorn upon a lovely Flower Because ‘twere not a Tree.
