THE SONNET.

Why scorn the Sonnet? Though one-thoughted, it May show us,—as by the minutest star The world’s bright order is evinced most far,— Through how much truth may pierce one subtle spark of wit. Besides the value of no Thing on earth Is in its bulk—but in its quality: Just as Man’s being doth more soul engirth Than doth behemoth’s, big as it may be; Or as the tiniest gem’s of greater worth Than any boulder washed up by the sea. Nor do we lour Upon a lovely flower, Because it happens not to be a tree.
