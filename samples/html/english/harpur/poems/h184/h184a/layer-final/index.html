Song

I’ll get me a new love,—the old is worn out; I will get me a new love ere Spring comes about, That, as to the sunshine the fields all rebloom, My wronged heart may awaken from coldness and gloom.

I’ll get me a new love, less Fortune’s cold slave; I will get me a new love more earnest and brave; Too pure for suspicion, too noble for doubt;— I would get such a new love ere Spring comes about.

There’s nothing in nature like love when ’tis new, And there’s nothing in fortune like love when ’tis true: But Fanny’s was doubtful,—its spell is worn out, So I’ll get me a new love ere Spring comes about.
