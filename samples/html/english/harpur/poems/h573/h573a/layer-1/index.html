73

A Feast for a Demi-God.

Take a flawless porcelain dish Brightly clean as eye could wish: And placing in it full a score Of strawberries red-ripe to the core, And newly gathered,—where espied In the dewy morning tide Through the leaves that sheltered them So freshly on their native stem; From a beaker stored with cream Frosting white above the brim Pour then with unstinting hand Of the lymph so richly bland, What gives the smothered fruit to be Like islets in a milky sea: And lo, before you there’s a feast For a demi-god at least.
