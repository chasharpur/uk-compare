XXXI.

The Disquisition:

or Why “the course of True Love never did run smooth.”

There’s a tentative sequence in the drift Of human life, apportioning the prize (In quality or color, shape or size) That each one seeketh to the seeker’s gift. 65 Hence he must suffer many a perilous shift Who into fame by martial deeds would rise; Hence look on Freedom with an eagle’s eyes Must he who makes Man’s progress yet more swift: Hence heaven’s best crown, more glorious than the sun, Is only gained by dying for our kind; And hence too, True Love’s prize on earth is won Only through agonies of heart and mind. Such, Rosa, is the fate (and therefore ours) Of all whom Love would gift with love’s divinest powers.
