(A Lover’s Day & Night) (A Second Trilogy.) (Companion Pieces. The Keepsake Rose &c Absence in the City)

A Third and last Trilogy.

1.

The Reason.

There is a trying spirit in the drift Of human life, apportioning the prize (In quality or color, shapes or size) That each one seeketh to that seeker’s gift. Hence must he suffer many a perilous shift Who into fame by martial deeds would rise; Hence look at liberty with lion eyes Must he who’d make the march of man more swift: Hence Heaven’s best crown, more glorious than the sun, Is only gained by dying for our kind; And hence too, True Love’s highest meed is won Only through agonies of heart and mind! Such, Rosa, is the fate, (and therefore ours) Of all whom Love would crown with faith’s divinest flowers!
