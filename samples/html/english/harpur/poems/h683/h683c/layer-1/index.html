XXVI.

Wicliffe.

One star in heaven, when the dark mists of earth Have hid all others from the asking eye, As they had forfeited their golden birth In the serene of the eternal sky, Doth keep our else blanked vision lifted high;— A fountain in some desert’s mighty dearth Set newly a flowing, is of sumless worth To all who else had journeyed thirsting by: So through the spiritüal glooms that crept Round England’s altars, Wicliff’s spirit shone; So Faith’s pent waves, by him unsealed, leapt Livingly forth; and therby overflown, Truths that were erewhile barren as a stone Bloomed into beauty and were fruitful kept.
