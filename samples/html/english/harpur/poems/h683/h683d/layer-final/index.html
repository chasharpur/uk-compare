Final Copy.

Wicliffe.

One Star in heaven, when the dank mists of earth Have hid all others from the asking eye, As they had forfieted their golden birth In the serene of the eternal sky, Doth keep our else-blanked vision lifted high; A Fountain in some desert’s mighty dearth Set newly flowing, is of sumless worth To all who else had journeyed thirsting by: 128 So through the ghostly glooms that coil’d and crept Round England’s altars, Wicliffe’s spirit shone; So Faith’s pent waves, by him unseal’d, out leapt In living splendor, and thereby o’erflown, How much that erst was barren as a stone Bloomed into beauty and was fruitful kept?
