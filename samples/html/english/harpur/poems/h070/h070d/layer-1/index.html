XXIII.

COINS AND ROSES.

If, as sage Emerson supposes, Coins are as beautiful as roses, When through that social medium we Regard them, which their need discloses; This follows as conclusively: That any joint-stool were as meet To picture as yon garden seat. Because of its utility! No doubt, the doctrine’s somewhat funny, But what a bloom it gives to money? And how it clothes in Eden beauty, Mere benches, doing kitchen duty!
