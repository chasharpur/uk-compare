As when a f stubborn ass, caught trespassing, Keeps munching still the corn of the tall crop Though many a sturdy stick on his gaunt sides Already hath been taken, and on which although The boys Keep spending all their feeble strength In still repeated thwacks upon his hide sounding; Nor can they; till the brute is cramed with food, Expel him, and with difficulty then: Even as the mighty son of Telemon So the great-hearted Trojans & with them Their far-called allies about Ajax thronged The mighty Ajax,—sorely beating so His stubborn valor, and on every side Stricking his broad shield with the flying shafts As, often wheeling, slid slowly, now he checked their might Now fled himself of him comforting sought The wedging force of the horse-taming Trojans; Then, turning warily, would fly himself
