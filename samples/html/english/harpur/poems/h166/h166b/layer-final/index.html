XLII.

A GUESS AT A WHY?

A bit of mischief’s dear to every one of us: And know we truly why, in that we know Its social opposite could be in none of us So much a merit, if it were not so? All being’s thus evinced:—lightness shows weight; Nothing were little but for something great; Nothing were lofty were there nothing low; And even Love, with its celestial glow, Hath its extreme proof in the flames of Hate!
