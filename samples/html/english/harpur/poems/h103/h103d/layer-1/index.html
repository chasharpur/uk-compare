The Drowned, Alive.

I was once so deeply drowned, That when the drag my body found, Twas void of motion, void of breath, And, to sensation, dead as Death.

In a languid summer mood, Lo, I plunge into a flood, That to the low sun’s slanting beams Gleameth only quiet gleams, Each, with a wide flicker, sheeting Up from its still floor—fast and fleeting. Such a flood as one would say Could never, or by night or day, Have drenched a man’s warm life away.

But what are these, down in its bed, That trail so long and look so red, Moving as in conscious sport— Are they weeds of curious sort?But 96 But I will dive to them, and see Into all their mystery.

Down I dive.—A plenteous crop! Some shall with to t me to the top: For here there is too dim a light, To show their character aright. I wind in my arms, intent To root them up in my ascent: But they resist me, and again I tug them with a stronger strain! Full well, I trow, they hold their own Gripping fast each bedded stone With their tuby roots, that go Thence down, through the stiff slime below. Well, the upshot is, that I Must leave them—But in vain I try! Fierce as lightning on my brain Smites the dread truth—I try in vain! Yea, more and more, in coils and flakes, Like long blood-red water snakes, The deadly things around me clasp, The more I tug, the more they grasp! My pent breath, growing hot and thin, Explodes with a dull booming din, While through my unclenched teeth, the wave Comes drenching! Is there none to save? None near to see—to guess—to trace Under the water’s gleaming face, As by some sudden whirl alone Or swift rush of bubbles shown, The dread extremity of one Thus fastened down? Ah! is there none?Wild 97 Wild as vain my struggles grow— Horror! horror! is it so?

Hope gives up her ghost—Despair! And as a fish dies in the air, In its element deep drowned, I am dying. All around The long weeds, erst so deeply red, Look, even where nearest, grey as lead, As ’mid them, settling down, I sway To and fro, and fast away Life keeps bubbling—bubbling aye Through my cold lips wide agape, And stiffening into that white shape They take at last (when done with breath) In the rigid face of Death.

And now, while sullen drummings make My spirit through mine ears to ake, Life-long memories, interwrought With all I ever felt or thought;— Sacred fancies, hidden long Lest the world should do them wrong— Held back feelings that for years Just below the sourse of tears Have lain perdu, like gems that bide In the bright sands unespied, Over which some Orient stream Floweth with a golden gleam;— Taking instantaneous wings, With a million other things, All at once—(yet each its own Shape keeping—marked, as if alone) All, thick interthronging, press 98 Through my drowning consciousness! All in the same instant, so Do they come, and do they go: Leaving one mixed thought to reign Sole within my failing brain— Full of pity, full of pain! One mixed thought of how my doom Must wrap my Mother in its doom; And give my Sire to hold his breath For anguish, hearing of my death; And to all home relations be Always a dread memory; And wound one fond heart to the core In the wide world evermore: Making Love to her appear Even when alive and near, Full of fate, as full of fear— Or seen through tears by Memory shed, But the wild manes of a dead Ideal of the soul—a glow That, like a sunset, long ago Went down into the dark of woe.

Lastly, a drear stupor, blent With a cold comfortless content, Into one mass of clammy clay Kneads body and mind. Thus drenched away, With one faint shudder, one last throe, Life stagnates, and its shell lies low, Swaying, weed-bound, to and fro, Void of all motion, as of breath,— How die we, if this be not Death?

Ah, what thrilling, thrilling pain Kindles through my heart and brain! Ah, what horrors, vague and fell, Come shadowing o’er me, as from Hell! Ah, those sudden glares of light, They fall like fire-brands on my sight! Ah, what vast and heavy world Is all at once upon me hurled, Massing into one immense Oppression, every tortured sense!

Yes; I now remember well How my sudden fate befell! And are we then, in death’s dread thrall, Thus conscious of our funeral? But where are they who most should mourn When my bier is graveward borne? With her whose face I yearn to see— Where are they? and where is she? Where the crape-trimmed followers all? Where the coffin and the pall? Or do Death and Nature strive Within me?—Is the drowned alive?
