PETRARCHIAN MUSINGS, OR THE HUNDRED SONNETS OF LOVE. BY CHARLES HARPUR.

THE COMPLAINT.

Oh, restless, unacknowledged love! my best Of peace you murder!—Never pen could write How my heart languishes both day and night For one fair being with whom I might be blest. And now, with agonies of doubt possess[e]d, I madden! ah, that such should ever fright Affection’s gentle Hope! or that I might Divine her will—or die and be at rest! Oh, for a brimmer of the wine of love, Or a deep draught of the Lethean wave! The power a mutual passion to emove, Or the forgetfulness that fills the grave! I would not be of beauty thus the slave, But ‘gainst it Solomon as vainly strove.
