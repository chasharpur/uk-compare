All is for the Best.

This world

is but a boastful weakness, Pale with terrors unconfessed; Yet Virtue may not lose her treasurer, And God is power exceeding measure:— Wherefore all is for the best.

The world is mountained o’er with trouble And with danger and distress: Yet all things tend to the supernal, And God is peace—yea, peace eternal:— Wherefore all is for the best.

The world is clouded up with error, And with folly unredressed: 75 Yet even wise men are forecasting, And God is wisdom everlasting:— Wherefore all is for the best.

The world is sowed with lies for lucre— Lies that every heart infest: Yet Fraud is overcome by Duty, And God is truth, and truth is beauty:— Wherefore all is for the best.

Power, peace, wisdom, truth and beauty, These are God—and will attest That Evil must be self-consuming— A bale-fire Final Good illuming:— All then—all is for the best!

But no man should less active be, Believing thus,—as not less free To use his strength, skill, wit, to reach The things attractive unto each: And somewhat good will ever run From all that’s well designed and done, Though out beyond our range it go, (Because by God’s hand prospered so) 76 Or wide of what we aimed at—lest All should not be for the best.
