ALL IS FOR THE BEST.

The world is but a boastful weakness— Pale with terrors unconfest: Yet Virtue may not lose her treasure, And God is power exceeding measure: All then—all is for the best.

The world is mountained o’er with trouble— Cragg’d with danger and unrest: Yet all things tend to the supernal, And God is peace—yea, peace eternal; And then—all is for the best.

The world is clouded up with error— Folly’s suckled at its breast: Yet even wise men are forecasting, And God is wisdom everlasting: All then—all is for the best.

The world is sowed with lies for lucre— Falsehoods every heart infest: Yet Fraud is overcome by Duty, And God is truth, and truth is beauty: All then—all is for the best.

Power, peace, wisdom, truth, and beauty, These are God—and they attest, That Evil must be self consuming, A bale-fire Final Good illuming: And then—all is for the best!

But Man should not less active be, Believing this,—as not less free To use his strength, skill, wit, to reach The things attractive unto each: And something good will ever run From all that’s well designed and done, Though wide of what was aimed at—lest, All should not be for the best.

Charles Harpur.

Sydney.
