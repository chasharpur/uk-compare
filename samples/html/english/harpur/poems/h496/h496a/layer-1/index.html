I knew a very diminutive fellow, who, though he liked poetry very well in general, yet loved one stanza in particular above all the stanzas in the world. It was

this one:

Though I could stride from pole to pole, And grasp all Nature in my span, I should be measured by my soul— The mind’s the stature of the man.

It is almost needless to add that this little-bodied individual believed himself to be large-minded: x In like manner, I doubt not, a swivel-eyed person is perilously disposed to believe in the deformity of eyes that are set very straightly. But it would matter little if this personal bias rested in mere physicals, and ran not also into our moral and spiritual concernments.
