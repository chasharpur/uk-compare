233

To N. D. Stenhouse Esqr.

Kendall had written me: “Alas! I fear Our dear good Friend is breaking”—meaning you; And suddenly mine eyes grew dim and blear, So that I might not read his letter through Until another time. Ah, misery! who Would care to breathe in this Samaria here, When, with our Norton, every worth-won frere He greatly prized were gone—the noble few! ‘Tis much but to have known you—But to say “He is in soul my very friend”, is what I would not be content to have decay Out of the values of my bardic lot, For aught besides my spirit hath begot Of kindred comfort, through my life’s whole way.
