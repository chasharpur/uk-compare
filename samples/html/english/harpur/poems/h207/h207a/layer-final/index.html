7b

JOSHUA.

[From Charles Harpur’s King Saul.]

When Joshua in the days of old Stood forth on Jordan’s western bank, And past the flood that backward rolled His host came dryshod, rank on rank; The warrior Angel of the Lord,— A glorious shining Creature, bared Before him there a flaming sword, And thus the mind of God declared: Lo, I am with you; Here shall dwell My chosen people, Israel.

The walls of Jericho are strong, And ribbed throughout with many a tower; And yet her Monarch’s armëd throng Stands trembling round his throne of power: For circling still those walls about, Behold, the ark of God is borne! Blow trumpets, blow! Shout Israel, shout! T’is done—and from the earth uptorn, At once they shatter and deform Like the rent bastions of a storm.

Five Kings at Gibeon are met, Five mighty Kings of ancient name, And they are boasting there to set A blood red bound to Joshua’s fame! But the sun stands fast on Gibeon’s hill, And the moon is fixed o’er Ajalon, That Israel’s host in vengeance still May thunder like a torrent on! And where are now those Kings? Yon cave Hides them in vain:—it is their grave!

From Merom, hark, there spreads a roar As from a tempest-trodden sea, For confluent there, together pour All Canaan’s might and royalty. But all this war how little worth, When tried by that prevailing sword In Israel’s front far flaming forth— For what are numbers to the Lord? That multitudinous array Is blown like morning mist away.

Thus Israel in the days of old Led on by Joshua and the Lord, Like a devouring tempest rolled Distinction o’er each race abhorred: Each people sold into the hand Of shame, by Him, the only wise, When through iniquity the land Grew leprous in his holy eyes. And thus my harp records in rhyme His wonders of the olden iime.
