26 Evening Haunts

In the cool twilight let me aye Along some ancient River stray, Where the wild oaks in sweeping ranks Befringe the verges of the banks, And in their watery mirror clear All so etherealised appear— Softer than life, as ever seem Loved faces in a silver dream. And then—while the awaking breeze Moans distantly among the trees, And far along the winding stream Where Evening’s imaged star may gleam, Comes on with a bright bickering As ‘twere some thousand-footed thing— So nicely stirred my soul shall be By love of these, and love of the[e] Who art their Angel—Poesy.
