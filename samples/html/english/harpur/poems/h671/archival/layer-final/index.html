V.

The Way of the World and the Remedy.

Who is a Hero, if he is not one, In whose soul, like a banner unfurled, Is the strength of self trust, with a hearty ‘well done!’ For himself, though contemned by the world?

Note.—This is the way of the world: the less you stand in need of help, comfort and encouragement, the readier will it be to aid, attend to, and applaud you. It will grease the fat sow with butter, but leave the lean one to anoint herself with mud. Practically, this is its religion, its morality, its social wisdom: and in this alone is it everlastingly consistent. Well then, what is there, from the centre to the circumference of such a world,—what can there be,—more thoroughly heroic, than a hearty, or even scornful, spirit of self-reliance?
