The Stranger.

‘Tis late—for it is midnight when the Moon Thus through yon casement shows her dream-pale face. No wonder that they worshipped her of old, So mystic in its beauty is her look. Wild and most lonely must his heart have been, Even as mine, who erst in vision brought Her nightly down, Love’s visitant, to him Whose dreams she impassioned in the mountain cave, The Carian boy, Endymion.

—Seems she not Heaven’s lonely Shepherdess, thus keeping watch Over the shining flocks that, clustering, graze Its wide ethereal fields: now gliding forth From some bright-bordered tent of woven cloud, Or gliding in.

Her gentle shape— That blue-eyed Girl’s, whose image comes in dreams And lays a soft and glowing cheek to mine, Turning the while two love-lit mellow orbs To meet the fond enquiry of my look With sweet assurance of a present joy, Albeit for ever in their azure depths Pensive exceedingly; as though they read The final fortunes of my heart—and mourned.

For I had read of Wood Nymphs, and in vision Had limned me bevies of those old Greek-born Divinities of Song,—those deified Humanities of high and glorious Art, Ere the damp influence of a darkened world Had quenched its morning fire,—limned them in hues Of thought so lovely, that my brain hath swum Dizzily, though with a triumphant joy! Till overwrought, it throbbed itself to rest As on the bosom of the Beautiful! But Fancy’s Angels, though thus richly born, Vailed when with her compared,—vailed, even as flowers Plebean, that befreckle forest glades, In posied contrast with that Garden Queen, The damask Rose: or as the image dim Of one who for an age hath been immured From sight of heaven, what time a vagrant ray 285 Fall in through some crevice of his dungeon, hits His faded eye, might paragon with a star Seen in its present glory—not beheld Through his grey memory in the clouded Past.

A breathing spell Fell round her, like an odour,—wildering In its wild joy him who might dare to gaze For his heart’s sake on one so exquisite. A smile like that which might indeed bespeak Aurora, played upon her rosy lips At intervals; and when, on strange approach, It hushed itself, and slumbered on its soft And rubious pillows, still it dreaming seemed, As there potentially, though not in act; Fostering a still air of voluptuous grace— A promissory hint of honeyed joy, About to break its shell. Her azure eyes Were opaline,—two richly mellow depths Of lustre, raying lustres; and the while She spake, a consciousness of pure intent Out of them flowed as from her living soul— As from a wide deep inland sea of truth And utter loveliness; and thence effused Over the whole of her sweet countenance A virgin royalty of mien—a grace So loftily lovely, so alone in power And powerful in its loneness, that it struck Some mystic chord of worship, which, till then, Had never shuddered in the gazer’s soul.

as it then strange, first seeing, thus to love A Being so lovely? And besides, there grew Something that yearned like interest in her eyes When turned on me,—encreasing as she passed Away—perhaps for ever! Heavily then, And damp, or so to me it seemed, the air Closed on the blank space her departure made! I could have called her, but I knew no name! I could have worshipped round her habitation, But none could tell me where the Stranger dwelt! Broad roaring seas may roll between us now For aught I know, or diserts wide expand, Or passless mountains heave.

Yea, see, at times, Slow disengaging from the shaded depth Of yon recess, that Shape, unstepping, seems To float a momen[t] forward, and then stand Solemn and still against the narrowed points Of the dim taper’s rays;—wearing at first An air in which seems blended heaven’s repose And earth’s solicitude; anon, a glow Of rapture, palpably effusing forth, Outlines its lineaments as with a fringe Of spiritual fire: while wholly swayed By its memorial witchcraft, and possessed With such sweet madness as Anchises once Conceived at sight of Venus in the shades Of moon-illumined Ida,—I avoid The admonitions of reality, And worship as some skyey Creature homed With God, in Paradise, this form so fair— This portraiture of Memory’s dreamy art Etherealised by sorrow.

ore than once, When soured by days of sordid toil, through which Advantaged nothing in my future aims, Or present loneliness, I’ve thrown me down To lose in sleep dark thoughts; and sleeping, Fancy Stealing the color of my waking mood Hath thickened it with dreams. Then have I seemed To wander lost under a starless sky, Where pitfalls gaped and ragged ruins hung In threatening indistinctness. All so still At first the scene, that even Silence there 288 Did tingle audibly, and ake around, Intenser every moment,—yea, until So keen the sense of her still presence grew It cut into my soul: When, wondrous change! Through Heaven’s black concave a tremendous storm Hath bellowed in its strength, and I, as though Befated with a wizard’s insight, saw Its guiding Demons from the foremost clouds Unmask, and dart, with bare blue arms, abroad Ten thousand thunders—globes of writhing fire! Till by the sight,—harrowed at first, and then Made daring and defiant, I have smiled In sorrowful pride, to know I bore the brunt Of such wild work—alone! I have smiled—and lo, From a deep fissure in the cloudy mass That hung aloft its lurid canopy, The blue-eyed Stranger’s semblance, bodying forth, Hath hovered o’er me, smiling. Suddenly, The fiends were laid, the thunders hushed, and all The clouds erewhile so foul, receding, shone Like pendent lakes of liquid gold embossed With pearly isles; and, to complete the change, Profluent rills mingled their murmurs round, Flowers sprang beneath, and, in as instant growth, The clasping tendrils of embowering vines Rustled above to songs of blithesome birds! Then, at her nearer presence, overcome With perturbations of a joy so rich— So honey-thick, that even its own wings Were clogged with its own sweetness;—thus o’ercome, Methought I could not but—though restless—sink Into Sleep’s drowsy arms—and I have slept— Slept dreamless, till the unwished Morn hath come And looked upon me with her cold keen face.
