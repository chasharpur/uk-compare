Rhymes to Henry Parkes

On reading his Sonnet beginning—Who would not be a Poet?

Who would not be a Poet? thus I read In thy proud Sonnet, my harmonious Friend; And then the streamy measures led My utterance on—But at the end The question made me ponder And out of mind let wander The Poet, seeing that for ever he! A Man of Sorrows is fore-doomed to be: Yet, unto this much my assent was given— There is not under the wide dome of heaven
