50

XLI.

To Doctor Lang, On reading one of his Books.

Right onward! For the genius of the land— The spirit of this virgin soil, so meet For a new birth of Freedom, doth command The Muse in loud heroic verse to greet All progress in the way which now thy feet Are treading. Be it thine too, in thy grand Old age, to fashion with paternal hand A charter that shall all our rights complete! Remit not! but now more than ever let ‘Right onward’ be thy motto: till thy name Become a household jewel richly set In that domestic glory yet to flame Wide o’er a free Australia—to the shame Of Wentworth’s memory meshed in Mammon’s net.
