SONNET TO DOCTOR LANG.

BY CHARLES HARPUR.

Right onward! for the genius of the land— The spirit of this virgin soil, so meet For a new birth of manhood,—doth command Its nobler destinies, through the muse, to greet All progress in the way which now thy feet Are treading. Be it thine too, in thy grand Old age, to fashion with paternal hand A Charter that shall all our rights complete. And to this end, now more than ever let Right onward be thy motto, till thy name Become a household jewel richly set In that great heart of Freedom which shall flame Yet in Australia’s bosom—to the shame Of Wentworth’s memory, meshed in Mammon’s net.
