TO TWANK.

Ah! Daniel mine, some Muse malign Hath skimm’d thy judgments cream away! But take a slice of “good advice”— Even that I proffer thee to-day.

Again read Shakespear by the hour,— Read Milton more—McDonald less And Wordsworth for his simple power, Not for his namby-pamby-nsss.

And know,—’twere better to esteem What’s bast in Byron’s godless “Don.” Than with crude Browning much to dream, Or wlre-draw thought with Tennyson.

And better at the “woes of Moore” To shed the artificial tear. Than doat with eunuch passion o’er The feeble beauties of De Vere.
