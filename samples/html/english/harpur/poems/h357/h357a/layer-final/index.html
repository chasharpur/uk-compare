2483d

On the Easter Illumination of St. Peter’s, at Rome.

BY CHARLES HARPUR.

II.

Transcends it? Yea! For many myriads strive In vain for bread and die in misery, To keep such pious mockeries alive! For not the specious prayer and trumpery fee Of Pope and priest and princely devotee, Upholds the cost; but levies on the hive Of ruined Labour while he knaws the gyve Of arm’d Religion, hungry to be free! And free he shall be yet! Else, Land of Fire! Land of Volcanoes! is thy modern line Of men resemblant to thy Pontine mire, And not to the wind-warring Apennine! Not to the storied vales and shores divine, Sounding to glory like a God-struck lyre!
