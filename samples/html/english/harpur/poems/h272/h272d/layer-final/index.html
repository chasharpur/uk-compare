Part III

I

The Message

You tell me she yet talks of me, And vainly would forget Those happy youthful days when we As granted lovers met; That most she’s wont the Wood to roam Where once with me she strayed— Still loves to haunt the bowery dome I wove within its shade: You tell me this, to soothe my pride,— But is she not Anothers Bride?

You say when old loved things suggest Some thought she fain would chase, A sigh will heave her pensive breast, A shade come o’er her face; That straight and startled air she’ll rise And every wont resign, To hum apart, with drooping eyes, One little song of mine: You say all this to soothe my pride,— But is she not Anothers Bride?

Go—tell her, he to whom she long Was Fancys only theme, Doth charge her by his burning wrong To cherish not the dream, That Love, if but return were free, His olden fires might stir;— A hope like this were mean in me, And infamous in her! 44 Say this—but breathe thou nought beside For is she not Another’s bride?

Or if naught more thou wouldst impart, Mark that I deigned to say, Had Love’s first roses in her heart Not flourished to decay, The moon, and each rare star that glows In heaven, from east to west, With every grace that loveliest grows In nature’s mother breast, Should through all ages have supplied Rich fancies of one Poet’s Bride!

What! they decieved her to the last? O say not so again! Lest all the madness of the past Rush, storming o’er my brain! Away! where’er mine eyes I turn, Whichever way I move, There, wakened into misery, mourn Wild memories of love Friend, leave me! lest perchance I chide! What wouldst thou? She’s Another’s Bride!!
