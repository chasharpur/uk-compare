73

Chorus.

Alas! the veriest human clod Hath still been happier far than he On whom the majesty and the mystery Of Thought hath fallen, like the fire of God! For those by nature gifted to pursue The Beautiful and the True, Have mostly in dishonor trod This bright green Earth,—as yet they do! 74 All Except an idle few whom Privilege and Fortune claim, As relatives, and who, though but for shame Those stormy shades of darkness old That poor-born Genius else, for misery, doth infold.

And who alone May finally dissipate this ancient gloom? Those Lights that under College wigs full blown (Grave Learning’s bushels) blink for self alone

Those sleek scholastic hypocrites, with whom The grace of genius and the height of mind, And all these promise unto Humankind, Are but a cant wherewith they beg for place Amid Oppression’s governmental few, Or play the sage-deep sycophant unto Some “lord, their God, his Grace”? Away with such—Learning’s mere slaves! the day 75a Of intellectual brotherhood no ray Shall gather from a crew So faithless to the Beautiful and the True!

But that this day may sooner come, Ye suffering sons of Genius, it is you Yourselves who must dispel the gloom That clouds you (even as of old) Yet In its doom so deadly cold! With your own injuries, let stern thought Of all the desolate death beds of those Who against this same Darkness fought (Each in his age, on which his spirit rose like some peculiar star of night, To burn Eternally apart—) Yea, let stern thoughts of those Now nerve ye to renew the fight, And follow Victory with unflinching heart!
