II

To my First-born, Washington.

My Beautiful! for beautiful thou art To me thy Father, as the morning light That makes all common objects fresh and bright— Yea, gives them out of the dun void to start As they were newly fashioned from the night! For long there was a darkness at my heart, Until thy Mother made her life a part Of mine, to pierce it with Love’s genial might, The Aurora she, and the young Morning thou, Of a new era in my worldly way: 486 Whence it behoves me heedfully to plough The future for thy sake, and for the vow That I have made, to make thee (if I may) One worthy of our great Country in thy day.
