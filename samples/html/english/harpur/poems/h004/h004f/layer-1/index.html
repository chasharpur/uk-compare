XXVII.

Absence by the Sea-side.

When heaven is waxing grey, Mary, The dawning isles between, And the stars are paling fast away In Morning’s eclipsing sheen: While the broad red sun is breaking then From out the burning sea, All life to gladness waking—then My spirit longs for thee, Mary

And when in noontide strength, Mary, He bears his beaming blades, Till Nature’s busy tribes at length Lie panting in the shades: While the sea, like white fire glowing then, Though cool and fresh it be, Is only heard far flowing—then I’m lonely without thee, Mary

When clouds in crimson dyed, Mary Bedrape heaven’s western gate, Till like a rare and royal bride Eve enters there in state:While 95 While yon cliff-born kids, all prancing then, Are mirrored in the sea, In its wide joy out glancing—then I love to think of thee, Mary

And when Night’s throned again, Mary, And all the stars are met, To sing their old creation-strain Ere Hesperus be set: While the silver moon is beaming then, And in the silent sea, Her other Self is dreaming—then My heart is full of thee, Mary
