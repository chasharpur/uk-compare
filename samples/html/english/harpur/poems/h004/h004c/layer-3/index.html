Absence by the Sea Side.

When heaven is waxing grey, Mary, The dawning isles between, And the stars are paling fast away In Morning’s brighter sheen: While the broad red sun is breaking then From out the burning sea, All Life to gladness waking—then My spirit yearns for thee.

And when, in moontide strength, Mary, He bears his flaming blades, Till Nature’s busy tribes at length Lie panting in the shades: While the sea, like white fire glowing then, Though cool and fresh it be, Is only heard far-flowing—then I’m lonely without thee.

When clouds in crimson dyed, Mary, Bedrape heaven’s western gate, Till like a rare and royal bride Eve enters there in state: While the cliff born kids all prancing then, Are mirrored in the sea, In its wide joy out glancing,—then I love to think of thee.

And when Night’s throned again, Mary, And all the stars are met To sing their old creation-strain Ere Hesperus be set: While the silver moon is beaming then, 536 And in the silent sea Her other self is dreaming—then My heart is full of thee.
