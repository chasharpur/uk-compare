A SONG OF THE SEA-SIDE.

When the Night first waxes grey, Mary, The dawning coasts between; And pale stars pale away, Mary, In Morning’s brighter sheen: Whilst the broad red Sun is breaking then From out the golden Sea, All Life to gladness waking—then I love to think of Thee.

When the Day in noontide strength, Mary, Unsheathes his flaming blade, And late loud Life at length, Mary, Lies panting in the shade: Whilst the Sea, like white fire glowing then, Though cool and fresh it be, Is only heard far flowing—then I love to think of Thee.

When the West in purple pride, Mary, Unbars the golden gate Whence like a royal bride, Mary, The Eve glides forth in state: Whilst yon snowy lambs, as prancing then, Are mirrored in the Sea, In its wide joy out glancing—then I love to think of Thee.
