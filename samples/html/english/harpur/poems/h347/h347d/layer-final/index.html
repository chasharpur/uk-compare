On a Law-mad Bush Justice

Squire Gabble’s restlessness of jaw Hath led him so to munch the Law, That nothing but a quibbling fuss ’tis When he thinks he’s doing justice. A point is all that he can see— He has no eyes for equity: And easier than one knat of right Immeshed in cobweb trammels, He’ll bolt with evident delight Ten legal camels!
