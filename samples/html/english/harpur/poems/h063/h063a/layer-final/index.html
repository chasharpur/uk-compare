V.

Change and Death.—

(An unrhymed Lyric).

We build but for Change and for Death: To these a like tribute pay Glory and Shame, For something must pass to give being to both! Yea, all things are rounded by change, and are perishing— All but the God-builded frame of the world.

Th

e bright stars of Glory but ray To illumine a path to the gloom of the grave, And the winter of Shame is soon over and gone! Of all we inherit, behold the Inheritors— Throned on the endless successions of time.
