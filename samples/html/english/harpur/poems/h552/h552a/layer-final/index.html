254c

SONNET.

Self-vindicatory, and by way of Appendix to the “Sonnets dedicated to Senators.”

Fat Fudge, the Publican (and sinner), o’er His counter swears, I m told, that I, the present Writer of these particularly pleasant Squib Sonnets, for some by-gone private sore Hate patriot Wentworth; and that therefore And therfore only, have in verse his public Character trounced so,—till ‘t is like a cub sick!— And more I learn, that some believe this lie. To purge even these then, of such “perilous stuff,” (As Boys are disabused ‘mongst whom a fuss Is started about bugbears, ghosts, or other lies,) It is, or ought to be, methinks, enough For me to simply state the matter thus;— I only hate all Rogues, political or otherwise!

AUSTRALICUS.
