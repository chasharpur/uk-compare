XIII.

To the Hon: James Martin.

Be prudent yet not crafty. Evermore Be doubtful of that seeming wisdom which From sleek Expediency draws all its lore, In reasons poor, in pretexts only, rich. Nor ever held the Heroic Fane a niche For such as palteringly bent before 472 Mob interests, mostly beastial at the core, Or those of traders, with their Mammon itch! Who lives but to the Present is a dunce, Who lives but to the Future, may at last Turn out a dreamer—and forgotten be: But True Men move the Present, and at once It is the sweet child of a bitter Past, And mother of a raised Futurity!

1866.
