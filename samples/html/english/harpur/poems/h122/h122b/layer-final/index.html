[EVENING IN THE COUNTRY]

Hail to the shadowy Hour! whose gleamy wings Fan into bloom Love’s rosiest visionings! That hour delicious, from whose glowing skies Drops visual balm for beauty-loving eyes! And when, or hurrying over in a throng, Or pairing still, from tree to tree, along, The wild birds warble their last,—sweetest song. While the mute symphony of Flora breathes From cedarn dingles which the woodbine wreathes, And where, the bees in heavy homeward flight, Seem drowsed and wearied with the day’s delight

the Sun has sunk cloud-curtained to his rest Beyond the mountain forests of the West: And while the crimson of the dying day Deepens, to fade into a dusky grey, A silvery fringe of many-pointed light 620 Rays, like a halo, from yon eastern height, Though yet the Moon, with an expected bride’s Amorous delay, beyond its cone abides: Whence, like a vast cerulean snake, the stream Its liquid length draws winding with a twilight gleam. While from the hills whose outlines, westward set, Are listed with a skiey brightness yet; And from the darkening wodlands, and the stream Winding amid them with a sunken gleam; And from the farms trand,—dimlier through the glooms Each moment, and from all their stirless homes, Whence late faint smoke wreaths (now unseen), arose, There comes one sense of manifold repose! The beasts are still; the birds are flown; and lo, The dew-glossed flowers, ere they fold their petals, show More richly in the mitigated light— Embroidering so the border of the Night: Or where young Linus, hurrying past, now sees Idalea’s dwelling top yon rounding trees, Shaken, they shed the dews like christal fire, Or crushed, in odorous ecstacies expire.
