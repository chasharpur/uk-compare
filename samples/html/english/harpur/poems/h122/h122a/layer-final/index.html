Evening in the Country.

Hail, to the shadowy Hour whose gleamy wings Touch into splendor Nature’s homeliest things! That Hour delicious, from whose glowing skies, Drops visual balm for beauty-loving eyes! And when,—ere hurrying over in a throng, Or nestward pairing in calm flight along, The wild birds warble their last,—sweetest song; While the mute symphony of Flora breathes From cedarn dingles which the woodbine wreathes, And where the bees in heavy homeward flight Seem drowsy with the long day’s long delight.
