The House of Riot.

It is the House of Riot—the chosen place Of much vain speech and simulated mirth, And fleshly feasting. From its dissolute door They have spurned Simplicity, and red-lipt Health, With native Cheerfulness, and Strength, and long- Forecasting Prudence, and domestic Peace, And Piety who loves to dwell with these; And as their household Deities instead, Have welcomed there Extravagance disguised As Bounty, joined with riotess Excess, And false Enjoyment, and false knowledge dressed In the stol’n airs and habit of a sage; And Atheism mantled in the gown Of a denuded Prophet.

O

we should honor Wisdom! since through her The great Immutable of Heaven devised The boundless world. Yea, she it was who first Elanced the sun on his imperial course, Day’s monarch, vested with celestial fire; Her hand it was that fashioned the round moon And all the golden stars. Or couldst thou soar Up to the throne of God, even there amid Unspeakable splendors she were found of thee; Or to the unfathomable gulfs of Hell Down couldst thou plunge, behold, there, even there Wisdom would meet thee, though obscured, as when The day in its meridian strength is seen Darkened with storms.

And songs mellifluous as those which he, The enthusiast, when to reason dying, hears In mountain glens, or wooded vales, or near Some lone lake crimsoned by the setting sun, Shall hail thy presence, cloying all the air With sweetness,—and awakening raptures sweet And soul-subduing, but delusive all As are the hopes of unrequited love: Sweet—sweet—but cruel as untimely death!

Wouldst thou he Should aid prophetic Genius, or assist Poor Learning, laboring in the mines of Truth To enrich a thankless world? The miscreant’s means Are all forestalled! What if his Country’s need Besought her sons to gather like a storm And sweep Invasion from her sacred coasts, Or thunder signal condemnation down On Kingcraft, and on all internal wrong? Her need might move him not: for he is one Whose energies long since were drenched away In midnight orgies by the flowing bowl.

Th

en in the House of Riot seek ye not For pleasure, nor for knowledge from its vain And gaudy Paracites, ye young ones, lest A taint of madness for the first ye catch, And for the other wed a lasting folly. Rather seek Wisdom in the house of woe; Or meditate her amid ruined tombs Charged with the dust of ages,—dust that once Was the warm human beauty of the world.
