A NEW SONG TO A QUEER TUNE.

BY A HAWKESBURY BOY.

When John Thomas Wilson, who bolted So soon as his debts became troublesome, Shall return to this city of Sydney, And pay all his “mad dogs” a double sum; When insolvent ———— ———— to swindle Shall feel what a burning disgrace it is, And have full as much gold in his pocket As brass in that damnable face of his!

Oh! then—when these wonders shall happen, The case the big Squatters would forge, Shall look like a “Grievance” to Stanley, And be “over the right” with Sir George.

When the “windy wee” Lawyer shall utter A speech that has a one grain so sense—the elf! And Nicky love aught but his stations, And R————n aught but his pence and self, When Lang shall be meek, like a Christian, And B———d shall change morals as politics, And when folly’s divorced from Bill B————n, And Ben, to one cause, as one Dolly, sticks!

Oh! then—when these wonders shall happen, The big Squatters their ends shall attain; And our Poor shall all serve them as Coolies, And our Yeomen all skulk in their train.

When Lowe shall write verse that’s worth reading, Or prose that is not from the moon at least; And old Berry, in treating of corn-laws, Be aught but an old wooden spoon at best; When that thin and most misty Consistence, The Atlas, declares for the Right to war, Or makes D————n cry out for assistance, In fighting the Colony’s fight—hurrah!

Oh! then—when these wonders shall happen, Shall Truth and the Faction agree; And his country in mad “Billy Windford,” A hero and patriot see!
