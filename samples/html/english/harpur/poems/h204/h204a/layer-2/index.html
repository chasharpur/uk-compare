A Jolly Good Fellow

Who ne’er treats a friend with proud rudeness Because he has less of mere pelf; Nor thinks himself wise, when his shrewdness Puts benefit by on a shelf; And who has a good word for all goodness, Though not very saintly himself: Though he fuddle sometimes till he’s mellow, And delight in a rollicking glee, Still, he is a jolly good fellow— A jolly good fellow and free: Yes; he is a jolly good fellow, And shell ever be held one by me.

Who never a true breath abateth, Through dread of a myrmidon’s pike, Or, like Dodge, for a safer time waiteth, When his honor’s in peril, to strike; And who ever and openly hateth All wrong and the devil alike: Though he fuddles sometimes &c

Who taketh advantage of no man Because he is lowly in life, And never makes light of a woman Because she is weak—or his wife: Yet has no more fear of lord Bo, than He has of—the back of a knife! Though he fuddles sometimes &c

Who never denies a poor sinner A dole he in reason can spare; Or helps a poor maiden to win her To wrong, by so baiting his snare; 396a Or is greedily glad o’er his dinner, Because it is more than his share: Though he fuddles sometimes &c

Who, being made drouthy by nature, Will sit o’er his cups till he wink, And yet, ere one dear kindred creature Thereby into ruin should sink, Or even be grief pinched in feature, Would sooner be damned than he’d drink! Though he fuddles sometimes till he’s mellow, And delights in a rollicking glee, Still he is a jolly good fellow— As manly and faithful as free: Yes; he is a jolly good fellow, And shall ever be held one by me.

With boys of this stamp in a fair lot Established on every hand, It would grieve me but little to spare what To raise model men is now planned, Nor should I much care if there were not A Temperance man in the land. 396e For though they whiles fuddled till mellow, And rejoiced in a rollicking glee, Yet each were a jolly good fellow— A jolly good fellow and free: Yes; each were a jolly good fellow, And a good enough fellow for me.

The Point

But since there’s no jolly good fellow That right to this standard comes up, I hold that no one should get mellow With a less perfect warrant to sup: While to sing of mere Sots were to bellow Pig-praises—so down with the Cup!
