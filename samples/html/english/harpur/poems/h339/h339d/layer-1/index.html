Old Billowy Hawkesb’ry.

Old billowy Hawkesb’ry, I see thee expanding The same as of yore, as abounding and free! Not so with the lone one beside thee now standing,— Him cold turns have shaken since last he saw thee! Yes, then could I mark on thy blue-flowing billow A vision-lit face with no hint of a furrough, But the fire-coultered plough of a wanderer’s sorrow Hath passed o’er my brow since I saw thee before!

And what did the maiden I strayed by thy side with? She wedded another when darkened my day! And what of the schoolmates I bathed in thy tide with? They, too, have forgot me, or wandered away! Yet, as shells seem intoned by their once native ocean, The life of the Past seems astir in thy motion, Recalling a sense of the fervid devotion I dreamt unto Glory when near thee before.

But vainly, alas! to forget I endeavour How I passed the old Cottage—my father’s no more! For bitter indeed was the task, my bright river, To feel like a stranger in passing its door! I plucked from a tree I had planted this blossom, And drew its green growth like a child my bosom, Mine eyes felt a fire, and I fled to repose ‘em On something unchanged since I saw thee before!
