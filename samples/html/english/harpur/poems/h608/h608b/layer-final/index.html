To H Parkes.

I sometimes wish my Muse a monthly nurse, Or something else—or even something worse; For here I’m still “Poor Jack”, without ”the shiners,” And thence unwelcome to all sorts of diners, While others clip their fleecy flocks, and sto[re] Bright gold, I’ve only so much verse the more Gainless, however gainful be the times; Even you turn ivory—I nought but Rhym[es] A vagrant—and the cause still, all along This damned unconquerable love of song.

I’ll cut it yet though,—and the means devising Can’t we get up an act ‘gainst poetising? I’ll cut it,—yes; as all my advisers bid [me] And get as rich as any Jew in Sydney

782

That is, I mean to turn an auctioneer, And take to buying cheap and selling dear. Gods! how I’ll puff the lots I’ll have to class, Even Stubbs shall stand astonished at my brass Stale cheese shall be ambrosia, and sour cape The blessed vintage of some heaven-born grape Filched by Prometheus, and for which Jove’s ire Was thundered at him—not for filching fire. Nay, even Gin half yellow to the eyes Shall be the very cream of Paradise; And when I think of what I’ll do for Rum However hell-fire-ish—but all that’s to come! While ‘neath my hammer lands that even a rat Would starve, shall wax than Lombardy more fat; Shall utter forth a murmurous song of streams And glow with verdure!

Are not those the dreams To foster? Is not that the one design For which ‘twere well to cut the coinless Nine? Being the sole one that ‘mongst Australia’s sons can Ere make me great—they’re all such Number Ones, man.

I’m serious:—henceforth I’m a Son of Pelf! But honest. Hark, how I advise myself. Let no low trick of Trade, Charles, by the trick, Nor cut out aught that makes men “cut their stick” And then “their lucky”, if thei’v luck to do it, If not they’re sure unluckily to rue it; Though quite the dealer always cut the sharper Or Parkes, thy Friend, will have to cut

Charles Harpur
