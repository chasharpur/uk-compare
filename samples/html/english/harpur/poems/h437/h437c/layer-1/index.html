[RECORDS OF A POET’S LOVE XX]

To day we part! me far away to dwell From this the Scene that saw our bud of love Bloom into beauty. The blue heaven above— These hills and valleys, with each rocky dell Where Echo hideth,—shall not they some spell Of our sweet vows retain? Shall they not guess Their gain and loss in our so rich distress, Till Time to Fame the eloquent story tell? To-morrow, and the sun shall crown yon hill Bright as before; all wingëd things shall wake To song as glad as we were listening still; The stream as mirthfully its wild way make! But I, pursuing Fortune’s wandering star, Shall see and hear them not—from thee and them afar!
