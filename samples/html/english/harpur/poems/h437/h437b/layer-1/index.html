XX.

The Parting.

To day we part! Me far away to dwell From this the Scene that saw our innocent love Bloom into beauty. The blue heaven above— These hills and valleys, with each rocky dell Where Echo hideth;—shall not these some spell Of our sweet vows retain? Shall these not guess 56 Their gain and loss in our supreme distress, Till Time to Fame the eloquent story tell? To-morrow, and the sun shall climb yon hill Bright as before; all wingèd things shall start To song as glad as we were listening still; This stream exult like a mirth-gushing heart! But I, pursuing Fortune’s wandering star, Shall not with these rejoice—from thee and them afar.
