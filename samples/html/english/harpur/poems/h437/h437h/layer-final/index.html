IX.

[RECORDS OF A POET’S LOVE XX]

To-day we part! I far away to dwell From this the scene that saw our bud of love Bloom into rosehood. The blue heavens above— These hills and valleys, with each rocky dell, Echo’s dim hold,—shall these retain no spell Of foregone passion? shall they speak no tale Of grief they shrouded in this shaded vale? Shall they of all our joy the story tell? To-morrow—and the sun shall climb yon hill Bright as before; all winged things shall wake To song as glad as if we listened still; The stream as mirthfully its wild way make. But I, pursuing fortune’s wandering star, Shall see and hear them not—from thee and them afar.
