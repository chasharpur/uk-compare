[RECORDS OF A POET’S LOVE XXIII]

A heavy loneliness benumbs my brain, And nightmare shapes begotten of the gloom Of desolate thought, with a thick sense of doom Smother me up! Or weariness, like a chain, Fetters my will when most it would maintain Life’s struggle,—here, where even the Spring bloom Of Youth itself but decks a living tomb Of lovely hopes, whose like are—never again! Thus, cold and drear, my days wear on; and she Who yet might solace me with her dear smile, Prompting some new dream of felicity, Is far, far from me all the weary while! Ah, that our love so beautiful, should be In Misery’s ocean but a desert isle!
