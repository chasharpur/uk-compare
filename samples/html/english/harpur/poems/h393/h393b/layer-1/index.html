76

A Political Gospel.

The world’s heart is kindless and grey and unholy As the head of the Wandering Jew, And can never be won from the curse of its folly Till Man to Humanity’s true! There’s a path to redemption—but that shall we miss, Till we seek it no more in the old warring manner, But are ready to fight a new battle with this Brave motto inscribed on our banner: To principles let us be loyal alway, And true to all good in Man’s general story; Not to that trumpery—Royal Display! And that blood-drinking Juggernaut—National Glory!

And though ever some one, to be doughty and noted, Like Nimrod, should aim at a throne, It were easy to leave him, a wight self-devoted, To brood o’er his project alone!Or 77 Or to meet him at once with a withering hiss From hearts out of love with ambition’s old manner, And fired for a new race of glory with this Brave motto inscribed on their banner: To principles let us be loyal alway, And true to all good in Man’s general story; Not to that trumpery—Royal Display! And that blood-drinking Juggernaut—National Glory!
