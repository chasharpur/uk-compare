Love’s Still Hour.

O come to the rill-girdled grove— Helouise! O come! ’tis the still hour of love— Helouise! For the voice of the day now is hushed, and at rest Is the beast on his couch and the bird in its nest.

The grasshopper sleeps in his green nook, And all chirping things, save the cricket, are still; And the Moon, like an armour-clad Queen—look, How she comes through the pines on the hill! And betwixt their dark branches Her radiance out launches— A torn flood of silver—sheer down on the rill!And

And yon plover—how dimly ’tis given As over it sails for some wave-beaten isle, While the stars leave the chambers of heaven To dance in their coming Queen’s smile; And with golden-rayed glances As still she advances, To look back the joyance it brings them the while.

And see in yon wavelets, all heaping And tumbling along as the night breezes blow, What star-glories, flame-like up leaping, In tremulous congeries glow: E’en as soul-splendors play in Thine eyes when they ray in The Poet-loved moonlight’s sweet silvery flow.

O then, to the stream-illumed grove— Come! In the still sweet spirit of Love— Come! For the voice of the day now is hushed, and at rest Is the beast on his couch and the bird in its nest.
