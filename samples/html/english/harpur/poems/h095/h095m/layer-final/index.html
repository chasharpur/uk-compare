Diana.

BY THE LATE CHARLES HARPUR.

A Huntress born of Nature Through the might of olden lore, Lithely on bounding, lightens right past me, Full as the morning of hurry and vigour, Whenever the rock-floods roar; —See her bow! I see it plain! And shafted quiver balanced nice! See her robes, blown back amain, Though looped in front by a sure device, Sweeping behind her like the train Of a bird of Paradise!

See her bow—a curve of light Across her arm upraised and bare! And see how backward streameth bright The torrent of her hair!

when the vales are sleeping In a silent silver sleep, Musefully strolling, not long am I lonely, Ere mystical melody, billow on billow, Wells from each echoey steep: Then an arc of rainbow light Suddenly spans some viny glade, And there I see her presence bright, Bound beaming from the deeper shade, All girt with dancing hymphs in white And festal robes arrayed!
