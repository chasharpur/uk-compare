1.

Dian.

A Huntress born to Nature Through the might of olden lore, Lithely on bounding, lightens right past me, Full as the morning of hurry and vigor, Wherever the rock-floods roar: See her bow! I see it plain! And shafted quiver balanced nice! See her robes, blown back amain Though looped in front with a sure device, Sweeping behind her, like the train Of a Bird of Paradise!

Or where the forest shadows Are dew-silvered by the morn, Lonely forth roaming, not long do I listen, Till suddenly sounding up, merry and mellow, There hear I her mystic horn: Then her buskined limbs how white They glance, as past she boundeth there! See her bow—a curve of light Across her arm upraised and bare! And see how backward streameth bright The torrent of her hair!

Or where the vales are sleeping In a silent silver sleep, Musefully strolling, not long am I lonely, Ere mystical melody, billow on billow, Wells from each echoey steep: Then an arc of rainbow light Suddenly spans some viny glade; And there I see her, boon and bright, Disclouding from the deeper shade, All girt with dancing Nymphs in white And festal robes arrayed.
