A Sylvan Vision

A Huntress, like Diana, In a dream of old delight, Flies for ever past me, when When I roam the mountain height: I see her rainbow robes amain, Though clasped in front with sure device, Sweeping behind her, like the train Of a Bird of Paradise!

Or when the forest shadows Are dew-silvered by the Morn, Roaming there, I ever hear her Her wind her golden horn: And then her buskined limbs how white They glance, as past she boundeth there! See! And see! how backward shineth bright The torrent of her hair.
