“My Friends.”

‘Tis a very sad thing to be true, That so soon as our years are not very few, We cannot say simply—”My Friends”, even while The cheek may be dressed in a fair-whether smile, And be, at the same time, exempt From a twinge of contempt.

The phrase may not stick in our throats exactly, Like the “Amen” of Macbeth; Yet rarely it rounds itself forth compactly, But grates in the passage, and lengthens our breath, As if it were forced uphill: Making us feel—not at all lyrical, But bitterish rather, and somewhat satirical, Even against our will.
