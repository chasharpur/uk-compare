Acrostic

Mine be the beautiful Girl that I name not, And were I sure the dear Creature would blame not, Rapture should tell her the heart in my bosom Years for her love as a bee for the blossom.

Did she but love me again I could wander Over the wide world to prove myself leal; Yea, for a tomb in her heart I could squander Life, like a prodigal,—if for her weal:— Ever, till death, growing fonder and fonder.
