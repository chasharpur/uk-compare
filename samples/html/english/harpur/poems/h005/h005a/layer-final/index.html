I.

An Acrostic.

Mine be the beautiful Girl that I name not, And were I sure the dear Creature would blame not, Rapture should tell her, the heart in my bosom Yearns for her love, as a Bee for the blossom.

Angels, as glowing in piety dream, Night’s fairest stars, as when brightest they beam, Not to my soul are more bright and divine, Earth, than this delicate Daughter of thine.

Dared she to love me again, I could wander O’er the whole world with a passion so sweet! Yea, for her heart in return, I could squander Life, or aught dearer than life, at her feet! Ever, till death, growing fonder and fonder.
