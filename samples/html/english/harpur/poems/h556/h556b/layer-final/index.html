I will annex a satirical sonnet which I wrote for Duncan’s Weekly Register, called “The spirit of Squattery with reference to a tea-allowance to hired servants” and supposed to be written by a “Squatter of the first water.” I annex it, because I think its irony is in keeping with that of the para- graph immideately above.

[THE SPIRIT OF SQUATTERY]

Why should hired laborers have Tea allowed? Seeing they only have a right to eat Because they could not work else! Being a treat Fit but for gentlemen, Tea makes them proud, And thence offensive to their masters all: And to offend either in meat or drink Affects Religion—See the Text; I think It will be found in the Apostle Paul. 76 But what if our stock-horses looked for Tea? Egad, we’d wonder! and for aught I see, Servants are just as much their Masters’ cattle During their term of hire, or ought to be! Thus should the case stand (howe’er some may prattle,) In sound home practice—damn all Theory!
