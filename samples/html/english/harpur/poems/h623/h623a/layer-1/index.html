To Myself June 1855.

What is this Eastern War to thee, Its craft and folly, blame & blunder? Its aims are dodges plain to see, Its battles shams with all their thunder.

Heed not its proud but passing things— The royal mischiefs of their day; But give thou Thought’s immortal wings 216 To glories of a purer ray:

To Freedom in her future prime, To Nature’s everlasting lore, To Science from her tower in time Surveying the Eternal’s shore.

Be such the subjects of thy thought Not old World Kings and Cabinets— And Liberties that flounder, caught Like fish in diplomatic nets.

For these, if pondered, can but hurt The straightness of thy moral view, And foul as with the Old World’s dirt The virgin nature of the New.
