III.

Poetry

Rising and setting suns of Liberty: Mountainous èxploits, and the wrecks thick strewn By stormy Passion o’er life’s treacherous sea, Relieved with shores of green delight, and boon And starry dreams, and the serene pale moon Of musical pathos;—these, thus sensuously Clothed in their emblems, are of Poetry The visual temple when to fitness hewn. And for its worship:—all that Mind can seize Of moral beauty, spirited with the might Of household ties and social sympathies, And that religion of the heart, whose flight Is strongest, and most heavenward, ’mid the blight Of mortal woe;—its inmost rites are these.
