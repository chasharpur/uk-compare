Love at its Height.

When with my heart’s elect One,—how The moments, wing’d with raptures, fly! 290 The Moon rose as we met—and now She’s midway in the sky! And ere for the long night we part, Vaguely unsatisfied of heart, In going oft I turn again With passion lightning through my brain, Bent vainly to discover How much—how utterly I love her!

O were to words a boundless reach— An all-informing radiance given; Or knew I that transcendent speech The Seraphs use in heaven: Or could mine eyes, by some weird skill, Paint forth the ardors of my will Or throw abroad in star-like glows Warm types of passion’s wishful throes, I haply might discover How much—how utterly I love her!
