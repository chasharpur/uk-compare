Love, the Crown of Nature.

Tis beautiful to mark the Morning rise Over the hills, as from her bower a bride Comes blushing—blushing with the shame-faced pride Of Love that now consùmmated, supplies All her full heart can wish; and to the eyes Dear are the flowers then, in their green haunts, spied 196 Glist’ning with dew: pleasant at noon the side Of shadowy mountains ridging to the skies: At eve tis sweet to hear the breeze advance Through the responding forest dense and tall; And sweeter in the moonlight is the dance And natural music of the waterfal: Yet to complete and fix the charm of all Love must be near us with its magic glance.
