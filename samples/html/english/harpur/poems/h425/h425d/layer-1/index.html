[RECORDS OF A POET’S LOVE VIII]

‘Tis beautiful to mark the Morning rise Over the hills, as from her bower a bride Comes brightened—blushing with the shame-faced pride Of love that now, consùmmated, supplies All her full heart hath wished; and to the eyes Dear then the flowers in green concealment spied, And wet with dew: pleasant at noon the side Of shadowy mountains ridging to the skies: At eve, ’tis sweet to hear the breeze advance Through the responding forest dense and tall; And sweeter, in the moonlight, is the dance And natural music of the waterfall: But even ’mid these, from her we prize, a glance Of tender love’s the crowning charm of all!
