1.

[RECORDS OF A POET’S LOVE VIII]

How beautiful doth the morning rise O’er the hills, as from her bower a bride Comes brightened—blushing with the shame-faced pride Of love that now consummated supplies All her full heart can wish, and to the eyes Dear are the flowers then, in their green haunts spied, Glist’ning with dew: pleasant at noon the side Of shadowy mountain ridging to the skies: At eve ’tis sweet to hear the breeze advance Through the responding forest dense and tall; And sweeter in the moonlight is the dance And natural music of the waterfall: And yet we feel not the full charm of all; Till love be near us with his magic glance.
