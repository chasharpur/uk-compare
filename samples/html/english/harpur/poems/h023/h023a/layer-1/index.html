Moods.

The swift succession of our moods Is such, that Life can never consistent be: One thought delights—another then intrudes— And together find shrinking the former into vanity, And Through the .

Oh that our life so wildly vast Had reason for the lord of all its doing!” I said:—and then my travel o’er the Past Brought up another mood, that thus went ruing:

“Ah, for the fancies fond and bright, That were the playmates of my trusting youth! Ah, thy insufferably glaring light Withdraw—withdraw, men do not love thee,—Truth!

For verily thy light is cold, And therefore withering. Who would lif, if Reason Ruld over us alone, and mad us old As Winter, even in Life’s season.“
