II

Thekla’s Song.

(From the German of Shiller.)

The oak forest bellows— The clouds gather o’er! To and fro walks the Maiden On the green of the shore: 618 The waves are up breaking With might, with might, While her voice waileth out Through the ominous night! Her eyes, wet with weeping, Glance gleamy and wild: (As thus in her sorrow Singeth Wallenstein’s child.)

My heart’s world is emptied— And how can the heart live When Love, by Fate beggared, Hath no comfort to give! Call home, thou Holy One,— Call home thy child, From this change-trampled Earth Now so woful and wild! My soul have I wearied With its pleasure and pain: I have lived; I have loved;— And what more can remain?

Euroma, Nov 1866
