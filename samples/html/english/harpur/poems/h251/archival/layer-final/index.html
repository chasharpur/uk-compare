Poems in early Life by Charles Harpur

A Lover’s Quarrel—And the Result.

She was all my heart’s desire, And I’d made her heart Love’s lyre— Yet we quarrelled, and a dire Vengeance vowed!

And we nursed our wrath so well, That the honest truth to tell, Soon a pallor o’er us fell Like a shroud.

Yet with a hard annulling eye, Or with but a gulphed-down sigh, We would pass each other by In the crowd.

Then we told our friends we deemed ‘Twas all nonsense Love had dreamed— 54 But in saying this we seemed Sadly bowed.

Yet we said our wrath must hold, And never could grow cold— Though we looked the while I’m told, Rather cowed.

And in short with “good advice”, We henceforward grew less nice ‘Bout the hate at any price We had vowed.

Nay, we owned how bad it was So to hate even with a cause, As a matter by God’s laws Disallowed.

And so we softened more As our lonely days on wore, And of kindness yet in store Thought aloud.

Till Love between us soon For its having jarred in tune, Came lovelier—like the Moon From a cloud.
