93

VII.

A LIFE SAVED.

A fat wealthy baillie was plucked from the Forth By a ferryman, also a son of the North: When finding who saved him from drowning, he gave Just a shilling, and waddled away with the lave. Poet Burns (it is said) who was by, saw the donor Depart, to say drily: Hech, sirs! but its owner (No matter what danger the ferryman braved) Should best know the worth of the life that he saved.
