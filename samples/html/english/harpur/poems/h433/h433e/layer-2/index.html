IV

Matins

Unfolded are the crystal gates of Light, And Darkness, like a worsted Ethiope, flies Before the Morning Star’s victorious rise, As o’er the eastern hills so queen-like bright She comes. And see too, at her coming, how Her skiey sisters seem to pale away With envy of her so eclipsive ray! Even thus to me, O Poesy, shin’st thou, Of bright things brightest; and thus always they Diminish in thy beauty’s richer glow, When, from before thy radiant car, depart All Care’s benighting glooms, though dense and low, By shapes of light displaced: So much thou art The Morning Star of all the glory I know!
