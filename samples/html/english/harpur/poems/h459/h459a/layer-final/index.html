RHYMES.

Blissless am I, except in this That I delight to sing of Bliss; And loveless, save that oft and long I love to sing of Love—a loving Child of Song! Even thus, like roes that on their mountains skip, By, hitherto, I have let my Years all trip Ungathering aught that Men call gain; Wherefore the kind World thinks,—and I Do sometimes think,—I’ve lived in vain, For letting Fortune give me so the slip! Most when some station-gilded thing, That, like a butterfly, Was yesterday a grub, comes flaunting by.

But soon I turn again and sing! By so much stronger than my will Is this strong love of Song—and still, Joy-eloquent eyes and passion-dewy lips, And things that garland best with these, Clear streams, fresh flowers, glad birds, and shady trees All starred with golden pips, Drive from my thoughts all thought of Pelf! Joy-eloquent eyes! and passion-dewy lips! Forever still of these My Fancy, like a honey-sucker, sips; Or suns contentedly in those Her little lazy loving self, Even like some Fairy Elf Pillowed the while upon a blushing rose: Yes; even thus the World by goes With me, while other—duller men enjoy Its real loaves and fishes! Each rosy substance of my rosiest wishes, Even till they cloy.

But if a thought like this Will mar at times my blissless Bliss, By so much stronger than my will Is still the love of Song—that still I turn me to the Muses’ Spring To dip my heart therein, and sing Of eloquent eyes That are of sunny thoughts the sunny skies; And gracious lips Stored with such honey as young Passion sips! And what associate best with these, Streams, and flowers, and birds, and trees All starred with golden pips.

And thus employed my purpose is most clinging: The more so, that, as to the Present I Am nothing, I would be Something to the better days My Country yet shall see;— Something for my sweet singing! A star of poesie in her Future’s sky Throbbing with deathless thought, and to the praise That honeyed lips shall breathe, and eloquent eyes shall gaze!

Yes; with this distant aim, in lowliest station, And nameless to the passing generation, Even like the Seers of Old, I labor heartily and long To fix each rich dream in as rich a song, As one should set some priceless gem In beauty-wreathen gold:— The gold of a glorious diadem Which through a high and mighty line Of monarch Minds is doomed to sway A new and noble People, half divine From nurture in the genial ray Shed around all from Truth’s all-venerated shrine.
