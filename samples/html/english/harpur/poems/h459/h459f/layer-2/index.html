[A RHYME]

While for all spirits inginitely free I sing of that great popular emprise Of hardy progress, which, well-willed, to be Tendeth the sooner in Man’s destiny— Willed by the bravely wise!

Or with as loud and bold a chime I sing of that yet braver time, When world-wide Justice from her alpine chair Shall read at length in the rich-reddening skies The gospel of her advent, and declare The sacred sign of her epiphany there Amid the purple dyes; While all true men—the bravely wise With heart and soul shall seek her where The grey faced prophet peaks arise Out of the shattering mist—the phantom sea Of now surpassing old iniquity! And thence high up, where glow sublime Those sun-flowers of the prime— Those herald glories of the golden time About at last to be! Through dense and rare shall seek her there, Breathing with lion lungs the strenuous mountain air Of a supreme, upclimbing, God-great Liberty!

And thus employed, my purpose is most clinging: For though unto the mole-eyed present I Am almost nothing, I would be no less
