X Lyrics. By Charles Harpur. X

Love the Idealiser.

Why smile you, as in sweet amaze, My Mary,—Mary mine, to see These olden things in Nature’s ways So pleasure-full for me?

For if I cannot well command My joy in aught of glorious frame, Though common,—beautiful or grand, Thy influence is to blame!

Here, gazing at those evening skies, I bathe as in their starry blue, Because I know thy loving eyes Are glorying in it too.

And more, ’tis simply thence, I guess, It seems too in itself so glad, And touched as with a lovingness It one time never had.

And aye how fondly can I trace Yon shining River’s winding stream, For having seen it glass thy face As in a happy dream.

Nor wonder, Mary, it should thence With that fond passion mix and roll, The fountain of whose profluence Lies deepest in my soul.

Lies deepest in my soul, indeed, And under its ideal skies, Doth flow in melody, and feed Its flowers of Paradise

And why for ever in my view So Muse-like looks the wild-fig tree? —’Twas in its shade that first I knew To rhyme sweet thoughts of thee.
