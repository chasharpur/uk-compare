Companion Pieces

I

Emerson at the Best.

The Old World, sectioned by its cold dead walls Of custom and convention, caste and creed, That interclude (were there no other thralls) The tides of liberty, man’s sovereign need, Had dwarfed or perished the original seed Of that large fruit which from thy genius falls In this thy precious Book, to meet the calls Of Mind yet hungering after truth indeed! And never was there Volume wherein shone More wisdom,—nuggetty, like virgin gold! Through every page what streams of nature run— What startling thunder peals of thought are rolled! Thou Bard of Self Reliance, stern and bold! Thou giant-minded mystic, Emerson.
