XXVIII.

The Resignation

We must forget! Thoug harsh the sentence be, ‘Tis born of truth—truth wedded to despair! Had I one lingering hope of good, I’d share Beloved and loving that one hope with thee! But these vile life-long failurs be’t for me Henceforth to bear alone; for selfish ‘twere Longer to wish that one so lovely and rare Should only waste on me her maiden loyalty. 17 Then, turn thee dear One from the Past away, And give the orphic music of thy heart To songs of loftier love; that so the array Which Fortune ever willeth to impart To excellence in Woman, may be thine: Thy life’s bright current must not darken into mine.
