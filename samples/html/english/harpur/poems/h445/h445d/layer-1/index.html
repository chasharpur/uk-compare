The Resignation.

We must forget! Though harsh the sentence be, ‘Tis born of truth—truth wedded to despair! Had I one lingering hope of good, I’d share Belov’d and loving that one hope with thee! But these vile life-long failures, be’t for me Henceforth to bear alone: for selfish ‘twere Longer to wish that one so lovely and rare Should waste on me her maiden loyalty! Then turn thee, dear One, from the past away, And give the orphic music of thy heart To songs of loftier love: that so the array Which Fortune ever willeth to impart To rarity in Woman, may be thine— Thy life’s bright current must not darken into mine.
