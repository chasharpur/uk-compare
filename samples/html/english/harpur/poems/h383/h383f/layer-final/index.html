Odes and Lyrics.

The Poet.

Both great and bountiful is he, The Poet,—he whose glorious gift, Free of the world, and making free, Heavenward on wings of melody Can all things lift!

Yes, well may we account him great Who through his every living line, Doth, elevated, elevate, And throw into the Mind’s estate A ray divine!

And bountiful as well as great, Who still, in every song of power, Commissioned, God-like to create, Bequeathes to Nature and to Fate A rarer flower.

Yea, howsoever Fortune lour, He still, with spirit-riches rife, Can bring into each passing hour The worth of an immortal dower, And higher life.

A rose of joy that cannot die! A gem of light that nought may dim! Some well of faith no drouth shall dry!Some 230 Some foretone of those anthems high The Seraph’s hymn!

Some glimpse of God—some mightier star Hung in a supersensual sky! Some glory without stint or bar— A value overvaluing far All Pride can buy.

All Pride can buy, or Avarice hoard, Or earth-born influence attain! A loveliness by Love adored— A stuff by royal natures stored, As priceless gain.

Then great and bounteous both is he, The Poet,—he whose glorious gift, Free of the world, and making free, Heavenward on wings of melody Doth all things lift.

Yea, whatso he hath seen or heard— As at the morning’s golden rise, The lark from his ground-nest is stirred, And lifted, till he flames—a bird Of Paradise!
