61

Song.

When the heart’s paining With memories raining Out of the dim-clouded dome of the Past, And to its sighing Some Spirit replying, Grieves like the sound of a Pine in the blast: Then be no creature Of passionless feature Found in the path of the dreamer forlorn; And if one tone then, Should mix with his moan then Be it the far mellow voice of a horn. 62 So shall love’s lessons That have no expressions Out of the bygone, to being restart; And time-slain emotions And buried devotions Be for a moment reborn to the heart.
