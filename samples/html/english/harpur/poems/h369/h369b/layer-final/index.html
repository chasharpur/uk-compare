The Master Mariner’s Song.

/Outward bound./

Away—away she plunges With the white sails o’er her spread, Like the sheety clouds that gather On some hill’s piny head: Still away she plunges rampant, Like a lion roused to wrath; And the proud wave lies humbled In the track of her path.

Ye ho! my gallant sailors, Wear her head from off the land: As his steed obeys the Arab, How she yields to the hand! Like a soul the world forsaking Now she leaves the coast behind; And the main’s her wide dwelling, And her spouse is the wind.

Then pledge a full measure To the Friends we left to day, Whose kind thoughts shall hover o’er us On our watery way; Where diurnally remind us Shall the same bright-brimming rite, Of the eyes that yearned blessings When we last knew their light.

Note. I will only observe, by way of Note to this Poem, that it possesses the peculiar merit, such as it is, /in common 179 with many of my Songs/ of being recurrently quantitative in the stanzaical arrangement of the measures: that is to say, each line in the first stanza has a metrical movement and proportion exactly correspondent equivalent to those of the correspondent line corresponding line in all of the following stanzas. Thus, for instance, the concluding lines of the first,

And the proud wave lies humbled In the track of her path,

have their precise numerical counterpart in those of the last,

Of the eyes that yearned blessings When we last knew their light.
