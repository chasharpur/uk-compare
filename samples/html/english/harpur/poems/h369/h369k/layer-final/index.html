Outward Bound.

Away—away she plunges, With her white sails o’er her spread, Like the sheety clouds that gather On some hill’s piny head! 231 Still away she plunges rampant, Like a lion roused to wrath, And the late-proud wave lies humbled In the foam-track of her path.

Ye ho! my gallant sailors Wear her head from off the land; As his steed obeys the Arab How she giveth to the hand! And the Deep is her wide dwelling, Her wild spouse the gipsy Wind, Like a soul from earth departing So she leaves the coast behind!

Then fill we a full measure To the friends we left to day, Whose kind thoughts shall hover o’er us On our wave-foaming way; Where diurnally remind us Shall the same bright-brimming rite, Of the fond eyes that yearned blessings When we last beheld their light.
