I.

The Keepsake Rose.

No Flower more beautiful, none lovelier blows In Natures fragrant bosom. Even where With rarest living gems some Garden rare Flames like heaven’s pavement, fairest amongst those For ever blooms the ruddy regal Rose! And this was given (to bear with me afar) By One whose image, always, like a star When ocean-mirrored, in my fancy glows. And daily henceforth,—ever at this hour,— I’ll look on it though faded, and so reap Rich solace from the Past: for then the Power Of mutual passion shall around me keep In mystic presence, and upon me shower Memories that bless me even while I weep.
