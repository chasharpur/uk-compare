[RECORDS OF A POET’S LOVE XIX]

No Flower more beautiful, none lovelier, blows In Nature’s fragrant bosom! Even where With rarest living gems some Garden rare Flames like heaven’s pavement, fairest among those It bloometh still—the ruddy regal Rose! And this was given (to bear with me afar) By Her whose image, ever, like a star When ocean-mirrored, in my fancy glows! And daily henceforth,—always at this hour, I’ll look on it though faded, and so reap Rich solace from the past: for then the Power Of absent passion shall around me keep In mystic presence, and upon me shower Memories that bless me even while I weep.
