XIX,

The Rose

O ’tis the beautifullest Flower that blows In Nature’s fragrant bosom; even where 55 Its various living gems some Garden rare Pours on the eye of Taste, while round them flows The freshening fountain;—the imperial Rose! And this in token of true love was given By her whose image with my hopes of heaven, Deep in my soul forever—ever glows! Daily, when far away, upon this hour I’ll look on it, though faded, and so reap Rich solace from the past: for then the Power Of destined passion shall around me keep,— Breathingly present,—and upon me shower Such memories as make it bliss to weep.
