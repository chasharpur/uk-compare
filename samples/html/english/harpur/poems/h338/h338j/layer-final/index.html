Personal and other Sonnets.

Noon, Evening, and Night.

With what content the forest dells are blest Near bubbling waters,—when at Noon the breeze Creeps, sighing sultry, over the opprest And browning herbs in the unsheltered leas! Till gradual come the mellowing degrees Of shadowy Eve, and in the dreamy West Cloud-wrought Elysiums hang in golden rest, Glowing forth bliss for every eye that sees. Then of deep Night the still mysterious mien How grateful, with her skiey pastures, bright With gold-fleeced flocks,—while eastward, o’er the scene, Walks their Queen Shepherdess with a step of light.— Alone ’mid these (in Memory’s despite) Almost I feel as wrong had never been!
