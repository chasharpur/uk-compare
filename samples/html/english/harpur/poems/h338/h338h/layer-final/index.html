XII.

[NOON, EVENING, AND NIGHT]

With what content the forest bowers are blest Near waters, bubbling,—when at Noon the breeze Creeps, sighing sultry, over the opprest And browning herbs in the unsheltered leas: Till sweeter come the gradual degrees Of shadowy Eve,—when in the dreamy West Cloud-wrought Elysiums hang in golden rest, And glow forth bliss for every eye that sees. Then of deep Night the still mysterious mien How grateful, with her skiey pastures, bright With starry flocks,—while eastward, o’er the scene, Walks their Queen-Shepherdess with a step of light.— Alone ’mid these, my soul forgets (despite Of all the past) that wrong hath ever been.
