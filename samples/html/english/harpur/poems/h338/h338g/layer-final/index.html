II. ☞

Noon, Evening, and Night.

With what content the forest bowers are blest Near streams cool bubbling, when at Noon the breeze Crawls, as with scorching feet, o’er the opprest An[d] browning herbs in the unsheltered leas! But sweeter far the gradual degrees Of shadowy Eve, when in the dreamy west Cloud-wrought Elysiums hang in golden rest, And glow forth joy for every eye that sees. Then of deep Night the still mysterious mien How grateful, with her solemn birds in flight 26 Dim gliding ‘neath the stars; whilst o’er the scene The Moon comes, queen-like, with a step of light. —Alone ’mid these, my soul forgets (despite Of all the past) that wrong hath ever been.
