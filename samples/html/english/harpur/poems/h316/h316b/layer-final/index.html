The Muse’s Ethics. ᴓ X

Hold to the right, and be loyal in all things: This is of Manhood the only true plan! Hold to thy faith, or in great or in small things: This is the statute God layeth on Man!

Build not thy structures upon the world’s notion, Deeming all good may be compassed with pelf; Nothing in fortune is worth thy devotion— Nothing’s worth loving that owns not itself.

34

Perish the wisdom that makes us time-servers! Perish the virtue that’s gain in disguise! Leave them to cowards, to seemers and swervers, Yea, make them the scorn of the valiant and wise!

Fear not when wrong power contriveth undue laws, That right shall flow pure through the peoples no more! Blench not when traitors abandon a true cause— For the truth of it liveth with God as before!

Nations benighted by failure, believe not; But wait for the morning, do thou,—and believe: Mammon’s high castes ban the poor, and relieve not; But make God thy debtor, do thou,—and relieve.

Live—die for thy own (be they many or few men,) Following love even into the grave! Thus, and thus only, can any be true men, Faithful in all things, and pious, and brave.
