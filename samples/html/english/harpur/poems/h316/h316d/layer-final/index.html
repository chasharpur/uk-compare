[THE MUSE’S ETHICS]

Hold to the right, and be loyal in all things: This is in morals the only true plan. Hold to thy faith, or in great or in small things: This is the statute God layeth on man.

Build not thy structures upon the world’s no- tion, That deemeth all good may be compassed through pelf: Nothing in fortune is worth thy devotion That any can buy, as not owning itself.

Perish the wisdom that makes us times-servers! Perish the virtue that’s ‘gain in disguise! Leave them to cowards, to weaklings, to swervers— Yea, make them thy scorn, as one valiantly wise.

Fear not, when wrong Power contriveth undue laws, That Right shall be fountained in manhood no more! Blench not, when traitors abandon a true cause— The truth of it liveth with God as before!

Nations benighted by failure, believe not; But wait for the morning, do thou—and be- lieve: Mammon’s high castes ban the poor, and re- lieve not; But make God thy debtor, do thou—and relieve.
