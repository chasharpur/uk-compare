POEMS BY CHARLES HARPUR.

I.

LIFE’S FIRST DESPAIR.

‘Tis thirty years ago—and yet The sweet pale face of a dead Child Lies cold upon my heart, to re-beget An ancient sorrow wild! His face, who was our youngest pride: Henry,—the little one that died.

I heard an outcry in the night, And then a deep sad voice that said: ‘Tis even so; my boding heart was right— Our poor sick Boy is dead! And yet my sense of loss but seemed Like some vague wild thing I had dreamed.

But when that solemn night was past, The feeling of how wide a sea Had in the ghostly darkness rolled its vast Black depth ‘twixt him and me, Even as I breathed it, damped the air With life’s first terrible despair!
