SQUATTER SONGS.

NO. IV.

A “GRIEVANCE” FIFTY MILES LONG.*

Full fifty miles of hill and dale Were Squatted at once by one Squire Dead nail (Chorus.) A capital grazing ground finder, O! And for this vast Tract, in acquittance clear, He has hitherto paid just ten pounds per year— (Chorus.) What a capital grazing- ground finder, O! And so, at the Government’s penetration, Whenever he rode o’er his beautiful station, With a “fun-poking” look Tow’rd his Super., he took— (Chorus.) Oh, such a deuce of a Grinder, O! But Stanley’s Regulations now Will cut his great run into ten, I vow— (Chorus.) Could any ten cuts be un- kinder, O! And when a cool hundred’s the yearly dump He’ll have to dub down if he sticks to the lump— (Chorus.) Ah, could any ten cuts be unkinder, O! Why then, at the Government’s penetration, Whenever he rides o’er his multiplied Station, With an outwitted shake Of the noddle, he’ll take— (Chorus) The deuce a bit of a Grinder, O! What wonder then, that Squire Dead-nail To thresh in the Atlas hath hired a Flail— (Chorus.) A capital right-handed winder, O! But never a word, I warrant, they’ll hear Of the part he sublets yielding ninety a year— (Chorus.) From this capital right- handed winder, O! Nor of how at the Government’s penetration, When erewhile he rode o’er his fifty mile station, He was wont, while he’d shake With sleeve-laughter, to make— (Chorus.) Oh, such a deuce of a Grinder, O!

* As it is confidently anticipated that these Songs, so soon as the series shall have been com- pleted, will be republished under the special pa- tronage of the Squatocracy, in the shope of “Tegg’s Warbler,” it were better, perhaps, that they should all bear specific titles as above. Let No. 1, then, be called “Bush Morality;” No. 9, headed with this motto from Esther—”And Haman told them of the glory of his riches (pas- tures), and of the multitude of his children (sheep);” adding, however, ‘Yet all this availeth me nothing, so long as I see Mordecai the Jew sitting at the king’s gate.” And to complete their christening as far as yet published, let No. 3, be entitled, “A prime case against the Go- vernment, if well got up.’”—(Author’s Note.
