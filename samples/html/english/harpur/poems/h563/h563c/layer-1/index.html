Squatter Songs. No IV.

[A “GRIEVANCE” FIFTY MILES LONG]

Full fifty miles of hill and dale Were Squatted at once by one Squire Dead-nail!— (Chorus.) A capital grazing-ground finder O! And in yearly acquittance for all this here, He has hitherto payed just ten pounds clear (Chorus.) What a capital grazing-ground finder O! And so, at the Government’s penetration, Whenever he rode o’er his beautiful Station, With a “fun-poking” look At his Super, he took— (Chorus.) Oh, such a deuse of a Grinder O!

But Stanley’s Regulations now, Will cut in ten his great Rim, I vow!— (Chorus.) Could any ten cuts be unkinder O! And when a cool hundred’s the yearly fare, That shall have to be payed for all that there (Chorus.) Ah, could any ten cuts be unkinder O! Why then, at the Government’s penetration, Whenever he rides o’er his multiplied Station, With an outwitted shake Of the head, he will take— (Chorus.) The deuse a bit of a Grinder O!

What wonder then, that Squire Dead-nail To thresh in the Atlas hath hired a Flail? (Chorus.) A capital right-handed winder O! But never a word, I’ll warrant, they’ll hear Of his yearly Subs paying ninty clear!— (Chorus.) From this capital right-hander winder O! Nor of how, at the Government’s penetration, When erewhile he rode o’er his fifty mile station He was wont, with a shake Of his noddle, to take— (Chorus.) Oh, such a deuse of a grinder, O!
