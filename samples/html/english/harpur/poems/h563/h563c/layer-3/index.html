Squatter Songs. No IV.

[A “GRIEVANCE” FIFTY MILES LONG]

Full fifty miles of hill and dale Were Squatted at once by one Squire Dead-nail!— (Chorus.) A capital grazing-ground finder O! And for this vast scope in, acquittance clear, He has hitherto payed just ten pounds—per year!— (Chorus.) What a capital grazing-ground finder O! And so, at the Government’s penetration, Whenever he rode o’er his beautiful Station, With a “fun-poking” look Tow’rd his Super, he took— (Chorus.) Oh, such a deuse of a Grinder O!

But Stanley’s Regulations now, Will cut his great Rim into ten, I vow!— (Chorus.) Could any ten cuts be unkinder O! And when a cool hundred’s the yearly dump, He’ll have to dub down if he sticks to the lump— (Chorus.) Ah, could any ten cuts be unkinder O! Why then, at the Government’s penetration, Whenever he rides o’er his multiplied Station, With an outwitted shake Of his noddle, he’ll take— (Chorus.) The deuse a bit of a Grinder O!

What wonder then, that Squire Dead-nail To thresh in the Atlas hath hired a Flail? (Chorus.) A capital right-handed winder O! But never a word, I warrant, they’ll hear Of the ninty paid by his Subs per year (Chorus.) From this capital right-hander winder O! Nor of how, at the Government’s penetration, When erewhile he rode o’er his fifty mile station He was wont, while he’d shake with sleeve-laughter, to take— (Chorus.) Oh, such a deuse of a grinder, O!
