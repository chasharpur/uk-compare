[...] So deaf is that dread gulf ‘twixt life & death Which, in a moment, widens to Eternity! And is it wholly well, that it is thus So deaf, so dumb, so blindly separative, That Love (though fabled to have moved even Hell) May stare tear-blinded from its hither shore And shriek to it in vain? that from beyond No quieting whisper may across it breathe Of peace from the Immortals? that no glimpse Of that [unclear] beauty which sunbathes Its heavenward coast, can reach us?—while we here Sit groaning—full of wild misgivings,—full Of mournful memories and embittered wonts; And so engloomed, so overcast by that slow Disquieting doubts, that we are often fain To leap from them at once, though out of life, Madly impatient to have done with time [...]
