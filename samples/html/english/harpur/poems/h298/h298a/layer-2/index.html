Memo Begun 22 May 1867

Obituary Lines

My dear dear Charley, can it be that thou Art gone from us for ever? While I sit Amid these forest shadows that now rest In somber masses waxed with shifting beams Upon thy early Grave; and think of all The love & joy that was our mutual lot So late, and during all thy little life— Thy thirteen years of sonhood, it is hard (So mystical it seems) to realise the solemn certainty; that thou art now In the Eternal World—as reft away, In one wild moment from thy Father’s heart— Yea reft as utterly, from his lonely side As hath thy Boy’s shadow, once so often there Hath vanished out of space Thy young intelligence from his lonely side- Reft so for ever!—& gone as utterly As utterly as hath thy shadow, once So often there, now gliding and now still— Thy slim Boy-shadow, leaving him alas! Thus sitting here, forlorn, and staring out Into infinitude, half blind with tears.
