The Anchor.

Strong trust of the bold Sailor On the shores of sudden storm, 32 How massive is its structure, And how able is its form! Old ocean’s bed rock gripping With the hurricane’s tug ‘twill cope, And therefore tis the emblem held Of Man’s eternal hope.

Where never plummet sounded By the bleak and stormy steep, Like a thunder bolt down rushing It is shot into the deep: Down where some mute sea monster Its so hideous bulk uprears, And mightiest Silence drowned hath lain For many a thousand years.

In some famed bay of battle When ’tis plunged with sullen roar— In the Nile, in Naverino, Or by Danish Elsinore— How meetly there it slumbers, Deep beneath the sounding waves, Amid the bones of gallant tars In Glory watery graves!

In the bright and land-locked harbour, The weary voyäge o’er, When only pleasant breezes Breathe sweet welcome from the shore, Its noble form down dropping To its quiet bed below, Gives signal to the first glad boat That beachward pants to go.

In the storm thou firm-fixed surety— Thou strong helper in the storm— Even a child, when first beholding All the fitness of thy form, Might well divine the dangers Wherewithal ’tis thine to cope, And take thee as an emblem meet Of Man’s eternal hope.
