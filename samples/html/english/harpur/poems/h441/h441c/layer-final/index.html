XXIV.

[RECORDS OF A POET’S LOVE XXIV]

We met yet once again! At which last meeting Her lustrous eyes with fond tears only dimm’d, And turned to mine as erewhile, overbrimm’d, With all that even Love’s large heart when beating With tenderness, could ask of tender greeting And passionate gladness! Then soul-treasured words Flowed, or were stilted, like interrupted birds, By the long kiss my lips would keep repeating 15 On her dear hand as by their own wild will— That dear hand, trembling as in fond constraint! And when at length our joy grew mute, the faint Perfumery of an orange grove did fill The air around, and make our breath:—and thus The world seemed Paradise, and to hold but us.
