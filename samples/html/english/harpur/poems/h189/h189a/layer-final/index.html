Impromptu.

Written on the verge of a beautiful bit of woodland scenery at Granbelang.

This tasty bit of scenery Idealised in picture, Might well become, as ‘twould, no doubt, A sweet poetic fixture;

With music of celestial tone Adown yon dim glade swelling From demigods and mythic Nymphs All in the distance dwelling.

But here, in the reality, It gives the Muse a sad shock, To know that out beyond us there We’ve only Bowman’s Pad-dock!

His Paddock whose mere image turns To ice all genial dreaming, And from whose frightful prosiness All poetry flies screaming!
