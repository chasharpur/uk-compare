[RECORDS OF A POET’S LOVE XXVII]

My hope hath lagged behind me, and my heart Hath but one well of faith—delved in the past— Whereat to slake its thirst; and whence, at last, My very life seems but a lingering part And parcel of a refluent tide of Love! The future’s beingless—but from the haze Of by-gone time, two warm eyes ever gaze Me-ward, in distant kindness, to emove Some recollection trancingly intense Of rich vows granted for the grace of granting! The present too is cold, and blank, and thence The Past grows dearer—and though dead, keeps haunting My memory, till it seemeth to my sense A living Spirit with destined passion panting.
