XXVII.

Hope foregone

My hope hath lagged behind me, and my heart Hath but one well, in the believing Past, Whereat to slake its thirst; and whence at last, My very life seems but a lingering part And parcel of some refluent tide of Love! The Future’s beingless—but from the haze Of by-gone time two warm eyes ever gaze Me-ward, in distant kindness—to emove Some recollection trancingly intense Of rich vows granted for the grace of granting! The Present too is cold and blank—and thence The Past grows dearer; and, though dead, keeps haunting My memory, till it seemeth to its sense A living Spirit with destined passion panting.
