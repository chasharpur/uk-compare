POEMS BY CHARLES HARPUR.

I.

ENGLISH BEAUTY.

Tell me not of maids Italian, or the stately dames of Spain; Let their beauty’s burning semblance fire some other brain! But a gentle English maiden, lily fair,—a flower of light! She has all the dainty graces wherein I delight.

Smiles as rich and rare as rubies,—utterance purely feminine,— Large blue eyes that drink the soul up, as a draught of wine; And with these a modest languor all the dearer that it seems The unwitting revelation of her loving dreams.

Sweet and lowly flowers that glare not are the latest to decay; Stars that bring their blessing nearest have the mildest ray: He who visioned Desdemona knew his countrywomen well; He who drew the heavenly Una loved an English belle.
