Morning.

With purple-tinct fingers Aurora’s back drawing The bed-curtain gear of the jolly faced Day, While in yon old ironbark loudly jawing Are two laughing jackasses—jawing away!

Beautiful change hath inspired the creation! And bobtails and cocktails sing sweet in the briar; In the gum-tree the gorger pours forth his oration, And below in the wattle the bald-headed friar.

Old soldiers are piping in every thicket, In flocks hopping charleys come chattering by, And see, doctor Fauster—that conjurer wicked! Is getting well trounced by a testy old pie.

Long-leggëd native companions are freaking ※ At times in the blady-grass patches below, While from tree top to tree top a hawk there sneaking (As some land-griper used) after Governor Fovaux.

※ These birds literally dance, and not ungracefully. The cock takes his place betwixt his two hens (two are his complement) and the whole three begin the caper together. Then the cock backs out of the line, and extends (say) the right wing, under which the hen on that side dips gracefully, passing out beyond him; and then the left wing is extended in like manner, for the other hen to dip under it, and out be- yond him, just as the first did: and so the dance proceeds and continues for perhaps ten minutes.
