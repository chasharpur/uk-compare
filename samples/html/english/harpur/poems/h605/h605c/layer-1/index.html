To certain Criticlings.

“Dogs or men! for I flatter you in saying That ye are dogs—you’re betters far.”

Byron.

Why howl ye at my star? It dooms you all! Why was the Galilean’s light maligned Of old, by all blind leaders of the blind? His rising was predictive of their fall!— I pour forth honey, and with tongues all gall Ye lap it! This my Country’s common wind Have I made lyrical—and lo, your kind All therefore hate me, and the effect miscall! But always when an Eagle with strong wings Comes sweeping o’er yon wood, a rable flight Of chattering pies send up their jargonings, And hustle round him in ludicrous spite; Whilst he himself, in his majestic might, Moves on unhindered by the paltry things!
