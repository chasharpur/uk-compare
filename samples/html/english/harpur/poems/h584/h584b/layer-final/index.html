[THERE’S NO WORLD LIKE THE ONE THAT WE LIVE IN]

There’s no world like the one that we live in, To spend, or to lend, or to give in; But to beg, in or borrow, or come by one’s own, ‘Tis the very worst world that ever was known: And the sooner we slip from its folly and fuss The better for it, and the better for us.
