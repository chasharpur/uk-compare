STANZAS.

Dear Henry, forbear to complain That my Muse so much singeth of Love, Since ever employed in like strain Are the fire-winged Seraphs above.

In Freedom’s high praises, aloft I will soar—I will thunder at Wrong! But still let me sweeten more oft With the manna of Beauty my song.

And what is there under the skies So complete for the voice of the Muse, When gracious, as beautiful eyes, And young cheeks with their rosiate hues?

And lips that as twin cherries smile, Breathing balm like an Indian Spring; And all these expressive the while Of the exquisite Spirit I sing?

And though these should fade, being crost, Then the more our remembrance should claim, The love-dreaming Girl that was lost In the measureless strength of her flame.

Then never again disapprove Of the passionate Lay! Were it wrong, Would the Stars sing together of love, And the Angels rejoice in their song?
