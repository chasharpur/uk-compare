Original Poetry.

A SONG OF DAVID INCITING TO DANCE.

FROM “KING SAUL.”

By The late Charles Harpur.

Behold the Spring is with us, Even she, whose sunny hand Strips the tempest-robe of winter From the bosom of the land, And the bright birds in joyous motion Wheel and tumble through the air, So well their happy spirits test Her genial presence there.

The roes upon the mountains, The white lambs within the vale, Are all bounding in their gladness As they drink the spicy gale; From the herded horse a trample Like a flood’s mad rapture rolls As o’er the glebe they thunder In the glory of their souls!

Yea, in the loneliest places A wide joy of motion swells The wild utterance of the echoes That are housed in mountain dells: And the sand of the far desert By the lion is up-hurled When the tempest robe of Winter thus Is gathered from the world.

So full is God of bounty, For all this in him has birth: The wide clearness of the heavens, The green gladness of the earth; Then with these, his grateful creatures, Join we all with one accord, To swell the triumph of the spring And dance before the Lord.

Euroma, May 20th.
