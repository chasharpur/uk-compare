Odes and Lyrics.

Mary Arden.

When a simple English maiden Nested warm in Wilmicote, Singing like a lark, when trailing Heavenward up its morning note— Did no English ear that listened, Even then, foretouched by Fame Tremble to the prophet music Fountain-headed in thy name: Mary Arden? Tell me, shade of Mary Arden! Shade of Shakspere’s Mother, tell me.

And to thee thyself, O tell me, Did no dazzling vision come (Like a sunburst over Stratford, Burning up its boreal gloom), Of the bardic grandeurs waiting On thy matron fate, when He Who in time should call thee Mother, Should all time’s Subjector be: Mary Arden? 235 Tell me, shade of Mary Arden! Shade of Shakspere’s Mother, tell me.

Then a Mother we behold thee, With that Babe upon thy breast— That great nascent soul so bird-like Babbling in its fragrant nest: O what feelings sweetly human— O what instincts mildly wise— Sucked he from those mother-fountains? Drew he from those mother-eyes? Mary Arden! Tell me, shade of Mary Arden! Shade of Shakspere’s Mother, tell me.

But shall we, now spirit-basking In the noon-blaze of his fame, Fail to read a sign prophetic In thy lovely maiden Name? No: it is the star that trembled O’er a royal Poet’s birth; And amongst immortal Maries Second to but one on earth: Mary Arden! Glory be to Mary Arden— Shakspere’s Mother! England’s Mary!x

x Long after the above Poem was written I came upon a passage in De Quincey which I extracted at once, both for the light which it adds to it, and for the fine spirit in which it justifies my own foregone enthusiasm. For unfortunately, in our present Australia, such is the poverty, the dog-leanness of all intellectual sympathy, that one [unclear] cannot seem to be too well guarded with a “sufficient reason” for heroic sentiments and proclivities of any kind. De Quincey says: 236 ”This Lady, for such she really was in an eminent sense, by birth as as well as by connections, bore the beautiful name of Mary Arden, a name derived from the ancient forest district of the County (Warwic); and doubtless she merits a more elaborate notice than our slender materials will furnish. To have been the mother of Shakspere—how august a title to the reverence of infinite generations, and of centuries beyond the vision of prophecy!”
