MARY ARDEN.

When a simple English maiden, Nested warm in Wilmicote, Singing like a skylark, trailing Heavenward up its morning note— Did no English ear that listened, Even then,—foretouched by Fame,— Tremble to the prophet-music, Fountain-headed in that name: Mary Arden? Tell me, Shade of Mary Arden! Shade of Shakspere’s Mother, tell me!

II.

Or to thee thyself, O tell me; Did no dazzling vision come,— Like a sun-burst over Stratford, Burning up its boreal gloom,— Of the Bardic grandeurs waiting On thy matron fate, when he Who in time would call thee mother, Should all time’s Subjector be: Mary Arden? Tell me, Shade of Mary Arden! Shade of Shakspere’s Mother, tell me!

III.

Then a mother we beheld thee, With that babe upon thy breast— That great nascent soul, so bird-like, Babbling in its fragrant nest! Oh, what feelings sweetly human— Oh, what instincts mildly wise— Sucked he from those mother-fountains! Drew he from those mother-eyes! Mary Arden! Tell me, Shade of Mary Arden! Shade of Shakspere’s Mother, tell me!

IV.

But shall we, now spirit-basking In the noon-blaze of his fame, Fail to read a sign prophetic In this lovely maiden name? No! it is the star that trembled O’er a royal poet’s birth; And amongst immortal Marys Second to but one on earth! Mary Arden! Glory be to Mary Arden! Shakspere’s Mother! England’s Mary.
