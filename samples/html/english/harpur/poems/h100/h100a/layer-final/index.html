COLONIAL LITERATURE. Poet’s Corner. ORIGINAL.

The Dream.

A LOVE SONG.

[BY C. HARPUR.]

While Augusta my dream I relate thee in numbers, Oh! mark how devoted’s the lover in me: When not e’en an image of fancy in slumbers, Can reign for a moment divided from thee.

Me

thought as I stray’d on my wild Native Shore, I saw roses of gold sudden bloom on a tree; And wond’ring approached soon to seize the bright store, When straight they were miniature copies of thee.

Ne

xt methought that I gazed on a nightly horizon, And beheld the moon broaden descending to me, And strangely enamoured I grew as she nigh shone, When straight she became a pale copy of thee.

Th

en methought all the Heavens were open’d around me, With a low rolling noise like the voice of the sea; And angels in thousands were gazing upon me, And every bright angel a copy of thee.
