SONNETS DEDICATED TO AUSTRALIAN SENATORS.

NO. XII.

“Bardolph.—The gentleman being, as we say, “fap” (drunk), his conclusions passed the barriers (proper limits).”

[SONNETS DEDICATED TO AUSTRALIAN SENATORS XIV]

Surely some spirit of Folly that much hates man Was prevalent in Windsor, in the shape Of high-drugg’d Rum, tis likely, when Bill Ape Was therein chosen to go forth a statesman! Yes; and what’s more, Bill, you were surely “fap,” sir, When weening first, so much Acratus flatters, Your genius fitted you for public matters As well almost as for a public tap, sir! What though your sheep were numberless indeed, Even richer yet were many a greater gaby! Nor would you therefore a right national stay be! Fine flocks are well,—but more Australia’s need Asks now of Providence a nobler breed Of men, sir; men!—as unlike you as may be.

A HAWKESBURY BOY.
