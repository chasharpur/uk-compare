Terrible Truth.

Lo, there are truths so terrible, that they seem To breathe a moral danger. Hence we fear To write them, but no less themselves they write On all our human doings. This is one: Nature’s a criminal to the core, and makes All stolen fruit the sweetest. Is not sin, Even sin, a sauce to pleasure, giving it Prime relish, and the keenest edge of joy? And though the conscience, or whate’er we call By that fine name, may sometimes shudder, yet The greater the invasion, all the more The apetite is freshened, and enforced. 761b What then? Is Nature devillish? or would she But testify her lusty scorn of all Mere moral starvelings, with their timid codes, And prim proprieties that would in vain Straight jacket the strong will, and pale the cheek, And pine the belly, and freeze all the heart’s Wild flowing riot to a bloodless calm?
