[THE FAMOUS NIGHT SCENE IN THE VIII ILIAD I]

The mouth-chafed and sweat-foaming horse forthwith from their traces are loosened And fastened in ranks by the cars, that gleam through the rents of the darkness. Fat oxen & sheep from the Town are brought in uncounted abundance With bread & wine for the troops, who meanwhile have hewn with their axes A huge store of fuel, and kindled their bivouac fires in thousands, Whence soon to the night wind are given the steam of their savoury supper. Even thus when the Moon is aloft; shining broad in the unclouded heaven, And in the wide fields of the air the stars with a manifold splendor Are burning alive in her train; all at once in the blaze of the brightness See the hills and the pinnacled rocks how they thrust themselves up into prospect With the brows of the slopes and the woods that erewhile were huddled in shadow! And even the valleys are filled with a glitter, like rivers of silver: While o’er them the firmament still seems to burst with a surcharge of glory, Till the signs of the year are all shown, and the heart of the shepherd rejoices. So many and thick are the fires before the proud city of Priam Bringing its structures by gleams substantially out from the darkness And reddening far on its way the bickering current of Xanthus! And wh by each several fire a guard of full fifty bold Trojans, Whose furbished arms glare as they move; while the bright brazen studs of the chariots Keep scattering into the darkness a broken and shadowing lustre; And near them the high mettled horse neigh loudly at times o’er their fodder As if, with their masters, they wished for the silvery light of the morning.
