John Hiki, (1847)

Should Switzerland’s rude rocks be felt the throne Of Liberty, God-chartered there to quell All tyrants‒there invincibly, alone! Because the echoes in their caves that dwell Hold converse once with the bold voice of Tell? And thine, New Zealand, yet be kept unknown To Glory, now that thy dark Chief hath thrown Around them a like patriotic spell? Should Wallace be a name Time may not lose? And Hofer’s, his who for his mountains died, Rather than see the Spoiler there abuse Freedom’s high altar, be a sound of pride? And Heki, thine remain unglorified In the proud annals of the Heroic Muse?

It seems that some American skippers are to be branded with miscreancy “des- [e]rving duration” (as wooden-legged Marky Flood used to say) for having supplied the Newzealanders with certain barrels of gunpowder lately. But what if Jonathan should send them a whole cargo and plead in vindication, as he very likely would, that he furnished the said “villanous saltpetre” under sanction of that national flag which the British Government accorded to these same Newzealanders a few years since? and then threw the matter, in their early shape, upon the national sympathies of the world, [unclear] as it may be and is? Could all the Special pleading which [unclear] might be pumped up on the other side, from the constitutional leakage of Downing Street, past even a tolerably clean face upon any attempt to outface such a national, and at the same time ‒ rational plea? x No: not if there is but one grain of political honesty extant in the nineteenth century:‒ As to John Heki if there be any such thing as patriotism ‒ he is a patriot. If Tell and Hofer were heroes ‒ he is also a hero. If the resistance which Leonidas offered to a foreign spoiler was noble ‒ so was his late outbreak, and so also will be any future one he may yet instigate and conduct. Noble I say, was that outbreak, whatever may be its ultimate issue; and consoling was it to every true patriot, however he might deplore its concomitant bloodshed: for it was an assurance to him, that the spirits of Sparta’s “three hundred”, with those of Wallace and Tell, and Hofer, and Emmet had not all abandoned the unworthy earth. C.H.
