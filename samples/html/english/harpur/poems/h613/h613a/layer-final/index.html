V.

To I. N.

Think you I have not skill to gather gold, If I could love it as some others do? Or that I lack the spirit of a bold And resolute man, in any cause that’s true, Because I will not juggle with some crew Of pitiless mouthers? Let the truth be told: Whatever I can value I can mould, To meet Occasion looming into view. “But fame he sought not with gold-grasping hand;” (This of my being let the future tell) “Nor through the tricks of popular command: But in seclusion, where the Muses dwell, That his life’s legacy might be a well Of loving water in a thirsty land.”
