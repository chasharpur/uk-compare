To James Norton Esq.

Think you I have not skill to gather gold If I could love it some others do? Or that I lack the spirit of a bold And resolute man in any cause that’s true, Because I scorn to juggler with some crew Of politic schemers? Let the truth be told: Whatever I can value, I can mould Right deftly to my ends—and boldly too. “But fame he sought not through a gainful hand,” (This of my being let the future tell) “Nor through the arts of popular command; But, in retirement, where the Muses dwell, That his life legacy might be—a well Pierian, in a wide and thirsty Land.
