VII.

A day-dream of Love and Happiness.

I once did think there was no happiness 45 In the wide world for me—so harsh its features Of social commerce, and its human creatures So barren of the will and power to bless: And like a flower that in some wilderness Pines in unfellowed sweetness, in my brain Each beauteous dream of love had bloomed in vain— Bloomed but to wither in untold distress. And thus my being unto hope was dying When Rosa smiled upon it;—then, as we While yet upon the skirt of Winter sighing, May hear the Spring’s voice through some glad bird replying, I heard a Vision singing—Yet for thee Shall Happiness a bride in Love’s green valley be.”
