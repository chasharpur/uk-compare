III.

Retrospective

There was in the whole world, I once did guess, For me no true content,—so base the springs Of all its commerce, and its men and things So barren of the will & power to bless: And like a flower that in some wilderness 546 Is loved of none, though lovely, in my doomed Life-loneliness, each dream of good had bloomed Only to wither—only for distress! And thus my being unto hope was dying, When a new power broke o’er it;—then, as we While yet on Winter’s skirt, may hear o’erflying Some warbled note, the new Spring prophesying, I dreamt that Happiness even yet for me, Should in the Muse a bride immortal be!
