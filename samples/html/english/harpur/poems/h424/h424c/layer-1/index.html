VII.

[RECORDS OF A POET’S LOVE VII]

There was in the wide world, I once did deem, For me, no happiness,—so harsh its features Of social commerce, and its human creatures So barren of the will and power to bless: And like a flower that in some wilderness Wastes in unmated sweetness, in my doomed Life-loneleness, each dream of love had bloomed Only to wither—only for distress! And thus my heart’s faith unto hope was dying, When Nora smiled upon it;—then, as we, While yet on Winter’s skirt, may hear o’erflying Some warbled note, the Springtime prophesying, A Vision’s voice passed, saying: Yet for thee Shall Happiness a bride in Love’s green valley be
