Adapted from Pope.

[SONNETS DEDICATED TO AUSTRALIAN SENATORS VII]

Sure Cumberland, at length, must feel ashamed Of thee, her Folly’s representative!— A conscious dunce! yet not content to give Thy vote in silence—no; thou wouldst be famed For something, and so calvedst a Bill—conceived In the true spirit of—”it sounds more clever, To us and to our heirs, my Lord, for ever:” And eke a speech, of mental tapeworms weaved. I doubt not thou canst tell when wool is good, Or count correctly in a shearing pen; Yes; and when flies shall have sufficient ken To comprehend a mountain’s magnitude, Thou shalt be fit to legislate for men, As well as sheep; but, C————r, not till then.

NO WOOL-GATHERER.
