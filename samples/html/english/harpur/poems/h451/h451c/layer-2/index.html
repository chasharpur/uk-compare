XLVI. X

Mine After All.

Mine after all—my Mary! Why should I That dear—that sweet name any more disguise? Now that beneath the conscious-seeming skies My joy may spread itself as openly! I weep,—yet fondly ask the reason why? Thinking my happy heart in no such wise Should keep effusing through my happy eyes Now that she’s mine by an enduring tie! Mine after all—my Mary! Lo, the past With all its tender dread and passionate sorrow But travailed with content! since thus, as Last, Love’s fervent light into my lot is cast; Even as the sun ariseth on the morrow Out of the broken storm—cloudless and vast!
