Original Poetry.

WISDOM.

Say, what is Wisdom? Ah ’tis but to know That all is vanity, or worse, below. ‘Tis to be fated with a prophet eye Without the strength to dare, the power to fly: O’er past Times’ shameful history to brood Without the hope of better, if as good. Intending ease to find the body whole The sickly captive of th’ ambitious soul; Or with an intellectual flight designed, The brutal body dragging down the mind. To have a touch so keen—a glance so staid, Life’s seeming substance proves an empty shade. ‘Tis while o’er others’ meannesses we groan In self discovering naught that might atone To read in others’ littleness, our own. To dread that if explored Fate’s stormy night— Rapt in its lightennigs to their farthest fight Would be to perish in insufferable Light!

C.

HARPUR.
