Wellington.

Great Captain, if you will! great Duke! great Slave! Great Minion of the Crown!—but a great Man He was not! He? the primary battering-ram— The master gun—the wide-mouthed model mortar Of mere Authority, though shrunk with age and worn, 621 Into a drivelling mockery of itself, And propt on crutches! He? the atheist Of a most earthy duty, To which the ‘Powers that be’ were simply not Of God, but in his stead! Shall we belie All rightious instinct and profane the truth, By calling great a man without a heart? Whose wire-like nerves never with pity thrilled? Whose soul grew never luminous with a thought Of mercy, or yearned with a benign desire? Nay, who was even too stolid for remorse To move with the remotest sense of blood— The blood that lay, like a red deluge, round All the proud landmarks of his dread career! One only mighty as a devil might be— Mighty for evil—mighty against good! Creating nothing lasting, nought beyond The commissariat meet for a campaign; And building, if at all, only redoubts; Designing only trenches, to be filled As monstrous graves with battle-broken bones In festering layers cohering,—glued together Like pavement, with a slime of puddled gore! He great? a man as feeling-cold as stone! Dull and inventionless—yea, dull as lead, Save in the learning of inhuman lore, And in the cunning of old devices, big With murderous ruin, ponderously malign! Bloodless as iron, and, in compliment, Thence clept the Iron Duke! Such a man great? One who, apart from the despotic wills Of crown’d Oppressors, knew no right, no wrong, No creed, no country, and no brotherhood! If he was great, I say,—may God most high 622 Spare henceforth to our universal race All greatness; seeing it may sometimes be The rigid, kindless battlement of Power Self throned and sanctioned only by the sword. And if, as Englishmen are proud to boast, He was their greatest countryman—alas, For England’s national sterility! But they who thus belaud him—lie! as all True patriots most feelingly perceive. Besides, however we account him,—great Or little,—Wellington, though to the claims Of her imperial pride so Swiss-like staunch— Yea self-sold, body and soul,—was not of England! He was an Irishman, with whom the name Of Ireland was a scoff! An Irishman, Who for a hireling’s ministry and meed Could spurn away from his disnatured heart (As it were somewhat all too motherly— Too pious to have any business there) The pleading memory of his Native Land!
