Wellington.

Great Captain, if you will! great Duke! great Slave! Great Minion of the Crown!—but a great Man He was not! He? the crushing battering-ram The master gun—the wide-mouthed model-mortar Of mere Authority, shrunk up with age Into a drivëlling mockery of itself, x And propt on crutches! He? the atheist Of a conventional and most earthy duty! To whom the ‘Powers that be’ were simply not Of God, but in his stead! Shall we belie All righteous instinct and profane the truth By calling great a man without a heart? Whose wire-like nerves never with pity thrilled? Whose soul grew never luminous with a thought Of mercy, or yearned with a benign desire? Nay, who was even too stolid for remorse To move with the remotest sense of blood— The blood that lay like a red deluge, round All the proud landmarks of his past career! One only mighty as a devil might be— Mighty for evil—mighty against good! Creating nothing lasting, nought beyond The commissariat meet for a campaign; And building, if at all, only redoubts! Designing only trenches, to be filled As monstrous graves with battle-broken bones Together with a slime of puddled gore! Being a man as feeling-cold as stone! Dull and inventionless—yea, dull as lead, Save in the learning of inhuman lore, And in the cunning of devices, big 624 With murderous mischief, ponderously malign! Bloodless as iron, and, in compliment, Thence clept the Iron Duke! Such a man great! One who, apart from the despotic wills Of crowned oppressors, knew no right, no wrong, No creed, no country, and no brotherhood! If he was great, I say,—may God most high Spare henceforth to our universal race All greatness; seeing it may sometimes be The rigid kindless battlement of Power Self throned and sanctioned only by the sword. And if, as Englishmen are proud to boast, He was their greatest countryman—alas! For England’s national stirelity! But they who thus belaud him—he! as all True patriots most feelingly perceive. Besides, however we account him,—great Or little,—Wellington was not her son! He was an Irishman, with whom the name Of Ireland was a scoff! An Irishman, Who for a hireling’s ministry and meed, Could tear away from his disnatured heart (As it were somewhat all too motherly— Too pious to have any business there) The pleading image of his Native Land!
