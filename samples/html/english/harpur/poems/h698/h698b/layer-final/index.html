Second

Sonnets on Poets and Poetry.

I

[A WORLD-POET]

The ever-influencing Bard is one Whose spirit is warm and genial as the soul Of Summer, when, through nature’s quickened whole, It teems in fruits that ripen to the sun;— Whose heart doth always in its fulness run Out tow’rds all beauty and all nobleness, And wheresoever there’s a rich excess In what is suffered, and in what is done. His too is faith,—heroic insight;—yea, So broadly doth the Godwardness of Man, As a tremendous revelation, ray For him the Future’s providential plan, That even beyon his own design, is he Thy trumpet, Truth! thy prophet, Liberty!
