Miscellaneous Poems.

The Emigrants Vision

As our Bark dashed away ore the night shrouded deep, And out towards the South I was gazing, First there passed ore my spirit a darkness like sleep Then the light of a Vision amazing! As rises the Moon, from the white waves afar Came a Goddess it seemed of love wisdom and war, And on her bright helmet encircling a star, Behold there was graven “Australia”.

Her robes were of green as the mantle of spring Newly spread by the streams that so mildly Elapse th[r]ough yon flock-dappled plains, or that sing Mid those blue-ranging mountaines so wildly: Her locks were as bright as the lustre that lies At morn on the seas of the South, and her eyes Were as deep in their joy as the clear sunny skies— The clear sunny skies of Australia.

O Stranger, she asked, hast thou fled from the home Which thy forefathers bled for so vainly! Does shame for its past thus induce thee to roam, Or dispair of its future constrain thee? In the far sunny South there’s a refuge from wrong, Where the Shiloh of Freedom, expected so long, Though genius already hath shouted his song: Tis the evergreen land of Australia.

There Truth her abode on the forest-clad hills Shall establish—a dweller for ever; And plenty rejoice by the gold-pebbled rills, And wedded to honest Endeavour: Till the future a numberless people shall see 97 All eaqually noble as eaqually free, And the God they adore their sole monarch shall be! Then come, build thy home in Australia.

This said, tow’rds the South she passed brightly away, And at once as from slumber I started; But the cadences sweet of its welcoming lay Yet breathed of the Vision departed. And when o’re the deep these had fadingly spread, The swell of my heart as it rose to my head Broke loud into words on my tongue—and I said Be the home of my hope then Australia!
