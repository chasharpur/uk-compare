Sapho’s Comparison

This Rose impaled with Morning’s dew, Called Fancy’s fondest praises on it; Nor might she find the garden through A lovelier minion for the sonnet.

Chained by its sweetness, Flora threw A captive brilliancy around it; 70 But fallen and forsaken too, Hath sultry Noon, in passing, found it.

So late my heart was all the power— The balmy power of Love would have it; And could emotion’s manna shower In purchase for each smile he gave it.

Won by its ardour, Poesy Ensphered it with a ray from heaven! But all how bootless, since to thee, Thou fell Despair, as soon tis given!

A ruthless blast dethroned the rose, And turned to Pity’s theme its glory: From truthless Phaon come the woes That tragic make my bosom’s story.
