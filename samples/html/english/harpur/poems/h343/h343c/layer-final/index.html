PART OF PSALM CXXXIX.

Whither—whither might I fly From thy presence, God most high? Whither—whither might I go From thy spirit’s overflow?

Up to heaven could I rise: Thou art mansioned in the skies! Did I make my bed in hell: Thou wert there—even there as well!

Fled I on the morning’s wings— Dwelt I in the deep sea-springs: Round me there would work thy will, And thy right hand hold me still!

To the dark night should I say Hide me in thy cloud alway: Vain would such a covert be— Darkness is as light to Thee!
