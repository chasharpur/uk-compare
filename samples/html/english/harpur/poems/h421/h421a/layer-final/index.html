ON SEEING HER DRESSED IN WHITE.

Dress ever thus should Rosa! yes, to me, That garb doth seem the emblem of her mind: Pure—spotless—innocently unconfined, In both chaste elegance of style I see Yet flowing as the breeze when it is free! With sweet conceptions of th’ angelic kind A virgin so arrayed, to a refined And loving soul, doth witchingly agree. Oh, that I were that graceful garb, infolding All that is covetable under heaven! Ah, vainly hath my ‘wildered spirit striven To love her measurably—for now beholding Her glowing beauty curtained thus in white, My brain is dizzy passion—thick my sight.
