[RECORDS OF A POET’S LOVE IV]

Thus always should she dress—in white. To me, Such vesture seems to symbolise her mind. Pure—spotless—innocently unconfined, In both what chastity of style can be Yet flowing as the breeze when it is free! And with sweet notions of the angelic kind, A Virgin so arrayed, to a refined And loving eye, doth witchingly agree. O that I were that graceful garb, enfolding All I most covet under God’s dear heaven!— How vainly in my cunning have I striven But simply to admire her; for, beholding Her form thus garmented, my will at length Is drowned in the wild waves of Love’s arousing strength!
