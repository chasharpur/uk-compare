A Lady in White.

Dressed all in snowy white! I saw her so, And thought her garb the emblem of her mind. “Pure—spotless—innocently unconfined— What chastity of style (I said) may be Yet flowing as the breeze when it is free. And with sweet fancies of the angellic kind, A Virgin so arrayed, and so inclined In spirit, doth most witchingly agree! Oh! that I were that graceful garb, infolding All I most covet in this world so wide! How vainly in my cunning have I tried But to admire her?—since, alas! beholding Her form thus garmented, my will at length Is drowned in the wild waves of Love’s arousing strength!
