X.

THE MISTAKE.

A poet once, who heard a voice he thought he knew, Thus spake its owner—Ha! Old Boy! and is this you? The creature thus addressed, being “bearded like the pard,” Upon the man of Rhyme turned fiercely. and stared hard; And—damn it, you’ve mistaken! then exclaimed most loudly, Throwing his nose the while into the air full proudly! I have, the Bard replied, as he, the fop did scan; Egregiously indeed;—an Ass, sir, for a Man!
