The Same very Literally.

And they loosed from the yoke the sweating steeds, and fastened them with halters, every one to his own chariot. Quickly brought they from the City oxen and fat sheep: and and they brought sweet wine and bread from their homes, and also gathered together much fuel. But the winds raised the savour [of their supper] from the plain to heaven

But they, exceedingly elated, sat all night long in ranks of war, and many fires blazed for them. As when in heaven the stars appear very obvious about the clear moon, when the air is used to be without a breeze, and all pointed rocks, and lofty hill tops, and groves appear; but the vast æther is opened in heaven, and all the stars are seen, and the shepherd rejoices in his soul.

Thus did many fires by the Trojans kindled, appear before Ilium, between the ships and the streams of Xanthus. A thousand fires blazed on the plain, and at the light of each blazing fire sat fifty men. But their steeds, eating white barley and oats, were standing by the chariots, awaiting the beautiful-throned Aurora.

Euroma, 1868.
