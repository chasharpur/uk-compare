[A LYRICAL LOVE STORY 3:3]

Thus let me solitary range, To muse o’er Visions I have nurst, Albeit, at length, o’er feeling’s change, I sigh as though my heart would burst!

The flowery earth is summer-bright, Glad birds are warbling in the trees;— Why hath my soul no kindred light? My heart no answering melodies?

And now;—the folding star is here, Bright witness of my happiest day; ‘Tis hanging in its pristine sphere, And burning with its olden ray:

But where are now the beauteous eyes That ‘neath it held me then in thrall? The hope that hailed its evening rise— The love that blest its nightly fall?

All left me! and in memory 161 Alone to leave a trace behind; More than spent billows of the sea— Than fragments of a desert wind.

And yet I would not be again The fond one of those happier years; Since Love could sting, and Hope could pain, And Rapture was the sire of tears.

Rather that earlier Being I’d trace, Through memory unincarnate shown; Unweighed upon by time and place, And living unto thoughts alone!

Then let me solitary range, To muse o’er Visions I have nurst, Albeit, at length, o’er feeling’s change, I sigh as though my heart would burst.
