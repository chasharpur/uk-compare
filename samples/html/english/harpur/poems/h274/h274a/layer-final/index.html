Original Poetry.

STANZAS.

Thus let me solitary range, To muse on visions I have nurs’d! Tho’ I, at length, o’er feeling’s change, Shall sigh, as tho’ my heart would burst.

‘Tis eve—and Heaven with glory glows; And birds are warbling in the trees!— But where’s my fancy’s kindred hues?— My bosom’s answering melodies?—

‘Tis evening’s star—love’s beacon fair! Bright witness of my happiest day,— ‘Tis hanging in its usual sphere;— ‘Tis burning with its olden ray:—

But where are now the sympathies, That, ‘neath it, held me then in thrall? The hope that hail’d its evening-rise— The love that blessed its midnight-fall?

Evanished!—and in memory Alone, have left a trace behind, More than the billows of the sea— Than fragments of the desert wind!

I look but in my heart to know, All I would find there, past or dead! ‘Tis paler than my mother’s brow— ‘Tis greyer than my father’s head!

Yet still I would not be again The gay one of my happier years, Since Love could sting, and Hope could pain, And Rapture was the Sire of tears!

Rather that earlier being I’d trace Thro’ mem’ry unincarnate given; Unweighed upon by time and place, And breathing of a nearer Heaven.

Then let me solitary range To muse o’er visions I have nurs’d, Tho’ I, at length, o’er feelings change Shall sigh as tho’ my heart would burst.

STEBII.
