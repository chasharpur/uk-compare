Nature’s Apparent Want of Benevolence.

The man by indigestion smitten, Drinks poison even in his gruel; The man constrained to drown a kitten Must feel that Nature’s sometimes cruel. Yet, in both cases, could we see Her final purpose, we should find (Of this I rest assured) that she Is cruel only to be kind.

Unforced, even through his food, to try Sheer abstinence, the man might die, Or deeper into misery fall; The kitten might be spared—for what? To find in some too cat thronged spot 397 A cuffed, scratched, miserable lot, And famish after all.

Besides, Health’s very means and plan Would ever be abused by man, But for the punishment combined With all abuse, and thence designed; Nor could those creatures ever be Kept extant, which are (as we see) Incapable of polity, But through a superflux of kind.
