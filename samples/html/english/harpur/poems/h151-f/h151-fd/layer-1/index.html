Chorus of the Hours.

Ah, that a happy childhood’s happiest days The more enjoyed should be the deadlier food, Therefore, for bitter-voiced Regret In after Misery’s mood; That all the more their innscent displays May bloom in Memory’s Eden yet, The harder still should seem the present’s barren ways!

And why, with soul so full of faith and free, Should young Humanity thus straitened be? Although pursuing its appropriate bliss, It can but live to rue the road That first in hope it trode! Ah, in a world so excellent as this, Why should a loving heart become life’s heaviest load!

To still these awful questionings of Pain, The statists of the earth have striven in vain Since outcast Love first wailed beneath the skies, And men to their divinest interest blind Grew careless of their kind; Although a voice in every soul replies— A voice that learnëd logic never deigns to heed How movingly soever it may plead, Renewing all the foregone wisdom of the wise Who were the wise indeed; While it declares that absolute Brotherhood And that equality it would devise, Is the one only social creed Whence simple, saving, universal good And life-long pleasantness can flow indeed.

And ever until Man To such large charity shall adjust his plan, His soul shall be a wanderer from the earth, Unsettled as a cloud, that from its birth Keeps wandering whither the every wild wind wills, Or as a hart amid the desert’s dearth Keeps panting on, in search of visionary rills.
