CHORUS.

Ah, that Childhood’s happiest days The more enjoyed should be the deadlier food Therefore for bitter-voiced Regret In Misery’s after mood; That all the more their flowery maize May bloom in memory yet, 38 The harder still should seem the present’s barren ways!

And why, with soul so free, Should young Humanity so straightened be? Although pursuing its appropriate bliss, It can but live to the rue the road! Ah! in a world so excellent as this, Why should a feeling heart become its heaviest load?

To still these questionings of Pain The Sages of the Earth have striven in vain, Love first wailed beneath the skies, And men, to their true interest blind, Grew careless of their kind; Although a voice in every soul replies— A voice their logic never deigns to heed, How movingly soever it may plead, Renewing all the foregone wisdom of the wise, 39 While it declares that absolute Brotherhood And the equality this would devise, Is the one only social creed Whence universal human good And life-long pleasantness can flow indeed.

And ever until truth-neglecting Man To such large charity shall conform his plan Of life, that now all brotherhood kills, His hope shall be a wanderer from the Earth; Unsettled as a cloud, that from its birth Keeps roaming whither the wind wills; Or as a hart amid the desert’s dearth, Still panteth on in search of hoped-for rills Down flowing from imagined hills.
