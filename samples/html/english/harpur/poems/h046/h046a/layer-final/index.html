Bishop Berkeley.

I love him mightily, and fain would know Whether he’d have me, or not have me, go; Yet read, to find him too recondite, since His logic puzzles so, it don’t convince. So wide his arguments, that we suspect them Of abberations, though we can’t detect them, Or else it dives so deep into the main Of Reason, that it ne’er comes up again. Splendid as meteors on their flaming way, They light us heavenward—till we feel astray; Or else so deep they dive into the main Of Reason, that they ne’er come up again.
