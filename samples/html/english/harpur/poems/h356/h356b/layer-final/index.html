XXII. X

The Easter Illumination of St. Peter’s at Rome.

Four thousand lamps of gold and silver light Suspended round the mighty dome, and o’er Those rows of statues at their awful height, And thence down to the ground,—together pour A blaze that might seem rift from Etna’s core, So fiercely brings it out of the black night The whole vast structure on the astonished sight— Towers, columns, windows, with their carven lore! 256 The spectacle is grand!—But can it call Deliverance unto those who pine and bleed Under the Austrian’s vandalic thrall? Alas! one thought of fire—one burning deed Struck from thy Garibaldi’s patriotic creed, O prostrate Italy! transcends it all!
