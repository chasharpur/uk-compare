XIII.

Anticipated Separation.

Dark days are coming, Rosa,—dark and drear! Even such, that sometimes I am quick to deem A fatal warp is woven with the dream That made and makes thee to my soul so dear. Yet there’s a sorrowful sweetness in the fear That haunts me thus; for in its gloom the gleam Of bygone transport dwelleth, to redeem From utter misery Passion’s secret tear. O why,—when in the world’s wide wilderness Two meet to love, to be unto each other All they have dreamt of having power to bless;— A dearer Sister and a fonder Brother! Ah, why should Fortune then—Love’s foe forever! Come in between and bid those beings sever?
