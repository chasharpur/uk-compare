5d

III.

To ——————.

I’ve walked alone in crowds when meet my heart Longed for the witness of a friendly face— Even as some dungeoned wretch in his disgrace Pines for the sunshine: I have stood apart, An unknown wanderer in the busy mart And well-thronged temple of my natal place: I’ve travelled with compelled and weary pace, Companionless for long, where nought did start The echoes of the solitary mountains Save the macaw’s wild cry—till for relief I’ve sought of mine own face in desert fountains A shadowy fellowship:—and yet the grief Of all past loneliness, how light, to what Now irks my spirit where thine cometh not.
