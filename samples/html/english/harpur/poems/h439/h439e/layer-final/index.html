XXIII

To the same.

I’ve walked alone in crowds when most my heart Longed for the witness of some friendly face, Even as a dungeoned wretch in his disgrace Longs for the sunshine: I have stood apart, An unknown wanderer, in the busy mart And well-thronged temple of my natal place: I’ve journeyed with compelled and weary pace, Companionless for long, where nought did start The echoes of the solitary mountains Save the macaw’s wild cry—till, for relief, I’ve sought of mine own face in desert fountains A shadowy fellowship:—and yet the grief Of all past loneliness how light to what Doth irk my spirit now, where thine comes not!
