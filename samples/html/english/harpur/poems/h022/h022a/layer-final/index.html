III.

AN EPITAPH.

Be his rest who sleeps below, Done to death by toil and woe, Sound and sweet. So much in fortune did he lack, So little meet Of kindness, as with bleeding feet He journeyed Life’s most barren track;— In short, so crude, or hard, or flat Was his whole worldly being, that None but a foe could wish him back.

But he sleeps well, where plants in bloom That sweetened not his living home, Prank the raised sod; Tokening perhaps that he who here Missed Fortune’s smile, hath met elsewhere The smile of God.
