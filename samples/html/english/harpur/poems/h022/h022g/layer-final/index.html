[AUTUMNAL LEAVES 3]

Be his rest who sleeps below, Done to death by toil and woe, Sound and sweet. So much in fortune did he lack, So little meet Of kindness, as with bleeding feet He journeyed life’s most barren track, That only hate in its deceit, Not love, not pity, would entreat To have him back. 168 But he sleeps well where many a bloom That might not grace his living home Pranks the raised sod: Tokening, perhaps, that one who here Missed the world’s smile, hath met elsewhere The smile of God.
