ORIGINAL POETRY.

IMAGINATION.

(By an Australian.)

Spirit of Dreams!—when many a wander’d height Shut Paradise from banish’d Adam’s sight, Two wedded Powers were given thenceforth to stray, On either hand, companions of his way: This, Hope, was named in heaven, whence he came, And that, of Melancholy bore the name: Thy parents, these—who wing’d thee with a ray Snatch’d from Perfection, as she pass’d away; And clothed thee with a mem’ry of the grace And splendor that suffused, ere fallen, Nature’s face!

Creator of Elysiums! and of dear Hesperian Gardens, thy fond suppliant hear! Send me thy hues!—which of all colors fair, And grand, on Earth, in Ocean, and in Air— From nameless flow’rs that wild in forests run, To the effu[l]gence of the setting sun— From the pa[l]e dew drops which impearl the sward, To rainbows vast, and Boreal meteo[r]s broad— Fr[o]m the white radiance that o’er fountains sleeps, To the phos-phoric lightning of the deeps— From yellow sands that glitter on th[e] shore, To blazing diamonds, and the flaming ore Thou dost compound!—thy hues now lend; for thy Bright footsteps I would tra[c]e—describe thy mini[s]try

Oh, for thy Milton’s voice[,] whilst first I sing How thou dost beautify the loveliest thing. Th[r]ice exquisite the Fair!—no mo[r]e; till thou Turn’st on them thy regard’s seraphic glow; At once, their beings sweeter sweets invest, Than breathe in gales of Araby the Bl[e]st; A thousand bright particulars are giv’n And they outshine the very stars of heav’n. Glorious this light!—yet shall thy secret ray Effulge an added bril[l]iance o’er the day. Lovely yon scene!—yet shall thy presence there, Burnish each glory of the golden year: Breathe but thine influence o’er the common plain[,] And Nature flushes round with Eden’s glow again?

Oh, he whose heart r[e]sponds to thy wild str[a]ins I[s] even rich where want [ ]n[ ]y, Exile, reigns: St[i]ll his possessions compass ev’ry grove, He reaps his treasur[e]s fr[o]m the mines of Love! For him, what wealth is in the morning’s hues! What diamonds flame in the pellucid dews! What costly mansions are the bro[o]k-side bow’rs! What gay assemblies are the nodding flow’rs! The thousand birds that pipe a matin song, Are then, to him, a tributary throng! Yea, then, a vassal to his sov’reign eye, Is ev’ry grace of Earth—each splendor of the sky!

And, when the sordid hums of Mammon’s bro[o]d Fail, like the voices of an ebbing flood: And, from the scene he m[ar]r’d with stern [a]rray The dragon, Pomp, hath slunk fatigued away[;] When the long beams of [d]ay no m[o]re invest The hills far east, but gather in the west Around their Reg[e]nt, like a summon’d host, Then slowly roll from the horison’s coast; A sumptuous banquet is that close of day To fill his vocal soul with harmony:— O’erflow’d is every thought and ev’ry sense With an august delight in God’s munificence.

With stilly step, when from the eastern height, Walks, like a spangled Ethiop Qu[e]en, the Night, And from her sable banner, wide unfurl’d, Shakes dreams and opiates on the drowsy world; No sordid appeti[t]e, no passion mean, Confines thy vot’ry to th[i]s mere te[r]rene! Thee freely following, he soars afar, From moon to planet, and from star to star! Thence onward still, if vent’rous, he may trace Thy sounding flight through the dim depths of space! Till, the apparent bounds o[f] order cross’d, He feel his being all in vasty wonder lost!

Now, in sad mood, if summer be the theme, The hopeless lover, or the faded dream, Whose halcyons gemm’d li[f]e’s limpid stream of yore, But fled, grim winter’s turbid floods before, Never to show their shining plumage more! Still thou, attendant, giv’st thy follower joy, Mak’st deepest anguish, highest luxury: For his comp[l]a[i]nt, unlike the worldling’s care, Is sadly pleasing, as a plaintive air Heard o’er a moonlit river, when half flown Hath night, and all the eyes of Heav’n are looking down.

And now, doth Danger threat, and Terror scream, O’er their wild fronts a reconciling gleam Thy wings reflect: The storm that rends the sky, And ploughs the deep, enchants if thou art nigh! An eloquence in madness thou canst find, That ravishes while it appals the mind! Cans’t strike from lone Remorse, and Horror fell, Tones which, to hear, make vengeance placable; Throw a fierce loveliness o’er Ruin’s face; And o’[e]r the cheek of Death an enviable grace: Nay; be thy glance but turn’d where devils dwell, And a sad glory lightens over Hell!

Spouse of the Bard! to sing I next assay, How thou hast brighten’d my peculiar way. When troubles most beset me, stern and new, When foes most triumph’d, friends were most untru[e], How much of sweetness, still, ’twas mine to know, With thee, unheeded, wand’ring to and fro: Then, as I breasted full the sounding gale, On the lone hill, or seldom trodden vale, Embower’d with whispering shades, that solemn spread Round some f[r]esh flowing fountain’s gleaming head, Thou medicined my soul’s distractions rude, With dreams of Beauty, Majesty, and Good!

Now, trav’lling lone, but poorly shod, and bow’d With weariness, on the rock-roughen’d road, I, meeting thee upon that desert way, Have found a palace in the cavern grey; And, rapt in high chivalric vision, there Have bearded recreant knights, and su[c]cour’d ladies fair.

Anon, slow-wending over fertile plains Enclosed, some worldling’s ill-deserved domains, I, warming ‘neath a vagrant ray of thine, Have felt, though his the soil, its vernal smile was mine!

Or serious more—on some cloud-turban’d h[e]ight, Which gave, at once, a region to the sight, And whose grey altar-stone, a pillar’d crowd, Of pines enroof’d—Religion’s best abode! Thou gav’st my soul a prophet pow’r to s[e]ize High marvels, and consoling mysteries.

Oh, Spirit! thus attend me to the last! For though thou turnest not, thou warm’st the blast Of bitter scorn, upon the lowly cast; Art to the burthen’d soul an eagle’s wing, And to the wint’ry fate the flow’rs of spring: Lap me in visions! and may naught destroy Thy spell—for nothing real deserves the name of joy!
