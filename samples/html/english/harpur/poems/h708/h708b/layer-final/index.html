YON GREEN WOOD.

BY CHARLES HARPUR.

I see, as in a vision, how face to face I stood Long years ago with Dora, in Yon Green Wood: Long years ago with Dora, the gentle and the good,— The Angel of my destiny,—in Yon Green Wood.

I said not that I loved her, but well she understood My bosom’s beating secret, in Yon Green Wood: I dared not say I loved her, although I wished I could Have told her so a thousand times in Yon Green Wood.

The budding joys of fancy were like the virginhood Of morning blooms ungathered (in Yon Green Wood:) Of morning blooms ungathered, while face to face we stood, ‘Mid Hope’s untrampled Eden both, in Yon Green Wood.

-

It saw Affection’s dawning, and Love’s first purple mood, And then two souls grow one soul, did Yon Green Wood. Alas! my heart is yearning—nor wonder that it should, Since what hath been can be no more—in Yon Green Wood!
