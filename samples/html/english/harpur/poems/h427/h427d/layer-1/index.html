A Trilogy.

I

Love Untold.

A vast and shadowy hope breaks up my rest Unspoken;—nor does even my pen dare write How my pent spirit pineth day and night For one fair Being with whom I might be blest! And ever with vague jealousies possessed, The more I languish, feeling these may so Oppress Affection, that for very woe She longs at last to die, deep buried in my breast! O for a brimmer of the wine of Love, Or a deep draught of the Lethèan wave! The power a mutual passion to emove, Or that repose which sealeth up the grave! Yet these my bonds are blameless: one more wise Had dreamt away his freedom, dreaming of her eyes.
