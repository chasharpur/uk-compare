A Trilogy

I. Love Untold

A vast and shadowy hope breaks up my rest, Unspoken;—nor dares even my pen to write How my pent spirit pineth, day and night, For one fair Being with whom I might be blest! And ever with vague jealousies possessed, The more I languish, feeling these may so Oppress Affection, that for very wo It longs at last to die, deep buried in my breast! 8 O for a brimmer of the wine of Love, Or a deep draught of the Lethèan wave! The power a mutual passion to emove, Or that repose which sealeth up the grave! Yet these my bonds are blameless: one more wise Had dreamt away his freedom, dreaming of her eyes.
