Coleridge.

His Genevieve

Hark! in the shadowy cool of golden eves, Stretched on Spring’s sward, or Autumn’s yellow leaves, He sings in strains rich as the purple light Whose radiance melts into the coming night, 350 That Legend of the Knight, where reason’s ray The Lady of the Land had witched away— That subtle passion-liberating Lay, 353c Which hangs aloft, all orphic odes above,— A peerless Evening Star in the deep heaven of Love.
