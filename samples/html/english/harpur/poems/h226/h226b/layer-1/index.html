A Look o’er the Sea.

I stood upon a Cliff, from which the Sea Spread boundless forth, like Faith’s eternity. The sun was sunken inland, yet h did seem To touch its sky-rim with a freakful gleam. The moon was faintly in the east descried, And yet more faintly mirrored in the wide Expanse of waters,—brokenly, as these 387 Ridged crimpling under a slow coming breeze, That, with a wasting sigh, along the shore Died as the waves pulsed with a gentle roar: While in the offin, through the twilight grey, A Ship went dreaming on her outward way— Dreaming of what might there awaiting keep Out in the dark fast settling on the deep, Or more remote, in fatefull embryo lie ‘Twixt the wild watry waste and incommunicative sky.
