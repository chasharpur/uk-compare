A POET BOY’S LOVE WISHES.

BY CHARLES HARPUR.

“Ah, that yon Moon, when thus silvery beam ing, Might come through the spells of my passionate dreaming. In the guise of a lady fair, blessing and blest, And pillow my head on her beautiful breast!

“Ah, that yon Star which in heaven is whitest, Might come when Love’s visions around me are brightest,— Come like a fancy-pale Girl from her rest, And rap me away to its home in the West!

“Ah, that the Spirit of Music by flowing When Love, the pure test of all beauty, is glow- ing, Might take such a shape as my heart can con- ceive And breathe in my arms like a daughter of Eve!

“Ah, that some snowy wing’d Angel of heaven But once to the sight of my spirit were given, So rich in the beauty born with her on high, That to love her were death—I would love her and die!”
