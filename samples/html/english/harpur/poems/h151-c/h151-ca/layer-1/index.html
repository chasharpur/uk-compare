Chorus.

Is there no dawning of a brighter morrow? O Nature, as a guilty mother starts, Even thou shouldst shake with a maternal horror, Beholding ever in thy bosom, hearts Blood-ripe for joy, yet knowing only sorrow!

And Love, how dark thy page! In the cold arms of gold-possessing Age, Or worse embrace of purple-robed Disease,— Under costliest canopies, On beds of loveless, and thence joyless state, Lo, loathing Beauty lies! Entrapt by interests beastial to the wise, 30 And all by Nature moulded good or great!

For Life’s mere crusts how many dedicate Their glowing beings to whom they scorn or hate? And for each feminine flower thus blighted A manly heart yearns undelighted, Is warm in vain, and fond and brave For Love, of which ’tis but a living grave, Lacking its natural mate!

And in the Future’s vision-built pavilions Innumerable millions The icy burthen of like woes await! Wait to be only in their generations, Through the perverted geniture of Nations, Unprofitted vessels of love-quickened natures! Yea, but there also shining in pure state, Time-ripened Wisdom shows her heaven -illumined features! With mercy and justice, wing’d, she hastes along 31 To chase to Hell fanatic Mystery’s creatures, Bad Prudence, Policy, and wealth-arm’d Wrong!

Semi Chorus to be added to the Chorus of the first Monologue.

Ah! that Death Should ever, like a drear untimely night Descend upon the loved in Love’s despite! Ah! that a little breath Expiring from the world, should leave each scene Where its warm influence before hath been, So empty to the heart in its despair, So painful to bereavement everywhere! But loving souls, Though sundered thus by Death, must tend again To union, since they die not, and no main Between them wide as the Eternal rolls. 151 This is implied by suffering. What is pain But prophesy of pleasure? What is grief But preparation for some meet relief?
