SONG.

O this wearing influence of Time— This spirit of decay, That brings to me so little, And takes so much away! O this failure of delight—since Sweet Fancy died of care: For even Spring flowers no longer look So fresh as once they were.

O

this feeling of the nothingness Of all that men pursue— This sense of the unwisdom Of what the wisest do! O this winter of the heart—when Its chords are all unstrung, And the very birds seem not to sing As long ago they sung!

Eu

roma, Sept., 1866.
