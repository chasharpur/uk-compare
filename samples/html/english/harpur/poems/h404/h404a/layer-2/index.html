Paraphrase of the I Psalm

That man is blessëd who alway From evil’s path refrains his feet; Who bends not to the sinner’s sway, Nor sitteth in the scorner’s seat; But with God’s law both day and night, His soul enriches as with light.

He shall be like a pleasant tree That by a shining river grows, And in its season blossoms free Perfuming every wind that blows; Or teems with golden fruitage, seen Through Englobing boughs of perfect green.

11

And whatsoever he shall do Shall prosper in the sight of God; Unlike the works of that bad few Who smite with the oppressor’s rod: On these the winds of wrath shall prey And sweep them yet like chaff away.

For never the ungodly yet Might bear the judgment of the Lord; Nor stand unblemished where are met The righteous both in deed and word: The sons of Truth alone are wise— The faithless perish in their lies.
