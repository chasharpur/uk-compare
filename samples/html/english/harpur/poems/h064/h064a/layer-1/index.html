Epitaph for an Able Dealer.

Here into dust is stealing One who throve by able dealing. He went to Church each Sunday, To Mart as duly every Monday. He thought the poor man still a sinner, And never stooped to share his dinner With any who might then not come From just as good a one at home. And thus he lived till he was hoary; Then died—and this is all his story. And whereso next ’twas his to go, Sir, There’s not a merer Jew there: But granting ’twas to Heaven, the poser, Is,—what he finds to do there.

It should not be inferred from the strain of this Epitaph, that I am disposed to decry an indefatigable attention to business, and all that. To a rational extent, this is quite proper and commendable. But I would be thought to favor the belief, that he who has no thought beyond the material substances of this world, and who shows by the trickery of his monday bargain that his sunday devotions were a a mere f customary formula, can certainly be qualifying himself himself—that is, the immortal part of himself, for no place in Heaven.

C.H.
