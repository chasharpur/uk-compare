44

The Carouse

Come Alan, I will flood the bowl, And thou shalt sing that song of mine, Which dates the storm that wrecked my whole And gave me to the strength of wine! Yea, often may I lift it up, To quench the burning sting of wrong, Ere I can drown in the deep cup The thought of her I loved so long.

45

As comes the Moon, when rising bright, To one who treads a foreign shore, She filled my life path as with light Of something I had loved before: The record of some former tie Seemed spirit written in my brain; Some mystic influence, old and high, Seemed cycling into act again.

I loved the glorious Night and Day, With every form of joy they hold— But most the Night, in her array Thus spangled with celestial gold: I loved all mediums too through which The soul of genius breathed—but she Of these,—of all, became one rich, One final ideality!

And was I loved?—Pour high the wine! Behold how to her tryste she trips! Again I feel the fire on mine That summer’d from her glowing lips! But there was treachery in her vow, And future blight in every kiss, With all the infamy that now Is in the conciousness of this!

And yet I greive—rememb’ring still How bravely sovereign in my soul That first love grew,—for good and ill Or to impel me or control! Alas! ’twas but an Eden bee That went the heavenward way it came, So soon as wrong and treachery Had touched it with consuming flame.

46

And daily then the morning flowers That flush the fields with joy’s excess, And all that makes the evening hours Instinct with thoughtful happiness; The gleaming waters of the earth— The glowing wonders of the skies— Grew massed into that mighty dearth Which wearies now my lonely eyes.

And since all sounds within the breeze Of bird or brooklet, near or far, With that dream-music mixed with these That filters from our mother star, Seem, while Regret in every place Stands listening with me when alone, As they had lost all native grace, And taken up a foreign tone.

Pour high the wine! I know too well Thy soul is deadly dark as mine, And hath a tale of wrong to tell That might excuse excess in wine. But met we not, my Friend, to quaff In Agony’s defiance? Yea, Untill our bitter hearts should laugh All memory of the past away!

But hark! that wail by Passion heard When wrecked on Life’s wild sounding shore, Which nothing to the heart it stirred But heart-delerium brings once more— Goes that tonight not dirging by A haggard mystery to thine? And what is’t else, even now, that I Would plunge from in the whelming wine?

47

No matter! Greet it with a smile! The wounded eagle highest flies! And be it ours to reach awhile That madness wherein misery dies! Yet when I think of times when we Were gladsome as the morning light, I weep to know that hearts so free Thus desolate were to be to night.

Fill high again the potent bowl! That thought hath stung me like a snake! And vengeance cloudeth from my soul Like mist from some Avernian lake! A shapeless Gloom—but lightning tongued With fierce regrets that now should be To one by Love so vilely wronged The burned out things of Memory!

But Alan, thou forgettest. Sing That Welcome to an Early Grave: For always it is wont to bring A lull o’er Misery’s tempest wave. And while its measures bandage up Love’s broken heart,—to crown the lay, I’ll pledge a last full-reaming cup To her who bruised my peace away!
