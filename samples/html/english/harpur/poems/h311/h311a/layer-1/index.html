XI. X

My Faith in Poetry.

O for a golden pen to write of thee, Poetry! Thou who to my soul hast been A Spirit solemn as eternity, Yet beautiful, delectable, serene,— And lovely in the mystery of thy mien, And full of faith and hope;—and making me, Even as thy strictest liege, but the more free In all things that are worthiest—thou my Queen! Yea, my soul’s Queen and wheresoever time May lead me, whether into dens and sties Where Want and Misery hide, or where upclimb To fame the attractors of all noble eyes, 248 Alike o’er all impediments shall I rise, Uplifted by thy ministry sublime!
