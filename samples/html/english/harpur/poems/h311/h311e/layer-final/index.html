VI

Spiritual Assurance at its height

O for a golden pen to write of thee, Poetry! thou who to my soul hast been A Presence solemn as eternity; Yet beautiful as the Moon in heaven’s serene, And, like her, lovelier for thy mystic mien And Godward farness;—and thence making me, Even as thy strictest liege, but the more free In all things that are highest—thou my Queen! Nor matters it where the fatal tides of Time May drift me,—whether under stormy skies Where Toil and Want contend, or where upclimb The attractors of all worth-discerning eyes,— Rayed with thy glories, I alike shall rise Burning aloft—one of thy Stars sublime!
