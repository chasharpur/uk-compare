THE MAN GOD.

A Man of sorrows and with grief acquainted, He bowed his beauteous head to the rude hands Of Pilate’s hireling bands; And while beneath their cruel scourge he fainted, Yet loved them—he, the heaven-descended Dove! Even with a Brother’s love.

And when upon the infamous Cross they nailed him, With Hatred’s mockery, and Scorn’s bitter smile, Even then he cried, the while Nature’s extremest agony assailed him,— Father, thy mercy even for These renew! They know not what they do.

And why, thus scorned and shamed, did he then trample Such natural indignation down, as now Pains, while we read, our brow? That Brotherly Love, perfected by example, Might crucify that enmity in Men Which crucified it then.
