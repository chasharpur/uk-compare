Exce Homo.

A Man of Sorrows and with grief acquainted, He bowed his beauteous head to the rude hands Of Pilate’s hireling bands; And while beneath their cruel scourge he fainted, Forgave them,—yearning through his shameful smart Even with a Brother’s heart.

And when upon the infamous Cross they nailed him, With mocking hatred and scorn’s bitter smile, Hark how he prayed, ev’n while Nature’s extremest agony assailed him: ‘Father, thy mercy even tow’rds these renew; They know not what they do.’

And why, thus wronged, did he in spirit trample Such natural indignation down, as now Pains, while we read, our brow? That Love, sublimed by his divine example, Might reach and so convert that hate in men Which crucified it then.

For the Great Precept of his Christianity Was always—’Live in charity; yea, live To love and to forgive; That so my spirit may through all humanity Pass ever downward with a widening birth, Till Peace possess the earth.’
