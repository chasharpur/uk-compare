Love in Vision

Three Visions of Love I have wrought into numbers That lit with thy beauty shall evermore be, While they show that no dream could be born of my slumbers And reign for a moment unblended with thee.

Methought as I took in the Garden my pleasure, Broad roses of gold flourished forth on yon tree, But when I had hastened and seized of the treasure— —Behold they were splendind madallions of thee!

ext methout as alone neath the moon I was roving, Though gossamer clouds she came stooping to me, And her orb, as she neared, looking more lovely and loving —When lo—twas a silver-set picture of thee!

en it seemed the whole heaven was opened above me To music that flowed, wave on wave like a sea; Disclosing the Angels in thousands—all lovely— All glory winged sisters—Gods pictures of thee!
