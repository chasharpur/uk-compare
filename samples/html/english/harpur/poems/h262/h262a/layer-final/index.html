LOVE IN VISION.

Three Visions, sweet Fan, I have wrought into numbers That sphered with thy beauty shall evermore be, While they show that no dream could be born of my slumbers And reign for a moment, divided from thee.

Methought, as I took in this garden my pleasure, Broad roses of gold flourished forth on yon tree, But when I had hastened and seized of the treasure— Behold they were splendid medallions of thee!

Next me hought, as alone ‘neath the moon I was roving, Robed in gossamer clouds she came stooping to me; And her orb, as she neared, looked more lovely and loving— When lo, ’twas a silver-set picture of thee!

Then it seemed the wide heaven was opened above me To music that flowed, wave on wave, like a sea, Disclosing the Angels in thousands,—all lovely— All glory-wing’d sisters—God’s pictures of thee!
