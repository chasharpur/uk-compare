God’s Man.

By Charles Harpur.

Who before the Lord shall stand? Who shall sit at God’s right hand?

He whose path of life is straight, Because to him the truth’s estate Is greater than a crownèd king’s: He who hates all whisperings Against his neighbor, speaking aye Only what his heart would say, And that in language loud, and clear Of all disguise, above all fear.

Singularly enough, there was no one subject on which the ancient Jews were more entirely in the dark, than on this of Usury. Wholly misunderstanding its nature, as a matter of commercial economy, or as it relates to capital, they wholly condemned it, as a cruel and grasping exaction. Nevertheless, their instincts, from the first, were more than a matchfor their ethics. Sub umbra, and from a born push in that particular direction, they had tried their ”prentice hand” upon it very extensively even in the days of David—the supposed author of the Psalm of which the above is a paraphrase.
