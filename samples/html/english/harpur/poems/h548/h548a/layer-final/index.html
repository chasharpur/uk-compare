SONNETS DEDICATED TO AUSTRALIAN SENATORS.

NO. XV.

“The devil is in that moon for mischief.” BYRON.

[SONNETS DEDICATED TO AUSTRALIAN SENATORS XIII]

The spirit of the moon, as to and fro She paced upon a mountain in her planet, (Which, though it seems from hence as you could span it, Is there no pimple,) looked at length below, And listening, to her mooncalves said—”Even so: I felt connaturally, that yon din meant Some Lunar triumph! Botany Bay s St. Vincent Is saddled with our earthly Euphuist, Lowe!” Wherefore, ye Statesmen of the Squatting Stations Be joyful! but, as doubtless he will soon shine’ I warn ye, on those luminous occasions, To take your dinners with ye, if you’d soon dine!— Now shall your cause be mantled in orations Brocaded with the very best of Moonshine!

NO “MINION OF THE MOON.”
