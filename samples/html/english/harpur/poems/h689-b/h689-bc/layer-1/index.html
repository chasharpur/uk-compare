Part II.

“Why am I here, in this my last resort, Purturbed with incense and anointings? Why Compelled to listen to the sound of prayers That smite me through as with the fire of God! O pain, pain, pain! What if this chamber be So full of us—for Legion is our name— Yet look around! Is it not also full Of our immitigable punishers? Full of avenging Angels, holding each A scourge of thunder in his potent hand, Ready to lighten forth! and who, thus armed, For ever chase and wound us as we fly! Nor end with this—but in each wound they make Pour venom sweltered from that tree Asgard, Whose deadly shadow in its blackness falls Over the lake of everlasting doom!

“Five hundred years ago, I who thus speak, Was an Egyptian living at the Court Of Ptolemy Philadelphus. To the top Of mountainous power, though roughened with unrest And girt with dangers as with thunder clouds, 189 Had I resolved, by all resorts, to climb, By truth and falshood, right and wrong alike: And I succeeded. Thus high built in power, Second alone to my imperial Lord’s, I crowned with its impunity my lust Of Beauty,—sowing, broadcast as it were, Such sensual baits wide round me, as should lure Through pleasure, or through interest entrap The fairest daughters of the Land—and lo, Their lustrous eyes surcharged with passionate light The chambers of my Harem! But at length Wearying of these, though sweet,—I set my heart On riches, heaped to such a fabulous sum As never one man’s hoard, before or then, Might match; and to acquire them, steeped my life In every public, every private wrong, In lies, frauds, secret murders: till at last A favored minion I had trusted most, And highest raised, unveiled before the King The so abysmal badness of my life But dearly he abode it; nor till now Guessed I how deadly grateful was revenge! I stole into his chamber as he slept, And with a sword whose double edge for hours I had wetted, to well fit it for the deed, There staked him through the midriff to his bed, Where he lay writhing till the morning came And showed the murder: meanwhile I had fled. But first I sent, as oft I had used before, A present to the mistress of the man Who had in secret prompted my betrayer, Of scented wine, and those Damascus cakes So prized by luxury. Ha, ha! On these Both feasted, and both fell together down, 190 Rolling and panting in their dying pangs, Like poisoned desert dogs!

The wild-shrieking winds And roaring waters held in vengeful chase Me only!—me, whose signal crimes alone Had brought on all this anger of the Gods!’ And thus reproaching me, with glaring eyes, They would have siezed and slain me: but I sprang Back from amongst them, and outstriking stabbed With sudden blow their leader to the heart; Then with my poinard scaring off the rest, I leaped the deck, and swimming, reached the shore: From which, in savage triumph, I beheld The battered ship, with all her howling crew, Heel, and go down amid the whelming waves. “I“I

should I not? I answered, quivering with a stormy haste— A rampant unreluctance! For so great Was still my fury against all mankind, And my desire of pomp and riches yet So monstrous, that I felt I could have drunk Blood, fire, or worse, to wear again the power That Fortune, working through my enemies’ hands, Had stript away from me, and thereby left My life so naked. Wherefore, word by word, I swore the oath as he repeated it. Nor much it moved me, in my eagerness, To feel a damp and earthy odor break, At ever[y] word, out of each several tomb, In all of which again too, there awoke A darkling stir and hissing, as of snakes: Yet, though the fell of hair upon my scalp Rose bristling under a cold creeping thrill, I failed not, but swore all the dread oath through! No sooner had he ended, and once more The tombs become all silent as their dead, Than straightway a new feeling as of youth And sensual vigor, in bold riot, coursed Through all my veins and summered in my heart! 193 Till there before him in my pride I stood In stately strength, and agile aptitude For instant action, as the wind express— Stood there before him in my stormy pride Magnificent as a desert-nurtured steed Of princeliest pedigree, with nostrils wide Dilated, and with eyes effusing flame! ‘‘Tis well!’ he said; ‘and now begone, prepared To live thy hundred years (no trifling lease) Of pomp, of power, of pleasure, and’—His voice Sighed off into the distance. He was gone: Only a single raven, far aloft, Was beating outward with its sable wings! And when I looked around the tombs as well Had vanished: only in the utmost view The level Desert, objectless and grey, Merged its whole circle with the bending sky.
