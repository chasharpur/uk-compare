IV

Be patient with a Fool.

The wise man’s patience is a fool’s mere due, Because to chide the creature, were to be Like one who, passing a fair orchard through, Casteth his missiles at a fruitless tree: Nought might reward his blow, however true; Wherefore in this we must at least agree, That even a Socrates who so should do Were, for the time, as much a fool as he.
