Social Charity.

Tis Life’s prime household wisdom not to scorn An erring friend too fiercely. For if tried Severely in some weakness, all will turn The wrong road sometimes, and make Friendship’s pride Blush with such shames as for the fallën burn: So much hath Charity in the best to mourn. 230 And knowing this we should be sadly kind When we reprove, not cruel,—lest we churn Folly to madness.—Though in One we find Those gems of worth, high motives, feelings pure, Set in the fine gold of a constant mind, Yet even He shall sometimes prove insure! As in the clearest well a sediment lies Ready, alas! if much disturbed, to rise.
