Poems in early Life by Charles Harpur

A Poet’s Wife

Oh wert thou mine, beloved Girl! How rich I were with such a mate, Albiet around me still might whirl The most injurious waves of Fate! No more disgust, no more despair Should snake-like coil about my life! So couldst thou bless me, didst thou dare To be a Poets Wife.

Before us see how frets the sea Of life, at best a changeful state! An think ere thou putte’st forth with me Where heart alone can cope with Fate, How many new and clinging cares, World-born to an unworldly life, Must be her crowns of thorns, who dares To be a Poets Wife!

A spirit gentle and resigned Must speak her boundless faith in love, And in the temper of her mind The lioness obey the dove: A heart above mere fortunes snares, And all the petty pride of life, 21 Must be her crown of thorns, who dares To be a Poets Wife.

And these are thine, beloved One! And I secure of such a mate, Beleive that Love’s full vision sun Can fill with light the dark of Fate; And that all needful toil can care Shall breath for me this psalm of life: Dare all for her who all could dare— A destined Poets Wife.
