Bits. By Charles Harpur.

I.

The Spouse of Infinitude.

Behold, the Moon is in the midst of heaven, Serenely shining,—like a giant pearl Amongst the fierier jewelry of Night, Infinitude’s dark Spouse;—dark, but no less With inconsumable and boundless beauty, And loveliness—the lovelier because Of its unfathomable mystery— Arrayed as with a robe. And who can look Into her dusk but so resplendent face, As thus, unfailingly recurrent, she Comes over in her fellowship with Time, Nor feel that she was nourished into being Upon the bosom of Almighty Power Illimitably active, and in strict Coincidence with Eternal Verity? And thence with Goodness?—yea, for what indeed Is Truth itself, as in the light of thought We see it purging all things into means For purer issues, but the final test And crowning evidence of deific love!
