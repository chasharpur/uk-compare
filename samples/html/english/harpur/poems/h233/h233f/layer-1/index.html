Love’s Everlasting Identity.

The dulcimer was sounding From within a mystic grove, Where, strown with blooms, rose gleaming A sculpturedl shrine of Love, Round which tall youths and maidens fair Went bounding in a row,— In the flowery dale of Sibma Three thousand years ago.

And we, a group glad-dancing On this fair Australian earth, Have met for the same Spirit To raise our happy mirth; To know it as those mouldered hearts Did its sweet influence know In the flowery dale of Sibma, Three thousand years ago. [...]
