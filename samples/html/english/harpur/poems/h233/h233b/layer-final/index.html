III.

Three Thousand Years Ago.

The dulcimer was sounding Within a twilight grove; Where, strown with blooms, rose gleaming, A sculptured shrine of Love: Round which, tall youths and maidens fair, Went bounding in a row: In the flowery dale of Sibma, Three thousand years ago.

And we, a group glad dancing Upon Australian earth, Have met that the same Spirit, Might raise our happy mirth: To know it, as those mouldered hearts, Did its sweet influence know, In the flowery dale of Sibma, Three thousand years ago.

And in the days, far coming On dusky Time’s dim wing: Some loving band thus meeting, Amid the blooming Spring— May muse of us, as we of them, Whose happy hearts did glow, In the flowery dale of Sibma, Three thousand years ago.
