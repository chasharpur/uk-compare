Song.

Where Beauty is praising the songs that I sing, And Pleasure is gushing like brooks in the spring— From these, and all charming that elswhere I see, I can turn away, Mary, to think upon thee.

38

When blasts of affliction blow cold on my rest, And the pang that will sleep not is loud in my breast; Yet, however clinging each trouble may be, I can turn from it, Mary, to think upon thee.

When Weariness sleepeth, and Care is at rest, And Memory dreameth of all she loves best; Then as the Moon gazes upon the bright sea, My Soul, o’er thy dwelling, looks down upon thee.x

x Had I not brought myself to look upon the circumstances which suggested this song, and others of like character, in the light of painful but necessary preparations for my poetical mission, I should be disposed to mourn that long- continued and continual waste of feeling of which they are but the occasional expression, even as one who would not be comforted.

C.H. 1847.
