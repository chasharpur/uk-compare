XI.

[THE FATE OF POETIC GENIUS IN A SORDID COMMUNITY]

30f Hapless is he who meditates the Nine Where Greed sits regnant, aiming there to build High domes of thought;—there where none deem ‘divine’ His eloquent Art, however he be skiled; And where the Ideal, as by the hectic killed, Fades early,—leaving him alone, to pine 30e O’er youth’s and hope’s and passion’s pale decline, Broken in means, and lastly—broken-willed! To Misery wedded then, as to a wife, 31 And bearing that great load—a noble mind Misvalued,—adown the outer ways of life, What has his genius gained him from his Kind? The worlding’s sneer;—a character maligned;— And thence, alas! with the ignoble,—strife!
