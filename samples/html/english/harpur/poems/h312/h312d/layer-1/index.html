XI.

[THE FATE OF POETIC GENIUS IN A SORDID COMMUNITY]

30d Hapless is he who meditates the Nine Where Greed is rampant, with intent to build Enduring verse; for there none may deem ‘divine’ His eloquent Art, however he be skilled. There the Ideal, as by the hectic killed, Fades early, leaving him alone, to pine 30e O’er youth’s and hope’s and passion’s pale decline, Broken in means, and lastly—broken-willed! To Misery wedded then, as to a wife, 31 And bearing that worst load—a loving heart Unloved, adown the narrow ways of life, What hath he gained by his harmonious art? The worlding’s sneer!—the vile envenomed smart Of Envy’s string! and with the ignoble,—strife!

And this in part Tells my life’s story—and the whole had told Had there not been much iron in my mould, To hard times tempering hard; to cold needs—cold.
