II.

THE PROPOSED RECURRENCE TO TRANSPORTATION.

The shame of bondage is upon the Land Even yet; and a wild cry of blood complains To Heaven, like Abel’s wheresoever band Bushward, the myrmidons of their selfish gains Who erst drave Mammon’s chariot scourge in hand! And shall they again, to puff their windy shows Of Aristocracy, horde hither those Who would perpetuate the shameful brand, The accusing cry? And this avowedly To cheapen Manhood? Better that this fair New world again one wilderness should be; Or that the cause of such unrighteous care, Our cattle, like the swine of Gadara, were Devil-entered all and drowned in the Sea!*

* No public could have vindicated itself more nobly than did that of Australia during the agitation of the subject of the above Sonnet. The base and sordid design of recurring to Transportation, though originated in the highest places of the 452 Land, was shrunk into an abortion by the strong voice and sturdy sense of more popular gatherings. And this should teach the people of Australia for all time, that they have but to be thus true to them- selves, to be able always to render even a faithless and profligate Legislature, nearly if not wholly powerless for evil.

C. H.
