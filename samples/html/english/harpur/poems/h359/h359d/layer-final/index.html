X XXXI.

On the Proposed Recurrence to Transportation.

The shame of bondage is upon the Land Even yet; and a wild cry of blood complains To heaven, like Abel’s, whereso bushward band The myrmidons of their polluted gains Who erst drave Mammon’s chariot scourge in hand! And shall they again, to puff their windy shows Of Lordship, spot us with the plague of those Who would perpetuate the shameful brand? The accusing cry? And this, through labor’s fee, To cheapen Manhood! Better that this fair New world once more a wilderness should be! Or that the cause of such unrighteous care, Our cattle, like the swine of Gadara, were Devil-entered all and drownëd in the sea!

1842.
