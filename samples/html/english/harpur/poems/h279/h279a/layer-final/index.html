LITERARY REGISTER.

STANZES.

Want ground the faces of the Prophets old, Our greatest Bards were poor in all but song; The golden-minded ever wanted gold, And they who wronged not ever suffered wrong.

Such, in this unintelligible World, Seems still the Sage’s and the Poet’s fate;— The mountain of its Scorn upon them hurled, Or grinds to misery, or constrains to hate.

To hate sometimes!—but with a Godlike will, To this superior they should ever prove! Though sacrificed, like Jesus, loving still Even their murderers with a brother’s love.

CHARLES HARPUR.
