IV.

TO A FRIEND, ON THE DEATH OF ONE OF HIS CHILDREN.

I know not, friend, what ’tis to lose a child, But I can feel what such a loss must be; Can apprehend how the heart waileth wild At the death-parting with a creature we Have loved so largely—to our misery! And how all objects that before have smiled In union with it must be thenceforth soiled By the damp shadow of mortality. Still, though thus hurt, let us no less adore God’s ways. Our loss is only a remove Of the Beloved to some heavenly shore, Whither she trails the clue her spirit wove, Up to our better home. The strength of Love Might teach us this much were it all our lore.
