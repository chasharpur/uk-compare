Trust in God.

Deep trust in God—for that I still have sought Through all the crude doubts that obstruct the soul, When in the amazement of far-reaching thought, We list the laborings that for ever roll Like dubious thunders through those clouded regions Where Night Time and Destiny the counsels keep Of Time developing his shadowy legions. And when I’ve stood upon some giddy steep Of Speculation—heaving up its bare And nebulous ridge high in the nebulous air Of endless change, and thence tremendously Throwing its shadow, like a blind man’s stare, Out through the dread Unknown,—deep trust in Thee, O God, hath likewise been my refuge there.
