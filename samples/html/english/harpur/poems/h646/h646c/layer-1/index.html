Trust in God.

Deep trust in God!—for that I still have sought Through all the dim doubts that beshade the soul, When, in the amazement of far-reaching thought, We list the laborings that for ever roll Their thundrous wheels within that clouded lair Where this world’s Destiny dost the secrets keep Wherewith Time’s mortal mystery is fraught, And when I’ve stood upon some fearful steep Of Speculation, that did heave its bare And rugged ridge into the nebulous air Of endless change, and thence tremendously Throw its dark shadow, like a blind man’s stare, Into the dread Unknown,—deep trust in thee, O God, hath been my object even there.
