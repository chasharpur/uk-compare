XIII.

[PALE AM I, WITNESSING THE THOUSAND ILLS]

Pale am I, witnessing the thousand ills Of this most jarring world. For wheresoe’er 32 I turn me, somewhat wrongful meets me there To murder Peace! and my irked bosom fills With questionings, thus: Must all that Genius wills In its fraternal hope and social care, Prove a dream always? and this bright Earth bear Such bitter fruit for ever—fruit that kills? Many held wise, see all I see, and yet Declare that as spectators none should be Thence so distressed; but at their words I fret, As but the utterances of appathy, Or of that iron wisdom which is set Only, and ever, on self-immunity.
