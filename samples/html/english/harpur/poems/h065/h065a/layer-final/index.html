Original Poetry.

CHARITY.

What were this world without thee, spirit pure?— A grinding niggard, wrinkle browed, and hoar With the blind lust of gold and selfish power! If not from thee, whence were there balm to cure The scornful strokes which lowly heads endure From pampered Privilege? Thou art the core Of Wisdom’s social aim; who, all the more Fierce Error scouts thee, toils to hold thee sure. Come! in thy motherly lap, awhile, I fain Would lay my head, and dream upon the time When thy sweet influence every land and clime Shall visit; and—as o’er some torrid plain Fresh airs breathe energy—give man to gain Capacity for Love’s millennian prime.

Charles Harpur.

Singleton, September, 1842.
