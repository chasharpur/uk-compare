IV.

Self Liberty.

I would not be dependent, even for love, On man or woman. Nay, I would—I will Be as the eagles through the heavens that move, In liberty though separate: and as still A strenuous torrent from the craggy hill Doth make its own best way, be’t mine to groove My individual wold-path, and approve Its fitness with a lonely rapturous thrill!

Thus large must be my freedom, for the need Is in my nature, and defies dispute; As the demand of an original breed Impels yon tree to bear its proper fruit. And though the pliant deem me a strange brute, What matter? Am I not myself indeed?

1855.
