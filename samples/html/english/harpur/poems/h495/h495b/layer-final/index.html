XXIV.

Self Liberty.X

I would not be dependent even for love On man or woman. Nay, I would—I will Be as the eagles through the heavens that move, In liberty, though separate: and as still A torrent dashing from some clouded hill Doth make its own best way, be’t mine to groove My individual world-path, and approve Its lonely fitness with a rapturous thrill. Thus large must be my freedom, for the need Is in my nature and defies dispute; Even as a bent peculiar to its breed, Constrains yon tree to bear its proper fruit: And though the pliant deem me a strange brute, What matter? Am I not myself indeed?
