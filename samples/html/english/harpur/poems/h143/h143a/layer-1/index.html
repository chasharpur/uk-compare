[UNTITLED FRAGMENT]

[...] Well, I scarce need say She played me false, for it was in her animal sap To do so, as an appetite for mice Is native to the maw-juice of a cat. But never was a poor crest fallen craven More humbled—lower struck than I was then By what in her was a mere act of kind. Yes, I, whom now, a very storm, methinks Would rather shun than strive to overcome If haply it might choose its own direction. My thews—my very heart strings, man, lost tone, Grew spongey, and incapable of tension, Like catgut in the damp. ‘Gods! how I scorn Myself this su while I think of it! But I got better,—and then write some rhymes, The first and last rhymes that I ever wrote, And sent them to her thinking they would singe The wanton down upon her conscious cheek. Poor fool! poor fool! a little scalding tea Splash in her face, had made her wince perhaps, And think upon her sin,—but my trim song What could it do, but feather, and that highly The harlot vanity of a Harlot’s soul? But you shall hear it.
