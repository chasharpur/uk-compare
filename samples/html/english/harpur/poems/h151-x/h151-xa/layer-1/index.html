Chorus.

Ah, that swill’d Luxury with its selfishness, And lack of noble brotherhood! Ah, that gaunt Poverty with its mean distress And fox-like cunning!—and with these the brood Of wolfish rapine, rude. should for ever In Earth’s green bosom still should heighten so, And widen into such a world-like woe, As even in the best gives all belief In human aptitude For righteous wisdom and fraternal good, To perish through excess of honest grief!

He is Truth’s enemy who but endures! Children of Light and Intellect, the deeds Which make the Earth (amid its jarring creeds) Endurable to God, are yours! 90 Powerful to suffer, are ye weak to do? No: and then wherefore are ye slow to be Supremely—nobly—wisely free From such vile burthens as now most ye rue? To be what through all time ye might have been, Had ye been only true To one another and the light within!

X

Crown’d Heads and mitered Dignities, The courtly Slave and ministerial Lord, And Earth’s mere Governments composed of these, And they who serve them with the sword, Backed by her Merchants and her worldly Great— What rational hope can such afford Of better human destinies? For such, skill’d only in state schemes of state And base sleights of war and trade, though therein sage, Lag aye behind the virtue of their age Where pride and spoil and interest allures— 91 Where pride and spoil and interest allures— The ultimate blurs and blots on History’s page! Then, do ye well record, Children of Light and Genius, that the deed Which gives the harvest unto Truth, is yours! Hold ye to her eternal creed, And with a spirit that not alone endures The evil by a foregone fate upthrown, But shapes as well the destiny that cures Earth’s ailment, and her day of health ensures, Arise ye to the hour of need, Claiming all sovereignty for Mind alone! Arise, and sow the better seed,— And reap your own!
