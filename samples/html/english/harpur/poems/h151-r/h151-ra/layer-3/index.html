CHORUS.

Lo, pampered Privilege securely squanders What Nature meant not to be bought or sold! Lo, Selfishness feeds fat his lust Through the world’s glittering pestilence—Gold! While penniless between them Genius wanders Bowed even to the dust!

How long, O Earth, is such to be thy fruit? How long shall thy most pleasant dwelling places Be but the lairs of what is mainly brute In Man? the nurseries of his disgraces?

53

How long shall Customs whose bad growth appeared First round the blood-fed altars of Old Time, Be by thy Rulers sedulously reared To choke each vernal promise of thy prime? Thus human Power hath still been Wrong’s handmaiden, And still each national code hath chiefly been A statist woven screen For arm’d Prerogative, and such as trade in The honors, privileges rights and creeds— All that for which each cheated People bleeds; But to the poor a yolk instead, Yea to starved millions an interdict on bread, An added burthen to the heavy laden: Making long life but more of toilsome breath But more of a stretched out lie! And finally Leaving the shackled soul that would be free No hope but from the liberty, of death!

Yet cheer the Earth! this may not always be! Much of thy dole is past! Though lying lips have said that misery And Evil is to be thy dower for ever, The day of Intellect is dawning fast, 54 Bringing with light full sanction to deliver, Bright with the fire of God, and gloriously vast! Bringing Salvation like a cleansing river Whose voice comes loudning up in the prophetic blast!
