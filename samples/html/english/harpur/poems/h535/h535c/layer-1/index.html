SONNET TO MR. BOYCE.

BY CHARLES HARPUR.

“The Rev. Mr. Boyce said that allusion had been made by a previous speaker, to pulling down the ensign which was then floating over them; but he should like to see the man who would dare to do so.”—( Vide Report of the Anti-Convict Meeting in Barrack Square )

Thrice-valiant Boyce! and so you are not merely A preacher, but would fight upon occasion!— Perhaps not Shaw the Guardsman more severely— Did you imply as much in your oration? You Clericals are fond of looking doughty Obliquely—but not being in Barrack-square, I cannot say to what tune you were party Exactly, or how much you meant to dare. But this you said—or the Reporters lie, (A thing they’ll do as fast as horses gallop,) That any one of all then standing by, Who dared to touch a certain flag—you’d wallop! ‘Twas well, Sir; but now say, without a stammer, Would you have done it with your Gospel hammer?*

* Id e t, with his fist—eh?—Printer’s Devil.
