The Late Charles Harpur.

We have received from Mrs. Mary Har- pur, wife of the late Charles Harpur, the first Australian Poet, copies of two of her late husband’s poems,—”A Rhyme,” and ”The Coming On of Freedom,” the latter partly in print and partly in manuscript. At a time like the present, when the whole world is in a state of ferment, when Labour is in organized revolt against conditions which are burdensome and oppressive, and when there are prospects of a brighter and a better time coming, these verses are singu- larly appropriate. Consequently, they are published gladly,—and should be read with deep interest by all, more particularly as they were written by one whom all should be proud to claim as their own Windsor poet. Portion of the poem was published in the “Maitland Mercury” in 1842:— 2b

The Coming On of Freedom.

The day must come—for Freedom must keep pace With Knowledge—when the accident of birth, Or emptier imposition of mere pelf, Shall neither arrogate to any man Distinction as of right, nor qualify Any for power, by a sole influence, Over his fellows. But all men shall stand Up proudly, under the wide roof of Heaven, As God-created equals: each the sire Of his own worth, and the joint sanctioner Of all political partainment—all Moral and social honour! Yea, for such Is Freedom’s Charter, written on the heart Of capable Humanity, when purged From the foul stains of Time, and his base rout Of arrogant hell-suggested superstitions, And customs born of Error.

And let none Despair of such an advent: for, as when Some solemn Wood’s familiar cadences, Deepening and deepening all around, portend The salutary storm: even so, the all- Pervading instinct of a sure revolt Against the ancient tyrannies of the earth, Breathes on before it in the living stress Of Knowledge,—omening the unborn change, By harshening still to the fine ear of Thought The daily jar of customary wrongs.
