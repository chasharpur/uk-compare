42

Loneness of Heart

Ah! bid me not new pleasures chase, Light griefs alone are cheated so; I am but wearied by the race— Not lightned by the load of wo.

No, rather, like a wounded bird, My heart should haunt the loneliest ways, Where but the moaning brook is heard— Where but the sighing woodbine strays.

For when amid the gay and vain I play a light and festive part, The fire is keener in my brain— The load is heavier at my heart

An echo of the mountain cave— The prairie’s solitary tree— A petrel o’er the midsea wave Is not more lone than I would be.
