Sir Gilbert Blount.

By Charles Harpur.

Sir Gilbert Blount’s a happy knight, And happier is he aye, To think his sole Child’s lovesome eyes Grow lovelier day by day. Two violets in a shady place, Where two are seen alone— Two well-springs filled with heaven’s blue face, ‘Neath willowy fringes shown. And so it hath been ever forth, Since first his heart could say: A Lily of Love’s green Valley Is born to me this day

Sir Gilbert Blount’s a jovial man— A jovial and a free! But why hath he provoked to day This storm of minstrelsy? It is as if his heart had run Stag-wild with mirth and wit, And he would have old Time’s grey one Go bounding on with it! But did you ask the reason why, The happy man would say: My Lily of Love’s green Valley Is seven years old to day.

Sir Gilbert was a jovial man— A jovial and a free! But silent in his hall is now The voice of minstrelsy: 518 It may be he hath felt his prime A-wearing from him fast, And so would make the coming time Redeem the heedless past. But did you ask the reason why, The altered man would say: My Lily of Love’s green Valley Is whithering away!

Sir Gilbert was a stalwart knight, And sturdy as a tree, Though genial as the warmth of wine In pleasant company: And can it be that he hath so Misused a year of time, That few his face again would know Who saw him in his prime? Ah! that is not the reason why His head is turned so grey: His Lily of Love’s green Valley Has wither’d forever away!!
