EPITAPH FOR A SAILOR.

In Port at last! Around his Bark no more The wave shall threaten, or the tempest roar. None ever voyaged the wild sea of Life Less rudened by its danger, toil and strife. A Man he was in every manly part, A Child in fervent innocence of heart: And I who mourn him, and who knew him well, Tell of him thus but what the Angels tell.

Note to the Epitaph for a Sailor

In moments of solemn afflication, when our dispositions are shaken with “thoughts beyond the reaches of our souls”, the vaguest imaginations are apt to move us with all the force and effect of distinct realities. On the evening of the death of the friend upon whom the above Epitaph was composed, I was standing at the back of the house in which his body lay, gazing in sorrowful abstraction at the twilight heavens. The sun had gone down a few minutes before in magnificent serenity, and nearly the whole western portion of the sky had at this time assumed the resemblance of a vast purple sea, opening out from the appearance of a deep shored bay just above the horizon, and extending along and beyond a bold coast line of golden cloud, in placid immensity. I noted this resemblance for some moments without any distinct sentiment besides that of pensive admiration – when suddenly apprehending a whitish speck in the very midst of it, which rapidly likened into a ship, as beheld at a great distance, my mind immediately surrendered itself, with infantine plas- ticity, to the impression, that the departed spirit of my sailor friend was there sailing away on its last long voyage.

Charles Harpur.
