The Charmer.

Yonder’s the charming One—look you! yon Creature Orbed, like a star, in her own glowing grace! So rosy of gesture! so perfect in feature! With a figure the twin-born delight of her face.

And see in her eyes how the lightnings of beauty Are vieled in so maidenly modest an air, That Passion by gazing is tran tamed into duty— An eagle transformed to a dove as it were.

Then, ‘neath her kerchief—oh, rapture! behold you What apples of Paradise breathingly rise! Give heroes their glory, and worldlings their gold too! But make thou, oh Fortune! yon maiden my prize.

And surely as Heaven is good and all-seeing, O’erlooked by its favor she never can be; Whence the love of so pure and so perfect a Being Would bring down its infinite blessing on me!
