IV.

To the Same.

Little perhaps thou valuest verse of mine— Little hast read of what my hand has wrought, Yet I with thy brave memory would entwine The Muse’s amaranths. For thou well hast fought For Freedom; well her sacred lessons taught; 467 Well baffled wrong; and delved with far design Into those elements where treasures shine Excelling those wherewith our hills are fraught. And when thy glorious grey head shall make One spot all hallowed for the coming days,— Tombed in the Golden Land for whose sole sake With labor thou hast furrowed all thy ways,— Well a young Nation shall thy worth appraise Even Through the grief which then shall o’er thee break!
