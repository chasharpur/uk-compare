A Piece of advice for all young Maids who wish never to be old ones.

In this precarious and chance-turning world, Never delay to seize a due enjoyment; For nothing keeps the heart so sweet and sound As its full share of pleasure, nor the head So healthful, and so patient of its toil Amid the struggles of maturing life. But most in Love, despite dame Worldly Prudence, Be ever with occasion, when ’tis honest; For every drop in its full-mantling cup That over brims untasted, is a loss— A miserable loss of so much happiness; A loss for aye: for life can store for each But one full beaker of the rosy bliss: Nay, ’tis a loss that leaves less beady and bright Even what remains within, though it were draind By Passion the next minute. Think of this, Ye soft-eyed, ruby-lipped, joy-loving Bloomers, And let the apes of Hell lead one another, 51 Or go unlead, as likes them:—But do you Think still of this, that Joy may get his due.
