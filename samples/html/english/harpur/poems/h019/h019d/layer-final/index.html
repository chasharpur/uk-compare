3.

The First Great Australian Poet.

His lot how glorious whom the Muse shall name His first High Priest in this so sunny clime, Clothing his life as with a robe of flame! With her creations of the olden time Much cònversant, and by their bulk sublime Moulding new matter, let him build to Fame, Quarrying from Nature’s everlasting frame The solid substance of his lofty rhyme. Then, meetly mirrored, o’er his polished page Shall glow his Countrywomen’s lustrous eyes, And future heroes thence a righteous rage Derive or stimulate:—all brave and wise And beautiful souls, under his native skies, Breathing his influence from age to age.
