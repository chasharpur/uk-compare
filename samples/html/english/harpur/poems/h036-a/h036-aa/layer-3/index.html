That peculiar and exalted beauty which mainly results from a spiritual empassionment of the material universe—that visible enrichment of bodily things which is in its cause invisible—is the theme and glory of the ensuing Lyric. Considered essentially, it is clearly either a spontaneous effluence or a prepared and sublimated effluence, of the Mind, reflecting from its objects; and is to the common aspect of Nature & the palpable order of the world, what lively emotions are to the human countenance, and the impulse of bright and graceful feeling, to all the airs and attitudes of an intelligent organism. It is Beautiful enhanced by a transfusion of the Feelingful:—a creation of delight of that which is, surpassing the fruitions of that which was, and which if commited to some form or channel of art stands not thenceforth as a detached spiritual substance— a “joy forever” in itself and is sofar independent of its origin, as to lean upon it only through a critical reference.

I annex, as a sort of companion piece to the latter portraiture of this note, some fugitive lines on

Animal Human Beauty

I knew a Youth, in feature and in form Shaped like a God; yet in the air of these There was a somewhat so all-animal— So fleshly, fierce and froward that they seemed Instinct with sensual venom (as it were) And which, whenever I beheld him, made His beauty frightful, even while it took The vision,—like a bold and splendid Snake! With a predominating intellect Forth shining from his front, and ordering all His attitudes, how different the effect Of lineaments so perfect, and a form So toweringly and beautifully built? Then such a Youth is a sustaining tone, And not a discord, in the March of Ma[n.]
