VIII.

MILITARY MERIT.

Were all men with true courage fired Mere warriors would be less admired. And gory merit, to my mind, However splendid in its kind, Is yet too gross to lure the eyes Of even Pagans that are wise: 97 For when appraised with sober heed, ‘Tis too much of a piece indeed With that of the prize-fighter, who On being pounded black and blue, I clep’d by Boxiana’s muses One mass of glory—meaning bruises.
