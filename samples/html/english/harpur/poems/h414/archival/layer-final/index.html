RADICAL BOB.

A New Song, by Charles Harpur.

“Radical Bob?” Then surely he’s one Who is first in the race of all progress to run; Firm—fixed as a rock undue power to withstand, Though expansive as light under Liberty’s wand? Not at all. Like a vane, he’s with every gale, And is just now a joint in Old Lottery’s tail.

“Radical Bob?” Then some principle plain Must guide him. Well, search for’t. Alas! ‘twere as vain As the quest of the madman who once thought to seize By the mane as it flowed and to bridle the breeze: For the truth of the matter is this, as you’ll find, The fellow had never a thing of the kind.

“Radical Bob!” Though the snake we detest, It is true to its seeming—an ocular pest: And though the lank dingo’s pernicious to man, He feigns not—but slaughters whenever he can: Hence this Charlatan more should our hatred compel, Who, both like and unlike them, ‘s like Satan as well.*

* With the above, I have done with Radical Bob, once for all. I have been long so far ashamed of his political Jim Crowism, so sick of the sorry figure that he is cutting in his old age (for he is indeed old in everything ratting included), that it was repugnant to my feeling of common countryship, to make him a subject either serious or comic. But I have at last gibbetted his once so much self-glorified radicalism; and having done so, I have washed my hands of him. I will not condescend again to touch, even in rhyme, upon one who, it would seem, is even happy in making himself the Jack Pudding of such leaden-witted Unrighteousness, as is the oligarchic portion of our present Council.

C. H.

September 5, 1853.
