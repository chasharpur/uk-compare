1b

THE LASS OF EULENGO.*

Let those who worship Fashion sigh At titled Beauty’s feet; Let him whose God is Mammon try A golden bride to meet; Let all whom affectation charms Frequent the city show— But give unto my happy arms The lass of Eulengo.

If beauty that hath bloom’d apart, Be loveliest to behold; A spotless name, and truth of heart, More excellent than gold; If sparkling arts that lure to sin Be nought to virtue’s glow, Then matchless were my portion in The lass of Eulengo.

I see her in my dreams by night, And hear her gentle voice; And when, in morning’s golden light, All living things rejoice, Each skiey tint of rarest grain— Each fresh delight below— All bring into my thoughts again The lass of Eulengo.

Oh, never may one frown of fate Upon her future lour, Nor villain guile there lie in wait To wound so sweet a flower! But happiness for her abound, And love for ever flow, A bright and gushing stream, around The lass of Eulengo!

*The aboriginal name of an estate on the Upper Hunter.
