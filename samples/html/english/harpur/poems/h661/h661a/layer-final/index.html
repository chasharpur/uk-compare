A Rhyme for Children.

Vesper—Evening’s lovely star, How I wonder what you are! Hanging in the mellow sky Like a golden lamp on high vesper—Evening’s lovely star, How I wonder what you are?

Clouds may hide you for a while, Yet, above them, still you smile, Every quenchless, ever there, Beaming through the upper air! Lovely Vesper—Evening’s star, How I wonder what you are?

Are you, say, an isle of light, In the shoreless Infinite Homing happy creatures who Pain or sorrow never knew? Lovely Vesper—Evening’s star, Let me wonder what you are.

iv

Live there in your chrystal sphere Fairer things than any here? Things that, breathing heaven’s breath Pure and perfect, know not death? Vesper—Evening’s lovely star, Then I wonder what you are?

What if you be Paradise, Showing in those evening skies Where the good who here have trod Once more Spirits, sit with God? Vesper—Evening’s lovely star Still I wonder what you are

And still gratefully you glow, Whither you be such or no: Hanging in the mellowy sky Like a golden lamp on high— Glowing still Heaven’s fairest star While I wonder what you are!
