Capital Punishment

To string a man up for the very worst crime, Is like smashing a watch for not keeping good time! It were wiser, no doubt, To use the bad man in a work of some good, And so patent a moral sublime, And, by cleaning it out, To make the wrong watch keep the best time one could:— What is this but the truth—though in rhyme?
