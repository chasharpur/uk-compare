[RECORDS OF A POET’S LOVE. EPILOGUE]

Here, in the moaning wind Of Memory, its sole though meet refrain, Snaps, dying pastward, this long-linkèd strain Of bardic love, born both of heart and mind; To leave with me, alas! a wild and vain And aching sense of mystic loss behind; Since now, at last, I only from it gain Thoughts of fond pleasures that gave birth to Pain! Yet well I know, that to all souls refined Its chastened splendors shall for aye remain, As pearls of passion precious in their kind.
