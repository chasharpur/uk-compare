V.

Introspective

O Poesy! what image may I start That best shall figure all the joy and pride My spirit hath in thee? In truth thou art A Passion that for ever draws my heart, Even as a fawn’s kept constant to the side Of its own proper dam. And as the tide Of light is fountained in the sun apart ..... 547 Thou art the golden source of all the free And glorious things I build for in the wide And wondrous Future! Rivers to the sea More surely run not, than each wish I frame Of personal influence hath its end in thee And in thy Queenship: for thy very name Is to my soul as honey to the bee!
