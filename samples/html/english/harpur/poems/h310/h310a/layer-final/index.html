V.

MY LOVE OF POESY.

O Poesy! what image may I start That best shall picture all the roving pride My nature hath in thee! In truth thou art A joy that keeps for ever next my heart, Even as a fawn is constant to the side Of its own proper dam. And as the tide Of morn hath in the sun its central part, Thou wert the Eye of that Utopian chart My earliest dreams unrolled in the so wide And wondrous future. Rivers to the sea More surely run not, than each wish I frame Of personal glory hath its end in thee, And in thy Queenship: For thy very name Is to my soul as honey to the bee.
