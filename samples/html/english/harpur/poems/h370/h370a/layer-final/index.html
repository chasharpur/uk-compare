OUTWARD SHOW.

For outward show, we barter competence, Family comfort, credit, friendship’s ties; And even love’s own dreamy Eden, whence The beautifullest flowers of life arise To breathe a soul-sweet incense to the skies! This social envy—this most prurient sense Of self-display, grows daily more intense, Infecting even the wisdom of the wise. Children are pincht with want and early woe, That she who bore them, Fashion’s fribble slave, To church or theatre full-drest may go; Sires, husbands, fops, all itch alike, and so Risk station and woo ruin, to o’er brave Some rival in the court of outward show.
