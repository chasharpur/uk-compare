STANZAS.

Mine heart doth ever ache to be The trusting thing it was before; Mine eyes do often weep to see What they may see no more! But mourns my heart, thus early old, Because that hope can no new flower unfold? And know mine eyes these visitings, That fancy no new vision brings? No: but that of its gold, Experience, with her scythe-like wings, Hath stript the ancient face of recollected things.

I know that vainly aches to be The trusting thing it was before; That these as vainly weep to see What may be seen no more! Yet, from each melancholy mood, My soul, methinks, distils a drop of good— A kindly aptitude to see That harshest things by charity Might be to grace subdued; That much by human love might be Ev’n rendered as divine as once it seemed to me.

Charles Harpur.

Singleton, October, 1842.
