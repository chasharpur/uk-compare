The Spirit of Love.

When broad o’er her hill-top hangs Eve’s glowing star, Comes a Spirit of beauty, as if from afar; 87 To dream through the gloaming’s heart-hushing degrees, And richen the odor-sweet breath of the breeze:

To mellow the echoes that bleat from the hills Where the shepherds are folding their flocks by the rills; In the wild woods to deepen the dove’s gentle voice, To live in all life, in all gladness rejoice.

Thus out of the sunset, as if from afar, Comes it ever, as now, with that broad-hanging star: Yea, ’tis breathing around, it is glowing above, That spirit of beauty—the Spirit of Love.
