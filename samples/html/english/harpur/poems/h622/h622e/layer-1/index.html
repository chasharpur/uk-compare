XXII.

To my Young Countryman, D. H. D. ☞☞

Who doubteth when the morning star doth light Her lamp of beauty, that the day is coming? Or where prime odors track the breeze’s flight, That rare flowers in the vicinage are blooming? Or where the wild bees all around are humming, That hony’s stored in some near cedar’s height? Or that the ocean’s heaving into sight When, more and more, long surgy rolls come booming? And surely as the observer understands What each of these foretokens in its kind, Thy future’s mental amplitude expands Before me in its omens, when I find Some early blossom breathing of thy mind— Some thing of promise fashioned by thy hands.
