SONNETS DEDICATED TO AUSTRALIAN SENATORS.

NO. XVII.

THE WINDY SUBJECT AGAIN.

I’ve said how Belial with the Patriot’s name Made merry. Then His Majesty, Old Nick, Retorted thus—Ha, of our servant Dick Whence cometh thy disrelish? Does his fame For indefatigable Wrongness, shame Thy jealous indolence? Or does his quick And sneering hate of every thing but trick, A rival’s envy even in Belial claim?—— To which the punning Feature answered—No! Yon barking fellow, though he had out-sinn’d me, I could not envy, Sire; because I know He’s hollow, even in a Council-shindy! And hence, with reference to whate’er might blow, I held that Windy-er must mean more windy.

THE CLERK OF THE WEATHER.

FINIS.
