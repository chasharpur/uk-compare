126 Final Copy.

To William Wordsworth.

[This Sonnet was the dedicatory one to a short series of Sonnets called “Thoughts”, published many years ago in Sydney, and which of course fell still-born from the press.]

With Earth’s whole bulk between us—lo, a child Of th’ Utmost South, remembering with what glee Of sacred fellowship his soul, beguiled By thy wise wonder-dreams, did grow to be More happy;—He, if on these Thoughts hath smiled Uranian light—a glow of passion, mild Though mighty,—thus would point that light to Thee! And haply that thy reverend head might bend Approvingly, and so accept the Wreath Which from his native wilds he thus doth send— This pious wis out of his heart did breathe So hymn-like sweet, that lowly bending ‘neath Australia’s heaven these votive lines he penned.
