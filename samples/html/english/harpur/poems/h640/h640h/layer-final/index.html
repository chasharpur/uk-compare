126 Final Copy.

To William Wordsworth.

[This Sonnet was the dedicatory one to a short series of Sonnets called “Thoughts”, published many years ago in Sydney, and which of course fell still-born from the press.]

With Earth’s whole bulk between—behold, a child Of th’ Utmost South, remembering with what glee Of sacred fellowship his soul, beguiled By thy wise wonder-dreams, did grow to be Even from the burthen of its mystery, More happy;—He, if on these Thoughts hath smiled Uranian light—a glow of passion, mild Though mighty,—thus would point that light to Thee! And haply that thy sacred head might bend Approval in acceptance of the Wreath Which from his native wilds he casts to send— This pious hope out of his heart did breathe So hymn-like sweet, that lowly bowing ‘neath Australia’s heaven these votive lines he penned.
