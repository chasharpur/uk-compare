XVI.

To Wordsworth. X

With Earth’s whole bulk between us, I, a child Of the Utmost South, rememb’ring with what glee Of sacred fellowship my soul, beguiled By thy wise wonder-dreams, hath grown to be Even from the burthen of its mystery, More happy;—if upon these ‘Thoughts’x hath smiled Uranian light—a glow of passion, mild Though mighty, I would point that light to thee. And haply that thy reverend head might bend Approvingly awhile over the Wreath Which from my native wilderness I send— This pious hope out of my heart did breathe So hymn-like sweet, that lowly bending ‘neath Australia’s heaven, these votive lines I penned.

x This Sonnet was the dedicatory one to a short series of sonnets called ‘Thoughts’, published in Sydney many years ago, and which, of course, fell still-born from the press.
