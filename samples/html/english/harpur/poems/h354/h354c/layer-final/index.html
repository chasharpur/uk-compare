ELEGY ON THE DEATH OF ESTHER PALMER, MY SISTER’S INFANT DAUGHTER.

And art thou gone, dear child—so early gone? But a few yesterdays, and thou wert flush As airs disporting the green mountains on, As verdure sprinkled by the fountain’s gush: Another day is past—and thou art found A mouldering fragment of the burial ground.

Oh

, tell us, for what purpose cam’st thou here? Tow’rd what did tend thy transitory bloom? Was it but to exact a mother’s tear, And swell the mournful trophies of the tomb? Or like a colour of the closing day, Only to smile thy little self away?

Or

thus, or not,—yet thine’s a full repose, And thine a shelter from the future storm; The hand which crushes the disclosing rose, Destroyeth also the ingend’ring worm; Giving it, so, to triumph o’er the might Of coming tempests and impending blight.
