II.

ASKING IN VAIN.

Still his little grave she seeketh In her mother-sorrow wild! Hush! while in her heart she speaketh To the spirit of her child! “Oh! were we not to one another Once the sum of all sweet gain? Say, then; say unto thy mother, Shall we ever meet again: Darling! shall we meet again, Knowing, loving one another?”

Nothing but the wind is heard Sighing in reply, When the long grave-grass is stirred As it passeth by.

“Ah! what weary, weary sorrows Have I known through loss of thee! And what comfortless to-morrows Wait me in this misery! And were we not to one another Once the sum of all sweet gain? Say, then; say unto thy mother, Shall we ever meet again! Darling! shall we meet again, Knowing, loving one another?”

Still the wind alone is heard Sighing in reply, When the long grave-grass is stirred As it passeth by.
