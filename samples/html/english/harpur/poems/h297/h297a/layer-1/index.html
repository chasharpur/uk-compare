obituary Lines.

I stand beside my Father’s grave. He died Too late it may be, for he suffered much, But not an hour too soon; for his bruised heart Had given up the world, and all his hopes Like withered leave[s] in winter had been blown From their deciduous hold on what we here Call Nature: he but followed in the[ir] wake. Yet died he a believer in the truth And fatherhood of God, holy and great, Mighty to help and merciful to sinners! Yea, in the heavenward reaching light of Faith His soul went forth, as in a sunbeam’s track Some close caged bird, from a long bondage freed, Goes winging up against the open sky.
