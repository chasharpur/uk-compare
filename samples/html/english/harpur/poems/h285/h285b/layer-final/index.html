73

Mary’s Love

Forget him I cannot—forsake him I will not! Oh, for the day when his Bride I may be! Dead-cold as a stone were my heart did it thrill not, To think, or but dream my bright Charlie of thee.

Oft does the wayward one make feel lonely, Wilding away like a wave of the main! But I know that he loves me—and loveth me only, And so I forgive him again and again.

Quick come the day—Oh, were it tomorrow! When in Love’s haven my wild one shall rest! But living or dying, in joy or in sorrow, I will bless him and bless him, as I have loved him and blest
