III.

Mary’s Love.

Forget him I cannot—forsake him I will not! Oh, for the day when his Bride I may be! Dead-cold as a stone were my heart did it thrill not, To think, or but dream, my bright Charlie, of thee.

Oft does the wayward one make me feel lonely, Wilding away like a wave of the main! But I knows that he loves me—and loveth me only, And so I forgive him again and again.

Quick come the day—Oh, were it tomorrow! When in Love’s haven my wild one shall rest! But living or dying, in joy or in sorrow, I will love him and bless him, as I’ve loved him and blest.
