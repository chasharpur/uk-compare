759

Sonnet

Alas! affliction’s cloud oershadows thee, And thy grey head is bow’d with care and woes, And many a furrow’d mark upon thy brows Tells that sad thought and dread anxiety Hath deepenëd the lines that [yet] the plough Of time hath [graven] there. Thoughts deeper now Than of Australia’s weel and liberty Dwell in thy brain; a son in shame held low, By laws by him unbroken.—what a blow To thee whose pride and pleasure ’twas to see Mankind [unshackled] independant go In mind and body, curbless as the flow Of some proud river roling calm and free To the unfathomed depths of an outmoving sea
