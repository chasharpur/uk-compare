A Vision of an Angel..

I had a Vision of an Angel—who Within the glimmering circle of my half Awakened consciousness, arising, stood Just where its dim verge met the dark of Sleep: Even as the Moon, on the horizon, stands Within the dream-gaze of the slumbering Earth— Stood radiantly disclouded, as it seemed, 70 From the illimitable dark of Sleep, And shadowing his snowy beauty so As but the more confirmed its glorious grace And heavenly excellence, with ample wings Whose paradise-plumes in their down-folding sweep, At every stir inter-reflecting, glowed With colors such as still intenselier mix In the bright borders of an evening cloud, When like a many-skirted fleece, it hangs Over the sunset, in the burning west.

So stood He, a resplendent Apparition! And could I now re-voice to human ears The wondrous things he spake—spake with a tongue Like those that, on the day of Pentecost, Flamed down on the first Christians, and from which His words came raying, each word like a star, Though with a subtle fusion flowing all, When uttered thus, into one luminous wave That broke on the soul’s ear, and breaking, chimed Such golden miracles of melodious sound As Beings attune whose breath of life is light, Not air impure, the vital draught of mortals!— Could I re-voice those oracles—O then, Should all who heard, in utterance jubilant, Conjoin a symphony in words like these:

“Nations no more shall trade in war; no more Shall banish concord from the homes of Earth; But all mankind, together knit in love, Shall henceforth move in concert with all truth, All excellence, all benefit, all delight, All goodness; while, as from a well whose springs Are central as that peace of God round which 71 All secular appearances revolve, As still they gather on the winds of Time, And, so revolving, waste like summer clouds,— They drink the spirit of eternal strength From one sole Faith, whose waves of health shall flow As in the beginning—out of Paradise.
