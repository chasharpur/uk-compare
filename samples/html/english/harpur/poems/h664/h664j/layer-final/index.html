A VISION OF AN ANGEL.

BY CHARLES HARPUR.

I had a vision of an Angel, who Within the glimmering circle of my half Awakened consciousness, arising, stood Just where its dim verge met the dark of Sleep: Even as the Moon, on the horizon, stands Within the dream-gaze of the slumbering Earth— Stood radiantly disclouded, as it seemed, From the illimitable dark of Sleep, And Shadowing his snowy beauty so As but the more confirmed its glorious grace. With ample-folding wings, whose paradise plumes At every stir, inter-reflecting, glowed With colors such as still intenslier mix In the bright borders of an evening cloud, When, like a many-skirted fleece, it hangs Over the sunset, in the burning west.*

So stood He, a resplendent Apparition! And could I now re-voice to human ears The wondrous things he spake—spake with a tongue Like those that, on the day of Pentecost, Flamed down on the first Christians, and from which His words came raying, each word like a star, Though with a subtle fusion flowing all, When uttered thus, into one luminous wave That broke on the soul’s ear, and breaking, chimed Such golden miracles of melodious sound As Beings attune whose breath of life is light, Not air impure, the vital draught of mor- tals! —Could I re-voice those oracles—O then Should all who heard, in utterance jubilant, Conjoin a symphony in words like these:

“

Nations no more shall trade in war; no more Shall banish charity from the homes of earth; But all mankind, together knit in love, Shall henceforth move in concord with all truth, All excellence, all benefit, all delight, All goodness; while, as from a well whose springs Are central as that peace of God round which All seculiar appearances revolve, And, so revolving, waste like summer clouds,— They drink eternal strength and godlike beauty From one sole Faith, whose waves of health shall flow As in the beginning—out of Paradise!”
