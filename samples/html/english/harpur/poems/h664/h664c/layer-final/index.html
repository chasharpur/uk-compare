A VISION OF AN ANGEL.

BY CHARLES HARPUR.

I had a vision of an Angel, who Within the circle of my consciousness Stood, where its dim verge met the dark of Sleep Even as the Moon, on the horizon, stands Within the dream-gaze of the slumbering Earth— Stood shadowing his snowy beauty so As but the more confirmed its glorious grace And utter loveliness of lineament, With ample-folding wings, whose Paradise plumes Glowed with such hues as, still intenselier, mix In the bright borders of an evening cloud. So stood He, a resplendent Apparition! And could I now revoice to human ears The wondrous things He spake,—spake with a tongue That seemed itself all spirit, and from which His words came raying, each one like a star, Through fusing all into one luminous wave, And flowing with a golden cadence, such As Beings attune who breathe not air, but light:— Could I revoice those oracles—O then Should all who heard, in utterance jubilant, Conjoin a symphony in words like these: “Nations no more shall trade in war, no more Shall banish charity from the homes of earth; But all mankind, together knit in love, Shall henceforth move in concord with all Truth, All Goodness; while, as from a well, they drink Eternal beauty from one heavenly Faith.”

November, 1859.
