TWO SONNETS ADDRESSED TO JAMES MACARTHUR, ESQ., OF CAMDEN.

NO. I.

[SONNET ADDRESSED TO JAMES MACARTHUR, ESQ. (1)]

I give thee credit for an honest aim, That thou, with some few more, didst interpose Between this country and its maniac foes, The influence of thy fair untarnished name.— The foulness of their principles might shame, If he stood forth alone, the worst of those Who now, a grasping horde, fiercely oppose All who would urge ‘gainst their’s, the public claim.— They seek to rob the public, and to gain For them and theirs, this goodly continent: Selfish, disloyal, and oppressive, bent By any means their object to attain; Of faith and freedom do they loudly rave, Yet strive to plunder, trample, and enslave.
