V.

The Aged Bard.

The mirky cluds thrang drizzlin’ ower, The sleety blast keeps thuddin’ by, Whiles he mann cross the lanely muir, Yon agëd Bard wi’ tearfu’ eye. His lang locks a’ are wintry white, And time as bleacht his saul as weel; Yet ance that weary wandering wight Was Scotia’s bonniest minstrel chiel.

And weel I min’ when he lang syne, Wham noo their cauld neglec’ sae wrangs, In Valor’s tower and Beauty’s bower, Was laureled for his fiery sangs: But noo, even when they luve his page, The Bard himsel’s let drift awa’, To wander in his weary age “Wi’ naething but a wreath of snaw.”
