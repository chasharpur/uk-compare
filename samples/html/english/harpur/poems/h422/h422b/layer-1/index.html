Rosa’s Evening Society.

As one who [unclear] Arabian wilderness Hath toiled a long and spirit-wearying space, And now, athirst, desires the gleaming face Of one known spring,—and ’mid the shadowless And fiery sand, rememb’ringly doth bless The sun-proof shelter of the storied race Of palms that there with leafy arms, embrace Each other in their verdant gracefulness: So ’mid the sordid cares it proudly brooks, And the dull daily tedium that it knows, /Despite consolatories found in books/ My spirit panteth for the evening’s close, When it may drink clear welcome from Her looks, And in the shadow of Her grace repose.
