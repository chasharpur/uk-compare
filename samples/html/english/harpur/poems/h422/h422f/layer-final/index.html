XIV.

To ————.

As one who o’er the Arabian wilderness Hath toiled a long, long, spirit-wearying space, Desireth in his thirst the gleaming face Of one known Spring;—and ’mid the shadowless And fiery sand doth then in memory bless The sunproof shelter of its storied race Of palm trees that, with plume-like arms, embrace Each one the other in green gracefulness: So ’mid the barren cares it proudly brooks, And the dull daily tedium that it knows, (Despite consolatories found in books) My nature panteth for the week’s set close, When it may drink clear welcome from thy looks, And in the shadow of thy grace repose.

Euroma 1867
