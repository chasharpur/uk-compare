[UNTITLED FRAGMENT]

Methinks I know the signs of passion well. A sleep-lidded eye——————————— ——————fingers tremulous, As they were playing ‘Love amongst the Roses’ On an imaginary heart-shaped lyre; A lazily luxurious gait;—when all These signs, and, haply, one or two besides, Concur in a girl’s courage,—why, she’s bound By some one, some how, and her maiden wings May flutter mightily, but they are free For actual flight no longer. But the love You speak of,—cold love, is a vacilous thing— Insipid as stale broth or lukewarm tea, Or ale that’s prickt—a beggarly potation! Love that’s unvaliant for its object, is Fate’s coward—
