TO THE REV. JOHN SAUNDERS ON HIS DEPARTURE FOR ENGLAND IN 1848.

If a large love of the whole human race, With charity that hopeth a meet cure For its worst evils, indicates the grace Of goodness, thine is such as will endure. And if our prayers to ‘stablish what is pure— If faithful—waste not in the voids of space, But Godward rising—pierce heaven’s starry face, Thine have been heard, and thy reward is sure. Farewell. This people might be well content To part with much besides, if so, it might Keep burning through its moral gloom, unblent With earthlier ardours perilous though bright, Thy steady flame of purpose in the right— Thy eloquent fervour kindling wise intent.

CHARLES HARPUR.
