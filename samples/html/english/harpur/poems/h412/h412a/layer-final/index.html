V.

Purse Display.

A special though inflated nurse Of Meanness, is the Pride of Purse.

He who is fond of making much display of his mere money, is mostly a man himself who esteems nothing else. If not God in his eyes, it is at least the grace of God. Hence with such a fellow, money is merit, and the want of it unworthiness. And no wonder: for it is the true gauge of his own significance, and without it hewere—nothing.
