764

Purse Display.

The special, though inflated nurse Of meanness, is the pride of purse.

He who is fond of making much display of his mere money, is mostly a man himself who esteems nothing else. If not God in his eyes, it is at least the grace of God. Hence, with such a fellow, money is merit, and the want of it unworthiness. And no wonder; for it is the true guage of his own significance, and without it he were nothing.
