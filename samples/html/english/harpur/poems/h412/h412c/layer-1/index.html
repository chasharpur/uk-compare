430

Purse Pride.

There is no trait, which telleth worse Of meanness than the pride of purse; And he that’s boastful of his cash Has mostly nothing else to flash: Wherefore with him it stands for wit, And merit is twin-born with it: Nay, if not God with such a beast, It is the grace of God at least. Nor wonder! In the creature’s pence Lies all his weight and influence, And without money, he would be An absolute non-entity: Except so far a[s] through disease He might be like a mite in cheese
