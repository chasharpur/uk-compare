A LOVE PERPLEXITY.

In vain, my Charmer, words I’d find To paint thy person and thy mind; For things so generous and fair Before united never were.

Thy precious-beaming eyes are bright With something higher than mental light! A nameless effluence of soul, Quickening—but quickening to control, The thrilling wish and swimming sense That herald Passion’s eloquence: A spiritual ray divine, Prompting—but prompting to refine, The tender joy and fervent thought From Love’s ethereal spirit caught.

Ho

w dull it were to call thee gay And genial as the smiles of Day! How thought-cold to declare thee bright And beauteous as the stars of night! Fresh as the dewy flowers that dress The earth in Nature’s healthfulness— Nay, as were those, with golden eyes That gazed at Eve in Paradise! Sweet as a dream from Heaven might be, And pure as Angel purity!

Ho

w dull—how cold indeed! For who, Thus sitting at thy side, could view Those soul-sweet eyes! that love-sweet smile! Then praise Thee in such beaten style?
