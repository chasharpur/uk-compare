15

Morning Love

What now in all the world might move My wearied being to the core, As once could love—that morning love Which I alas! may feel no more; When Dora, fonder for the theft, In haste would meet him—soon to part Which backward looks that ray-like left Their glances in my heart.

Her presence made all nature bright, And all within, and all without; Or absent, she was one delight To dre[a]m ten thousand things about! Alike to me in shine or shower The day and night away might roll, While the sweet spirit of my power Was reigning in my soul.

Ah what in all the world might move My wearied being to the core, As could that love—the morning love Which I, alas! may feel no more? 16 The mountain measure of its might— Its joy so ocean-like and vast, Can now be but a thought delight— A Vision of the past!
