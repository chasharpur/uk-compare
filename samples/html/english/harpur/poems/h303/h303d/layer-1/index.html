102 Finally Copied

Love in the Past.

I.

What now in all the world might move My wearied being to the core, As once could Love—that morning love Which I, alas! may feel no more; When Dora, fonder for the theft, Would meet me—hurriedly to part, With backward raying looks that left A sunshine in my heart?

Her presence made all nature bright, All time unshadowed with a doubt; Or absent, she was one delight To dream ten thousand things about! Alike to me in shine or shower The day and night away might roll, While the sweet spirit of her power Was regnant in my soul.

Ah! what in all the world might move My wearied being to the core, As could that Love—the morning love Which I, alas! may feel no more? The mountain measure of its might, Its joy so ocean-like and vast, Can now be but a thought delight— A Vision of the Past.
