Virginal Love. X

I love him so, That though his face I ne’er might see, In the assurance that he so loved me, This happy heart would glow With pulses sweeter than the sweetest be That colder ones can know.

I love him so, That to my thought ‘twere sweet to sleep Even in death, believing he would keep, With solemn step and slow, In sabbath memory my grave, and weep For her who slept below.

I love him so, That all desires when he is by Shrink even from the import of a sigh; As flowers unseen that grow, Being mute, must so remain; as in the sky Are stars that none may know.
