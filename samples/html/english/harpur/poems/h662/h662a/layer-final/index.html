LITERARY REGISTER.

I LOVE HIM SO.

I love Him so, That, though his Face I ne’er might see, In the assurance that he loved me, My virgin Heart would glow With pulses sweeter than the sweetest be Which colder Ones can know.

I love Him so, That to my Thought, ‘t were sweet to sleep Even in Death, believing he would keep, With solemn steps and slow, In Sabbath Memory my Grave, and weep For Her who slept below.

I love Him so, That all desires, when he is by, Shrink even from the import of a sigh: As Flowers unseen that grow, Being mute, must so remain; as in Sky Are Stars which none may know.

CHARLES HARPUR.
