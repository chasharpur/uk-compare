HAYNAU IN DEATH.

A SONNET BY CHARLES HARPUR.

The tiger-man is dead—Haynau accurst! The heart that burst as with hell-fire is cold! Most devil-like of the devilish creatures nurst In the foul lap of Despotism old, Community with whom now must thou hold? With blood-damned Underlings—thyself the worst? What, must thou herd with such? thou who coulds’t burst Through Brescia so conqueror-like and bold! Dids’t think thy own life wasted to a span When heaping under the unsparing sword Thy victims? or when fire and slaughter ran Through Hungary at thy commanding word? Thou peerless Murderer in a murderous horde! Thou Fathomer of the merciless in man!
