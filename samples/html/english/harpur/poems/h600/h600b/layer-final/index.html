VIII

To a Lady on the death of her child.

My heart aches, hearing thou has lost thy child, So well I feel what such a loss must be, 469 And for how long Affection, worn and wild, Shall mourn her severance from a Creature she Did love so largely—yea, so utterly! And how too, all home objects that once smiled In union with that love, are now all isled In loneness, as in a waste-moaning sea. Still, though thus hurt, let us no less adore God’s ways. Our loss is only a remove Of the Belovéd to some heavenly shore, And whither her spirit, like a mystic dove, Hath trailed memorial clues: the strength of Love Might teach us this much were it all our lore.
