TO MARY.

Could I impose a heart on thee That, ah! too much before hath loved— Oft frantic thro’ the memory Of errors, world-reproved, And warm no more with truth divine— Then, Mary! then this heart were thine.

Yet I was once a child of truth, Yea, honest as a God might be— And nearly, at that time of youth, As innocent as thee! But passion’s fire, and reason’s frost, Have ate away my soul almost!

And why? long since (I breathe no name) I loved a maid, whose charms might be Worthy of Argive Hellen’s fame, And twice as vile as she! She stung me—and I, hope-despoiled, Waywardly-daring grew, and wild.

Few love me! and ‘twere costly joy Now, in the day of darkened fame, To gather up old feelings by A new and sacred flame, Such as thy love—to find the pride Of a contemning world my bride.

But this is selfish all! and weak Are these, thy tears—and that, thy sigh! Think not evasively I speak! Oh! were I sure that I Could love thee, as thou shoulds’t be loved, I would not stand thus self-reproved.

Yet, all this told, I feel a sweet Assurance smooth my lightened brow, That it were still salvation meet Coulds’t thou but love me now— That, spite of fate, my soul were given Again to love, and truth, and heaven!

C. H.
