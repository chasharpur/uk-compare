The Maiden I left behind me.X

I wandered east, I wandered west, For long years, hoping yearly That fortune at last would give me rest With the One I loved most dearly: Hoping ever it might be so, As the spirit of fancy inclined me, So purely my heart’s queen long, long ago Was the Maiden I left behind me.

To wander aye would seem my lot, To wander aye, though weary; Striving in vain to compass what My spirit hath prized most dearly: 64 No longer I deem it may be so, For fancy no longer can blind me— Ah, well-a-day for the long, long ago, And the Girl I left behind me!

It may be, had we mated been, And I had never wandered, The Eden of Love ere this had seen Its beauty and bloom all squandered! But Oh! in my heart, a vacant ake At thought of the past will remind me How lonely it is for the sad sweet sake Of the Girl I left behind me.
