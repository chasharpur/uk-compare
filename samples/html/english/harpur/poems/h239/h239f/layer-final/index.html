III.

[THE GIRL I LEFT BEHIND ME]

I wandered east, I wandered west For long years, hoping yearly, That Fortune at last would give me rest, With the One I loved most dearly: Hoping ever it might be so, As the spirit of fancy inclined me, 106 So purely my heart’s queen long, long ago Was the Maiden I left behind me.

To wander aye would seem my lot, To wander aye, though weary, Striving in vain to compass what My spirit hath prized most dearly: No longer I deem it may be so, For fancy no longer can blind me— Ah! well-a-day, for the long, long ago And the Maiden I left behind me.

It may be, had we mated been, And I had never wandered, The Eden of Love ere this had seen Its beauty and bloom all squandered! But oh! in my heart, a vacant ache At thought of the Past will remind me, How lonely it is for the sad sweet sake Of that Maiden I left behind me.
