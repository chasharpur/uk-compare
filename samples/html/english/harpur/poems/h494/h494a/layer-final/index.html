SELF-DEPENDENCE.

I who write this have practised what I preach, Have the hard road of Self-Dependence trod, And all who truest herohood would reach Must in themselves trust only—under God. And what a glorious thing is Labour! which For manly freedom manfully undergone, O’er all detraction (while it maketh rich) Will give us victory though we stand alone. Depend not upon friends! I once believed In many, and I yet believe in one: But then why need we risk our being deceived— Why trust until our social faith’s undone? Yet though thus self-dependent, tow’rds our kind Still should we bear a just and generous mind.
