(THIRD PAPER.) VI.

That peculiar and exalted beauty which results mainly from a spiritual impassionment of the material world— that visible enrichment of things which is in its cause invisible—is the theme and glory of the ensuing lyric. Considered essentially, it is clearly either a spontaneous effluence, or a prepared and sublimated influence, of the mind, reflecting from its objects; and is to the common aspects of Nature and the material order of the world, what lively emotions are to the human countenance, and the impulses of bright and graceful feelings, to all the airs and attitudes of an intelligent organism. It is the Beautiful enhanced by a transfusion of the Feeling- ful;—a creation of delight out of that which is surpass- ing the fruitions of that which was, and which, if com- mitted to some form or channel of art, stands out thenceforth as a detached spiritualised substance,—a “joy for ever” in itself,—and is so far independent of its origin as to lean upon it only through a critical reference.

LOVE THE EXALTER OF NATURE.

Off from all Being, as a worn-out part, The husk of time’s indifference is hurled, And the brute earth becomes a dreaming heart, When Love is born into the world.

The Old hath passed away, and in the New Nothing is undelightful, nothing mean, But all life passions into beauty through A purple light before unseen.

A sudden concorn runs all Nature’s round— Runs through the utterance of all vocal things: Yea, Music, wheresoever there is sound, Doth gift it with her golden wings.

And wheresoever our impassioned looks Are thrown abroad, there’s Picture: objects dull To uncharmed eyes, together flow as brooks Into the world-wide Beautiful.

And Poesy no longer latent lies, But all the air breathes her ethereal writ, And all the hues of the diurnal skies Are cloud-sung peans exquisite.

For from all Being, as a worn-out part, The screen of old indifference is hurled, And the brute Earth becomes a dreaming heart When Love is born into the world.
