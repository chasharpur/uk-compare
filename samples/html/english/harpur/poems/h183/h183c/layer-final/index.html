Hybrid Freedom

Freedom that’s half a fact and half a fiction, What is it but a sorry contradiction? Free men were sacred men; not such as might Be ridden down by whom they paid to fight Their foes—not them. Yet such might be th’ event In any—yea, the finest Government Yet founded by the laggard mind of Man!— Whether monarchic or republican! But not in a true commonwealth, in which None were a vagabond because not rich; None were a rebel but the traitor wretch Who’d be, for hire, her liberty’s Jack Ketch! And all were tyrants who from power would keep The People, and be-law them into sheep. Pure Despotism’s honest at the least, Nor cloaks the hoof that intimates the Beast; But draws from reigning Power’s ascendant star The one sole sanction of its Jove-like Czar!
