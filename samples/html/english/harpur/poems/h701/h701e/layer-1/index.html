A Worldling.

Worldly prosperity is often less A proof of having than of wanting sense, And most is thwarted by the

noble

excess Of wit, love, valor, or benevolence. I know a bloodless hunks at whose expense No true thing thrives; yet is he, in the guess And careful feel of mere selfishness, More mathematic than a spider;—whence 229 Although in look unsightly as an elf, Nor favored otherwise, and aught but clever, Nothing but death can cut him short of pelf: The instinct of the brute may err; but never Might even passion from its object sever A thorough Worldling’s providence for self.
