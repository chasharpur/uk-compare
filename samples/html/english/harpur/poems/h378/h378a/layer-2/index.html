Small Pride.

If you’d long in state bide At Fizgig’s festive hall, Never mind his great pride, But be tender of his small.

And if your heart be some bit In Widow Blaze’s purse, Hint rather she’s a strumpet, Than that once she was a Nurse.

Note. I have hitherto alienated, from time to time, in my career of life, many of my professed well-wishers, —especially those of the ‘pon-my-honor gentility genus, by unwittingly treading upon the bunions of their small pride; which I mostly found to be infinitely more sensitive than their great;—if indeed, of the latter sort, they had any at all. Now having none about myself of the small Conventional stuff I speak of, /though not in the least deficient, I believe, in that higher self-valuation which constitutes our great pride/, I am heinously apt to overlook the possible possession of it by others. And in the effect of this habitual neglect of it in others,—namely, a certain straightforward and unmincing avowal 70a avowal of social impressions, lies the whole secret of my by-past want of success in general society: 69a : one of the million of matter I have since found out in my solitary enquiry into causes. But men will yet know me better—and then the character to which I refer will become an admitted merit and a social recommendation; and instead of being voted a fellow with too little respect for “facts & matter”, and all that I shall be universally countenanced as a very tolerable Bruin. Hitherto, however, it has stood in my way : 70b for what is so pestilently prevalent in world as petty pride? —a tremblingly sensitive petty pride? Your Lady Flamingo,—who may be a publicaness as well as a marchioness,—is “all alive” with it. Opine that she cheats at cards, or double chalks in the reckoning, and she may pass it off with a petulant “Well I’m sure!” or “You’re very complimentary” but innocently suppose that her silken finery has been scorned or that she has a sister a washerwoman,—and the offense is deadly. Your genuine Snob too,—and his name is Legion, and his grade of every sort, and his place everywhere;—he is ”all compact” of it. Pull his nose, and in process of time he may forgive you; but tell him, in the honesty of your belief, that it bears a striking family resemblance to the Pieman’s,—and he is your enemy for life.

P.S. The social significanc of the term Snob, in the peculiar sense in which it is used above has never been defined, perhaps, so tersely as it deserves to be. It indicates Figuratively, it signifies an ass that would look like a lion and means literally, any paltry human + starveling that would seem to be cutting a dash without the means or the soul for cutting anything but but a sorry figure and the most ridiculous capers: in which sense a lord may be a Snob, and his shoemaker not. A genuine Nobody of this kind, having to announce in the obituary of his Newspaper, the awfully accidental death of his only child, will make even it the occasion for tracing his descent, with painful minuteness, up to an apparent Somebody; though his family tree shall have produced but one such apple,—and though that too, in its native quality, shall have been little better than a crab.
