THE WORLD’S VICTIMS.

“They tell me He in riot now, For whom I would have died, Hath sunk all memory of the vow I made to be his Bride: But then the happy Past will swim Before mine eyes again; And one wild Thought of love and him Seems rooted in my brain.

“I would not trust him more, for nought Could mend his broken vow: Nay, were the False One hither brought, I would not name him now! And yet, from one long tearful gaze How might I then refrain, With this wild Thought of other days Thus rooted in my brain?

“He surely sometimes thinks of me? But can he love me yet?— Alas! this mood must sinful be— My part is to forget! I feel the bright deceit is o’er— That I have loved in vain! And yet this wild Regret the more Keeps rooted in my brain.”

Thus day by day a Maiden pale, And oft the long-long night, Was heard, in fond despair, to wail Her Lover’s broken plight: But ah! her cold and cruel Friends, Regardless of her woe, For base—for mean and selfish ends The Youth had slandered so.

And much they urged that Maiden wan, When now a year was past, To wed another—richer Man, And they prevailed at last: But then, to show how vile their part, Her Lover came again— Alas, it broke his manly heart! It turned her weary brain!

And then, in hopeless grief, away He wandered o’er the Sea; And soon at rest that pale Girl lay Beneath the Churchyard Tree. All heart-truth shall the World thus doom To ruin evermore? And Love’s most precious flowers but bloom To perish as before?
