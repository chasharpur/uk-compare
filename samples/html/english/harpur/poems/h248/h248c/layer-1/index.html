The Wrath of Love.

To have the heart misread by one Who is alone Love-thronëd there, Evokes a bitterness that none May utter for despair.

Till anger brings a fierce relief, And then our words flash forth amain— The lightnings of a stormy grief That thunders in the brain.

But soon this tempest of the mind Is hushed away by pleading fears; And if it leaves a cloud behind, That cloud dissolves in tears.
