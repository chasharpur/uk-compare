[LOVE THE IDEALISER 4]

IV.

To have the heart misread by One Who is alone entreasured there, Evokes a bitterness that none May utter for despair.

Till anger brings a fierce relief, And then wild words flash forth amain— The lightnings of a stormy grief That thunders in the brain.

But soon this tempest of the mind Is hushed away by pleading fears, And if it leaves a cloud behind, That cloud dissolves in tears.

Till Love, self-reconciled, again Becomes the exalter of our life, Educing pèrfumes out of pain— And rainbows out of strife.

Whence rather should we find our part Through loving, in them both, I ween, 73 Than bear about with us a heart Where Love hath never been.

For none but natures cold and dry Could feel self-satisfied, to know Exemption from them in the high, By resting in the low.
