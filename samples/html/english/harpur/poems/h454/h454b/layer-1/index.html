A Regret.

There’s a Regret that ever from my heart Melts forth a dirgy sweetness, like a rain 123 Of death-ward love; that ever in my brain Uttereth such tones as seem a foregone part Of all the natural harmonies that start Into the dayspring, when some rarest view Unveileth its Tempèan grace anew To meet the sun—the great world’s fervent heart! ‘Tis that, though living in his tuneful day, My boyhood might not win the gentle smile, Nor drink the praise of Shelley; that away His soul had journeyed, ere I might beguile In my warm youth, by some fraternal lay, One thought of his tow’rds this my native Isle.
