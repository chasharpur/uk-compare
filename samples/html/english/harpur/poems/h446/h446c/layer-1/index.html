[RECORDS OF A POET’S LOVE XXIX]

There is a pain of poesy in my brain, And ever in my breast a vacant ake, As though my heart were dead. In vain I make My voice go forth in melody; in vain I roam for solace over hill and plain, Or by wild streams;—whichever way I take, There woful Memory doth her wild locks shake, Wailing for Passion by the dread world slain! For she is lost—the Nora of my song! She whom it lifted the blue heaven above, Even to the brightest of the starry throng, Hath with removal, let her faith remove! Nor less do I regret the cruel wrong That Time hath done to poesy through Love!
