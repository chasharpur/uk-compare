VIII.

There’s a worn sense of failure in my brain, And ever in my breast a vacant ache, As if my heart were dead. In vain I make My voice go forth in melody; in vain I roam for solace over hill & plain, Or by wate;—whatever way I take There woful Memory doth her wild locks shake, Wailing for Passion by the dread world slain! For she is lost—the Hesper of my song! She whom it lifted the base earth above Even to the brightest of heaven’s starry throng, Hath, with removal, let her faith remove! Ahme! ah me. the cruel, cruel wrong That hath been done to Poësy through love!

1847
