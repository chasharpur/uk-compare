XXIX.

The Regret.

There is a pain of poesie in my brain, And ever in my breast a vacant ake, As though my heart were dead. In vain I make My voice go forth in melody; in vain I roam for solace over hill or plain, Or by the streams;—whichever way I take, There woeful Memory only is awake, Wailing for Passion by the cold world slain! For she is lost—the Rosa of my song! She that I lifted the blue heaven above Even to the brightest of the starry throng, Hath “bent with the remover to remove”! Nor less do I regret the cruel wrong That Time hath done to Poesie through Love.
