Finale to the Love Sonnets

There is a pain of posie in my brain, And ever in my breast a vacant ake, As though my heart were dead. In vain I make My voice go forth in melody; in vain I roam for solace over hill and plain, Or by the stream;—whichever way I take, There woeful Memory only is awake, Talking of Passion by the cold world slain! For she is lost—the Rosa of my song!— She that I lifted the blue heavens above Even to the brightest of the starry throng,※ Hath “but with the remover to remove”! Yet her I now regret not—but the wrong Chill Time hath done to Poesie & Love.
