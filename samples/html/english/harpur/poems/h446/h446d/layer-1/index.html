X XXIX.

[RECORDS OF A POET’S LOVE XXIX]

There’s a vague pain of poësy in my brain, And ever in my breast a vacant ake, As though my heart were dead. In vain I make My voice go forth in melody; in vain I roam for solace over hill and plain, Or by wild streams;—whatever way I take, There woeful Memory doth her wild locks shake, Wailing for Passion by the dread world slain! For she is lost—the Nora of my song! She whom it lifted the base earth above Even to the brightest of heaven’s starry throng, Hath, with removal, let her faith remove! Ah, me! ah, me! the cruel, cruel wrong That Time hath done to Poesy and Love!
