A SUPERNATURALISED BATTLE PIECE FROM HOMER.

BY CHARLES HARPUR.

Up he rose in the might of his soul, Achilles, the favored of Heaven, And azure-eyed Pallas then slung The broad shield of Jove o’er his shoulders; And she put round about his bold head A golden mist, lightning with splendour— Like a cloud of the morning inflamed With the mountain-top glory of sunrise: And as, when a smoke goeth up From a town in some outlying island, Whose foes, from their own stronger hold Pouring out, have the day through assaulted, So soon as the set sun hath drawn Down with him the grey strip of twilight, How thickly the nimble-tongued fires Are seen to rush up through the darknes, Making a glow in the sky, And pathing with light the dim waters, Where, haply, some help-bringing chief Comes ploughing the deep with his galleys— So, up from the head of Achilles Went glowing aloft an effulgence.

On the mound of the trench he stood forth Beyond the camp wall, though still keeping Aloof from the host of the Greeks, Being held by the hest of his mother; And there, standing up like a tower, In his terrible ardour he shouted! And his shout, as it wrathfully rang O’er the slaughter-gored fronts of the battle, Minerva enforced with a cry That fell as if shrieked out of Heaven! And lo, as that clear-ringing voice Tore out, like the blare of a trumpet When it threatens the gates of a town Which implacable foemen beleaguer, In the host of the Trojans there rose A wild-multitudinous tumult! 5 And when, too, that brazen cry fell Among them, fell with it confusion, And a terror that broke their array, And a horror that shuddered within them,— Their legions deforming, as clouds Are torn by the winds of a tempest! For even the haughty-necked steeds Swerved, huddled, and backed on their haunches, Foreseeing full well, as it seemed, Calamity clothed in the omen! And the hearts of the charioteers Were smitten, as still they kept staring At the fire on the terrible head Of the God-like great-minded one burning, As Pallas ordained—raying forth With an active and ominous glory!

Thus thrice o’er the trench did he shout, And the Trojans as oft, with their allies, Were whirled into dust-clouded heaps And rolled back in utter disorder: So that twelve of their captains renowned In the rout and astonishment perished, Crushed dead by the jambing of wheels And under the wrecks of their chariots.
