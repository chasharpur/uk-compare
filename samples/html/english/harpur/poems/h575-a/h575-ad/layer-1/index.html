A Supernaturalised Battle-Piece from the XVIII Iliad.

I.

As Paraphrased in dactylic hexameters.

Up he rose in the might of his soul, Achilles, the favored of heaven, And azure-eyed Pallas then slung the broad shield of Jove o’er his shoulders; And she put round about his bold head a golden mist, lightning with splendor— Like a cloud of the morning inflamed with the mountain-top glory of sunrise. As when a great smoke goeth up from a town in some outlying island, While its foes, from their own stronger hold pouring out, all the day long assault it, So soon as the set sun hath drawn down with him the grey strip of twilight, How thickly the nimble-tongues fires are seen to rush up in the darkness, Making a glow in the sky, and pathing with light the dim waters, Where haply some help-bringing Chief comes ploughing the deep with his galleys— So up from the head of Achilles went glowing aloft an effulgence.

On the trench-mound stood he forth, beyond the camp-wall, though still keeping Aloof from the host of the Greeks, being held by the hest of his mother; And there, standing up like a tower, in his terrible ardour he shouted! And his shout, as it wrathfully rang along the gored fronts of the battle, Minerva enforced with a cry, that fell as if shrieked out of heaven! And lo, as that clear-ringing voice tore out like the blare of a trumpet, When it threatens the gates of a town which implacable foemen beleaguer, There rose in the host of the Trojans a wild multitudinous tumult! And when too her cry fell amongst them, fell with it confusion, And terror that broke their array, and horror that shuddered within them, Their legions deforming, as clouds are torn by the winds of a tempest! And even the proud steeds swerved, and huddled, and backed on their haunches, 90 Foreseeming full well, as it seemed, calamity clothed in the omen! And the hearts of the charioteers were smitten with panic, while staring At the fire on the terrible head of the God-like great-minded One burning,— As Pallas-ordained—raying forth with an active and ominous glory!

Thus thrice, o’er the trench, did he shout, and, with their allies, were the Trojans Whirled as oft into dust-clouded heaps, and rolled back in utter disorder: So that twelve of their valliantest captains in the rout and astonishment perished, Crushed dead by the jambing of wheels and under the wrecks of their chariots.
