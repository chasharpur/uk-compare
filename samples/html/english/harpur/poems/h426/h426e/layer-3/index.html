[RECORDS OF A POET’S LOVE IX]

In sleep my brain was with a sweet pang wrung, And straight, I seemed to shoot in spirit away Into some world whereon a lovelier day, Than ours, glowed purpling amid arbors, hung With star-bright fruit; and where bird-spirits sung, Unceasingly, a bliss-inspiring lay; While a stream of glory in its play, Chimed to it, as with many a mellow tongue. I seemed alone;—when from a bowery place A silver-wingèd Shape came moving fast, And as I looked on its approaching face, Methought some thrilling memory of it passed Into my soul—and then, that wingéd Grace Was Nora! smiling as I saw her last.
