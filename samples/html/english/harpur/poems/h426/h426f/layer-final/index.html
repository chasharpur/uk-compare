VII.

[RECORDS OF A POET’S LOVE IX]

I[n] sleep my brain was with a sweet pang wrung, And straight I seemed to shoot in spirit away Into some world whereon a lovelier day Than ours glowed purpling amid arbours, hung With star-bright fruit; and where bird-spirits sung, Unceasingly, a bliss-inspiring lay, While streams of living splendour in their play Chimed to it, as with many a mellow tongue. I seemed alone;—when from a bowery place A silver-winged shape came moving fast, And as I looked on its approaching face, Methought some thrilling memory of it passed Over my soul:—and then, that winged grace Was Nora! smiling as I saw her last.
