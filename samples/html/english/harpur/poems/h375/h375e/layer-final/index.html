XLV.

THE PATHETIC.

Mere art can make a poem smooth as satin, But compass feeling can mere art in no man, Chiefly because its tears are brought too pat in, And not as if they from a real wo ran: See, for example, elegies in Latin Written by any but an ancient Ro-man, What bits of ice they are, however sung; For Art,—not Feeling,—mourns in a dead Tongue[.]
