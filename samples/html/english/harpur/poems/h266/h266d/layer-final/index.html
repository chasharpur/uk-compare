Doubts.

O say, if into sudden storm Some doom-cloud we in vain would shun, Should break, and Love’s bright world deform, His and thy Poet leaving, one Scorning and scorned of heartless men;— Belovëd, would you love me then?

Stung by the world’s eternal guile, Should grim defiance—grim and strong, Plant on my cheek its bitter smile, To waste and whiten it so long, That it might freshen ne’er agen;— Belovëd, would you love me then?

Should years of gainless absence yet Wear o’er us, and, as heaven vast, 292 Such breadths of brine be twixt us set, That even Hope might ask at last: How can you ever meet agen?— Belovëd, would you love me then?
