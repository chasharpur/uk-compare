Lyrics.

No Mean Dwelling.

I envy no greatness, I bow to no pride, I am lord of what none can be lord of beside, Though, lonely and landless, I dwell in no more Than a Lodge where the wild woods hang shadowing o’er.

And Love, if heroic, right healthful and strong, Like the passion whose amaranth bloometh in song, As bees hive their honey, it sweetness might store In a Lodge where the wild woods hang shadowing o’er.

And a Poet, like Shakspere, the heart-seer profound, Or like Milton, whose genius but heaven could bound, 92 Might build up his judgment and chasten his lore In a Lodge where the wild woods hang shadowing o’er.

For where there’s a free heart, blood royal is there, And true love’s a honey-bee everywhere; While the Muse never heeds how we house what she gives, Since no Dwelling is mean where a thorough man lives.
