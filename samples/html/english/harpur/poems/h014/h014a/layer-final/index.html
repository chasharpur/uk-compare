THE APPLE OF LIFE.

That bright dream of passion which only may be In life’s rosy morning poured over the brain, When once it dissolves, as a rainbow might flee, Or withdraws, as the white moon sinks over the main— Oh, the apple of life has been plucked from the tree, And the leaves, the leaves only remain.

‘Tis true we may yet with the joyous and free Be numbered in vanity’s fluttering train; The bright things of nature yet precious may be, And the load-star of beauty attract us again— Yet the apple of life has been plucked from the tree, And the fading leaves only remain.

But when in the world’s cruel struggles we see Our hopes trodden down like the grass of the plain, Oh then—as the banished gaze homeward—of thee, Thou bright dream, in despair, we are dreaming again— For the apple of life has been plucked from the tree, And the bare branches only remain.

Charles Harpur.
