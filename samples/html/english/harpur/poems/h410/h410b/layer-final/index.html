PSALM CXXXI.

O Lord, no more my heart is proud, No longer lofty are mine eyes, Nor do I now in things too high My spirit exercise: But henceforth all my hope shall be, And all my trust,—in only Thee.

From all that once disquieted My froward soul, with pride beguiled, Have I not wean’d it now, O Lord:— Yea, wean’d it—like a child? And henceforth all my hope shall be, And all my trust,—in only Thee:
