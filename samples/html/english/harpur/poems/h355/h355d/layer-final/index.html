XLIII.

On the disrespect which certain of our Legislators are in the unseemly habit of showing towards Doctor Lang.

I see the “old man eloquent” arise To strip some ‘job’ of its fallacious hue, With motives nobly naked as the skies When not a cloudlet films their summer blue. ‘Tis done:—and straightway a most graceless crew Led by snob-tory Buzfus, lift their cries And cavils, venomed with exploded lies, Or imputations not a whit more true! So have I seen an eagle with strong wings 52 Come sweeping o’er the wood;—when straight a flight Of chattering pies sent up their jargonings, And hustled round him in ludicrous spite, While he in conscious majesty and might Moved on, unruffled by the paltry things.
