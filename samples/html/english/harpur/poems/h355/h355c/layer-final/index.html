XXIX. X

On the Disrespect which certain of our Legislators are in the brave and seemly habit of evincing towards Dr Lang.

I see the “old man eloquent” arise To strip some job of its fallacious hue, With motives nobly naked as the skies When not a cloudlet films their summer blue. 261 ‘Tis done:—and straightway a most graceless crew Led by snob-tory Buzzfuzz, lift their cries And cavils venomed with exploded lies, Or imputations not a whit more true. So have I seen an eagle with strong wings Come sweeping o’er the wood;—when straight a flight Of chattering pies sent up their jargonings, And hustled round him in ludicrous spite, While he in conscious majesty and might Moved on, unruffled by the paltry things.
