Similes from Homer.

I.

As when upon the loud-resounding shore The long sea-swells, beneath the north-west wind Which sets them into motion, are impelled In mountainous successions, ridge on ridge, With a continuous bursting: from afar First come they, rolling in their bulk aloft, Till, dashing full upon the quaking land, They break with a dread roar; nor yet retire, But swell and eddy round the craggy bluffs Spitting their white foam wide about the while: So thick the Grecian phalanxes moved on, Rank after rank, incessant, to the fight.
