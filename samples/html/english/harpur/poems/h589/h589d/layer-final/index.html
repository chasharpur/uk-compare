Original Poetry.

A Thought Sting.

By the Late Chas. Harpur.

Ah me!—but in this sunset hour, One sad thought with its serious sting So pricks me,—that all outward power For pleasure, can small pleasure bring.

How full of God those evening skies Arranged in utter loveliness: I say—but think how many eyes Are wet, hearts aching none the less.

Nay, hearts are aching, eyes are wet The more that they so richly glow, Since in the past some glory set To leave them in the dark of woe:

To leave them dark, and such a tinge O’er every after sunset throw, That it should only seem to fringe The pall of a dead Long Ago.

Ah! well a-day: but so it is; To think is almost to despair! And pain and loss we cannot miss, And sorrow—it is everywhere.
