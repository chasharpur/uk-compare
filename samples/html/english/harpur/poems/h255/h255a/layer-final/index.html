II.

LUTHER AND KNOX.

There are two kinds of moral heroes. One Consists in suffering passively all wrong For Truth’s great sake,—yea, all that can be done To blame it; or in carrying what’s begun By hardier spirits persuasively along; But should the tide turn, all its farther skill Is to die well. The other, in a strong Self-sanctioning sublimity of will, Prompt to besiege the error it would kill, The bondage it would break with battering shocks, As ocean’s mighty waves assault the shore; Nor less firm to withstand the refluent roar Of popular remorse, than reef-based rocks: And of this last kind Luther was—and Knox.
