POETRY.

DELIA.

From where the streaky gates of light unfold, The planet of the morn approaching steals In silent majesty:—his shades uproll’d, Night, like a wounded Ethiope, ‘frighted reels Before her conquering march. The first stars now Grow sick with envy, and fast melt away In the superior glories of her ray. As she among the stars, so, Delia, thou Compared among the daughters stand’st; and they Thus grow diminished in thy beauty’s way; When, at the bidding of thy smiles, each thought Of sadness, by the night of absence wrought, Departs as I behold thee! Yea, thou art The morning of my soul—the Aurora of my heart.

C. HARPUR.
