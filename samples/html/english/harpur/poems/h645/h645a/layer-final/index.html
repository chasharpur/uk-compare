The True poise of Manhood.

Trust not too much in him whose cold clear light Comes sharply shining only through his reason; No[r] him whose mental rampires are so slight That he mi[s]deem all self reliance treason:

Though one appear to penetrate the core Of Nature, with the shaft of thought severe; And though the other like an angel soar Through the fine element of feeling’s sphere:

For one might b[u]y or sell you did it fit Some theory to do so; and the other, To purge the earth of all unholy wit, Might burn you at a stake—were you his brother

Then choose we for our Seer, Then choose we for our Seer,—him only, who In thinking widely, feeleth deeply too.
