II.

A WINTER MORNING.

It is Morn—and the frost bleaching hills are all white, Like the bones of a summer world dead; And the ice-crusted waters blink blind in the light, Like the eyes in a sightless man’s head.

And the birds, they are mute, like emotions fore- done, When Love wanders forth an outcast; And yon willow’s locks hang like the thin air of one Who stands lost in some dream of the past:

And the old hollow trees—ah! how they must ache With the cold of a morning like this; Incoiled by the blast, as dead cold, like a snake, It strains through the woods with a hiss.
