III

To the Same, On his recovery from sickness.

My manikin! my wilful, wayward pet! My own wild dark-eyed Boy!—at once my care, My happiness, my dearest hope,—and yet My one great fear! God knows I would not spare The most self-flattering possession fair I hold beside thee, to avert the threat Of all such evil from thy life as met Mine in its morning—stript to fortune bare! Not to be greater in the realm of verse Than Homer, or than Milton, greater still, Would I forego thee, or have Death disperse Thy young bright being like a squandered rill! Not to have Fortune waiting on my will— Not to be Regent of the universe!
