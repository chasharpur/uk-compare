To Washington. (20 months old.)

My mannikin! my wilful, wayward pet! My own wild dark-eyed Boy! at once my care And happiness;—my dearest hope, and yet My great, great fear! God knows I would not spare The most self-flattering possession fair I hold beside thee, to avert the threat Of all such evil from thy life, as met Mine in its morning,—more than Peace could bear. Not to be greater in the realm of Verse Than Homer, or than Shakespear, greater still, Would I forego thee, or have Death disperse Thy young bright being, like a squandered rill:— Not to have Fortune waiting on my will— Not to be Emperor of the Universe!
