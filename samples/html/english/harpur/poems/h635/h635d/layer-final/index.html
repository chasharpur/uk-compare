XLVIII.

To Washington Harpur, Aged Two Years, Awhile after his recovery from sickness.

My manikin! my wilful, wayward pet! My own wild dark-eyed boy! at once my care, My happiness, my dearest hope,—and yet My great, great fear! God knows I would not spare The most self-flattering possession fair I hold besides thee, to avert the threat Of all such evil from thy life, as met Mine in its morning—stript to Fortune bare! Not to be greater in the realms of Verse Than Homer, or than Shakspear, greater still, 278 Would I forego thee, or have death disperse Thy young bright being, like a squandered rill! Not to have riches waiting on my will! Not to be Regent of the Universe!
