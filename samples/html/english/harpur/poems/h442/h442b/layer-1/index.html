[RECORDS OF A POET’S LOVE XXV]

How much, my Nora, in this world—how much Has thy surpassing faith made rich for me, Of what was once unprized? A nameless tree— A wilding flower—the merest tints that touch The common clouds—lit summits—verdant leas— With every woodland murmur, every song Of bird or brook, as heard to gush along In each fresh on-’scape of some bough-caught breeze, Have thence a charm to sweeten to the core, My being—quickening its capacity Of loving joy: such as was mine before Only in vision, and that floweth free From things thus frequent, now, because they store Some genial memory of love and thee.
