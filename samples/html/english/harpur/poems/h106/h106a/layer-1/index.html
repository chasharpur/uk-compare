Early Summer.

‘Tis the early summer season With its skies so clear and blue; And the wide warm fields are glad with corn As green as ever grew; And the hill-side growing wattles Are golden to the view; And the woods are snowed all over With the white cocatoo.

O there is a conscious smiling In a heaven so purely blue; And it must—it must be a felt joy That thus comes blooming through The great mother heart of Nature, When the golden year is new; And the woods are snowed all over By the white cocatoo.
