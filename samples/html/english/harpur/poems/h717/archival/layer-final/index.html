WOE IS ME.

As the moaning wild waves ever Fret around some lonely isle, There are griefs that no endeavour For a moment may beguile, Beating at my heart for ever— Beating at it now; Beating at my heart—and aching Upward to my brow.

Like the wild clouds flying over, High above all human reach, There are joys that I their lover Cannot even woo in speech! Flying o’er my head for ever— Flying o’er it now; Flying o’er my head—and shading With despair my brow.
