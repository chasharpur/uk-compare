[THE PLACID EVENING AND THE TWILIGHT GREY]

The placid Evening and the Twilight grey No[w] followed, pensive, the departed day. Repose diffused her reign o’er all the scene, Nor bird nor beast disturbed its calm serene, Save that sad Ph[i]lomela all night long From bowery shades pleased Silence with her song. Now glowed the zenith with unnumbered stars, And rolled the planets in their golden cars, Until the Moon, the queen of heaven confest, Bared in the brightening east her radiant breast, And, rising o’er the envious clouds to view, Her silver mantle o’er the darkness threw.
