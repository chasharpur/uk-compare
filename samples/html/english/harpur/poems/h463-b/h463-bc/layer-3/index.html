II

[RHYMED CRITICISMS III. MILTON:]

His Minor Poems

As through some stream’s most christal purity The virgin gold is seen below, we see Through the immortal Mask, as by it flows In lengthening music to its happy close, That chastity of feeling and of thought Which stored the youthful Poet as he wrought; With all the visionary affluence That tempered the stern grandeur of his sense.

Then in his Carol for the Virgin’s Son, When like a trumpet for some victory won ‘Tis sounding,—’mid the Shepherds placed, we mark Heaven’s high heraldry descend—and hark, While burning choirs, in star-like clusters, gem The midnight gloom that shroudeth Bethlehem, How swelling up in utterance divine Their tidings peal through each triumphant line! The theme is changed: wild lamentation loads The ear of Doom: for each Infernal God,— Now, from his throne pontific,—suddenly hurled, Foregoes, despairing, the re-conquered world; And hallward all, in the tumultuous verse, Like far off thunders heavily disperse.
