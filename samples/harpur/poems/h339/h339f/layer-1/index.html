XXI.

Old Billowy Hawksb’ry.

Old billowy Hawksb’ry, so bravely expanding, The same as of yore, as abounding and free! But alas! for that lone One beside thee now standing; How failure hath shrunk him since last he saw thee!Then 84 Then was glass’d in thy blue depth, untouch d by To-morrow, A vision-lit face with no hint of a furrow, But the fire-coultered plough of a wanderer’s sorrow Hath passed o’er his brow since he saw thee before!

And what did the Maiden he strayed by thy side with? She wedded another when darkened his day! And what of the Schoolmates he bathed in thy tide with? They too, have forgot him, or wandered away! Yet as shells breathe the tone of their once native ocean, How the life of the Past seems to stir in thy motion, Recalling a sense of his youthful devotion To Beauty and Friendship, when near thee before!

But back comes the cold pang that made his heart shiver, When his alienate home in the Past met his eye; Such hardship to him did it seem, though bright river, To feel like a stranger in passing it by! While he thought of fond words that within it sounded From welcoming Parents, as tow’rds them he bounded, With a Brother that now in Churchyard lies mounded, Side by side with his Sire,—since he saw thee before!

And alas! for the days that are gone, he cried lowly, With all that they yielded, and promised as well; Since feelings and hopes that once made yon roof holy, Have now such a mournful home-story to tell! Then he plucked from a tree he had planted, this blossom, And laid it away, like a child, in his bosom, His eyes ached—and lo, he hath come to repose ‘em On something unchanged since he saw thee before.
