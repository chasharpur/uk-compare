Old Billowy Hawkesb’ry.

Old billowy Hawkesb’ry, I see thee expanding The same as of yore, as abounding and free! But alas! for the lone one beside thee now standing,— How failure hath shrunk him since last he saw thee! Then was glassed in thy blue depth, untouched by To-morrow A vision-lit face with no hint of a furrow, But the fire-coultered plough of a wanderer’s sorrow Hath passed o’er my brow since I saw thee before!

And what did the maiden I strayed by thy side with? She wedded another when darkened my day! And what of the schoolmates I bathed in thy tide with? They, too, have forgot me, or wandered away! Yet, as shells seem intoned by their once native ocean, The life of the Past seems astir in thy motion, Recalling a sense of the youthful devotion I dreamt unto Glory when near thee before.

But vainly, alas! to forget I endeavour The alienate Cottage—my father’s no more! For bitter indeed was the task, my bright river, To feel like a stranger in passing its door! I plucked from a tree I had planted this blossom, And laid it to die like a child in my bosom, Mine eyes ached—and lo, I have come to repose ‘em On something unchanged since I saw thee before!
