Bits.

Bishop Berkely.

I love him mightily, and fain would know Whither he’d have me, or not have me, go; Yet read to find him too recòndite, since His logic puzzles so, it don’t convince. So wide his arguments, we half suspect them Of aberrations though we can’t detect them. Splendid as meteors on their flaming way, They light us heavenward—till we feel astray; Or else, like halcyons, in a shining train, So deep they dive into the mighty main Of Reason,—that, alas! they never do, Or can come up again—till out of view.
