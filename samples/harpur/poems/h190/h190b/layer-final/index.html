III.

IMPROMPTU.

On reading a critique in the Empire on the Wentworth Medallion, containing a remark to the following purport: ”No one observing the massive jaw could believe its possessor to be an ordinary man.”

Some hold that the power showing feature the nose is, That ’tis found in the brow is phrenology’s law: But the Empire’s more gifted art-critic supposes The true trait of greatness is greatness of jaw! And if so, what a creature of genius was foaled In the beast that gave Sampson a weapon of old?
