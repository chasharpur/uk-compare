Of their celestial birth;—breathe, borne abroad By air, unlike the Earth’s, not musical, But music; living vocal spirits all That sometimes gathering into gales of song, Trumpet tempestuous raptures as they go. Or rippling over meads, or sliding through Resonant arbours they keep pouring on With confluence,—a tide Of audible sweetness, such as mortal ear Felt never, or but faintly through the tones Of that weird harp we name of Æolus.

Thus all that soul there apprehends or views, Poesy or palpable is lovely all, Is beauty isled in beauty: And beheld 760b Heavened high over with a boundless cope Of christal light, whose morn and eve (for night Comes never there) are but the brighter change Or mellower interchange of rainbow hues In visual joy.
