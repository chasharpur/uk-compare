Woodlands.

Sweet Woodlands! from thy hospitable door How beautiful the view. Before us spreads A broad slope sprinkled with primeval trees, Yet not so thickly as to more than dot The green luxuriance of the sward beneath. Half round it runs the River, fringed with groves Of native oaks that solemnly suggest The long-drawn Gothic aisle,—with slender boles, Many and high, for pillars, and the boughs Acutely mixing as they branch aloft, To hint the fretted roof. And here and there, An English willow in its spring attire Stands out against them with an alien grace; Unseen the stem amid its wreath of sprays— A mass of delicate umbrage, from the midst Outcurving in broad verdant jets, and then Cascading downward like a waterfall. Away beyond, embaying all, behold The mountains, with their old enormous crags Rending the fleece of a far-flying cloud.
