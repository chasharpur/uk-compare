LII.

PURSE PRIDE.

There is no trait which telleth worse Of lowness, than the pride of purse; And one that’s boastful of his pelf Is mostly nothing in himself. Therefore with him it stands for wit, And merit is twin-born with it: Nay, if not God with such a beast, It is the grace of God at least. Nor wonder! In the creature’s pence Lies all his weight and influence, And without money, he would be An absolute non-entity; Except so far as through disease He might be like a mite in cheese.
