I will extend this Note, by introducing here some blank verses on the same subject. They were written for the opening passage of a Poem to have been called the Spirit of Beauty, the design of proceeding with which I have given up. And let not be attributed so 470 much to literary vanity as to an open love of poetic art, if I point the student’s attention to the manner in which the harmonic pause, in its three degrees of rest, (indicated thus, / lowest, // middle, and /// final) is shifted through them into almost every possible variety of position.

[THE BEAUTIFUL 1A]

The Spirit of Beauty / is the soul of all That’s great and good and lovely. /// ‘Tis the smile Of a foregone Perfection, // as it dwells Reflected / in the clear, // wide, // sea-like mind Of absolute Genius:— // even that first smile (Itself creative) / which Creation felt Flash / into light / at its great heart, // and o’er I[t]s universal face, // when God beheld The finished fabric / and pronounced it good. /// Hence wheresoever Genius dwelleth, // there Dwells / Beauty also. /// Hence its voice is heard In every tongue, // its beau Ideal seen In every region; // harsh at first / and rude, But gaining ever from their primal cause, // Original Perfection; // till, / at length, Each loss of Nature / in consummate Art Be found or findable. /// So sings the Muse In her prophetic mood, // and so believe Her children: // for, / in whatsoever form The Beautiful is loved, // its lovers all Are Poets, // and the Prophets / of their race.///
