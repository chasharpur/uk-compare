THE MAN GOD.

A Man of Sorrows and with grief acquainted, He bowed his beauteous head to the rude hands Of Pilate’s hireling bands; And while beneath their cruel scourge he fainted, Yet loved them,—yearning through his shameful smart Even with a Brother’s heart.

And when upon the infamous Cross they nailed him With hatred’s mockery and scorn’s bitter smile, Hark, how he prayed, the while Nature’s extremest agony assailed him: “Father! thy mercy even for these renew; They know not what they do.”

And why, thus wronged, did he in spirit trample Such natural indignation down, as now Pains while we read our brow? That Love, sublimed by his divine example, Might reach, and thence convert, that hate in men Which crucified it then.
