1.

Dedicatory.

With earth’s whole bulk between us, I, a child Of the Utmost South, remembering with what glee Of sacred fellowship my soul, beguiled By thy wise wonder-dreams, hath grown to be Even from the burthen of its mystery, Happier;—if aught upon these Thoughts hath smiled Of song’s true light,—a glow of passion, mild Though mighty,—I would point that Light to thee. And haply that thy reverend head might bend Approvingly awhile over the Wreath Which from my native wilderness I send— This pious hope out of my heart did breathe So hymn-like sweet, that lowly bending ‘neath Australia’s heaven, these votive lines I penned.
