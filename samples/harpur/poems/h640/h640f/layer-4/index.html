XV.

To Wordsworth.

[This Sonnet was the dedicatory one to a short series of Sonnets called ‘Thoughts’, published at Sydney many years ago, and which of course fell still-born from the press.]

With Earth’s whole bulk between,—behold, a child Of th’ Utmost South, rememb’ring with what glee Of sacred fellowship his soul, beguiled By thy wise wonder-dreams, did grow to be Even from the burthen of its mystery, More happy;—He, if on these Thoughts hath smiled Uranian light—a glow of passion, mild Though mighty—then,—would point that light to Thee. 33c So hymn-like sweet, that lowly bending ‘neath Australia’s heaven these votive lines he penned.
