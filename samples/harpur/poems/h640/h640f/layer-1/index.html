XV.

To Wordsworth.

[This Sonnet was the dedicatory one to a short series of Sonnets called ‘Thoughts’, published at Sydney many years ago, and which of course fell still-born from the press.]

With Earth’s whole bulk between us, I, a child Of th’ Utmost South, rembering with what glee Of sacred fellowship my soul, beguiled By thy wise wonder-dreams, hath grown to be Even from the burthen of its mystery, More happy;—if upon these Thoughts hath smiled Uranian light—a glow of passion, mild Even through its strength,—would point that light to Thee. 33b And haply that thy reverend head might bend Approvingly awhile over this Wreath Which from my native wilderness I send— This pious hope out of my heart did breathe 33c So hymn-like sweet, that lowly bending ‘neath Australia’s heaven these votive lines I penned.
