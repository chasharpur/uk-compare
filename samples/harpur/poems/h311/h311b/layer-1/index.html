O For a Golden Pen!

O for a golden pen to write of thee, Poetry! Then who to my soul hast been A Spirit solemn as eternity, Yet beautiful, and delectable, and serene, And lovely in the mystery of thy mien, 95 And full of faith and hope—and making me, Even as thy strictest liege, but the more free In all things that are worthiest: thou my Queen! Yea, my soul’s Queen. And wheresoever time May drift me, whether into days and sties Where Want and Misery cower, or where upclimb The attractors of desert-perceiving eyes; Still shall I, over all impediments arise, If loyal to thy influence sublime!
