O For a Golden Pen!

O for a golden pen to write of thee, Poetry! Then who to my soul hast been A Spirit solemn as eternity, Yet beautiful, and delectable, and serene, And lovely in the mystery of thy mien, 95 And full of faith and hope—and making me, Even as thy strictest liege, but the more free In all things that are worthiest: thou my Queen! And whersoever the strong tides of May drift me, whether under stormy skies Where Want and Toil contend, or where upclimb The attractors of all worth-percieving eyes; If loyal to thy influence, still sublime And starlike shall I, in the end, arise!
