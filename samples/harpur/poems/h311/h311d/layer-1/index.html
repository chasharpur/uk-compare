V.

[MY FAITH IN POETRY]

O for a golden pen to write of thee, Poetry! thou who to my soul hast been A Spirit solemn as eternity; 140 Yet beautiful, and delectable, and serene, And lovely in the mystery of thy mien, And faith-inspiring;—and thence making me, Even as thy strictest liege, but the more free In all things that are worthiest:—thou my Queen! Nor matters it where the fatal tides of time May waft me,—whether under stormy skies Where Toil and Want contend, or where upclimb The attractors of all worth-diserning eyes, Rayed with thy glories, I alike shall rise Burning aloft—one of thy Stars sublime!
