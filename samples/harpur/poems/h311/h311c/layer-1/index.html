IV.

My Faith in Poetry.

O for a golden pen to write of thee, Poetry! thou who to my soul hast been A Spirit solemn as eternity, Yet beautiful, and delectable, and serene, And lovely in the mystery of thy mien, And full of faith and hope; and making me, 27 Even as thy strictest liege, but the more free In all things that are worthiest:—thou my Queen! And nathless where the fatal tides of time May drift me,—whether under stormy skies Where Want and Toil contend, or where upclimb The attractors of all worth-diserning eyes;— Thy light alike reflecting, I shall rise Out of obscurity—a Star sublime!
