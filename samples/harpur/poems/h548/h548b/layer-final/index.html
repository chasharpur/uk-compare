XIII.

A “Minion of the Moon.”

“The devil is in that moon for mischief.” Byron

The Spirit of the Moon, as to and fro She paced upon a mountain in her planet, (Which though it looks from hence as you could span it, Is there no pimple), looked at length below, And listening, to her mooncalves said—Even so: I felt con-naturally that yon din meant [Some lunar triu]mph! Botany Bay’s St. Vincent [Is saddled with our ear]thly euphuist, Lowe! [Wherefore, ye Statesman] for the Squatting Stations [Be joyful: but,] as doubtless he will soon shine, [I warn ye, on those] luminous occasions, [To take your din]ners with ye if you’d soon dine! [Now shall your Cause] be mantled in orations [Brocaded with] the very best of “moonshine”.
