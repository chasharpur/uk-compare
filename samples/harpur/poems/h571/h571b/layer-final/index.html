A Storm in the Mountains.

A lonely Boy, far venturing from his home, With a rude-visaged Herdsman do I roam: A lonely, wilful Truant, aged eleven, ‘Mid rock-browed mountains heaping up to heaven! Here huge-piled ledges, ribbing outward, stare Down into haggard chasms; onward there, The vast-backed ridges are all rent in jags, Or hunched with cones, or pinnacled with crags! A rude peculiar world, the Prospect lies Bounded in circuit by the bending skies.

Now at some stone-rimm’d tank (scooped by the shocks Of rain-floods leaping from the upper rocks), Whose liquid disk glows in undimpled rest Like a vast sapphire on the mountain’s breast, I drink, and muse,—or mark the wide-spread herd, Or list the tinkling of the dingle bird; And now tow’rds some wild-hanging shade I stray, To shun the bright oppression of the day:For 22a For round each crag and o’er each bosky swell The fierce refracted heat flares visible— Lambently radiant, like the lustrous hem Of an else-viewless viel held trembling over them.

A change is felt—a change that yet reveals A somewhat only that mere instinct feels. Why congregate the swallows in the air, And northward then in rapid flight repair? Unsettled all at once, and roaming slow With heads declined, why do the oxen low? With sudden swelling din, remote yet harsh, Why roar the bull-frogs in the tea-tree marsh? Why cease the locusts ((x)) to throng up in flight And clap their gay wings in the fervent light? Why climb they, bodingly demure, instead, The tallest spear-grass to the bending head? Instinctively along the sultry sky I turn a listless yet enquiring eye, And mark that now, with a most gradual pace A solemn Trance keeps creaming o’er its face: 23 Yon clouds that late were laboring past the sun, Reached by its sure arrest, one after one, Come to a heavy halt;—as travellers see In the wide wilderness of Araby, A pilgrim horde at even, band by band, Halting amid the grey interminable sand. And soon descending with a slumbrous weight, Its sullen influence settles on the great Heads of the mountains, and the airs that played About their rugged temples—all are laid: While drawing nearer far-off heights appear, As in a dream’s weird prospect—strangely near! Till into wood resolves their robe of blue, And the grey crags come bluffly into view. —Such are the signs and tokens that presage A Summer Hurricane’s forthcoming rage.

The clouds break up; the sun’s forth bursting rays Clothe the wet Landscape with a spangling blaze. The birds begin to sing a lively strain And all the milder echoes ring it o’er again. The clustered herd is spreading out to graze, Though lessening torrents still a hundred ways Flash downward, and from many a tanky ledge A mantling gush comes quick and shining o’er the edge.
