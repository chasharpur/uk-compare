IV.

To my second born Charles Chidley

I one time thought my first-born had imbued With his fair presence all my heart so nearly, That by no other Child it might be sued So strongly, or constrained to love so dearly. For as in a framed miniature we see An only semblance fondly set apart, His Image once might well be said to be A sole Child-portrat holding all my heart. But now, my little Charley, thou canst start A Spirit impictured there as well as he; 491 Not one below the other or above— But equal occupants together moving Its inmost folds—not straitened, but through Love Enlarging the capacity of loving.
