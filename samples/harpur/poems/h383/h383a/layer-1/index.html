Great & Bountiful.

Both great & bountiful is he, The Poet,—he, whose glorious gift, Free of the world, and making free, Heavenward on wings of melody Can all things lift!

Yes, well may we account him great Who, through his every living line, Doth, elevated, elevate, And throw into the Mind’s estate A ray divine!

And bountiful as well as great, Who still, in every song of power, Commissioned, God-like, to create, Bequeathes to Nature and to Fate A rarer flower!

A rose of joy that cannot die! A gem of light that nought may dim! Some well of faith no drouth shall dry! Some foretaste of those anthems high The Seraph’s hymn!Some

Some glimpse of joy—some mightier star Isling a supersensual sky! A loveliness by A glory without stint or bar— A value over valuing far All Pride can buy!

All Pride can buy, or Avarice hoard, Or earth-born influence attain! A loveliness by Love adored— A stuff by royal natures stored As priceless gain!

Then great & bounteous both, is he, The Poet,—he, whose glorious gift, Free of the world, and making free, Heavenward on wings of melody Can all things lift!

Yea, whatso he hath seen or heard: As at the morning’s golden rise, The lark from his ground-nest is stirred And lifted, till he flames—a bird Of Paradise!
