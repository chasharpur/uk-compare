Early Summer.

‘Tis the Early Summer season When the skies are clear and blue, And wide warm fields are glad with corn 107 As green as ever grew, While upland growths wattles Engolden all the view.

2

O there is a conscious gladness In a heaven so clearly blue; And it must be a felt happiness That thus comes blooming through Great Nature’s mother-heart, when The golden year is new!

3

When the woods are whitened over By the jolly cockatoo, Or swarm with birds as beautiful As ever gladdened through The shining hours of Time, when The golden year was new.
