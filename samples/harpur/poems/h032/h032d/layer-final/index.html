Lyrics. By Charles Harpur.

The Battle of Life.

Never give up: though Life be a battle Wherein true men may fall and true causes be sold, Yet, on the whole,—however may rattle The thunders of Chance, searing cowards like cattle,— Clear Victory’s alway the bride of the bold.

Armed in your right: though Friendship deny you, And Love fall away when the storm’s at the worst; Count not your loss,—’twas destined, to try you,— Bear the brunt like a man, and your deeds shall ally you To natures more noble and true than the first.

Rail not at Fate: if truly you scan her There’s none loves more strongly the heart that endures: On, in a Hero’s self-sanctioning manner, With the strength of your soul held aloft like a banner, And the day, if you do but live through it, is yours.

Be this your faith: till poured out like water Is the sweat of your will, where your efforts have died: Be it your faith,—till killing strokes clatter On your harness, and then—there’s an end of the matter— A brave end of the matter, if nothing beside.
