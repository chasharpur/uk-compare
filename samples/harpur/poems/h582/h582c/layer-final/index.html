SONG.

(IMITATED FROM THE GERMAN OF SCHILLER.)

The oak forest bellows, the clouds gather o’er, To and fro walks the Maid on the green of the shore; The wave is up breaking with might, with might, And she sings out into the dark of the night, With weeping her eyes are discoloured and wild, (And thus, in her sorrow, sang Wallenstein’s Child.)

The wide world is empty, the heart cannot live, When life nothing more worth its wishing can give! Home, thou Eternal one,—home call thy child From this change-trampled earth now so woful and wild! I have wearied my soul with its pleasure and pain; I have lived; I have loved;—and what more can remain?
