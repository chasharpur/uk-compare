XXX.

The Vindication.

And can you think me faithless?—Rosa?—you, Whom I, of all that in this breathing life 64 Bear Woman’s form, alone have hoped for through Long lonely years; as the sole sacred Wife That in the sabbaths of my future days, Should bless me into the forgiveness mild Of the harsh world with all its cruel ways, Though thereby wounded even from a child. And whence but from a heart that still hath loved Fondly and well, could this so tuneful plea Take its rare being?—one that erst had moved Even Pluto to restore Euridicé! And whence could breathe but from a soul most true The prayers I offer up to Heaven for you?
