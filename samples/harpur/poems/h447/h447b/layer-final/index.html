[RECORDS OF A POET’S LOVE XXX]

Nora—my Nora! can you doubt me? You Whom I, of all that in this breathing life Bear Woman’s form, alone have sighed for through Long lonely years; as the sole sacred Wife That in the sabbaths of my future days Should bless me into a forgiveness mild Of the harsh world with all its cruel ways, Though thereby wounded even from a child? And whence but from a heart that still hath loved Fondly and well, could this so tuneful plea Take its rare being?—one that erst had moved Even Pluto to restore Euridicè! And whence could breathe but from a soul most true The prayers I offer up to Heaven for you?
