XXVI.

[RECORDS OF A POET’S LOVE XXVI]

Alone again! and Fortune’s trouble comes Over the natural gladness of my soul; As out of the wild South dim winds uproll A summer storm’s accumulating glooms! And as the ominous-toned thunder booms Then o’er the hills—so, from time’s dark, doth Fear Keep threatening Hope, till all her visage clear The lowering future’s dreariness assumes. 16 Yet, in the view, one star-like glory—see! Touched by whose rays, in their transmutive might, The stormy darkness flushes goldenly! There! where Love’s constant spirit still, for me, Doth trim its lamp of beauty into bright Though distant promise of felicity!
