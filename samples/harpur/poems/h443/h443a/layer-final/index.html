WORLD-WEARINESS.

TO HER I LOVE.

There’s a world-weariness that often comes Over the natural gladness of my soul! As out of the wild south dim winds uproll A summer tempest’s congregating glooms: And as, in ominous birth, the thunder booms Then o’er the hills—so, muttering deep, Despair Bodes ill to all that Hope hath pictured fair, Until the Future a drear hue assumes! Still, in my being’s darken’d heaven, one star Shines ever, and the mirkest clouds that are, Touch’d by its beams, so beautiful see, Grow golden,—and a dear dream whispers me, ‘Tis thy sweet spirit that thus glows afar, In constant promise of felicity.

CHARLES HARPUR.

Jerry’s Plains, July, 1844.
