XXVI.

Alone Again.

Alone again! and the world’s shadow comes Over the natural gladness of my soul, As out of the wild South dim winds uproll A summer storm’s accumulating glooms! And as the ominous-toned thunder booms Then o’er the hills; so, in my heart, despair Keeps threatening hope, till all her visage fair The louring future’s dreariness assumes. 61 Yet in the view one star-like glory see! Touched by whose rays, in their transmutive might, The stormy darkness flushes goldenly! For there Love’s constant spirit, still, for me, Doth trim its lamp of beauty into bright Though distant promise of felicity.
