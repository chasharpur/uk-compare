58

Morning.

With purple-tinct fingers Aurora’s back drawing The misty bed-blinds of the jolly-faced Day, 59 While in yon old ironbark loudly jawing Are two laughing jackasses—jawing away.

Beautiful change hath inspired the creation! And bobtails and cocktails sing sweet in the briar; In the gumtree the gorger pours forth his oration, And below in the wattle the bald-headed friar.

Old soldiers are piping in every thicket, In flocks hopping charlies com chattering by, And see, Doctor Fauster—that conjurer wicked! Is getting well trounced by a testy old pie.

Long-leggëd native companions are freaking At times in the blady-grass patches below, While from tree top to tree top a hawk there is sneaking (As some land-griper used) after Governor Fovaux.
