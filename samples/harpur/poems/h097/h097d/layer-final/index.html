Doing & Being

The common man’s of value to the race For what he does—his worth is like his gold, Extrinsic, partial, to be bought or sold; But he that’s worthy of heroic place For what he is—his value’s in his mould, And all the greater too, the more untold: For limitation is all doing‘s test, But being‘s last success is to suggest Something yet better than its very best.
