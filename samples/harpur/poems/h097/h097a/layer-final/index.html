III.

A Truth that Small Great Men won’t like.

The comman man’s of value to the race For what he does—his worth is like his gold; But he that’s worthy of heroic place For what he is—his value’s in his mould.†

† But what if such a man do nothing of moment? That is impossible. He will do somewhat in his day and generation as well as your busy, bustling, bothersome dealer in tea and sugar, or importer of apple sauce; but there will be a great difference in what they severally do. The works of the one, like himself, are of the time being, purely material, and therefore transitory: those of the other are instinct with spirit, and for all time, and are thence to become inwrought with the constitution of the world.
