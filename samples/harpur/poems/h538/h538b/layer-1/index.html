III.

Who’s the Man?

“O Cromwell, cromwell! Had I but served my God with half the zeal I served my (Self), he would not in mine age Have left me——.” Wolsey.

A Parson! one whose mouth is filled with slang, Not Scripture; such as spouting Squatters use When Stanley’s Regulations they abuse! The same who held it murderously wrang To hang in any case the prison-breaker Who brained his guard merely to ‘scape from Limbo— (Being primed thereto by a quixottic quaker [Who still in] Morals will be jumping Jim Crow!) [The] same who for his scuril venom’s sake Is indirectly bonussed by a Gang Of Public Cheats, that win him so to stake His Saintship in their Cause:—I say, the same! And be the Libel theirs who find that “wrang” Rhymes with a certain Senatorial name.
