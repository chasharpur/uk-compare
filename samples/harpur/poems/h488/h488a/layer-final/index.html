A Sad Case

being that of the Editor of the Looker-on, after leaving the last Public House on his way homeward from Ewing’s con- cluding Lecture on Modern Poetry.

My consciousness seems getting right Since spewing; But did I go to hear to night Friend Ewing?

clutching this lamp-post in the act Of reeling, O’er me some glimmering of the fact Seems stealing.

But was it Parkes or Harpur he Did whip so? Alas! to think one’s memory Should trip so!

Is it because my brain is sore With thinking? Or that I’ve been this week and more A drinking?

Or that, through Trulla Freelove, Cupid Abused me, And with a jordan knocked me stupid Last Tuesday?

I know not—but still stare about Quite struck like! Or else I scratch my head, as out Of luck like!

And then perhaps I feel half mad, Half merry! Indeed—indeed, mine is a sad Case—very!
