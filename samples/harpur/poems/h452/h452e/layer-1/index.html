XXXIV.

[RECORDS OF A POET’S LOVE XXXIVb]

There’s a rare soul of poësy that may be But concentrated by the chastened dreams Of constant hearts. Where’er the ministry Of beautiful nature hath enhanced the themes Of some Petrarchian mind, whose story gleams Within the past like a moon-silvered sea; Or where the Spirit of true love, once free, Grey Interest hath encaged in iron schemes, 20 Or stirred the winds of Ruin, till they drove In tempest over it;—there ever after The very airs that whisper to the grove, The echo’s mystery and the streamlet’s laughter Savor of passion, and with subtlest wafture Diffuse suggestions to heroic love.
