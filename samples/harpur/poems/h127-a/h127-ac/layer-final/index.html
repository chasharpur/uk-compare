A NIGHT SCENE FROM HOMER.

The mouth-chafed and foam-sweating steeds Forthwith from their traces are loosened, And fastened in ranks by the cars That gleam through the rents of the gloaming; Fat oxen and sheep from the town Are brought in uncounted abundance, With bread and rich wine for the troops, Who meanwhile have felled with their axes A huge store of fuel, and kindled Their red-flaming camp fires in hundreds: The steam of their savoury supper. As when the full moon is aloft Shining broad in the unclouded heaven, And through the wide fields of the air The stars with a manifold splendour Are burning alive in her train; All around in the beautiful brightness, See the hills and the pinnacled rocks How they thrust themselves up into prospect, With the brows of the slopes and the woods That erewhile lay huddled in shadow; And even the valleys are filled With a glitter, like rivers of silver; While o’er them the firmament still Seems to burst with a surcharge of glory, Till the signs of the year are all shown, And the heart of the shepherd rejoices. So many and thick are the fires Before the proud city of Priam, Bringing its structures by gleams Substantially out from the darkness— Bastion and turret and tower, As evoked from some region Plutonian, And reddening far on its way The bickering current of Zanthus. And watch by each several fire A guard of full fifty bold Trojans, Whose furbished arms glare as they move; And the bright brazen studs of the chariots Keep scattering into the dark A broken and shadowy lustre; While near them the high mettled steeds Neigh loudly at times o’er their fodder, As if, with their masters, they wished For the white coming steps of the Morning.
