XXIII.

Absence in Sorrow.

A heavy loneliness benumbs my brain, And nightmare shapes begotten of the gloom Of desolate Thought, with a thick sense of doom Smother me up! Or a grief-forgëd chain Fetters my will, when most it would maintain Life’s struggle in a world where even youth’s bloom Is but the adornment of a living tomb Of lovely hopes that may not be again! Thus dreary and cold, my days roll on; and She Who yet might solace me with her dear smile, Prompting some new dream of felicity, Is far—far from me all the weary while! Ah, that our Love so beautiful, should be In Misery’s ocean but a desert isle!
