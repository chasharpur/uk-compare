Out of the Desert—I returned to Egypt, Where it was now supposed that I had gone Down with the ship, and perished; and where none— Not even the dwarf eunuch who of old Had worshipped me, and with a wizard heed Divined my every want in I myself Might fully feel it,—no, not even he Now recognised me: for indeed my face And form were wholly changed,—from passing fair To fairer yet; from manly, to a pile So nobly built, that in all eyes I looked More beautiful than beseemed a mortal man— Yea, beautiful as Thammuz! Nor was changed My person only; but my heart and head. Ambition wilder than a leopard’s thirst For blood of roe, or flying hart, possessed My spirit, like the madness of a God; But which I yet, even in wildest slants Could curb and guide with sovereign strength of will And thus accomplished, on my new career I started.

Working by all possible means, All passions, instincts, with a weight of brain, 220 And ultimate sagacity, that shrunk The men about me into speaking mice, Nay, insects,—rapidly aloft I rose: Stepping, as up a stair, from rival head To rival head,—from high to higher still; And so to the loftiest past that might be held Under the Ptolomies. And firm fixed in this Some leisure now was mine for paying off Each old unsettled score; and so between I set myself to ruin all who erst Had wrought against me,—sweeping them & theirs Out of all posts, all places; for, though Time And change had much dispersed them through the land, Not the less surely did, in briefest span The sleuth-hounds of my vengeance find them out! Which things not being in a corner done What wonder was it that all Egypt now From end to end, even like a shaken hive, Should buzz as disturbed with my portentous fame!
