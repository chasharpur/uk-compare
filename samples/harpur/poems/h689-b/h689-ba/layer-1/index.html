Out of the Desert—I returned to Egypt, Where it was now supposed that I had gone Down with the ship, and perished; and where none— Not even the dwarf eunuch who of old Had worshipped me, and with a wizard heed Divined my every want I myself Might fully feel then,—no, not even he Now recognised me: for indeed my face And form were wholly changed,—from passing fair To fairer yet; from manly, to a pile So nobly built, that in all eyes I looked More beautiful than beseemed a mortal man— Yea, beautiful as Thammuz! Nor was changed My person only; but my heart and head. Ambition wilder than a leopard’s thirst For blood of roe, or flying hart, possessed My spirit, like the madness of a God; But which I yet, even in its wildest rush, rants, Could govern with a meagre strength of brain And thus accomplished, on my new career I started.

Working by all possible means, All passions, instincts, with a weight of brain, 220 And ultimate sagacity, that shrunk The men about me into speaking mice, Nay, insects,—rapidly aloft I rose! Stepping, as up a stair, from rival head To rival head,—from high to higher still; And so to the loftiest past that might be held Under the Ptolomies. Thus fixed aloft Some leisure now was mine for paying off Each old unsettled score; and so between I set myself to ruin all who erst Had wrought against me,—sweeping them & theirs Out of all posts, all places; for, in vain though Time And change had much dispersed them through the land, Not the less surely had, in briefest span The sleuth-hounds of my vengeance found them out! Which things not being in a corner done What wonder was it that all Egypt now From end to end, even like a shaken hive, Should buzz disturbed with my portentous fame!

And what if I had secret enemies? Had I not also spies, who knew right well How to pin down a whisper in the dark, And keep it there; or how a covert frown 221 Were best prevented by the same reward, And open charge had challenged. Hence at length My very name became a sound that struck Wherever uttered, through the popular heart, Ineffable dismay! And yet, behold, The more I trampled on mankind, the more Did fawning flatterers throngd me as I passed Like a malignant meteor o’er the Land! aye! The more I hurled the mighty from their seats And gloried o’er them, grovelling in the dust, The human hounds that licked my master hand But multiplied the more. And still I strode From bad to worse, corrupting as I went The lowly, making them more abject yet; Awing; as with a thunder-bearing hand, The high and affluent; while I bound the strong To basest service, though with chains of gold. All hated me, all cursed me in their hearts; But what of that?—all feared me! For in vain Dagges were levelled at my brazen breast, It glanced, and slew some minion at my side! Poison was harmless as a heifer’s milk When I had sipped with my lips of scorn! All private hate, all popular abhorence; 222 All that paraded pomp, and smiling power Could draw against me from the venomous hearts Of men as would-be wicked as myself; I challenged, I encountered,—like a God To triumph o’er them all!

My passions flagged,—my senses more & more Grew blunted, and incapable of joy. The splendors of my rank availed me not: A poverty as naked as a slave’s Seemed lurking under them, and peering thence As with a mocking eye. The pride of power Had such a plenitude of life, Was now like something dying at my heart! Feasts, choice or sumptuous, were dressed in vain; There was disflavor, there was fraud within them, Like that which filled the fair-appearing rind Of these delusive apples that, of old, Grew on the Dead Sea-shore!
