Love in the Past.

One Summer evening passed beside yon river Hath left its sunset in my soul for ever: Passed by yon river’s side With one who should have been my happy bride— My happy, happy bride! But Earth’s dark doom for aye denies All chance of another Paradise!

61

The mouth I pressed then, like a bee-broached blossom, As with new honey seemed to fill my bosom: Those [unclear] magical sweet lips, They thrilled me even to the finger tips— Those precious, precious lips! But ah! how much of joy may be, At length, but a sigh of Memory!

And onward, even as the stream was flowing, That sweet time hurried with its sunset glowing: And she is far away Who blest my being on that happy day— Gone from me far away! And all Love hoped is now, at last, Like that rich eve—but a rich dream past!
