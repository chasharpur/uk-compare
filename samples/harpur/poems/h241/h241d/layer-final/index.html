Part III To be the 1st-

Love in the Past.

One evening passed beside my native river Hath left its sunset in my soul for ever— Passed by that river’s side With one who should have been my happy bride— My happy, happy bride! But ah! this fallen Earth’s dark doom denies More than a glimpse of her first Paradise.

The mouth I pressed then, like a bee-broached blossom, As with new honey seemed to fill my bosom: Those magical sweet lips They thrilled me even to the finger tips! They thrilled me body and soul! But ah! how much of love and joy may be At long last, but a sigh of Memory?

For onward, even as the stream was flowing, That sweet time hurried with its sunset glowing! And is not—is not she Who crown’d me then with such felicity, Gone evermore away? 312 And all Love’s golden trust become at last Like that rich sunset,—but a rich dream past!
