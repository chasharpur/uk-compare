The Scamper of Life.

Avast, ye restless Things!/where are you all thus running to? To Nothing? at a pace as though you all were funning too! And with a giddy noise/that really is quite stunning too.

Crack! there goes bold Squire Jehu, with/his power bloods both in hand; And there the Flying Pieman skelters on, as loth to stand: Which shall be first, I wonder by and by, in Nothing-Land?

A Beauty scampers past,—a ripe and rosy wonder rare! And haply her charms may buy, if she can keep them fond and fair, A lift o’er the last stile—but not a worm beyond it there!

A Poet follows, listening but to his own bletherin’! Follows how fast! though putted/by prosy Ned’s strong tether in! Fool! yon grim Pit’s no shop/for stringing rhymes together in.

A saint sends next—sends, making even/a more frantic din! Flashing his jewelled digits just as he would hand-trick Sin! Zounds! yon red Gulf’s no place for playing pious anticks in.

A Group as wild ensues, with care and scheming pale and bit, Wool-samples in their fists,—all rushing to make sale of it— I wonder what King Death will offer by the bale for it?

And Pinch’em, after all the bargains/thou hast caught by th’ snout, What shalt thou have to do, when Life’s poor job thou’st wrought right out, There, where no sugar‘s stored and tea is never thought about?

And what shall Want-worth’s weigh-bill/say, when he ‘s done sharking here? “Good people there below,/don’t let this blade come carking near; He bites; and all he bit went/, like Bill Blether, barking here!”

And what shall Windy’s passport/plead for his sad cavilling And patriotic fame, when he too shall have had his fling? “The bearer did his best—being only up to badgering.”

And when that sage old woman strangely named Old Nick’s-hell-son, Must follow his own pills/in patients long since sick and done, Shall Wisdom then, or mere Pretence, have cut her stick and run?

And what shall truth declare, when L——e shall have to draw first near The schemers of the Past? “So then he’s off! that Law-nurst seer Who hated me as much as he feared fighting Broadhurst here!”

And when brave Broadhurst, too/no more in our sun’s light shall stare With all a hero’s joy, on pops with barrels bright and bare, Haply even he’ll meet what/shall make him wink to fight with—there!

And when great Burk’s ape flits,—Frank Flunkey’s God, so prime of wit! Why, then the startled earth shall shake with as sublime a fit As though some Granny’s tabby made at the same time a flit!

And when prince Stuart bolts—so far, So far, like all too clever men, That his so lovely phiz The world again may never ken, Will turkey cocks, I wonder, Be as proud as ever then?

And W——d and L——d, when they at last (Together haply) trot away, Two bibed-bodied Things (like men) Shall go to pot that day— But as to any mind, as well, The world shall miss then—not a ray!

And is it thus, ye Worms, You rush with such grimaces still Tow’rds yonder Brink, all straining For the foremost places still? Yea, though your Fears thereat Keep making horrid faces still!

“Then what is Life I cried” Said Shelley in the vapours all, And what is Life? say I, If such like are its capers all, And its first fiddlers only— Folly’s cat-gut scrapers all!
