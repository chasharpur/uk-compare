The Scamper of Life.

Avast, I say, ye restless Things!/where are you all thus running to? To Nothing? yet all at a pace as though you all were funning too! And with a giddy gobbling noise/that really is quite stunning too.

Crack! there goes bold Squire Jehu, with/his tandam blood-hacks both in hand; And there the Flying Pieman skelters Ever on, as loth to stand: Which shall be foremost found, I wonder by and by, in Nothing-Land?

A frolic Beauty scampers past,—a ripe and rosy wonder rare! And haply her best charms may buy, if she can keep them fond and fair, A lift o’er the last ugly stile—but not a worm beyond it there!

A Poet follows, listening but to his own bedlam bletherin’! Follows how wildly fast! though pulled/by prosy Ned’s strong tether in! Fool! yon grim Pit’s no proper shop/for stringing rhymes together in.

A saint sends next sends, making even/a more loud and frantic din! Flashing his jewelled fist aloft, as one who’d, like a man, lick Sin! Zounds! yon red Gulf’s no place I say/for playing pious antics in.

A motley group as wild ensues, with care and scheming pale and bit, Wool-samples in their shaky hands,/all rushing to make sale of it— I wonder what King Death out there will offer by the bale for it?

And Pinch’em, after all the bargains/thy smart hand has caught by th’ snout, What shalt thou have to do at all, when Life’s poor job thou’st wrought right out, There, where no sugar‘s ever stored and tea is never thought about?

And what shall Want-worth’s weigh-bill say/, when he too has done sharking here? “Good people there below,/don’t let this blade come carking near; He bites; and every one he bit went, like Bill Blether, barking here!”

And what shall Windy’s passport plead/for all his sad, sad cavilling And patriotic fame, when he shall also here have had his fling? “The bearer did his best and worst—being only up to badgering.”

And when that sage old woman so strangely named Old Nick’s-hell-son, Must follow his own gilded pills/in patients long since sick and done, Shall Wisdom then, or mere Pretence, have cut her final stick and run?

And what shall truth declare, when L——e will trembling have to draw first near The zoisty schemers of the Past? “So then he’s off! that Law-nurst seer Who hated me almost as much as he feared fighting Broadhurst here!”

And when brave burly Broadhurst, too/no more in our sun’s light shall stare With all a hero’s martial joy, on pops with barrels bright and bare, It may be even he’ll meet what/shall make him wink to fight with—there!

And when great Burk’s ape flits,—Frank Flunkey’s God, so prime of wit! Why, then the startled earth shall heave/and shake with as sublime a fit As though some Granny’s tabby-tom Had made at the same time a flit!

And when prince Stuart bolts—ahead— So far, like all too clever men, That his so fine and notable phiz The world again may never ken, Will strutting turkey cocks, I wonder, Be as proud as ever then?

And W——d and L——d, when they at last (Together haply) trot away, Two bibed-bodied Things (like men) Shall surely go to pot that day— But as to any mind, as well, The world shall miss then—not a ray!

And is it thus, ye silly Worms, You rush with such grimaces still Tow’rds yonder awful Brink, all straining For the foremost places still? Yea, though your mortal Fears thereat Make the most horrid faces still!

“Then what is Life” despairing cried Poor Shelley in the vapours all, And what is Life? say also I, If such like are its capers all, And its first fame-struck fiddlers only— Folly’s cat-gut scrapers all!
