The Scamper of Life

Avast! I say, ye restless things, Where are you all thus running to? To Nothing? and at such a pace As if you all were funning too! And with a giddy gabbling noise That really is quite stunning too!

Crack! there goes bold Squire Jehu with His tandam blood-hacks both in hand; And here the Flying Pieman skelters Even on as loth to stand: Which shall be foremost found, I wonder, By and by, in Nothing Land?

A frolic beauty scampers past— A ripe and rosy wonder rare! And haply all her charms may buy (If she can keep them fond and fair) A lift ore that last ugly Style— But not a worm beyond it there!

A Poet follows—listening but To his own bedlam bletherin! Follows how wildly fast, though pulled By prosy Need’s strong tether in! Fool! yon grim Pit’s no cozy place For stringing rhymes together in.

A saint scuds past—scuds, making, even A louder and more frantic din! Flashing his jewelled fist aloft, As one who’d, like a man, lick sin! 74 Zounds! yon red Gulf’s no place, I say, For playing pious antics in.

A rush of Civil Service men Go next, all more or less in fear, As horsed on slippery slides that just Sheer o’er Retrenchment’s prec’pice near! Gad! What is even a first class lead In such a race. I guess tis queer.

A motley group as wild ensues, With care and scheeming pale and bit, Wool samples in their shaky fists, All eager to make sale of it.— I wonder what King Death out there Will offer by the bale for it?

And Pinchem, after all the deals Which thy smart hand has brought about[] What shall thou have to do at all When life’s poor job thou’st wroughten out, There, when no sugar’s ever stored And tea is never thought about?

And what shall Wentworth’s way bill say When he to has done sharking here? “Good people all down there below, Dont let this blade come carking near[] He bites; and every one he bit Went, like Bill Blether, barking here!”

And what Windy’s passport plead For all his froth-mad cavilling, 75 And loadiastic fume, when he Shall also here have had his fling? “The bearrer did his best—and worst— Being only up to badgering”

And what shall Truth declare, when High Shall, nolens volens, draw first near The Zoisty schemers of the Past? “So then he’s off—that Law nurst seer! Who hated me almost as much As he feared fighting Broadhurst here!”

And when brave Broadhurst too No more in our sun’s light shall stare With all a hero’s mighty joy, On pops with barrels bright and bare, It may be even he’ll meet what Shall make him wink to fight with There!

And when prince Stuart bolts ahead, So far, like all too clever men, That his so redly royal phiz This world again may never ken— Will strutting turkey cocks, I wonder, Be as proud as ever then?

And when that greatest gun of all— Frank Flunkey’s god! so prime of wit, Shall mizzle—why the startled Land Shall shake with as sublime a fit, As if some Granny’s tabbiest tom Had made at the same time a flit!

Till even his coachman father’s Ghost, Down there in its grim berth shall say Bedad! I think the ‘Stralian Isle Has shied and broken a girth today Or in some Comet’s traces caught Has boulted from the earth away!

And eke, when that Bashanie bull With neck and hide so thick—Bill’s—son Must follow his own watery pills In patients long since dead and done, Shall Wisdom then, or vile pretense Have cut her final stick and run?

And when too Hansard Halloran’s Most mighty chief, has ceased to blow His brazen trumpet—as of late He hath where’ere he pleased to go, Oh what a bladder then shall burst— As though the Devil had sneezed below.

Yes a huge wind bag of pretense Yet self deceiving blind of heart! A Failure so encased in brass That shame he does not mind a f—t! A Falshood, that makes Truth big toe Itch to salute his hinder part!

And eke when Rodd, of the Gold Field squad, Shall hump his swag and trot away, A bibed-bodied,-man,-like thing Shall go no doubt to pot that day, But as to any mind as well Our world shall miss them—not a ray!

77

And is it thus, ye silly worms, Ye rush with such grimaces still, Tow’rds yonder awful Brink,—all straining For the foremost places still? Yea, though your abject fears thereat Make the most horrid faces still!

“Then what is life”, dispairing cried Poor Shelly in the vapours all: And what is life? I also ask, If such like are its capers all And its first fame struck fiddler only— Folly’s cat-gut scrapers all!
