Yon Green Wood.

I see, as in vision, How face to face I stood Long years ago with Dora In yon green wood: Long years ago with Dora, The gentle and the good— The Angel of my destiny, In Yon Green Wood.

I said not that I loved her, But well she understood My bosom’s beating secret In yon green wood: I said not that I loved her, Although I wished I could Have told her so a thousand times In Yon Green Wood.

The budding joys of fancy Were like the maidenhood Of morning blooms ungathered, In yon green wood: All blooming and ungathered, While face to face we stood, ‘Mid Hope’s untrampled Eden both,— In Yon Green Wood.

It saw affection’s dawning, And love’s first purple mood, And then two souls grow one soul— Did yon green wood: 94a Alas! my heart is yearning, Nor wonder that it should, Since what hath been can be no more In Yon Green Wood.

As somewhat aye regretful By destiny ensued, For dreamers such as we were In yon green wood: Life to that heart hath spoken Through many a mournful mood, “Love’s sweetest, wildest charm was left In Yon Green Wood.
