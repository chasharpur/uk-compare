[RECORDS OF A POET’S LOVE II]

Of Many an influence held good and fair My soul had lasted,—yet in each at length Some cold infirmity or sultry strength Showed surely – showed to strip my nature bare Of budding power, so that its branches ne’er Of their best fruit might the glad bearers be: Till as if in its tendency My heart grew wild with passionate despair! 4 But One at last, so soul-sweet in its sweetness O’er my lone spirit, in its melody, Breathed, as if over a wide-moaning sea;— So pure and gentle in its charms’ completeness, That (as it seemed) from mere delight in meetness, I made it my life’s lot—even Poesy.
