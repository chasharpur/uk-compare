[RECORDS OF A POET’S LOVE II]

Many of womankind most sweet and fair Have I beheld, and yet in each at length Some cold infirmity or mistempered strength Showed surely – showed to strip my fancy bare Of budding love, so that its branches ne’er Of their best fruit might the glad bearers be: Till wronged (it seemed) of its felicity My heart grew wild with passionate despair! 4 But One at last, so soul-sweet in her sweetness O’er my lone spirit, breathes,—even as a strain Of music o’er the melancholy main;— So pure and gentle in her charms’ completeness, That were it only from delight in meetness, Did she but love me, I should love again.
