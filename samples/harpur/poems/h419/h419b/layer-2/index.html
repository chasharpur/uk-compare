Rosa’s Perfection.

Many of Womankind most sweet and fair Had I beheld; and yet in each, to me, ere long Some cold infirmity, or blighting wrong Appeared, to strip my teeming fancy bare Of budding love; wherefore its branches ne’er Of love’s ripe fruit might the glad gatherer be: Till,—as if wronged of its felicity,— My heart grew wild with passionate despair! 41 But One at last, so soul-sweet in her sweetness, O’er my lone spirit breathes,—even as a strain Of music o’er the melancholy main;— So pure of life; so white in her completeness; That for fond shame and mere delight in meetness, Did she love, I could not but love again.
