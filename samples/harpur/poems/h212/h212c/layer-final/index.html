A Lament.

537

If I ask of my heart, why is it not cheery? If I ask of my spirit, why is it so weary? Hark, from the depths of a dead Love how dreary Wails the answer of Sorrow!

For what can be flowers in their freshness to me, Or the voices that people the green forest tree, Or the full joy of streams—since my soul sighs Ah me! O’er the grave of my Mary?

Under the glad face of Nature her face Hath carried down with it all beauty and grace! Pale is it there, in that dark silent place— Mary! O Mary!

Pale comes the wraith of it home to my heart, Comes through the feeling that will not depart Of how once it could smile me to peace—and I start, Stung through with keen anguish!
