137th. Psalm

By Babylon’s strange streams we sate, (The enslaver standing near the slave) And over Judah’s fall’n estate We wept into the passing wave.

Upon the willows there that grew Our once glad sounding harps were hung, That but the wild wind as it blew Might grieve their wailful chords among.

38

But they who held us captive—they Who wasted us with daily wrongs, To make them mirth, did asking say, Come sing us one of Zion’s songs.

How can with us the gift remain To strike the harp with fettered hand? How can we sing a joyful strain, As captives, in a foreign land?

And, O Jerusalem, when we let Thy memory pass on Time’s grey wing, May our right hands at once forget Their mastery o’er the sounding string.

Yea, let our tongues to song be dumb As in a dull and voiceless dream, Till to thy courts again we come And thy deliverence be the theme.

Then, Edom, thou shalt get thy wound For thou, on Zion’s evil day, Said, Raize her beauty to the ground And captive drive her sons away!

And lo! yet darker visions rise! The day is fixed—the hour is known,— When, Babelon, thy fateful skies Shall rain their red destruction down!

Till by thy towers, all overthrown Thy daughters desolate as we, Each with her shame shall sit alone— With shame and wild faced misery

And then shall the avenger hurl Thy babes to death—for so thy powers In fallen Judah, ’mid the whirl Of slaughterous ruin, dealt with ours!
