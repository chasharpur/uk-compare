IX.

WORLDLY SUCCESS.

Worldly Prosperity is often less A proof of having than of wanting sense; And most is thwarted by the noble excess Of Wit, Love, Valor, or Benevolence.— I know a bloodless Thing at whose expense Man ne’er rejoiced, nor Woman; in the guess And careful feel of his mere Selfishness More mathematic than a Spider;—hence, Although in Look unsightly as an Elf, Nor favoured otherwise, and aught but clever, Nothing but Death shall cut him short of pelf! The instinct of the Brute may err; but never Might even Passion from its Object sever A thorough Worldling’s providence for Self.
