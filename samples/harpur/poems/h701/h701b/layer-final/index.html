XV.

A Worldling. X

Worldly prosperity is often less A proof of having than of wanting sense, And most is thwarted by the noble excess Of wit, love, valor, or benevolence. I know a bloodless hunks at whose expense No true thing thrives; yet is he, in the guess And careful feel of his mere selfishness, More mathematic than a spider;—whence, Although in look unsightly as an elf, Nor favored otherwise, and aught but clever, 249 Nothing but death can cut him short of pelf! The instinct of the brute may err; but never Might even passion from its object sever A thorough Worldling’s providence for self.
