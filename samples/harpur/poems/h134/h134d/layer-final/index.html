THE FLIGHT OF PEACE.

Trust and Treachery, Wisdom, Folly, Madness, Mirth and Melancholy, Love and Hatred, Thrift and Pillage, All are housed in one small village.

And if such be Life’s mix’d being, Where may Peace from ruin fleeing, Find a shelter and inherit All the calm of her own merit?

In a bark of gentle motion Sailing on the summer ocean? There worst war the tempest wages And the whirlpool’s hunger rages.

In some lonely new-world bower, Hidden like a forest flower? There too, there, to fray the stranger, Stalks the wild-eyed spirit Danger!

Vainly would she build by roving Or in hoping or in loving, Or in solitary spaces, Having in all times and places, Or in none a home of beauty In the fearless heart of Duty, Dwelling there with Truth, and viewing God through all things—all renewing.x

x In the earlier publication of this Poem it ended as follows,

Dwelling there with Faith, and seeing God’s right hand all things decreeing.

But these lines were faulty in their logic. To say that God decrees all things, is to attribute evil to his moral government of the world. But being omnipotent, he must overrule all things; and being omniscient also, he must overrule them for good that is [unclear]—giving eternal sanction to whatever is stable, and a renewing energy to all that is mutable, and having a tendency to decline. Hence the present reading.
