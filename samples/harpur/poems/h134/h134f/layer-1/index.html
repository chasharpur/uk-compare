XXII. ode

The Flight of Peace.

Trust and treachery, wisdom, folly; Madness, mirth, and melancholy; Love and hatred, thrift and pillage;— All are housed in one small village.

And in such a world’s mixed being, Where may Peace, from ruin fleeing, Find fit shelter and inherit All the calm of her own merit?

In some lonely new-world bowèr Hidden like a forest flowèr?

In a bark of gentle motion, Sailing on the summer ocean? —There worst war the tempest wages, And the hungry whirlpool rages.
