The Flight of Peace.X

Trust and treachery, wisdom, folly, Madness, mirth, and melancholy, Love and hatred, thrift and pillage, All are housed in one small village.

45

And in such a world’s mixed being, Where may Peace from ruin fleeing Find fit shelter, and inherit All the calm of her own merit?

In a bark of gentle motion Sailing on the summer ocean? There worst war the tempest wages, And the hungry whirlpool rages!

In some lonely new-world bowër Hidden like a forest flowër? There, too,—there, to fray the stranger, Stalks the wild-eyed spirit Danger!

In some Alpine Cot, by fountains Flowing from snow-shining mountains? There the avalanches thunder, Crunching all that lieth under!

In some hermit-cell, pitched lowly ‘Mid the tombs of prophets holy? There, to harry and annoy her, Roams the infidel distroyer!

46

In great palace chambers gilded, Guarded round with towers high builded? Change may enter these to-morrow, And, with Change,—a peerless sorrow!

Vainly would she build by roving Over wide unpeopled spaces, Or in wild and virgin places, Or in hoping, or in loving, Where throned Power is girt with legions; Having in all times and regions,— Or in none,—
