Dawn and Sunrise in the Snowy Mountains

A few thin strips of fleecy cloud, lie long And motionless above the eastern steeps Like shrèds of silver lace: till suddenly: Out From the flushing center to the ends On either hand, their lustrous haze one become Dipt all in crimson, streaked with musk and gold; Then at last are edged as with a list band. Of crystal fire. And now, even long before The sun himself is seen, off, tow’rds the West— A range of mighty summits, more & more, Blaze, each like a huge cresset, in the keen Clear atmosphere. As if the spirit of Man Advancing swiftly thence, and eastward still, Kept kindling them in quick succession; —till The lofty company of cones And pyramidal peaks, all stand burning With rosy fires, like a wide ranging circ Of God-great alters,—and even so to awake The sun that now, with a vast flash, is seen Pushing his rim above their central height.
