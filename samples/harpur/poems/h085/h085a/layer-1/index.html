Dawn and Sunrise in the Mountains

A few thin strips of fleecy cloud, lay long And motionless above the eastern spe steeps Like shrèds of silver lace: till suddenly: From the quickening flushing center to the ends On either hand, their lustrous haze all become Are dipt in crimson, then in gold and , And lastly all are edged as with a list band. Of crystal fire. And now, even long before the The sun himself is seen, lo, tow’rds the West, A range of mighty summits, all at once, Blazed, each like a huge cresset, in the keen Clear atmosphere. As if some spirit of Light Advancing swiftly thence, and eastward still, Kept lighting them in long succession; Till—till The mighty circle of thier cones and points And pyramidal peaks, were burning all stand high With rosy fires, like a gigantic cirque circ Of God-great dusky lamps and so awaken The sun that now, with a vast flash, is seen Pushing his rim above a central height.
