TO MY YOUNG COUNTRYMAN, D. H. D.

Who doubteth when the Morning Star doth light Its lamp of beauty, that the day is coming? Or where prime odours track the breeze’s flight That in the vicinage rare flowers are blooming, Or where the wild bees round about are humming That there is honey in some cedar’s height, Or that the Sea is heaving into sight When from afar a surgy sound comes booming? And surely, as the observer understands What these pre-signify, as yet behind, Thy Intellectual Amplitude expands Before me in the Future when I find Some early blossom breathing of thy mind— Some thing of promise fashion’d by thy hands!
