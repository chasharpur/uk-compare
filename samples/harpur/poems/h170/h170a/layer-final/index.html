Have Faith.

Have Faith in God! for surely He Is good as he is wise and great: Have Faith—for here or elsewhere, we Shall prove the harmony of Fate! Though storms the depths of Life up toss— Though breakers strew its shores with loss, And Love be slain by Hate, Believe that Evil understood, Were but the shadowy test of still prevailing Good.

That this is worthy of belief The heart-thrill it creates declares: The holy calm it brings to Grief— The promises, like heavenly airs, It breathes into the soul, to fill The failing music of the will, When broken by despairs; Till, touched as with Apollo’s rod, Even Death’s the bard of Hope—O, then have Faith in God!

Have Faith in Man! for with a mind More roomy than the world; a heart That opened by true love of kind, Might inexhaustibly impart; He yet—even he, though erring long, From the dark spells of ancient wrong Shall, like a dreamer, start! With all-atoning heed to scan The ways of Love and Truth:—have faith in God and Man.

Such was the language of my Lyre, As high on Kobanbonia’s bald And craggy top, mine heart did fire With ardours from the Past recalled; Ere the world’s warfare might prevail O’er life’s first hopes—alas, too frail, Too easily appalled! But on such altar was it vain To pray for that large trust in God and Man again? x

x As an implicit belief in God’s goodness is the fountain head of all true religion, so a large faith in the capacity of human nature for good, is the root of all genuine morality. To think nobly of our original is to respect ourselves, and thence come chastity honor and fortitude; while to believe it radically incapable and vile, is to live and die (speaking universally) on a level with this degrading estimate.
