SQUATTER SONGS.

NO. III.

[A PRIME CASE AGAINST THE GOVERNMENT]

[I] bought five thousand sheep on tick, And took them straight away To where through herbage green and thick The Wybong’s waters stray; And there, amid a mighty scope Of clear and bevil ground, And swelling with delusive hope, I called my shepherds round; And sung that song to squatters dear, Like Archy Boyd so clever, I’ll found a fleecy Earldom here For me and mine for ever.

But soon I found the Run was cleft By water-courses strange— But thirty miles unto the left Would take me in a Range; And so, upon that very night, Ere I could gain repose, I wrote unto Sir George for right To drive where’er I chose: Then sung that song to Squatters dear, Like Archy Boyd so clever, I’ll found an Earldom somewhere here For me and mine for ever.

But ah! that cruel man of blame, Just then, had ta’en in hand Some anti-earldom-founding claim That touched upon the land! And while my case before him lay, For one whole month there fell Such rain, that on the twentieth day The stream began to swell! While I, poor man! was singing clear, Like Archy Boyd so clever, I’ll found an Earldom somewhere here For me and mine for ever.

The waters hemm’d the Run; then spread Its surface wholly o’er: Their drowning charge the shepherds fled, (I fled the day before!) And now, why I no sheep have got, I’ll have it proved by Lowe, Is clearly through Sir George—and not The Wybong’s overflow! And why that song to Squatting dear, And Archy Boyd so clever, Is changed to—There’s no Earldom here For thee and thine for ever.
