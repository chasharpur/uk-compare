MY FIRST SONG.

The voice of the city with pleasure is loud, And I mix in the whirl of its festival crowd, Where others are happy and I would be gay— Yet my heart is afar in the Wildwoods away.

Where clouds robe the mountain as sails clothe a mast, And the challenge of echo rides down on the blast, And the sway of the forest is heard day and night,— There my heart, like a wild bird, is taking its flight.

Where the hunter’s halloo is glad melody when The kangaroo bounds up the gum-shaded glen; Or the roar of the wild ox blends hoarse with the bay Of the resolute dog—there my heart is, away.

Where the magpie his horn windeth merry and well, And the wood-pigeon coos from the oaks of the dell; Where love-singing birds are the fruit of each spray Round the home of my childhood—’tis there, far away.

There all voices and things, from the bird to the brook, Give life to the pages of Memory’s book! Oh, when shall I join in the many-toned strain That rejoiceth the depths of the forest again?
