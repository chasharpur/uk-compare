II.

As closely rendered.

The sweating steeds are loosened from the yoke And bound with halters, each to his own chariot. Out from the city oxen and fat sheep Are meanwhile brought, with wine and home-made bread. Then gathered faggots into fires are built, From which the savour of their supper soon Spreads o’er the plain upon the winds of heaven: While in the cheerful blaze, in ranks of war, Greatly elated feast the Trojan troops.

As when [unclear] the golden stars appear Keenly outshining round the lucid Moon, And not a breath under all heaven is blown, Distinct, the jaggēd rocks, and lofty tops Of mountains, with the darker woods, are seen Against the luminous æther, wide disclosed X And crowded thick with stars: while in his watch Gratefully gladdened is the shepherd’s heart.

Over the wide extended plain, even thus, So many and thick, the Trojan kindled fires Before the towery walls of Ilium shine, And ‘twixt the ships and the lit streams of Zanthus: A thousand fires up blazing on the plain, While in the red glare of each fire there sit Full fifty armēd men. But ranged apart, Beside the chariots o’er their fodder bide The steeds, unharnessed,—as awaiting so The beautiful-throned Aurora’s orient rise.
