Mary of Eulengo.

Let those who worship Fashion sigh At published beauty’s feet; Let him whose God is Mammon, try A wealth-dowered bride to meet; Let all whom affectation charms For city maidens glow;— But give unto my happy arms Sweet Mary of Eulengo.

If beauty that hath bloomed apart Be loveliest to behold— A spotless name and truth of heart Yet richer things than gold; If sparkling arts that lure to sin Be nought to virtue’s glow,— How matchless were my portion in Sweet Mary of Eulengo?

I see her in my dreams by night, And hear her gentle voice, And when in morning’s golden light All living things rejoice, 606 Each skiey tint of rarest grain— Each fresh delight below— All bring into my thoughts again Sweet Mary of Eulengo.

Oh, never may the frown of Fate Upon her future lour, Nor villain Guile there lie in wait To wound so fair a flower! But happiness for her abound, And love for ever flow, A bright and brimming stream, around Sweet Mary of Eulengo.
