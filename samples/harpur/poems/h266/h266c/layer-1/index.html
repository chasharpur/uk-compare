The last piece of the first Part of the Beautiful Bower

The Enquiry.

If into desolating storm Some future cloud we may not shun Should burst—and Love’s bright world deform, His and thy Poet leaving, one Scorning and scorned of heartless men— 640 My Beautiful, would you love me then?

Stung by the World’s eternal guile, Should the defiance of Despair Plant on his cheek its bitter smile, And writhe so long, and whiten there, That it might freshen ne’er agen— My Beautiful, would you love me then.

Should long dull years of absence frown, And ‘tween us ‘neath this starry Cope Such lengths of sea and land be thrown, That question thus might even Hope: How can ye ever meet agen?— My Beautiful, would you love me then?
