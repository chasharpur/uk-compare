THE DREAM.

While Fanny, my dream, I relate thee in numbers, Oh mark how devoted the lover in me, Since not e’en an image of Fancy in slumbers, Can reign for a moment divided from thee.

Methought, as I strayed thro’ this garden for pleasure, I saw golden roses flame thick on yon tree, And wond’ringly hastened to sieze the bright treasure— When straight they were miniature copies of thee.

Methought next, as ‘neath the white moon I was roaming, She brighten’d and broaden’d—descending to me; And strangely enamoured I grew at her coming— When straight she became a pale copy of thee.

Methought, then, that heaven was opened beyon’ me, With noises that rolled like the voice of the sea, And angels in thousands were gazing upon me— And every bright angel a copy of thee.

Charles Harpur.
