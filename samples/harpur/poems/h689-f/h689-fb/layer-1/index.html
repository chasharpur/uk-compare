An to this end I now enrobed my self in whites Th Aereal ultimates of perfect whiteness While scarfs of purple such as only Queen May purchase from the Indus caravans Trailed them with rainbow splendors; Of purest gold, whose midst throughout was raised With a full row of pearls—all perfect ovals— Held in the wavevelets of my sunny hair: A gem that seemed indeed a frozen mass Of white fire, flashed upon my silk soft brow And, in my loveliness, even thus arrayed I swam into his presence (though indeed My hid feet rowed) graceful as a swan!

The Harem Girls applauded: while within Even in is own dul eyes almost a flash As of extorted pleasure but which at once At the next moment a malignant scoul Which had its source in a thought like this I learned after from a Nubian Girl Who allways waited on his baser needs “What did a soft and ignorant mix like me Think to entrap again man like him Who was the sharpest fellow, past all dout In all the orient. “Did a mink like me Think to outwit a piece of such intelligence By floating in before him, like a swan A little better feathered than at first.

While this went on I slipped away to where my go[o]ds were pack —and to provide for flight Nor had I long withal—ere to the chamber The Nubian Girl came running; telling me That soon the king had with a mighty start Fall prostrate; while the Ladies all away Had scattered all away like gossamer clouds And that the chamber now was thronged instead To suffocation with a musty mass Of bare-poled hakims and gre[y]haired Physicians; Who all concurred at last in this—the dark Affair in hand was quite unlookt for quite! 703 That all around There were strong symtoms of a “poison-case.” And as she told her news the Nubian laught With infinite delight—so loved are kings. But no time might be wasted—and we fled Together, loaded both with costly spoils. Still as we passed beneath the chamber Wherein the Monorch suffered, all in vain I strove to dash the [unclear] womanly desire To have a last look at him. So aloft I mounted, on the slaves bent back—and looked The king was sprawling on the marbled floor With quite a herd of anxious quaks about him: 704 Jews & Armenians, infidels & slaves, All ranks, all races; and as still he took Their nostrums, with grimaces horrible He still kept threatening them, that if they failed To cure him ere that dreadful night was passed The dawn should see them headless—every man! Then would he curst the though that made him cho[o]se Pomegranates for his supper. But the most He cursed his blunder, and the palpable trick By which a silly woman had entrapped him! Then, would he sprawl, and bellow like a beast. And so I left him sprawling, while the sound His brute bellowings followed me beyond The garden walls, as through the dark we fled!
