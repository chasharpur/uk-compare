II

Her Eyes.

Now sunny as the noontide heavens are The eyes of my sweet Friend, and now serene And chastely shadowy in their maiden mien; Or dream-power, sparkling like a manifold star, Fills all their blue depths,—rapping me afar To where, in the rich Past, through Song is seen Some sovereign beauty—Knighthood’s mystic Queen, Pluming with love the iron brows of War! 52 Bright eyes before, with subtle lightning glance, Have kindled all my being into one Wild tumult; but a charm thus to enhance My heart’s love loyalty before had none! And can this witchcry be the work of chance? I know not—I but know my rest is gone.
