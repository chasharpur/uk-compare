III.

Rosa’s Eyes.

As sunny as the noontide heavens are The eyes of my sweet Friend, and yet serene, And shadowy for delight, like evening’s scene: And fairy Fancy, like a wingëd star, Moves in their glowing depths,—luring afar The gazer’s soul from all things dull or mean, To where the Spirit of old romance is seen Pluming with love the iron brows of War! 42 Bright eyes before, in the world’s ways, with glance Ethereal, have made my being one With passion; but such charm before had none My sense of the ideal to enhance! And works this witchery by Fate or Chance? I know not—I but know my rest is gone!
