SQUATTER SONGS.

NO. II.

[TOM BROWN, OR HAMAN IN THE BUSH]

I had a noble station As any in the land— Its hills were free from gibbers, Its valleys free from sand! But what of this? since if I rode The creek three miles adown, There sat, like Mordecai the Jew, One curst Tom Brown! Tom Brown, who With his five mile of a run, Cut my hundred thousand acre one Almost in two.

I courted the Commissioner; I feasted him; and when He praised my finest pony, I gave it to him then: I gave it to him, hoping he Would aid me in the down I had upon the station of This curst Tom Brown! Tom Brown, who With his five mile of a run Cut my hundred thousand acre one Almost in two.

But ah! the false Commissioner, He failed me at the last! Tom Brown remained and flourished, While I was breaking fast! My station grew so hateful then, I came to live in town, And took to grog, and dice, and all Through that Tom Brown! Tom Brown, who With his five mile of a run, Cut my hundred thousand acre one Almost in two.

At last I sold my station; And now with nought to do, And still a little money, I drink till all is blue! And lo, the grimest of the Fiends That haunt me up and down, Is like—even in its blueness like! That d——d Tom Brown! Tom Brown, who With his five mile of a run, Cut my hundred thousand acre one Almost in two.
