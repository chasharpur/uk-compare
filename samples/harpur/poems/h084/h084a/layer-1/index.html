A cut-and-dried Epitaph for T. C., whenever it may please the Devil to take him.

Within this tomb lies grim and cold He who was once the usurer C—ll—n: Grim and cold, without the gold He farmed so graspingly of old: But with ten worms per cent his skull in (or more perchance if all were told) Instead! And this, though it provoke a laugh, Is all that should of such a hunks be said Even in the way of Epitaph.
