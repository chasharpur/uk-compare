SONNET.

On the disrespect which certain members of the Legislative Council are in the valiant habit of evincing towards Dr. Lang.*

By Charles Harpur.

I see the “old man eloquent” arise To strip some scheme of its fallacious hue, With motives nobly naked as the skies When not a vapour stains their summer blue. ‘Tis done:—and straightway a most captious crew, By Murray led, or Morris, lift their cries And cavils, venomed with exploded lies, Or imputations not a whit more true So have I seen an eagle with strong wings Come sweeping o’er the wood:—when straight, a flight Of chattering pies sent up their jargonings, And hustled round him in their petty spite, While he, in conscious majesty and might, Moved on, unruffled by the paltry things.

* When these unseenly displays were at their height, this Sonnet was sent to the Empire for insertion, but rejected.

C. H.
