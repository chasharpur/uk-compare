One Great Question answered by Another.

Why should some suffering with each Living lot be Inevitably blent? Why should it not be?

Note. Liability to Pain is the inevitable conjunct of all physical life. Existing of necessity, in the nature of mundane things, wherever there is sentient being, having a material organism, there must suffering occasionally obtain, as a contingent of its condition: because such an organism could only be insensible to pain, under a great variety of circumstances, in the event of its being also insensible to pleasure under even a greater variety of circumstances. Hence pain or suffering is in nowise chargeable, as a direct result, upon the divine government. But why did Providence institute physical life, if it must be thus conditionally obnoxious to such a liability? The answer is triumphant. It was instituted by Providence, because this fact was in its foreknowledge, that enjoyment upon the whole would infinitely exceed the possibility of suffering; that the one would be a universal prevalence, perpetuated through millions,—the other but a partial discord (so to speak) terminating in individuals.
