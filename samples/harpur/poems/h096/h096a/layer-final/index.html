The Distance of the Dead.

How far off in a moment are the Dead! Round Mamre’s cave, four thousand years ago, A long procession up from Egypt led, Closed mourning, like a sable cloud of woe. Loud was the grief, and wide about a throe Passed from it into Nature—passed and fled! And now even where the patriarchal head Lay grey in the cold gloom, no man may know. All of those mourners died too—died at last, And fell like shadows from the world away! How far back in the dawn of time they passed Into the mortal mystery of decay; And yet our brother who but died to day Is sundered from us by a gulf as vast!
