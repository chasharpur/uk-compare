AUTUMNAL LEAVES. By Charles Harpur.

I.

Ah! that Death Should ever, like a black untimely night, Descend upon the loved in Love’s despite! Ah! that a little breath Expiring from the world, should leave each scene Where its warm influence before had been, So empty to the heart in its despair Of all but misery—misery everywhere! But loving souls, Though sundered thus by Death, must tend again To union, since they die not, and no main Save that eternity between them rolls Which in itself were nothing, did not Mind Inhabit its blank depth, and o’er it reign In spiritual kind! This is implied by suffering. What is pain But prophecy of pleasure? What is grief But preparation for some meet relief?
