The Postmaster General’s Bones.

“He hesitated not to say, that ere he would transfer his allegeance to any other sovereign or power than the Queen of England, he prayed that his bones might sink into their grave.”※ see the debate on the N. Coast:

And so at the heel of a dallop of jaw As crude as the brawl ‘twixt a rasp and a saw, He must hint that if ever our burial stones We’d enrich with so loyal a warrior’s bones, We must stick to a Chamber of Life Nominees, And the flag that has braved both the battle and breeze!

His bones? They were something indeed to bequeath If in substance at all like an elephant’s teeth; Or had they been only camp-hardened, instead Of softened so much in a snug feather bed: As it is, were a Rainer※ but asked for them here One farthing, he’d think them confoundedly dear.†

Hang his bones! If so loyal and plucky, why let him To India, where the Queen’s service would sweat him, Till perchance some gaunt Afghan might stretch him amain Like a warrior taking his rest on the plain! His bones! Of what worth were they here, I would know, Except as manure to make cabbages grow?

† At all events, a “bruder Bones” of the Ethiopians were the only kind of artist, perhaps, in the whole community, who could shape them to any purpose, or rattle anything like a tune out of them.

C.H
