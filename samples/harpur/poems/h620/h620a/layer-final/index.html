To my little Daughter Mary (1866.)

My Second Mary! Can I choose but write Of Thee, who now, in our domestic chart Dost mark the pole,—as with a starlet bright? And wherefore hast thou this so central part In all home tenderness? Because thou art Our last, thou luckily belated sprite! Thou innocent household doveling of delight With nests so many—every kindred heart! A rosebut, o’er thy Mother’s wearier days Breathing a feeling of life’s springtime bloom: A sun-flush in thy Sire’s autumnal haze: Oh, what a failure in all time to come Would meet us, even in this pleasant home, Without thy smiles and all thy pretty ways!
