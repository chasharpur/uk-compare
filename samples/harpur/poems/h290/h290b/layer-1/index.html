Noon in the Forest at Midsummer.

Not a bird disturbes the air, There is quiet everywhere; Over plains and over woods What a mighty stillness broods.

Only there’s a drowsy humming From yon Summer rill side coming: ‘Tis the dragon-hornet—see All bedaubëd gorgeously With crimson, splendid to behold Dusted o’er with mealy gold; Only there’s a droning, where Yon bright beetle gleams the air, And, rising in the sunshine higher, Seems sharded as with gems on fire.

Every other thing is still, Save the ever-wakeful rill, Whose cool murmur only throws A cooler comfort round Repose; 26 Or some ripple in the sea Of leaves, when, intermittently, Summer, in her forest bower, Turning from the noontide hour, Heaves a slumbrous breath, ere she Once more slumbers peacefully.

Oh, ’tis easeful here to lie Hidden from Noon’s scorching eye, In this grassy cool recess, Musing thus of Quietness.
