1E

THE DYING SORROW OF THE WIDOW’S BOY.

[FOUNDED ON FACT.]

I do not fear to die, mother, I do not fear to die; For is there not a better world Beyond the starry sky? But when past happy days and nights Come floating through my mind, With all thy tender looks, I weep At leaving you behind.

‘Tis leaving you behind, mother, And little sister Bell, Whom we do love so tenderly, Who loveth us so well: I’m thinking of a thousand things That we have done in play, And that she will be brotherless When I am gone away.

And who will bring you sticks, mother, And water from the well? And who—for ’tis a lonely way— Will go to school with Bell? I know ’tis sinful to repine, And good to be resigned— But, oh, I cannot cease to think Of leaving you behind.

Charles Harpur.

Jerry’s Plains, October, 1843.
