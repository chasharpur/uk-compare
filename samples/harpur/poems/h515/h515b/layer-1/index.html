The Snow Child.

Beware Child, in your gambols, Of the fatal rift below:— Thus did a Switzer warn his Boy, Amid the mountain snow. But the snow will never harm me, Replied the wayward child, As back into his Father’s face He gayly looked and smiled.

And forward then he started Like a spirit through the drift,And 118 And many a wild bound ventured he, Unmindful of the rift; Till in his heart the Father said With a feeling strange and wild, Canst thou be my own Herman— So like a Phantom Child?

And then he looked a moment out To where, with thundrous jar, An avalanche had bounded From a glittering Alp afar— He had looked away,—a moment Did hardly intervene; When lo, the little Herman’s form Was nowhere to be seen!

With a great ineffable horror Crushing in upon his heart, He stood;—then shook the glaciers With an upward shuddering start, As o’er the snow he shouted forth The vanished Herman’s name— But from those dreary echoes—hark! The only answer came!

Answer me! Come to me! he cried, In wild search to and fro, Unmindful of the fatal rift Beneath the piles of snow: Still “Herman!” shrieked the Father, As he madly rushed about— But only some hoar cavern still Responded to the shout.

And when that childless Switzer Sought his kindred in the vale, His late dark locks hung snow-like O’er a forehead icy-pale; Dread tokens of a sorrow That had bleached him, though so brief, Like the breath of many winters In a single blast of grief.

Then his kindred left him lying In the serpent coils of woe, And up into the mountains went, Amid the piles of snow: But in vain their search was heedful, Day after day,—in vain— 120 The wayward little Herman Was never seen again!

And never on the mountains Did his Sire again rejoice, Filling their icy gorges With his bold and Tell-like voice: For the vanished Herman’s Image, There snow-snatched from his side, Came freezing to his blasted heart Until the day he died.

No; never upon the mountains Did his Sire again rejoice, Filling their icy gorges With his bold and Tell-like voice: Though evermore his Child he sought, Spring after Spring,—in vain— The body of little Herman Was never seen again.

But often since the mountaineer Amid the snow-storm wild, 121 Sees through the white drift bounding A lovely Phantom Child! There bounding through the white drift, As with a frantic joy, And straightway he bethinks him Of the Hunter’s wayward Boy.
