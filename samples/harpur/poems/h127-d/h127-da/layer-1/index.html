[THE FAMOUS NIGHT SCENE IN THE VIII ILIAD.]

IV.

The same as literally as possible.

And they loosed from the yoke the sweating steeds, And fastened them with halters, Every one to his own chariot. Quickly brought they from the city oxen and fat sheep: And they brought sweet wine, and bread from their homes, And also gathered together many faggots. But the winds raised the savour (of their supper) From the plain to heaven.

But they, greatly elated, sat all night long In the ranks of war, and many fires blazed for them. As when in heaven the stars appear Very obviously around the lucid moon, When æther is used to be without a breeze, And all pointed rocks and lofty summits and groves appear; But the immense æther is disclosed in heaven, And all the stars are seen, And the shepherd rejoices in his soul.

327

Thus did many fires, by the Trojans kindled, Appear before Ilium, Between the ships and the streams of Xanthus. A thousand fires blazed in the plain, And at each blazing fire sat fifty men. But their steeds, eating white barley and oats, Were standing by the chariots, Awaiting the beautiful-throned Aurora.
