EPIGRAMS, NOTES AND OPINIONS, &c. By Charles Harpur.

I.

“Property is funded talent.”

To hold that wealth is funded nous, Is just about as right in fact. As that Tom Noodle built the house Which was his great grandfather’s act.

Note.—The above saying of Emerson is far more specious than wise. Funded acquisitiveness, good or bad, property may mostly be, though not always; but funded talent, in the best sense, it is not, however widely we consider the matter. Nay, it is often funded roguery of the lowest grade, and funded mal-appropriation of all sorts. In some cases it is even funded stupidity; and in others, (a miser’s hoard for example) it is funded insanity. The saying, then, is not wise:—it is only sweeping and specious. It is one of those smart things, in which half the truth is sacrificed to the necessity of shutting up the other half of it in a few terse words. Or, it might be likened to a barn broom too sparely birched, and which therefore, in the using, leaves as much corn after it as it carries to the heap.
