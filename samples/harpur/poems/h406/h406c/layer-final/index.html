PSALM XIII.

How long, O Lord, wilt thou forget Thy supliant’s need? For how long yet Withhold from me in this disgrace, The sovereign comfort of thy face?

How long must I commune apart With daily sorrow in my heart? How long exalted o’er me be My unrelenting enemy?

Consider, Lord, and hear my cries, And lighten with thy peace mine eyes, Lest sighing forth life’s painful breath, I sleep the heavy sleep of death!

And that mine enemies should say Our curse hath withered him away; Even God hath judged us in the right, And wholly swept him from our sight!

But I, for succor, none the less Have trusted, in thy righteousness: And yet shall teach my harp’s glad strings The healing strength thy succor brings!
