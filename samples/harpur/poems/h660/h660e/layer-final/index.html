[HEAVEN’S WHOLE ROUND WAS BRIGHT AS NOW]

Heaven’s whole round was bright as now, When homeward, long ago, I trod: Blest!—having won my Dora’s vow To love me next to God! And as homeward then I went Upon that happy far night, Up, into the lit firmament My glowing spirit rayed, and lent Even unto the star-light.

But years are gone—and Dora now Is gone too, under the green sod: No!—gone to Paradise with her vow To love me next to God.And 270 And as here aloft I gaze As on that happy far night, Heaven’s whole round is all a-blaze, And up my spirit, yearning, rays— But high above the star-light.
