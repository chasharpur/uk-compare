THE POET BOY’S LOVE WIsHEs.

(From the Poems of Charles Harper, Esq., the Australian Poet.]

O that the moon now so silverly beaming, Might come like a Lady love, blessing and blest, All in the nights of my heart restless dreaming, And pillow my head on her beautiful breast.

O that yon star which in heaven is whitest, Might come like a passionate Girl from her rest, Wheo visious of Love round my spirit are brightest, And rap me away to it’s home in the West.

Oh that the spirit oi Music by-flowing, Might take such a shape as my heart can conceive, When Love, like the soul of all Beauty is glowing And breathe in my arms like a daughter of Eve.

O that some angel of heaven appearing, But once to my wish, might descend from on high; So terribly lovely aud bright that my daring To kiss her were death—I would kiss her and die!

But the Moon with pale love of the day-god was beaming, And the Star of some star that attracts her was dreaming, And the music remained an invisible birth, And no daughter of bliss might descend to the Earth; Yet not all vainly his spirit was burning Tow’rds the rare and the deathless—the fruits of its yearning Had blossom in Eden—nor shall they be hurled Away with the perishing weeds of the world[.]
