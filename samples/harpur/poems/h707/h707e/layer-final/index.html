Yearnings.

“Ah, that yon Moon, when thus silverly beaming, Might come through the spells of my passionate dreaming, In the guise of a lady fair, blessing and blest, And pillow my head on her beautiful breast!

“Ah, that yon Star which in heaven is whitest, Might come when Love’s visions around me are brightest,— Like a fancy-pale Maiden dream-drawn from her rest,— And rap me away to its home in the West!

“Ah, that the Spirit of music by flowing, When Love, the pure test of all beauty, is glowing, Might take such a shape as my heart can conceive, And breathe in my arms like a daughter of Eve!

“Ah, that some snowy-wing’d Angel of heaven, But once to the sight of my spirit were given, So rich in the beauty born with [her] on high, That to love her were death—I would love her and die!”

Thus sang a Poet Boy wandering lonely Whilst yet he had tasted life through his dreams only: 106 Born deep into beauty, of beauty he dreamed As the queen of all being—how vainly it seemed!

For the Moon with pale love of the day-god was beaming, And the Star of some star that attracts her was dreaming, And the Music remained an invisible birth, And no Daughter of bliss might descend to the earth!

Yet not all vainly her spirit was burning Tow’rds the rare and the deathless;—the fruits of its yearning Had blossom in Eden, nor shall they be hurled Away with the perishing weeds of the world.
