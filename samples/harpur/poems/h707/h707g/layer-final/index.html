Yearnings

“Ah, that yon moon, when thus silverly beaming, Might come through the spells of my passionate dreaming, In the guise of a lady fair, blessing and blest, And pillow my head on her beautiful breast!

“Ah, that yon star which in heaven is whitest, Might come when Love’s visions around me are brightest,— Like a fancy-pale Maiden dream drawn from her rest And rap me away to its home in the West!

“Ah, that the Spirit of music by flowing, When Love, the pure test of all beauty is glowing, Might take such a shape as my heart can conceive, And breath in my arms like a daughter of Eve!

“Ah, that some snowy-winged Angel of Heaven, But once to the Light of my spirit were given, So rich in the beauty born with her on high, That to love her were death—I would love her and die!”

Thus sang a Poet Boy wandering lonely Whilst yet he had tasted life through his dreams only: Born deep into beauty, of Beauty he dreamed As the queen of all being—how vainly it seemed!

For the Moon with pale loss of the day-god was beaming, And the Star of some star that attracts her was dreaming, And the Music remained an invisable birth, And no Daughter of bliss might descend to the earth!

Yet not all vainly his spirit was burning Tow’rds the rare and the deathless;—the fruits of its yearning 99 Had blossom in Eden, nor shall they be hurled Away with the perishing weeds of the world.
