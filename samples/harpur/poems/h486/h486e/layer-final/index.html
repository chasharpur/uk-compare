[A RURAL PICTURE]

All simple sights of rural life to me Are fresh or beautiful. Look down the stream Where past the first broad shade a dappled Cow Stoops her meek head over the grassy brink, And then with rapid and strong gulphs, indraws The liquid joy: and though at length sufficed, Still over it she keeps her neck declined And breathes an herby breath upon the water, Ruffling the image of herself below, As there, with pendent head and dappled sides, It stands reversed: Herself and Shadow,—both Total expressions of an utter comfort.
