POETRY.

EVENING AND EARLY NIGHT.

(From the “Poet’s Wish.” an unpublished Poem.)

[...] Then woodward whilst I drove the unharnessed steer, Or for the kine was searching, somewhere near Grouping full-fed, in ruminating mood, The Sun should light upon his western hill, And slant his last beams through the shadowy wood, And up the gleaming rill:

Then should he sink, and make the cloulds above Seem rich idealisms of the love My heart poured out on Nature,—and on Her Now waiting me at our peace-hallowed board! Thus placed, who’d care amongst the great to stir, Or with the rich to hoard?

The pens secured—the final meal in haste Dispatched, though savoury, both should forth, to taste Eve’s odourous breath; and with renewed surprise, To find Elysiums painted in the West: And then we’d look in [to] each other’s eyes, To feel that we were blest!

112b

And when the gloaming followed evening’s flight, And over yonder hills a skiey light Yet mellowed upward, near to where, first seen, The glowing leader of the starry quire Comes wingedly from out the blue Serene, Even like a bird of fire;—

The hushing spirit of such twilight hours Sinking into our Souls, as in the flowers’ Balm-breathing bosoms fall the silent dews, Should temper, so each feeling mild and wise, And thus o’er all our charities diffuse The quiet of the skies.

So should the Night come on, in solemn quise, To look with all her far ethereal eyes Upon my happy Life, and draw my soul To wander like a star the stars among, And homeward point from the resplendent pole Uranian beams of song.

Or whilst the Moon, the world’s apparent queen, Came whitening up in Majesty serene, Reminding us of some sweet long past night, I’d chronicle in rhyme the many things Of lovely thought, that from her mystic light Had woven then their wings.

CHARLES HARPUR.
