My only Hate.

I never thoroughly abhorred a creature, Except one man, the grossness of whose race, And instincts, was expressed through every feature— A burly beast with a huge faithless face! His presence cursed me first with a despair, Mighty and mournful, which I could not chase; 409 And then came absolute hatred! seeing there Before me, breathing the same vital air, God’s image in such man-begot disgrace! And even after he had passed away Out of my world-path, ne’er to meet me more, My brotherhood in human nature lay In my esteem, how infinitely lower Than when, in the glad morning of my day, I took my measures from the Muse in chief, To robe—a Dream! in her divine belief. Yet was then what in that dream of yore Of which made Prometeus a thief— That fire of heaven efficient to instore The earth-dulled Spirit of Humanity; With what we now but in pure vision see; Because in being he would higher be— Else were all prophesy illusion’s core, And soul itself, when most God-like, and free, Only the soaring dupe of its own piety
