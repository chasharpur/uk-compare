My only Hate.

I never thoroughly abhorred a creature, Except one man, the grossness of whose race, And instincts, was expressed in every feature— A burly beast with a hard faithless face! His presence cursed me first with a despair, Mighty and mournful, which I could not chase; 409 And then came absolute hatred! seeing there Before me, breathing the same vital air, God’s image so infernally debased! And even after he had passed away Out of my neighborhood, ne’er to meet me more, My brotherhood in human nature lay In my esteem, how infinitely lower Than when, in the glad morning of my day, I looked at Manhood through the Muse’s lore To assess—a Dream! in her divine belief. Yet was then somewhat in that dream of yore Of which made Prometeus a thief; Else were this world illusion to the core, And soul itself, when God-like most, would be Only the dupe of its own piety
