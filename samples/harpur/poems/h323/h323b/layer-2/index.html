My Only Hate.

I never utterly abhorred a creature, Except one man, the grossness of whose race Yet more embruted in his carnal case, Was bluntly written upon every feature— A burly beast with a huge faithless face! His presence cursed me first with a despair, Mighty and mournful, which I could not chase; And then came absolute hatred! seeing there Before me, breathing the same vital air, God’s image in such Man-begot disgrace! And even after he had passed away Out of my world-path, ne’er to meet me more, My brotherhood in human nature lay In my esteem, how infinitely lower Than when in the glad morning of my day I took my measures from the Muse in chief, To robe—a Dream! in her divine belief! Yet was there somewhat in that dream of yore Of what once made Prometheus a thief, Because in being he would higher be— That fire of heaven efficient to restore The earth-dulled Spirit of Humanity! Else were all prophecy illusion’s lore, And Love itself when most godlike and free Only the soaring dupe of its own piety!

219

(Note to my only Hate)

This was written a long time ago, and was then pretty true of me, as being expressive of my only decided social hate. But I have since had to pass through the ordeal of the world—and consequently, the subject of the epigram, has somewhat multiplied upon my hands. Ah, that ‘consequently‘! It flows out of a truth, but an infernally ugly one.
