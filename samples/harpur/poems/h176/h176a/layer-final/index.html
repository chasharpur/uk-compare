[THE HONEY BIRD]

Wherever in some wildwood bower There blooms a honey-yielding flower, There too dwells a bird to sup Out of its delicious cup, And sing betimes, lest it should be O’erfed into satiety: So wherever Loveliness Dwells retired—dwells to bless, Not dazzle: there some destin’d spirit, Feeding on its luscious merit, Can at peace with Passion be Only through sweet Poesy.
