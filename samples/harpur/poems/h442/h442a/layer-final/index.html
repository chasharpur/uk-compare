XXV.

The Influence of Love through the Memory.

How much of God’s great world—Rosa, how much Has thy surpassing love made rich for me Of what was once unprized! A nameless tree— A wilding flower—the merest hues that touch The common clouds, and mountain summits,—these; 60 With every woodland murmur, every song Of bird or brook, as heard to gush along In each escape of some bough-tangled breeze;— Have thence a charm to sweeten to the core My heart, and quicken its capacity Of loving joy—such as was mine before Only in fancy, and that floweth free From things thus frequent now, because they store Some interwoven memory of thee.
