The Apology.

If, when its highest tone Your revelry shall gain, 298 I stand, unconsciously alone, As lost in dreams of pain; Friend, be the fact not lightly told, For wheresoever turned they be, Mine eyes are aching to behold What they no more should see.

Another’s lack of truth Hath slain my spirit’s rest! The rose of joy I plucked in youth Lies blasted on my breast! A drear cloud broods o’er all I see, Though steeped in pleasure’s burning ray, Since eyes that brightened once for me Have turned their light away.

And yet through the wild woe, At times some Power benign Will whisper of a strength to grow From weakness such as mine. Struck low, abased, but unaccursed— Unmaddened even by despair, My peace I may retrieve—but first This burthen must I bear.
