Wellington.

Great captain, if you will! great duke! great slave! Great minion of the Crown! but a great Man He was not!—He? the iron instrument Of mere Authority! the atheist Of a conventional and most earthy duty! To whom the ‘powers that be’ were simply not Of God, but in his stead! Shall we prophane All righteous instinct, and belie all truth, By calling great a man without a heart? One who, apart from the despotic wills Of crown’d Opressors Oppressors, knew no right, no wrong, No faith, no country, and no brotherhood? If such a man were great, may God most high Spare henceforth to our universal race All greatness—seeing it may sometimes be A rigid, kindless battlement of Power Self-throned and sanctioned only by the sword. And if, as Englishmen are proud to boast, 324 He was their greatest Countryman, alas! For England’s national sterility. But they who thus belaud him—lie! as all True patriots most feelingly perceive. Besides, he was not England’s son at all: He was an Irishman with whom the name Of Ireland was a scoff! an Irishman, Who for a hireling’s meed and ministry, Could tear away from his inhuman heart The pleading Image of his Native Land!

Chas: Harpur.
