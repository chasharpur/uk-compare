Personal and other Sonnets.

A Love-Dream.

In sleep my brain was with a sweet pang wrung, And straight I seemed to shoot in spirit away Into some World whereon a lovelier day Than ours glowed purpling amid arbors, hung With star-bright fruit; and where bird-spirits sung, Unceasingly, a bliss-inspiring lay, While streams of living splendor in their play Chimed to it, as with many a mellow tongue. I seemed alone: When from a bowery place A silver-wingéd Shape came moving fast, And as I looked on its approaching face, Some thrilling memory of its beauty pass’d Over my soul;—and then, that wingéd Grace Was Rosa! smiling as I saw her last.
