[RECORDS OF A POET’S LOVE IX]

In sleep my soul seemed with a sweet pang wrung, And straight in spirit I shot beam-like away Into some world whereon a lovelier day Than ours came purpling over arbors, hung With star-bright clusters; while bird-spirits sung, Unceasingly, a bliss-inspiring lay; And streams of glory, as in conscious play, Chimed into symphony those shades among. I seemed alone:—when from a bowery place A silver-wingëd Shape came moving fast, And as I looked on its approaching face, Methought some thrilling memory of it passed Into my soul—and then, that wingëd Grace Was Nora! smiling as I saw her last.
