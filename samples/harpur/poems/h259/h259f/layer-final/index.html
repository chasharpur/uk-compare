IV

Love Budded

The mimosas are blooming, The summer is coming, 31 I felt her warm breath in the forest to day: Where the river is streaming And nature lies dreaming And now love and beauty; come, dearest, away!

Wh

ere, as infancy clinging, The woodvine is ringing All wildly the boughs of the kerrijong tree, There like Loves first feeling, It’s joy half concealing, Already the blue bell is blooming for thee.

Th

e gentle mate wooing, The wood dove is cooing Where tall oaks o’ershadow the path we will take; Come forth then,—well knowing How greatly all glowing And beautiful beings please more for thy sake

We

will wander joy drinking, Untill the sun, sinking, Shall set all the West with his glory a-blaze; Where homeward returning With poesy burning, I’ll mint from those splendors a song in thy praise
