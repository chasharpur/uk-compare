LITERARY REGISTER.

SONG FOR THE SPRING TIME.

The mimosas are blooming, For summer is coming, I felt her warm breath in the forest to day; Where the river is streaming And nature lies dreaming Of new love and beauty—come, dearest, away.

Like passion’s first feeling, Its joy half concealing, In each green nook some wild flower is blowing for thee; Where, as Infancy clinging, The wood-vine is ringing, All wildly, the boughs of the Kerrijong tree.

His gentle mate wooing, The wood pigeon’s cooing In the oaks that o’ershadow the path we will take; Like music outflowing, Come forth, that all glowing And beautiful things may please more for your sake.

We will wander, joy drinking, Until the Sun, sinking, Shall give the deep west with his glory to blaze; When homeward returning, With poesy burning, I[’]ll mint from those splendors a song in your praise.

CHARLES HARPUR.
