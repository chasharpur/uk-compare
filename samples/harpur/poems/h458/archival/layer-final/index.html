A RESOLUTION.

What shall prevent me, if I will that Homer, Virgil and Shakspeare; Spencer, Milton, Keates, Wordsworth and Shelley, do in death become My spiritual Kindred all? And what Prevent, if by a resolute exercise Of thought I will me to attain a mind Replenished with all high and lovely things That play, like smiles in a bright Countenance, Over the frank and open face of Nature? Man cannot, and God will not!—this I feel! And therefore wed me to a Resolution, Which henceforth of mine inmost soul shall be The all-inseparable Spouse; to walk Daily abroad with me, as hand in hand; And nightly, at the failing of the lamp,— A last look taken of the yearning stars, And beautiful throned moon alive in Heaven,— Sleep in my bosom, strengthening as it sleeps.

1841.
