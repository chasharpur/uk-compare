IV.

[AUTUMNAL LEAVES 4]

Such is the strange succession of our Moods That life can never quite consistent be; One thought delights—another then intrudes To shrink the former into vanity! “Ah! that our Life so wildly vast Had Reason for the lord of all its doing!” I cried aloud, the world of dreams eschewing: And then my travel o’er the lossful past Which hath such grace at last, Brought up another Mood that thus went racing: “Ah! for the fancies fond and bright That were the playmates of my trustful youth! Ah! thy so cold and disenchanting light, Men do not love it, Truth! For verily that light is ever cold, And therefore withering. Who would live, if Reason Ruled over us alone, to make us old As winter even in life’s greenest season?”
