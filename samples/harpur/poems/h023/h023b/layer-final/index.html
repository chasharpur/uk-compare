III

Moods.

Such is the swift succession of our moods That Life can never quite consistent be: One thought delights—another then intrudes, To shrink the former into vanity! “Ah, that our Life, so wildly w vast, Had reason for the lord of all its doing!” I cried:—and then my travel o’er the Past Brought up another mood that thus went rueing: “Ah, for the fancies fond and bright That were the playmates of my trusting Youth! Ah, thy so cold and disenchanting light Withdraw—withdraw,—men do not love thee,—Truth! For verily thy light is cold, And therefore withering. Who would live, if Reason Ruled over us alone, to make us old As winter even in Life’s greenest season?”
