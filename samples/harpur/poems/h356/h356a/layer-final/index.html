3483d

TWO SONNETS.

On the Easter Illumination of St. Peter’s, at Rome.

BY CHARLES HARPUR.

I.

Four thousand lamps of gold and silver light Suspended round the mighty dome, and o’er Those lines of statues at their awful height, And thence down to the ground,—together pour A blaze that might seem reft from the sun’s core, The while it bringeth out of the black night The whole vast structure on the astonished sight— Towers, columns, windows, with their chiselled lore! ‘Tis beautiful! ’tis grand!—But can it call Deliverance to those who pine and bleed Under the brutal Austrian’s bloody thrall? Alas! one thought of fire—one burning deed, Struck from Mazzini’s patriotic creed, O prostrate Italy, transcends it all!
