On the Easter Illumination of St Peter’s at Rome.

Four thousand lamps of gold and silver light Suspended round the mighty dome, and o’er Those rows of statues at their awful height, And thence even to the ground,—together pour A blaze that might seem reft from Etna’s core! Bringing, at once,—lit suddenly,—on the sight The whole vast structure out of the black night— Towers, columns, windows, with their carven lore! The spectacle is grand: But can it call Deliverance unto those who pine and bleed 127 Under the Austrian’s vandalic thrall? Alas! one thought of fire—one bur deed Evoked by Garibaldi’s patriot creed, O sundered Italy! transcends it all!

1860.
