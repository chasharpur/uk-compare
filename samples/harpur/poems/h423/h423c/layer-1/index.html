[RECORDS OF A POET’S LOVE VI]

It was a tale of passion that we read— Of two who loved, not happily, but well; And evermore her gentle breast did swell Like a twin billow,—for her feelings fed Upon its musical sorrow—and, brimming, shed Such dews of pity as can only fall From spirits full of sweetness, when the pall Of Tragedy o’ershadows them with dread. 6a Then, as I looked, in her raised eye there stood A gem more excellent than ever shined In that famed mine beyond the Indian flood: And looking, how I longed to have enshrined Even in my yearning heart, that dewy sphere— So to baptise its love with an immortal Tear.
