XXXII.

The same subject in another view.

The voyage to the haven of True Love Was ever stormy since the world began, Or story from its earliest fountain ran; Teaching us truly that the Fates approve Nought that is powerless Nature’s heart to move, In the superior destinies of Man. Hence was Leander’s life so brief a span, 66 When, weltering a mortal while above The bursting wave, he sent his soul to where His Grecian beauty from the watch tower’s height Looked for his coming through the troubled air, Nor knew that he had died for her that night: Hence Sappho’s fatal leap (the cause the same): Hence Petrarch’s heart was martyred to his flame.
