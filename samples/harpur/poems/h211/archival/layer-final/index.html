ORIGINAL POETRY.

THE KNIGHT OF BUNKUM.

By Charles Harpur.

Well stored with feeds, a plain M. D. He swore he would no longer be, And knowing that the readiest way To curry fame in Botany Bay, (Where merit wins not,—no, nor sense, But cunning lapped in impudence) Was to turn Senatorial Quack, With squatter influence for his back: And so the hustings heard him spout His mental ginger beer about, Till folly sanctioned his intents. His merits?—bah! His sense?—ha, ha? But he was in at all events.

ll, Billy’s belly aked one day, Till, crack! he charmed the blast away! And a great squatter roared with gout, Till, splash! his physic cleaned him out! A liberal, too, could nought but cough, Till, lo, his lung-spells killed it off! And then to each, with that bland smile That creams him when he would beguile, He said, should my ambition e’er Mount tow’ards yon presidential chair, Your votes will surely plume its wing? My merits may Be dull as clay, But then I’m dying for the thing.

e way he worked the oracle With others, let those worthies tell. But when M’Leay resigned the chair, He sat in all his sleekness there. Yet not quite satisfied:—I ween, Quoth he, that we should nudge the Queen, How much through me she might reflect On this great Council her respect. And so the hint was given to Vic, Who could not but divine the trick: Yet since ’tis wished—ands ince (said she) His claim ‘s a dish That’s bunkumish, The Knight of Bunkum let him be.
