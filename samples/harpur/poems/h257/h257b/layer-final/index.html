The First Thrill

Her hand in mine was laid By chance—her hand so fair, And straight within my bosom played A thrill so keenly rare, 30 ‘Twas as a bee had stung my heart, And then, by honeying o’re the part, Turned all to sweetness there.

And if an unmeant touch Of her fair hand, though[t] I, A thrilling power of such A stinging sweetness;—why, To drink the fragrance of her breath While tasting her rich lips, were death! The death that I would die!
