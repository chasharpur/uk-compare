HOPE’S PHILOSOPHY.

Power’s a cheat, success but trying, Even pleasure bears a sting; Still ’tis useless—useless sighing! Rather list to Hope replying: “Flowers will come again with Spring! And in the trampled way we’re going There’s yet one stream of comfort flowing— Hark! I hear it murmuring.”

Fame’s a liar, beauty fading, Love too hath a wandering wing; Still forbear—forbear upbraiding! Rather list to Hope persuading: “Flowers will come again with Spring! And in the desert way we’re going There’s yet one stream of bounty flowing— Hark! I hear it murmuring.”

Friendship turns, itself denying, Even truth the heart may wring; Still, though faith be daily dying, Listen still to Hope replying: “Flowers will come again with Spring! And in the blasted way we’re going There’s yet one healing current flowing— Hark! I hear it murmuring.”

All is dream like, all is fleeting, All that time and tide can bring! Then comes death!—’tis vain intreating! Rather list to Hope repeating: “Heaven is one eternal Spring! And through the midst there rolls a river Wherein to bathe is joy for ever— Hark! I hear it murmuring.”
