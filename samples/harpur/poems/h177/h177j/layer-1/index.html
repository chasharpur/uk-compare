3 X

Lyrics. By Charles Harpur.

Hope On.

Power’s a cheat, success but trying, Even pleasure bears a sting; Still ’tis useless—useless sighing— Rather list to Hope replying: “The Flowers will come again with Spring! And in the trampled way we’re going Streams of comfort yet are flowing— Hark! I hear them murmuring.”

Fame’s a liar, state’s invasion, ※ Love too hath a wayward wing; Still, hence seek not for occasion To impugn Hope’s sweet persuasion: “That Flowers will come again with Spring!” And in the world-wide way we’re going Streams of pure good yet are flowing— Hark! I hear them murmuring.

Friendship turns, itself denying, Even truth the heart may wring; Still, though trust be daily dying, 4 Listen still to Hope replying: “The Flowers will come again with Spring! And in the blasted way we’re going There’s yet one healing current flowing— Hark! I hear it murmuring.”

All is dream-like, all off wingeth— All that time and tide can bring! Then cold Death his black pall bringeth— But what matter while Hope singeth: “Lo, Heaven is one eternal Spring! And midway through it rolls a river Wherein to bathe is joy for ever— Hark! I hear it murmuring.”

※ This expression was once objected to by an intelligent reader to whom the poem was read, as being too obviously sought for the rhyme’s sake only. But the expression had been well considered, and is true on all sides. State,—whether a squireen’s or a monarch’s,—is emphatically invasion upon our time, our means, our peace. First it is invasion in every way upon the self disposal of clients, and then, through them, and cumulatively, upon that of its individual centre. So much for hasty criticism.
