LYRICS. By Charles Harpur.

I.

HOPE’S PHILOSOPHY.

Gold’s a cheat, there’s no denying; Love too hath a wandering wing; Still ’tis useless,—useless sighing— Rather list to Hope replying: “Flowers will come again with Spring! And in the desert way we’re going There’s yet one stream of bounty flowing— Hark! I hear it murmuring.”

Fame’s a liar; Beauty fading; Hope herself may plant a sting; Still forbear,—forbear upbraiding— Rather list to her persuading: “Flowers will come again with Spring.”

Friendship turns, itself denying— Yea, itself! the heart to wring; Yet, even though all faith be dying, List the while to Hope replying: “Flowers will come again with Spring! And in the desert way we’re going There’s yet one stream of bounty flowing— Hark! I hear it murmuring.”
