112

Hope’s Persuasion.

Power is risk, success but trying, Even pleasure bears a sting; Still how useless is all sighing— Rather list to Hope’s voice crying: “Life shall blom again with Spring! And in the trampled way we’re going Streams of comfort yet are flowing— Hark! I hear them murmuring.”

Fame’s a liar, state’s invasion, Even joy may sorrow bring; Still hence seek not for occasion To impugn Hope’s sweet persuasion: “Life shall bloom again with Spring! And in the world-wide way we’re going Streams of pure good yet are flowing— Hark! I hear them murmuring.”

Friendship turns, itself denying, Love too hath a wayward wing; Still though trust be daily dying, Listen still to Hope’s voice crying: “Life shall bloom again with Spring! And in the blasted way we’re going, There’s one healing stream yet flowing— Hark! I hear it murmuring.”

All is dream-like, all off wingeth— All that time and tide can bring! Then cold Death his black pall bringeth— 113 Yet what matter, while Hope singeth: “Heaven is one eternal Spring! And midway through it rolls a river Filled with health that holds for ever— Hark! I hear it murmuring.”
