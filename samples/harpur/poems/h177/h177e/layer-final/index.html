Hope On

Gold’s a cheat, there’s no denying, Love too hath a faithless wing; Still ’tis useless, useless sighing— Rather list to Hope replying: 26 “Flowers will come again with Spring! And in the desert way we’re going There’s yet one stream of bounty flowing— Hark! I hear it murmuring.”

Fame’s a liar, Beauty fading, Hope itself may plant a sting; Still forbear, forbear upbraiding— Rather list to its persuading: “Flowers will come again with Spring!

Friendship turns, itself denying,— Yea, itself! the heart to wring; Still, even though all faith be dying, Ever list to Hope replying: “Flowers will come again with Spring! And in the desert way we’re going There’s yet one stream of bounty flowing— Hark! I hear it murmuring.
