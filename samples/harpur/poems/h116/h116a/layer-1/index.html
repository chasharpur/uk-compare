The Emigrant Girl

She is going in the beauty And the freshness of her youth; From the honest hearts that love her In the spirit of their truth: For comfort or for sorrow, To obey or to command, Beneath another heaven In a far and foreign land.

There is grief upon the waters! For the anchor now is weighed, And the ship is moving outward, And the last farewel is said, By the fond old friends who knew her In her better days of yore, And whose hearts are moaning in them— We shall never see them more!

There is hope upon the waters! And waved is many a scarf From the boat of those returning, In their sadness, to the wharf: And she is calm, though pensive, For the bout of grief is o’er, And her soul is roaming outward Tow’rds a far and foreign shore

Could we look into the future! Could we charm its cloud away! Beneath another heaven, With some mountain vale, Beside some savage river Or on some prarie wide, We might see a wretched outcast Or behold a happy bride.

But vainly would we vision What the future must conceal, And let it comfort nature To wish largely for her weal: And is she not a Woman? And how can aught so dear, From straits that are human Have anything to fear?
