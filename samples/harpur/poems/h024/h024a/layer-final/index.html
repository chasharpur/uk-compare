Fragment.

A thousand million souls arise From out the cradle of To-day, And pass like a storm ‘neath the calm blue skies; But when To-morrow’s sun His journey hath begun— —O, Where are they? Asks pale Dismay, And Faith, even Faith herself, cannot say!

I

confess, that When I view a whole generation of men—my own (for a particular instance) as streaming stormily forward in the expanse of time it knows not whither, the vision well-nigh appals me. I cannot contemplate, without a possitive feeling of dismay, its mighty mass of living soul, just bursting up as it were out of the past, to reveal itself for a moment in one broad bright lapse of present thought and passion, and then sink into the Unforeseen like a human Niagara forever. The tremendous myst[er]y of life, thus bodily amassed, will, for the occasion, shrink even my spiritual faith into a physical shudder—and I apprehend the 142 above fragment to be the extemporaneous expression of some such contemplative mood as I have here forth shadowed by the way of note to it.

C

.H.
