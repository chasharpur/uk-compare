II.

A thousand million souls arise From out the cradle of To-day, And pass, like a storm, ‘neath the calm blue skies: But when To-morrow’s sun His journey hath begun, Where are they? Asks pale Dismay, and Faith, even Faith herself, cannot say!

Note. Whenever I regard a whole generation [of] men, my own for instance, as streaming stormily [for]ward in the expanse of time, it knows not whither, [the] vision well nigh appals me. I cannot contemplate [with]out a positive feeling of dismay, its mighty 600 mass of living soul, just bursting up as it were out of the past, to reveal itself itself for a moment in one broad bright eddying torrent of present thought and passion, and then sink into the Unforeseen like a human Niagara forever! The tremendous mystery of life, thus bodily amassed, will shrink, for the occasion, even my spiritual reliance into a physical shudder: and I apprehend the above Fragment to be an extemporaneous ex= =pression of some such contemplative mood of mind as I have here forthshadowed by way of Note to it.
