THE COMING ON OF FREEDOM.

The Day must come,—for Freedom must keep pace With Knowledge,—when the accident of birth, Or emptier imposition of mere pelf, Shall neither arrogate to any Man Distinction as of right, nor qualify Any for power, by a sole influence, Over his Fellows! But all Men shall stand Up proudly under the wide roof of Heaven As God-created Equals; each the sire Of his own worth, and the joint sanctioner Of all political pertainment—all Moral and social honor! Yes; for such Is Freedom’s Charter, written on the heart Of capable Humanity when purged From the foul stains of Time, and his foul rout Of arrogant, hell-suggested Superstitions, And Customs born of Error. And let none Despair of such an Advent: for, as when The solemn Wood’s familiar cadences, Deepening and deepening all around, portend The salutary Storm; even so, the all- Pervading spirit of a sure revolt Against the ancient Tyrannies of the Earth, Roams on before it in the living stress Of Knowledge,—omening the unborn Change By harshening still, to the fine ear of thought, The daily jar of customary Wrongs.
