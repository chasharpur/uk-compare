A Groan in Spirit.

A Laborer’s groan in spirit, Truth-touched with a little of comfort yet, In a Rhyme with the gold of Truth for its merit, Like a gun, will I set.

A brave poor man, with a want-ground face, Winning by toil a large family’s dole, Daring to run it, though tired of Life’s race, Hark, how he is groaning in soul:

“Poor, poor—evermore poor! Daily I toil, and yet daily endure (While my sweat raineth down on the sin-accursed sod) Evils for which I can compass no cure, And with no help from Man, with none even from God! For, in sooth, so it seemeth—Alas, for the poor! For Destiny’s outcasts—the evermore poor!

But though I must labor thus, early and late, In that is there aught that I should be misdeemed for? No: he who maintains a brave battle with Fate, Hath the more in himself to be praised and esteemed for The sheerer his lack of inherited means, And the less upon others his poverty leans.”

And so doth his groan Expire in a truth, which, though cold as a stone, Hath some comfort for one who much needs it— 384 The suff’rer himself, since he at least heeds it. Nay, stands in the strength of it nobly alone, Through his blood through his suffering feeds it.
