The [unclear] ”Temple of Infamy” was the result, in part, of the spectator’s refusal to insert the following Letter and Squib, elicited by a spiteful attack made by this “eminent pen” upon the author’s poetry in the concluding Lecture of a Series delivered by him at the “School of Arts.”

618

[THE TEMPLE OF INFAMY]

Till, by the virtue of her touch, a ray Of grace renewed me—when she said, Away! Lest breathing longer the unholy air Of this bad place, thy mental health impair. All hast thou seen, the vilest of their kind, ‘Gainst whom the lightning that shall strike them blind Thou hast to launch! Be resolute. For know As well might roses upon thistles grow, As those of Truth and Beauty, ’mid their hate And envy, flourish uncontaminate! Nor fear thou Villany in high estate, Even though the unthinking World esteem it great: The noblest cast of mind is that which can But venerate the dignity of Man; And not the mere puffed dignities of men, Which oft, in truth, are but a social wen. The sword of Scorn, unsparingly to wield, I give thee—sweep them from the public field! To such thou must be cruel, to be kind Unto thy Country’s morals, and her mind!

She ceased: and hastily we then withdrew: “To morrow to fresh fields and pastures new.”
