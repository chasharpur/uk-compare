The Poet’s Burial

Not in the shadow of your pompous fanes Where drilled Convention,—not free Nature, reigns, And hirelings pile the monumental stones,— Presume not there to lay the Druid’s bones! But when his mission underneath the skies Is ended, and the destined Poet dies, Bury him—bury him in a time-hewn cave On some lone Cape, where the perpetual wave, For ever rising to the wind’s long roar, Beats a loud dirge upon the sounding shore. 66 Lay earthly heads beneath the crowded sod— But his was halved with the fire of God! They at the feet of Power their conscience cast— But he was Freedom’s pilgrim to the last! They played a legal or a canting part— But his great law and gospel was the heart! Should you then mingle in your common graves Even his ashes with the dust of slaves.
