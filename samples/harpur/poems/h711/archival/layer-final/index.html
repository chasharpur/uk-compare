PETRARCHIAN MUSINGS, OR THE HUNDRED SONNETS OF LOVE. BY CHARLES HARPUR.

ROSA’S FRIENDSHIP.

Since fortune with her precious friendship crowned me, Joy in my heart its ancient place can find; As a long lost dove, driven by the wind Far seaward, might regain the land’s green bound’ry. But ’tis not that her beauty s charms surround me So much as that her loveliness of mind Hath brightened my experience, and refined The rugged bond wherewith my fate hath bound me, And the harsh objects of my daily life; Soothing my growing tendency to strife With the stern world, by the transfusion mild Around me of her spirit undefiled: Well now I weet, how with a blessed wife, A man may grow as toward as a child.
