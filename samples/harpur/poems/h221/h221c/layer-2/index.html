ᴓ Lyrics. By Charles Harpur.

Desiderium.

‘Tis long ago—but oft, even yet, The death-calm visage of a Child Comes shrouded from the past, to re-beget An ancient sorrow wild! The face of him—our gentle-eyed Henry,—the little Boy that died.

I heard an outcry in the night And then a deep sad voice, that said ‘Tis even so; my boding heart was right— Our poor sick Boy is dead! And yet my sense of loss but seemed Like some vague wild thing I had dreamed.

But when that solemn night was passed, The feeling of how wide a sea Had in the ghostly darkness rolled its vast Black depth ‘twixt him and me, Even as I breathed it, damped the air With life’s first terrible despair!I

I sought a lonely spot, and lay Prone on the ground for very woe! And what (O God!) if my wild grief that day Did in its wildness show That we, on Time’s change-beaten shore Had parted—never to meet more!

But no! that cannot—cannot be! In soul we yet must meet again— This love, my Brother, mourning yet for thee, Can ask it not in vain; Nor Heaven design a rent more fell Than were reunion though in Hell!
