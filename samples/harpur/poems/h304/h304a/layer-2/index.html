Mr Mort’s Testamonial

676b I have a query to propound upon the text But first I would further premise a few words. 676c I believe Mr. Mort to be a very worthy man. 676d I believe that when Mr Manning said that he “had never heard any expressions of him, in public or private matters, that did do him honor” he spoke sincerely, and was not altogether overpraising him. Nay, I even think that his permanent withdrawal from amongst us would be a social loss. 676e With this much premised, I will now propound my query. Suppose Mr Mort to be in every respect just as worthy as he is: but suppose, at the same time, that instead of his having amassed by his personality and enterprise carreer an immense fortune, he had only been able, through the shortcomings of others or through some malevolence of fortune, to have just kept himself afloat; and with this fact patent to the community, were about separting from our shores—would the intending Testimonialists, whose names are blazoned at length in the public prints be altogether so ready to come forward with their valedictory incense to his worth and conduct? No not likely they would, and I think nobody else believes they would or that the Herald and Empire would keep ... columns open to catch the drippings of the occasion. 675a Nay, many an honest fellow who shall read this, will be ready to assert, on the strength of his own experience, that some of them, in the reversed case I have supposed, would scarcely turn upon their heels to give him a parting smile.
