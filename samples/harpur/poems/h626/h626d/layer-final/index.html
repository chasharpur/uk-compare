To Robert Sydney, the Infant Son of my Friend, Mr. Henry Parkes.

Ay, crow away! Thy little Being thrilling is Like the embodied carol of a bird! Thine eyes are like two fountains under stirred By their own gush—and a new-minted shilling is Less bright than thy whole face, as full up at me ‘Tis lifted now with a most gamesome meaning, Giving much merry roguery to my gleaning, Till hindered by some bold attempt to pat me! When from thy sterning mood, methinks, a spirit Of lion-mettle, and yet mild withal, Looks out, prophetic of thy future merit And pith of Manhood! May it so befall, Even so, my Boy! and all who know thy Father, For thee a garland of like hope must gather.
