LOVE’S EVEN SONG.

BY C. HARPUR.

Fast fade the hues in the cheek of the west, And the voice of the day is hush’d to rest; The grasshopper’s gone to his bed ‘neath the flower, And all, save the cricket, is still; And the moon seems to sit like an armour-clad queen, ‘Mid the pines on the top of the hill: The martin wheels silently over To his nest in the isle of the sea; While the stars throng out from the chambers of heaven, To dance in their Sultana’s way. Then come to the rill-girdled grove, Lady! Then come to the bower of our love, Lady! For the bright moon comes through the pines of the hill, And all, save my lute and the cricket, is still.

Al

ight is each wave of the stream now As they prance to the night-breeze’s tune; In them many a planet doth gleam now, As love-thoughts lie In thy beautiful eye As it rolls in the light of the moon. Then come to the rill-girdled grove, Lady! Then come to the bower of our love, Lady! For the bright moon has broke o’er the pines of the hill, And peers o’er their tops at herself in the rill.
