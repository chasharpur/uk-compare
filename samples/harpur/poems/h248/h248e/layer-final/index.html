The Wrath of Love

To have the heart misread by one Who is alone Love-thronëd there, Envokes a bitterness that none May utter for dispair,

6

Till anger brings a fierce releif, And then our words flash forth amain— The lightnings of a stormy greif That thunders in the brain.

But soon the tempest of the mind Is hushed away by pleading fears; And if it leaves a cloud behind, That cloud disolves in tears.
