[GENIUS LOST. THE SORROWS PART THREE: THE MUSE’S FIRSTLINGS]

Simple Effusions of my earlier days, Could I so long forget you? Could it be That ye have lain thus long neglected by, Unsought for, unremembered, unperused, And yet so once beloved? Once—and even now, Sweet songs,—even now, perusing you, I feel Regenerate within me that bright faith In the all-capable spirit of the world— That glowing, glorying expectancy Of Good, which blest me then; and gave the hours Of wakeful meditation golden wings, Meet to onbear them in their shining range Over all possible and exalted forms 66 Of Beauty, with its effluence,—even Love; And rendered those that shed the balm of sleep Visits to Heaven, wherein Angel smiled On Man, and Man on Angel, and their God Paternally on both! Ye must be lovely, For from a heart all lovingness you flowed Whene’er ’twas given to transport! Yes, and thence This fragrant influence that even now Rolls through that heart a renovating tide. Ye must be pure as lovely; for while thus I con you o’er a holy Presence seems To live around me, deepening the hush—as though, My better Angel, willing once again To rule my sympathies, would have me heed In you the relics of a primal worth, As vestiges whereby to model anew A one sure claim to Heaven.

Even thus In some distracted State, degenerate much To what she was, when in her simpler days 67 Of unaffected greatness Justice framed Her laws, and Wisdom in her councils shone, And Freedom swept her lyre,—some patriot voice Unsilenced yet, with earnest eloquence Charged with the spirit of a glorious Past, Beseecheth her to turn and ponder well Her former self, that so inspired, she may Her broken institutions reconstruct Upon their pristine plan: sure means by which Once more to prosper and be just again.

Though since I have ridden upon bolder Themes, And with the music of my lonely lyre, Though struck but in the intervals of toil Even for my daily bread, and ’mid the stings Of thickening disappointments, have not failed To win scholastic auditors;—though since I’ve clomb the difficult promontary whence The Epic Muse surveys the boundless sea Of Time and Change,—not by those royal roads 68 Which Learning’s slaves for Fortune’s minions smooth, And finger post, and fence where perilous held, Those Want had shut against the friendless Bard— But by adventurous leaps from crag to crag; And with the notion of a glorious fraud There smitten, as by inspiration, taught My lyre the accents of a buried Age, And called harmonious thunders from the grave! Till I grew unfamiliar with myself, And felt disturbed, and wondered at the depth Of mine own spirit!—yet, ye simple Songs, Beyond all these I love you; for you formed The mental garden of my Boyhood, where I wandered innocent, and over which The rosy rising of a darkened morn Shed all its orient wealth.

Then knew I all The inward glorying transport that exalts 69 Him who is skilled to modulate the verse Sounding or soft,—a torrent or a brook! And coin the golden phrase, wherein to set The smiles of fancy and the tears of love; Without his studious cares who hopes to leave His voice to future times. For Fame held then No council in my thoughts, as since, whene’er I swept the lyre;—no, it was Love alone That prompted,—sweet first love of all we see And hear of beautiful on earth, or dream Celestial regions hold. He, as the ray That earliest warms the warbler into voice, When dipping in the flushing cloud his wing, Or fluttering in the dew-illustrious bush, Came o’er me, hovering in balm-dropping winds, And mantled with the streaky scarf of morn, And girdled with the choicest hues of even: For, like a wild bird of the woods, I knew His presence chiefly in those loveliest hours That usher in the day, and those that lead. 70 Between the earth and its descending scale Fair Twilight, queen of new-appearing stars. He came; and of his visionary cup Giving me to drink largely, bade me next, Thus sanctioned, and inflamed of heart, to indulge The willing Muse. Him I obeyed, and forth Flowered these my earliest Songs.

What though they be To simple for the pedant, who would have Poor Nature hunted up with furious phrase And baited till she roar? What though they be Condemned by ledger-learnëd critics, who Nothing admire but stilted odes in praise Of new-raised civic halls, and stock exchanges, And widened streets, and late-invented pipes For cheating stagnant water of its right, Time out of mind, to aldermanic cellars? Though by the generous and the just they be But classed as promissory blossoms, yet, Methinks, that he who on the fire-plumed wings Of wild-eyed Ecstacy hath known to soar; Or he who in his warm and loving youth, 71a And nobly ignorant of the world, hath roved Freely in Fancy’s faery land, can ne’er Despise them—no but he may prize them well! And wed them to the memories of Love In many a cherished air;—such airs as breathe The flavour of a sweet dead Past, and seem To insphere the souls of buried Feelings, known Only to Life’s glad morn.

Despise such strains Who will, I care not,—but for me, as oft As the cold-blooded villany of the world, Through an redounding indignation, shocks Into fresh act the tendencies of truth And justice, with each brave protesting impulse, That in the secret places of my soul Yet linger,—even as the seers of old, Proscribed and banished, from the mountain caves Of their unworthy Land yet lifted loud Their warning voices,—I will utter them Over and over to my listening heart, So to revive its purer fires.—But ah! 72 Not without tears sometimes,—tears shed to think Of the lost freshness that bedewed the days Which they commemorate! Ah, that I could Be once again the wild and trusting Boy That sang them in the Past!—the wish is vain. And I must now content my hungry heart As best I may, with songs conceived in pain, In vain attempt to give its burthen meet Expression: songs inscribed with

“Misery.”

As the moaming wild waves ever Fret around some lonely isle, There are griefs that no endeavour Stilleth, even for a while, Beating at my heart forever— Beating at it now; Beating at my heart—and aching Upward to my brow.
