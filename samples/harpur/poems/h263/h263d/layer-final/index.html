Love’s Still Hour.

O come to the rill girdled grove— 34 Helouise O come! tis the still hour of love— Helouise For the voice of the day is now hushed, and at rest Is the beast on his couch and the bird in its nest,

The grasshopper sleeps in its green nook And all chirping things, save the cricket are still And the moon, like an amourous-clad Queen—look, How she comes th[r]ough the pines on the hill! And betwixt their dark branches Her radiance out launches— A torn flood of silver—sheer down on the rill!

And yon plover—how dimly tis given As over it sails for some wave beaten shore While the stars leave the chambers of heaven To dance in their coming Queens smile; And with golden-rayed glances As still she advances To look back the pleasure it brings them the while.

And see in yon wavelets, all heaping And trembling along as the night breezes blow, What star glories, flame like up leaping, In tremulous congeries glow: E’en as soul-splendours play in Thine eyes when they ray in The Poet-loved moonlights sweet silvery flow.

O then to the stream illumed grove— Come! In the still sweet spirit of Love— Come! For the voice of the day now is hushed, and at rest Is the beast on his couch and the bird in its nest.
