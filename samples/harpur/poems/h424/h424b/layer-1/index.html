[RECORDS OF A POET’S LOVE VII]

There was in the wide world, I once did guess, No happiness for me,—so harsh its features Of social commerce, and its human creatures So barren of the will and power to bless: And like a flower that in some wilderness Wastes in unmated sweetness, in my doomed Life-loneliness, each dream of love had bloomed Only to wither, only for distress! And thus my being unto hope was dying, When Nora smiled upon it;—then, as we, While yet on Winter’s skirt may hear o’erflying Some warbled note, the Spring-time prophesying, A Vision’s voice passed, saying: Yet for thee Shall Happiness,—a bride,—in Love’s green dale be lying.
