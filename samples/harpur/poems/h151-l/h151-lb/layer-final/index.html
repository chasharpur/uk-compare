A PASSAGE FROM “GENIUS LOST.”

By Charles Harpur.

This then was the world I sought so soon and found. But chiefly whence Is all the evil thus empoisoning it? It cometh not by Fate, nor does it come By Nature,—but, because of a self-willed Defect in Man; the error he adopts; The mischievous policy his craft, installed In Wisdom’s office, calls the rule of right; The dread necessity of standing well Amongst his fellows, as a genius,—shown By ever beating upward in the world, Not wisely onward in the track of Peace; His folly. and the excusive tenderness With which he blames it, and thereby abets, Because it is sheer folly——and his own; The avoidable baseness he consents to, when The world’s example shields it; these, I say, Are the great causes that o’erflow his cup With misery and wrong.

Or thus it is, Because a privilege! Few must bloat themselves With Earth’s abundant bountihood, and waste In pomp and riot what were happiness, Rightly dispensed, for all! O surely, G[od] Never intended this! As surely, Nature Through the indignant and complaining hearts Of countless millions, speaks from age to age Her motherly abhorrence of a state So slanderous of her justice! Yes; and yet The wise and brave of all thus wronged, defer From age to age, to arise at once, and doom By word and deed this universal fraud To its meet dungeon in the depths of Hell For ever and for ever!

Ah! my heart—my so untameable heart Is like a core of fire within my breast, High blown and bursting into flames of blood! I would avoid this agony, albeit I do believe it righteous:—and perhaps It yet shall largely profit me that thus Matters evolve; seeing my habits thence Are shaken from their too tenacious hold Of time and sensual things.—And come thou meek, Religious trust, that sometimes to my soul Fliest friendly, like a heaven-descended dove With wings that whisper of the peace of God; Come and assure it now, that all thus seen Of evil, by the patience of the One Almighty Master of the un[i]verse, Is but allowed to dash our vain repose On Time’s foundations, and all mad belief In human consequence; that finally, Amid the death of expectations fond, Discoveries diurnal that the pomps And pleasures of the world, are but bright mists Concealing ’mid its height of glory and fame Its depths of degradation; that all weal, And beauty and peace, even in their permanence, Are but the florid riches of a soil That crusts the cone of some yet-masked volcano, Whose darkling fires but wait the dread command, “Up to the work appointed!”—we at length, Even thus admonished, thus in hope and heart Subdued and chastened, might be so constrained To look between the thunder-bearing clouds That darken over this mysterious ball’s Blind face, for surer better things beyond Its flying scenes of doubtful good, commixed With evident evils: Yea, conclude at last, That whereso in the universe of God Our better homes may be, it is not here: Then here why build we?

Yes, thus let me learn To school my stricken heart; and with an eye Towards contentment in this troublous life, From by-gone d[i]sappointment strive to draw A rigid satisfaction, while I deem These loftier moods of thought, for all the waste Of uninformed hope, and all the dreams Of a too wild and virtuous fancy, blenched By the stern strictures of unsparing Time, M[e]et compensations—yes, their perfect fruit, Though forced, and moral end.

O then farewell Fancy and Hope, twin Angels of the past! Thee, Fancy, chiefly, as of my younger life The spiritual spouse, fa[r]ewell! with all Thy pictured equipage: the [shap]es sublime Of universal Liberty and Right. Dethroning tyrants, and investing worth Alone with power and honor; and with these Those visions that come shining to the heart Like even[i]ng stars from a serener air, Of Generosity in rapture high At rival excel[l]ence; of Charity Living in secret for her own sweet sake; Of Mercy lifting up a fallen foe; Of Pity yearning o’er the child of shame; Unselfish love and resolute friendship;—all, Even to common trust,—farewell! These ligh[ts] May never burn in the grey dome of Time Or constellate for me the world again! No more! no never more!
