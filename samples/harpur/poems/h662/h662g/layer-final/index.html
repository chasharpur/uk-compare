5c

VIRGINAL LOVE.

I love him so, That though I ne’er might see His face, in th’ assurance that he so loved me, This heart of mine would glow With pulses sweeter than the sweetest be That colder ones can know.

I love him so, That to my thought ‘twere sweet to sleep In death, believing he would keep In Sabbath memory my grave, and weep For her who slept below.

I love him so, That all desires when he is by, Shrink even from the import of a sigh, As forest flowers unseen that grow, Being mute, must so remain; As in the sky are stars that none may know.
