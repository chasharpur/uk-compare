XXIX.

Trust in God.

Deep trust in God!—for that I still have sought Through all the dread doubts that obstruct the soul, When, in the amazement of far-reaching thought, We list the laborings that for ever roll, 44 Like dubious thunders, through those clouded regions, Where Night and Destiny the counsels keep Of Time, developing his shadowy legions. And when I’ve stood upon some fearful steep Of Speculation—heaving up its bare And rugged ridge high in the nebulous air Of endless change, and thence tremendously Throwing its shadow, like a blind man’s stare, Out, o’er the vast Inane;—deep trust in Thee, O God, hath also been been my refuge, there.
