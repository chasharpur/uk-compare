V.

Rogueish Pietism.

Of all the kinds of Scoundrel, great and small, That legally figure on our social list, Defend me from the Fellow who will bawl Loud prayers to God—the roguish Pietist.

Note. I am mightily disposed to keep myself aloof from your very religious man, unless I know him to be, at the same time, a very rational one: for some of the most heartless and unscrupulous scoundrels I have hitherto known, have been religious scoundrels! Nor were they pretendedly religious, but sincerely so: (for fanatics are always deficient in conscientiousness.) And here lay the evil. They founded, like Holy Willie, in their presumed favor with Heaven, all kinds of licenses for all sorts of moral transgression and social shortcoming and wrongfulness. And why not? Were not they and theirs “the Saints”, to whom mere human laws and social duties were but things extrinsic and relatively inapplicable? Were not they and theirs the only Israel of God, and all the rest of mankind the veriest Philistines and sons of Belial? If they were of any of the various Protestant sects, was not their peculiar form of worship the only gate to the grace of heaven, the only virtual induction to the heirship of Earth? If, on 80 the other hand, they were Romanists, was not their Church the only true Church, and all beyond its pale either schismatics or heretics, whom it would not only be morally lawful, but meritorious also, to fist, and even burn, if they had the power to do so as well as the will? Sans doubt! Hence it has long struck me, that we can only remove all such mischiefs out of religion, by making religion itself thoroughly rational. In other words, we should so universalise its spirit, as to render all bigotry—that is, all individual and sectarian assumption of peculiar holiness, not only untenable, but as ridiculous withal.
