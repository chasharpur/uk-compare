[LOVE THE IDEALISER 2]

Why smile you, as in sweet amaze, My Mary—Mary mine, to see These olden things in Nature’s ways So ever-fress to me?

To some, my lark-like raptures fine Might seem indeed to mount too high; But you at least—you, Mary mine, Should guess the reason why.

If gazing at those evening skies, I bathe as in their starry blue, It is because thy loving eyes Are glorying in it too.

And more, ’tis simply thence, I guess, It seems too in itself so glad— Yea, spirited with a lovingness It one time never had.

Here too, how fondly do I trace Yon shining river’s winding stream, 71 For having seen it glass thy face As in a happy dream.

Nor wonder, Mary, it should thence With that sweet passion mix and roll, The fountain of whose profluence Lies deepest in my soul:

Lies deepest in my soul, indeed, And under Joy’s ideal skies Doth flow in melody, and feed Its flowers of Paradise.
