LITERATURE.

LOVE THE IDEALISER.

By Charles Harpur.

Why smile you, as in sweet amaze, My Mary—Mary mine, to see These olden things in Nature’s ways, So pleasureful for me?

For if I cannot well command My joy in aught of glorious frame And substance—beautiful or grand, Thy influence is to blame.

When gazing at those evening skies Why joys me so their starry blue? Because I know thy loving eyes Are gladdened by it too.

And more, ’tis simply thence, I guess, It seems, too, in itself so glad— Nay, touched as with a lovingness It one time never had.

And why do I so fondly trace The shining river’s winding stream? Because therein I’ve seen thy face As visioned in a dream.

Nor wonder, Mary, it should thence In that fond passion have its part, The fountain of whose profluence Is deepest in my heart.

And why for ever to my view So Muse-like looks the wild Fig-tree? ‘Twas in its shade that first I knew To rhyme sweet thoughts of thee.

And why, when bending o’er the rose, Is there such sweetness at my heart? Because its sister-likeness shows Thy Floral counterpart.

Nay, why to me seems any flower Itself with very feeling rife? Because some influence of thy power Hath passed into its life.

For Beauty’s God’s flower, Mary mine, And Nature, tending up to God, Blooms with some trace of the Divine Wherever she hath trod.
