[DESPAIR]

Yet were he safe from aught of further ill As if he filled his grave. He hath forgot The taste of fear—the faculty hath lost To feel vicissitude; events to him, Whether by Fortune brightened or begrimmed, Being hueless as the tide of Time itself On which they hurry past. Even such art then, Despair! and such I feel thee at my heart.

Note The leonine image in Shakspear which is here alluded to, occurs in the following passage from Troilus and Cressida, Act III. Sc.3.

—Rouse yourself; and the weak wanton Cupid Shall from your neck unloose his amorous fold, And like a dew-drop from the Lion’s mane, Be shook to air.
