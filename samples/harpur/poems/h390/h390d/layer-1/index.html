A Poet’s Wife.

O wert thou mine, belovëd Girl! How rich I were with such a mate, Albeit around me still should whirl The most injurious waves of Fate! 116 No more disgust, no more despair Should snake-like coil about my life! So couldst thou bless me, didst thou dare To be a Poet’s Wife.

Before us see how frets the sea Of life, at best a changeful state! And think ere thou putt’st forth with me Where heart alone can cope with Fate, How many new and clinging cares, World-born to an unworldly life, Must be her crown of thorns, who dares To be a Poet’s Wife!

A spirit gentle and resigned Must speak her boundless faith in love, And in the temper of her mind The lioness obey the dove: A heart above mere fortune’s snares, And all the petty pride of life— These must be her rich dower, who dares To be a Poet’s Wife!

117

And these are thine, beloved One! And I, since thou wilt be my mate, Believe that Love’s full risen sun Can fill with light the dark of Fate; And that all needful toil and care Shall breathe for me this psalm of life: Dare all for her who all could dare— A destined Poet’s Wife.
