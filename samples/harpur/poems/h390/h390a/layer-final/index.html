Stanzas

Mary, before us swells the sea Of Life, at best a changeful state; Then think, ere venturing forth with me Where Love alone can master Fate; How many new and daily cares, And troubles, born of worldly strife, Must be to Her as naught, who dares To be a Poor Man’s Wife

A self-devoting self controle Must speak her boundless faith in Love; And in the temper of her soul The lioness obey the dove: A mind above Wealth’s golden snares, With trust in Providence through life, Must be her dowery, who dares To be a Poor Man’s Wife

The answer

Why paint Life’s perils thus to me? I know it is a changeful state; 16 Yet will I venture forth with thee Where Love (you say) can master Fate. Then speak no more of golden snares, Of lowly toils or worldly strife They daunt not One (like me,), who dares To be a Poor Man’s Wife

Feb 23/49 18

Come then, my brave confiding Girl, A Hero I with such a mate! What though the Future round me whirl The most injurious waves of fate? No more I faint; no more despairs May drown the spirit of my life! Buoyed heavenward by Her love, who dares To be a Poor Man’s Wife.
