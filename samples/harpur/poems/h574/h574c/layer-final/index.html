4d

A SUMMER NIGHT SCENE.

Let us go forth Beloved! for the Night Is beautiful: the wide fields deep in maize, And orchard-bosomed homes, and (zoning all) The old and solemn woods Float in a silver sea Poured from the fountains of the risen moon, Who lingering yet on her hill-top, seems there A tiar or crest of figured gold On Earth’s uplifted head.

Heaven’s joyous multitudes have crowded out To hail their coming Queen, and interweave Their fervent peans with her milder strain; But over all beneath Reigns Silence—undisturbed, Save when, at solemn intervals, there hangs Within the viewless tresses of the breeze, Some accent of the prone And distant gleaming brook.

Come then, Beloved! Lo, our garden walk Expects thee. On its bough-enwoven arcs, And trellissed borders, wreathed with vines, bright drops In constellations wait The reign of thy blue eyes— Twin moons of Love! presiding nightly there Over a little yet sufficient world Of pure and gentle joy! A world! and all our own.
