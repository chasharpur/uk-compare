AUSTRALIAN LYRICS.—No. 2.

THE NIGHT SCENE.

Come, my Idalia, forth!—the night Is beautiful: the maize-embattled fields, And orchard-bosomed homes, and, zoning all, The old and solemn woods, Float in a silver sea, Poured from the fountains of the silent moon; Who, lingering full orbed on her hill top, Seems on some giant’s head A casque of figured gold.

The heavenly choirs have clustered forth To hail their coming queen, and then to fade In stellar music round her pathway pale: But over all beneath Sage silence reigns supreme, Save when, at solemn intervals, there hangs Within the viewless tresses of the breeze An accent of the prone And distant-gleaming brook.

Come then, Idalia, forth!—our bower Expects thee: on its bough-enwoven roof, Trunk-pillared sides, and shaded floor, the dens In constellations wait The reign of thy blue eyes— Twin moons of love! presiding nightly there Over a little yet an ample world Of inexpressible joy! A world! and all our own.

Charles Harpur.
