III.

An Australian Night Scene.

Let us go forth, Belovëd! for the Night Is Beautiful: th maize-embattle fields, And orchard-bosomed homes, and zoning all, The old and solemn woods Float in a silver sea Poured from the fountains of the silent Moon, Who lingering yet on her hill-top, seems there A tear of figured gold. On Earth’s uplifted head.

Heaven’s joyous multitudes have crowded forth, To hail their coming Queen, and empty all Thin fierier beams in music round his path But over all beneath Reigns Silence undisturbed; Save when, at solemn intervals, there hangs Within the viewless tresses of the breeze, An accent of the prone And distant-gleaming brook.

Then let us forth, Beloved! So, our Walk Expects thee. On its bough-enwoven ares, And trellissed borders, wreathed with vines, bright drips In constellations wait The reign of thy blue eyes— Twin moons of Love! presiding nightly there Over a little yet sufficient world Of pure and gentle joy! A World! and all our own.
