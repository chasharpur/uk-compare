XXV.

Parting.

To day we part! me far away to dwell From this the Scene that saw our bud of love Bloom into rosehood.—The blue heaven above— These hills and valleys, with each rocky dell, Echo’s dim hold,—shall these retain no spell Of foregone passion; and shall no one guess How great their gain was in our hasful stress Till Time to Fame the eloquent story tell? To-morrow—and the sun shall crown yon hill Bright as before; all wingèd things shall wake To song as glad as if we listened still; The stream as mirthfully its wild way take! But I, pursuing Fortune’s wandering star, Shall see and hear them not—from thee and them afar!

Euroma, 1866.
