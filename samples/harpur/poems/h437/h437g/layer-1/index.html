A Second Trilogy

1.

[RECORDS OF A POET’S LOVE XX]

To day we part! me far away to dwell From this the Scene that saw our bud of love Bloom into rosehood. The blue heaven above— These hills and valleys, with each rocky dell, Echo’s dim hold,—shall these retain no spell Of foregone passion, and shall no one guess How great their gain was in rich distress, Till Time to Fame the eloquent story tell? To-morrow—and the sun shall climb yon hill Bright as before; all wingèd things shall wake To song as glad as if we listened still; The stream as mirthfully its wild way make! But I, pursuing Fortune’s wandering star, Shall see and hear them not—from thee and them afar.
