A RHYME FOR CHILDREN.

Vesper!—Evening’s lovely star, How I wonder what you are! Hanging in the mellow sky Like a golden lamp on high! Though no stranger, yet so far, How I wonder what you are!

Clouds may hide you for a while, Yet, beyond them, still you smile, Ever quenchless, ever there, Beaming through the upper air!

Are you, say, an isle of light In the shoreless Infinite, Homing happy creatures, who Pain or sorrow never knew?

Live there in your crystal sphere Fairer things than any here— Things that breathing heaven’s breath Pure and perfect, know not death?

83b

What if you be Paradise, Showing in those evening skies Where the good who here have trod, Once more Spirits, sit with God?

But, whether you be such or no, Not less gratefully you glow, Hanging in the mellow sky Like a golden lamp on high— Glowing still heaven’s fairest star, While I wonder what you are!
