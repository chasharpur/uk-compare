Vesper A RHYME FOR CHILDREN.

Vesper!—Evening’s lovely star, How we wonder what you are! Hanging in the mellow sky Like a golden lamp on high! Though no stranger, yet so far, How we wonder what you are!

Clouds may hide you for a while, Yet, beyond them, still you smile, Ever quenchless, ever there, Beaming through the upper air!

Are you, say, an isle of light In the shoreless Infinite, Homing happy creatures, who Pain or sorrow never knew?

Live there in your crystal sphere Fairer things than any here— Things that breathing heaven’s breath Pure and perfect, taste not death?

One or all—an isle of light In the shoreless Infinite, Or a Paradise of God Only by the Immortals trod.

Not less gratefully you glow, Vesper, whether such or no, Hanging in the mellow sky Like a golden lamp on high— Glowing still heaven’s fairest star, While we wonder what you are!
