A Child’s Song.

Lovely Vesper—Evening’s star, How I wonder what you are! Like a golden lamp on high Hanging in the azure sky Lovely vesper—Evening’s star, How I wonder what you are?

Clouds may hide you for a while, Yet, above them, still you smile, Every quenchless, ever there, Beaming through the upper air! Lovely Vesper—Evening’s star, How I wonder what you are?

Are you, say, an island of light, In the shoreless Infinite Homing happy spirits who Pain or sorrow never knew? Lovely Vesper—Evening’s star, How I wonder what you are.

iv

Live there in your chrystal sphere Is Fairer things than any here? Things that, standing in God’s eye Pure and perfect, never die? Lovely Vesper—Evening’s star, Still I wonder what you are?

Or are you a Soul-Paradise, Showing in those evening skies Where the good who here have trod Now immortal, meet with God? Lovely Vesper—Evening’s star Still I wonder what you are

And still gratefully you glow, Whither you be such or no: Hanging in the mellow sky Like a golden lamp on high— Glowing still Heaven’s fairest star While I wonder what you are!
