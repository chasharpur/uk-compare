[RECORDS OF A POET’S LOVE XVIII]

Dark days are coming, Nora,—dark and drear! Nor wonder then that I am quick to deem Some fatal weft is woven with the dream That made and makes thee to my soul so dear! Yet there’s a borrowed sweetness in the fear That haunts me; for, ev’n through its gloom, the gleam Of foregone joy makes sunsets that redeem From utter saltness, Disappointment’s tear! But why—when in the world’s wide wilderness Two meet to love, and be unto each other All they have dreamt of having power to bless, A dearer sister and a fonder brother— Ah! why should Fortune then—Love’s foe for ever! Come in between, and bid those beings sever?
