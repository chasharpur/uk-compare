Outward Show.

For Outward Show we bartar competence, Family comfort, credit, friendship’s ties, And even Love’s dream-hallowed Eden, whence The beautifullest flowers of life arise To breathe a soul-sweet insence to the skies! This social envy—this most prurient sense Of self-display, grows daily more intense, Infecting even the temper of the wise. Children are pinched with want and early woe, That she who bore them, Fashion’s fribble slave, To church or theatre full-dressed may go: Sires, husbands, fops, all itch alike,—all brave Even Ruin, to eclipse some gaudy knave, Their rival in the court of Outward Show.
