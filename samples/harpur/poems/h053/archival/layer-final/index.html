The Bower of Love.

Awake my Muse! Love is the chosen theme— Love, pure as the pellucid dews, which gleam Around me, forming in the breathing flow’rs, Baths for the fairies of the moonlit hours. Awaken, then, and plume thy wings of light; For, as the golden=pinion’d dove, in flight, Most radiant glows while tracking a sun beam, Love to the Muse is aye a glory=giving theme! Avaunt, pale passion of the formal court, Or sordid city—vanity’s resort! I aim not in my lay to blazon thee, Unmeet to grace my simple minstrelsy: Thou, fraudful, ownest but of love the name, Abortive birth of Flatt’ry, Wealth, or Fame; And, artificial, like the loom=wrought bloom, Hast much of glare, but nothing of perfume.
