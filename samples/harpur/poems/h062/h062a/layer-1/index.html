The House of Revelry.

It is the House of Revelry—the place Of much vain speech and simulated mirth, And fleshly feasting. From its dissolute door They have spurned Simplicity, and red-lipt Health, With native Cheerfulness, and Strength, and long- Forecasting Prudence, and domestic Peace, And Piety who loves to dwell with these; And as their household Deities instead, Have welcomed there Extravagance, disguised As Bounty, leading riotess Excess, Misnamed Enjoyment, with Chicanery, dressed In the stol’n airs of bland Philosophy; And Atheism, mantled in the garb Of a denuded Prophet.

O

we should honor Wisdom! since the Great Immutable of Heaven through her devised The boundless world. Yea, she it was who first Elanced the sun on his imperial course, Day’s monarch, vested with celestial fire; Her hand it was that fashioned the round moon And all the golden stars. Or couldst thou soar Up to the throne of God, even there amid Unspeakable splendors she were found of thee; Or to the unfathomable gulfs of Hell Down couldst thou plunge, behold, there, even there Wisdom would meet thee, though obscured, as when The day in its meridian strength is seen Darkened with storms.

eturn we to the House Of Revelry, the place of much vain speech, And fleshly feasting, and pretensive mirth. 177 There congregate the frivolous of heart, As garrulous parrots gather to the snares Set on the borders of the ripening maize. There Phrenzy in the name of earnest Joy, With wild embarrassed brow, o’erflows the cups With blazing wine, while Passion in the plight Of Love inflames his horrid fires withal. There too Intemperance takes his constant round As seneshal, high heaping every board With palatable death, while round them drew The gluttonous, like swine about their troughs Slushed with slab garbage.

And songs mellifluous as those which he, The enthusiast, when to reason dying, hears In mountain glens, and wooded vales, or near Some lone lake crimsoned by the setting sun, Shall hail thy presence, cloying all the air With sweetness,—and awakening raptures sweet And soul-subduing, but delusive all As are the hopes of unrequited love: Sweet—sweet—but cruel as untimely death!

Wouldst thou he Should aid prophetic Genius, or assist Poor Learning, laboring in the mines of Truth To enrich a thankless world? The miscreant’s means Are all forestalled! What if his Country’s need Urged all her sons to gather like a storm And sweep Invasion from her sacred coasts, Or thunder signal condemnation down On Kingcraft, and on all internal wrong? Her need might move him not: for he is one Whose energies long since were drenched away In midnight orgies by the flowing bowl.

en in the House of Revelry, or from Its gaudy Paracites, forbear to seek Pleasure or knowledge, O ye young ones, lest A passing madness for the first ye catch, A lasting folly for the other find. Rather seek Wisdom in the house of woe; Or meditate her amid ruined tombs Charged with the dust of ages,—dust that once Was the warm human beauty of the world.
