Protective duties Versus Premiums

A fiddling difference once was said to be Just that ‘twixt Tweedledum and Tweedledee: So might we say of all Protective duties (Drawing our metaphor from rival fiddles) That they are Tweedletricks whate’er their beauties, While public premiums are just Tweedlediddles.

Mr. Parkes who seems to have a positive mania for doing all that in him lies towards diverting the stream of our superfluous cash (if we have any?) in the direction of some far-from-home object or flighty enterprise—towards the heroes of Inkermann, or the sufferers from the late innundations in France— or the frogs in the moon, who must be badly off for water— for espousing the Premium-on-cotton- growing crotchet of Mr Hold, attempted to show that there is a great difference between such like premiums and protective 681 duties. But he was evidently reasoning on the dark of the matter, however philosophical he himself may have supposed such a line of reasoning to be. A protective duty is money taken from the public [unclear] and apportioned to individuals or classes of individuals, to induce them to continue in some some set occupation—for their own benefit; while a premium of the Kind waged, is also money taken from the public at large to be, granted to individuals with a view of encouraging them to enter into or enlarge some promising though novel pursuit—for their own benefit. There is then no essential difference between them; and the assu[med] reason as to the—advantage of either is logically the same,—namely, that one in the end does benefit the public at large, and that the other may or will. +
