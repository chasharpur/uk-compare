SONNET.

To my friend, Mr. J. J. Walsh, on his advising me to keep up my hopes and spirits.

Think you I grieve?—I’ll never grieve again! Think you I weep?—Except for others’ woe From these stern fountains tears no more shall flow! And Melancholy!—Thou who long hast lain Upon my bosom like a bride—insane Deceiver! pass ਏਏ Do I not daily grow Mighty in soul? and feel accomplished so To triumph o’er all evils—even pain? Do I not feel superior in mind To chance and change?—to the world’s pride and hate? Yea, even its smiles—although in heart inclined To follow Glory with the truly Great? And thus equipt for Life’s rough journey, say, Shall sorrow be my mate upon the way?

CHARLES HARPUR.

May 3, 1843.
