To Mr.. J. J. Walsh.

In answer to a Letter of Encouragement from that gentleman, written under the impression that the Author was giving way to despondency.

Think you I grieve?—I’ll never grieve again! Think you I weep?—except for Others’ woe, From these stern Fountains tears no more shall flow! And Melancholy, thou who long hast lain Upon my bosom, like a Bride,—insane Deceiver, pass!—Do I not daily grow Mighty in Soul, and feel accomplished so To triumph o’er all Evils—even Pain! Do I not feel superior in mind To Chance and Change? to the World’s pride and hate? Nay, even its smiles? although in heart inclined 645 To follow Glory with the truly Great! And thus equipt for Life’s rough journey, say, Should Sorrow be my mate upon the way?
