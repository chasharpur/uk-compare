To ———

Long ere I knew thee—years of loveless days, A Shape would gather from my dreams and pour The soul-sweet influence of its gentle gaze Into my being, to thrill it to the core: Then would I wake, with lonely heart to pine For that nocturnal Image:—it was thine!

Thine—for though long with a fond moody heed I sought to match it with the beauteous creatures I met in the world’s ways, ’twas but to bleed With disappointment; for all forms, all features Yet left it void of living counterpart— The shadowy Mistress of my yearning heart.

Thine—for when first seen thou didst seem to me A being known yet beautifully new! As when to crown some sage’s theory, Amid heaven’s sisterhoods, into shining view Comes the conjectured star!—his lucky name To halo thenceforth with its virgin flame.

But I forget! Far from thy rural home Behold I journey through primeval woods In which but savage things are wont to roam, Mixing fond questionings with Solitude’s Wild voices, where amid vague glades and dells Enwrapt in twilight trance her shadowy Presence dwells.

And now the Hunter with a swollen speed Rushes in thunder at my side, but wears A softened charm whene’er its reaches lead My vision westward,—where pale Fancy rears Thy wood-nest by that brook, whose murmurs erst As with a flattering heed my love’s new gladness nurst.

And with the river’s torture, oft a tone Of that far brook seems blending; accents too Of the dear voice there heard—that voice alone To me unparagoned,—like a silver dew, Honied with manna, dropping near me seems, As oft I listen—lost in rich memorial dreams.

But vain these musings! Though my spirit’s bride, Thou knewst not of my love! Though all my days Must henceforth be inevitably dyed 227 Or bright, or dark, through thee,—this missive says Thy lot in life is cast, that thou wilt be Homed with another in the world’s fenced ways Ere I again may look (if e’er again) on thee!

The bardic doom encoils me! Poet’s make Beauty immortal and yet luckless miss The charms they sing,—martyrs at Fortune’s stake! As if their soul’s capacity for bliss Might else mix earth with heaven, and so annul That want which for its own redemption’s sake As Love’s queen thrones at last, the world-wide Beautiful!

Yet ye wild woods and waters of the earth, How changed (with all things) shall ye grow to me! And even the spirit of your summer mirth Moan pine-like in the winds of memory, As saddened in the temper of its worth, Though, as in influence, deepened it may be! Still shorn of nearer joy, my heart alone Out in the mother-whole may henceforth seek its own.

Wreathe then the Poet’s brows with blossoms bright! Let waters ever and the sway of trees 228 Sound through his thoughts, as the renewed delight Of Evening flows around him in a breeze Laden with dying voices—till the night Enroof him with her starry mysteries! For then through Nature can there be for his First infinite love alone some palingenesis.
