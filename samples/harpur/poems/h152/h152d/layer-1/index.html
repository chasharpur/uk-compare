Lyrics. By Charles Harpur.

George Washington.

Even as the one sole star of morning Shines that Name upon the earth! Even as a God-lit light adorning A great Nation’s clouded birth! Or like an altar-flame it rises From the field of Freedom’s battle won: Then send it out,—o’er the whole wide world; That patriot name—George Washington.

To breathe it, is to brand all trading With dominion for a throne; To know it, is to curse all wading Into blood for power alone! While like an altar-flame it rises From the fields of Freedom’s battles won: Then send it out,—o’er the whole wide world; That stainless name—George Washington.

No cry is it for frantic glory Such as sworded hirelings laud; 36 No greatness is there in its story Or of conquest or of fraud! But like an altar-flame it rises From the fields of Freedom’s battles won: Then send it out,—o’er the whole wide world; That sacred name—George Washington.
