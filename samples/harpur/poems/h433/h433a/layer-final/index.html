XVI.

Rosa and the Morning Star.

Unfolded are the streaky gates of Light, And Darkness, like a worsted Ethiope, flies Before the Morning Star’s victorious rise, As o’er the eastern steep, silently bright, She cometh. And at her bright coming now, Her skiey Sisters how they pale away, As if with envy of her richer ray! And what ’mid them she seemeth, Rosa, thou, To me art ’mid the daughters, who even so Diminish in thy being’s rarer glow, When at the bidding of thy smiles depart All thoughts that Absence darkened into woe, By shapes of light succeeded! For, thou art 53 The Morning Star of all the joy I know.
