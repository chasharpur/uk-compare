16.

[RECORDS OF A POET’S LOVE XVI]

Unfolded are the crystal gates of Light, And Darkness, like a worsted Ethiope, flies Before the Morning Star’s victorious rise, As o’er the eastern hills, so gladly bright, She cometh: and at her bright coming, how Her skiey sisters seem to pale away As if with envy of her eclipsive ray! And what ’mid them she seemeth, Nora, thou To me art amongst women, who, even so, Diminish in thy being’s richer glow, When at the bidding of thy smiles depart All thoughts that absence darkened into woe, By shapes of light succeeded! for thou art The Morning Star of all the joy I know.
