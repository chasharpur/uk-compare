Sonnets relating to Poetry

[RECORDS OF A POET’S LOVE XVI]

Unfolded are the crystal gates of Lights, And Darkness, like a worsted ethiops, flies Before the Morning Star’s victorious rise, As o’er the eastern hills so queenly & bright She comes! And see too at her coming, how Her skiey sisters seem to pale away As if with envy! of her eclipsive ray! Even thus to me, O Poesy, shin’st thou Of bright things brightest; and thus always they Are ‘minished in thy beauty’s richer glow, When at thy royal bidding, soon depart All Care’s benighting glooms, though dense and low,— Displaced by shapes of light: So much thou art The Morning Star of all of the glory I know!
