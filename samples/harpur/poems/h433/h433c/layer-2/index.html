XVI.

[RECORDS OF A POET’S LOVE XVI]

Unfolded are the crystal gates of Light, And Darkness, like a worsted Ethiope, flies Before the Morning Star’s victorious rise, As o’er the eastern hills, so gladly bright, She cometh. And at her bright coming, how Her skiey sisters seem to pale away As if with envy of her eclipsive ray! And so thus to me O Poetry, art 11 Brightest of all bright things—and even so They ‘minish in thy beauty’s richer glow, When at the bidding of thy smiles depart All thoughts that absence darkened into woe, By shapes of light succeeded! For thou art The Morning Star of all the joy I know.
