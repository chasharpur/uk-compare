Bower Building.

284

My sweet One, in our favorite shade A summer Bower my hands have made, That we, what time the noon is nigh, May shroud us from the sun’s fierce eye, And list the cool brook babble by.

Of two mimosas branch-locked o’er I formed the door; A larger growth, half circling round, I bent into a dome; then bound The boughs all in—all interwound.

And o’er the whole a forest vine Is taught in many a wreath to twine; And through the green sprays as they grow Its tresses all mass forward so, As out upon the wind to flow.

The space half round it, and before, Is fancifully broidered o’er With all such wild flowers as dispense Prime odors, breathing on the sense A rich, though wordless, eloquence.

And thus complete, and all in bloom, A Fairy-builded emerald dome Frosted with gold, and sprinkled through With silver bugles not a few, It seemeth:—youbuilded thus for you!
