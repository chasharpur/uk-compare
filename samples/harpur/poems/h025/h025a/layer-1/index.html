FRAGMENT.

Since thou art dead!—since thou art dead, My Brother, though I see The blue sky bending o’er my head So Full of beauty shower’d free; Though scenes of love be round me spread; Though o’er the hills, with venturous tread, I wend, as once with thee, Where through dark woods broad rivers fret And lighten to the sea: 98 All, all unchanged—unchanging,—yet How different is the world to me!

You will understand the following fragment: and tell me what you may think of it.

The light as with a living robe Doth clothe the world when night is past; The sun with his great golden globe Doth crown the mountain vast; The moon and stars look down on Earth 99 As I have seen them from my birth: But O thou light and Sun and Moon, And o ye stars so bright and boon, 100 How great and glorious you be I yet may feel as fervently As I have felt before,
