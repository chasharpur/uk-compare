XV.

Prefatory to a M.S. volume.

These Poems! Let the Worldling, if he will, Misdeem them dreamy. None the less shall they Be of the choral Voices that shall thrill With a memorial melody alway, My Country, in her nobler national day— Volubly sounding upon many a hill Yet to be famous, and by lake or rill Predoomed to lighten under Glory’s ray. The spirit that is in them shall evoke Beautiful passion, tenderness and grace; Or stamp, as with a mighty engine stroke, On many a bold Stripling of my race, A character no world-usage shall efface, Or bend to other than Truth’s noble yoke.
