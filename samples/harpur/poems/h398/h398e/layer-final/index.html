[PREFATORY TO A M.S. VOLUME]

These Poems! Let the Worldling, if he will, Misdeem them worthless. None the less shall they Be of the Choral Voices that shall thrill With a sonòrous melody alway My Country, in her nobler national day— Volubly sounding upon many a hill Yet to be famed, and by lake, river, and rill Predoomed to lighten under Glory’s ray. The spirit that is in them shall evoke Beautiful passion, tenderness and grace; Or stamp as with a mighty engine stroke On many a bold Stripling of my race, A character whose strength shall hold it base To bend to other than Truth’s noble yoke.
