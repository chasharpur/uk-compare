LVI.

Epilogistic to the Whole.

These Sonnets! Let the worldling, if he will, Misdeem them cheap. Not thence the less shall they Be of the household Voices that shall thrill With a memorial melody alway, My Country’s broad breast in her national day;— Volubly sounding upon many a hill Yet to be famous, and by lake or rill That then shall lighten under Glory’s ray. The spirit that is in them shall evoke Beautiful passion, tenderness and grace; Or stamp, as with a mighty engine-stroke, On many a bold Stripling of my race, A character no base usage shall efface, Nor bend to other than Truth’s noble yoke.

1865.
