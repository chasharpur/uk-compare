63

Chorus.

No rest, no love, is in thy bosom, Earth, For searching Natures of the noblest size Because monopolised to the selfish use And dissolute abuse Of Fortune, Accident, and Birth, In men nor great, nor good, nor beautiful, nor wise!

And hence thy prime Mentalities, day by day, Throughout their sojourn under these bright skies, Stung from all actual place, All fitting sympathetic ties, By dry conventions and their mean dismay At worldly disappointment and disgrace, Can only waste upon the Far Away 64a The spiritual riches of their love; And madden with the sense that they Cannot flee—whither? like the Psalmist’s dove!

170

Semi Chorus.

Behold yon, when The manifold hills forsaken of the sun Are dusking into one Featureless mightiness, how wildly then In the grey solitude of heaven afar There shineth a sole star: Even so the memory of One adored With all affection’s hoard Of golden feelings treasured up for truth In vain—in vain, in pleasure and in pain, Throughout Life’s fervent youth, A far bright mystery, ever shines a part O’er the wide vacancy of Love’s lone heart.

64b

Chorus (in continuation)

But yet the morning of a happy change Shall purple o’er thy pale and thoughtful features, Even through the mighty range This thoughtfulness is forced to take By sorrow, for Redemption’s sake— Redemption for thy myriads of love-restless creatures! When Wisdom, ripened to her heart’s deep core, Shall scatter away the fraudful awe That now a bribed Religion throweth o’er The unfraternal policy of the Few; Declaring Love the only law Wherewith mankind can righteously be bound! And then as general as the dew Of Heaven, all healthful joys shall all alike surround,— By Fraud and Policy untainted; 65 Making the Near and Actual, day by day, Throughout Life’s pleasant way, All Poesy only hitherto hath painted The visionary Far Away.
