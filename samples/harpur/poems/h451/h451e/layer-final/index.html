Domestic Sonnets. By Charles Harpur

I

[RECORDS OF A POET’S LOVE XXXIVa]

Mine after all—my Mary! Why should I That most loved, sweet name any more disguise, Now that beneath the conscious-seeming skies My joy may spread itself as openly? I weep—and fondly ask the reason why: Thinking my happy heart in no such wise Should keep effusing through my happy eyes, Now that she‘s mine by an enduring tie! Mine after all—my Mary! Lo, the Past With all its doubt and dread and passionate sorrow But travailed with Content! since thus, at last, Love’s whole light full into my lot is cast: Even as the sun ariseth on the morrow Out of the broken storm—cloudless and vast!
