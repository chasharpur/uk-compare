5c

VII.

MINE AFTER ALL.

Mine after all—my Mary! Why should I Give to that sweet name longer a disguise? Now that beneath the conscious-seeming skies My joy is all as open and as high! I weep, and fondly ask the reason why? Thinking my happy heart should otherwise Than thus keep melting through my happy eyes Now that she’s mine by an enduring tie? Mine after all—my Mary! Let the past, With all its loving dread and passionate sorrow, Be to me now—all peace; since Love hath cast Its long hid glory o’er my life at last; Even as the sun ariseth on the morrow Out of the broken storm—cloudless and vast!
