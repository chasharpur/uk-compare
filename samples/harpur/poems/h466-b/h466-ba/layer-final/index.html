63

[Pope]

[His Rape of the Lock.]

What silver peals of music here are rung! What pearls of fancy in what order strung On golden lines that fuse and stream upon the tongue! How smiling is the satire! and the wit How forceable, and yet how exquisite! What gay creations, too, enlarge the theme, And “bask and whiten” in the Poet’s dream! As silken blossoms waving on their sprays, All dewy, glint in the Moon’s mystic rays; As silver mists upon the mountains born, Flit in the first white glances of the Morn.

Most finished Lay! like (in bright semblance seen) The bridal mantle of some Eastern Queen, All starred with gems of price, and figured through With inwrought beauty,—such as, in his view Of Paradise, the Prophet saw employ The daughters of imperishable Joy.
