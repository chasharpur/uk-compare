MY LOVE.

She was not beautiful, they said, The cold of heart and stern of head, Who measure by some given line The meet, the graceful, and the fine: But there was something round her Being That gifted me with rarer seeing; And there was that within its voice Which made my very soul rejoice With an exceeding joy—for She Was more than beautiful to me.

I asked not, are her glances bright? I felt them lovely in their light: I asked not, is her visage fair? I felt a soul of sweetness there: I asked not, is her bosom swelling? It was my fancy’s dearest dwelling: I asked not, is her form complete? My heart was kneeling at its feet, To feel,—and ’twas enough,—that She Was more than beautiful to me.
