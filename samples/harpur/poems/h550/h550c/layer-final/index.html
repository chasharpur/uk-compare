XV.

Wentworth’s Council-Dream.

“I have had a dream,—past the wit of man to say what dream it was. x x x Methought I was, and methought I had.—But man is but a patched fool, if he will offer to say what methought I had.”

Bully Bottom.

After a brimstone speech in Council made, Great Bill grew drowsy. First one eye he closed, Then V t’other; and when both were shut, he dosed. And as he dosed, behold, a Mabbish Jade Called Blow-the-bladder, hovered o’er, and laid On his sick fancy a wide-bloating vision. Himthought, the rod of Gipps’s damned decision Snapt over him at last; wherefore he said— Now—now, if not a Canibal Island King, A “marquis Carrabas” at least I’ll be Beyond the Bounderies here!—But ah, the spring Which then he gave to grasp his Phantasy, Awoke him, wondering, from that glorious dose, And he had clutched instead—”the Doctor”, by the nose!
