SONNETS DEDICATED TO AUSTRALIAN SENATORS.

NO. XVI.

WENTWORTH’S COUNCIL DREAM.

After a brimstone speech in Council made, Great Bill grew drowsy. First one eye he closed, Then t’other; and when both were shut, he dozed. And as he dozed—behold, a Mabbish jade Call’d Blow the-bladder, hovered o’er, and laid On his sick fancy a wide-bloating Vision. Himthought, the Rod of Gipps’s d———d decision Snapt in his gripe at last, wherefore he said— Now! now, if not a “Cannibal Island King,” A “Marquis Carabas,” at least, I’ll be Beyond the Boundaries here!—But ah, the spring Which then he made to grasp his Phantasy, Awoke him, wondering, from that glorious doze— And he had clutched instead———”the Doctor,” by the nose.

MARTIN GALVIN.
