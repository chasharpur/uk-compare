Odes and Lyrics.

To a Girl.

Dear Maiden, I have often thought To frame a verse or two for thee, That henceforth thou might’st feel as taught By something learned of me.

Tho knowest, seeing ’tis thy lot To commune with a Sire refin’d, That loveliness which fadeth not Is only of the mind.

But there’s another phase of Truth Which yet, perhaps, thou dost not, And which, if given to thy youth, May teach thee how to grow.

There are ev’n in the body springs That mix with what we still approve, And thereby mould it to the things We most admire and love.

With goodness then keep friendship still, With meekness, chastity and grace, And lo! the sweetness of thy will Shall settle in thy face.

With poesy communion make, And with all hearts of gentle kind, 264 And lo! thy maiden form shall take The temper of thy mind.
