TWO POEMS. BY CHARLES HARPUR.

I.

TO A LITTLE GIRL.

Dear Mary, I have often thought To frame a verse or two for thee, That henceforth thou mightst feel, as taught By something learned of me.

Thou knowest (seeing ’tis thy lot To commune with a sire refined) That loveliness which fadeth not, Is only of the mind.

But there’s another phase of Truth Which yet perhaps thou dost not know, And which, if given to thy youth, May teach thee how to grow.

There are even in the body springs That mix with what we still approve, And thereby mould it tow’rds the things We most admire and love.

With Goodness then keep friendship still— With Truth, and Chastity, and Grace; And lo! the sweetness of thy will Shall settle in thy face.

With Poesy communion make, And with all Arts of gentle kind; And lo! thy maiden form shall take The temper of thy mind.
