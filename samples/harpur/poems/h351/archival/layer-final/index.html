ORIGINAL POETRY.

ON INTELLECTUAL PRIDE AND SELF- EXAGGERATION.

There is an enemy that dwells Hid in the chambers of the mind, That chains religion down, and swells Ambition into scorn of kind, And makes all just regards, like fabled Justice, blind.

Thus ruled, even thought outlaws the soul, And knowledge is a dangerous thing, That, like the spirit of the bowl, Illumines all our powers [UNCLEAR] More bitterly and deep with after suffering.

Thus ruled, ’tis fearful but to know That want of principle is power; And thus in wisdom’s soil may grow Temptations for the evil hour That stings with signal loss, or rains the golden shower.

And thus was one’s destruction wrought Whose memory lives in mighty verse; Thus a weak worm became in thought The centre of the universe— A scorner of his God—a coward and a curse!

A coward! for even such is he Who is irresolute for truth, And dares not in our nature see The germ of an immortal youth, To bloom beyond this scene of wickedness uncouth.

Oh, let us then be lowly-wise, Nor god the principle within, Lest pride-warpt reason so disguise Evil and good, that we begin To bridge our worldly way even at the cost of sin.

Charles Harpur.

Jerry’s Plains, 10th Sept., 1843.
