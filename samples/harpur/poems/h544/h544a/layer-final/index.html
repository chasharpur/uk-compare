SONNETS DEDICATED TO AUSTRALIAN SENATORS.

NO. XI.

[SONNETS DEDICATED TO AUSTRALIAN SENATORS IX]

How stripped of all that giveth life to glow, Becomes the light of day’s illustrious lamp, Ere it can pierce into the chambers damp Of yon old law be-lumbered [gap]ile*! and so, Dear Foster, shorn of all its warmth, and dim, Must Truth become ere it can passage find Amid the legal cobwebs of thy mind— Dexterous in word craft, and propense to trim! Hence all thy vaccillation—all thy twaddle— Thy would-and would-not sliding to and fro! Give up at once, man! For such doubtful faddle What Government on earth would give a crow, Much less a Place,—and in the “Patriot’s” ladle There’s nought that’s touchable for scum you know!

* The old Court-house, King-street.
