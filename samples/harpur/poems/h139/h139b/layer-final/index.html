Lyrics. By Charles Harpur.

The Forgotten.

He shone in the senate, the camp, and the grove, The mirror of manhood—the darling of love.

44

He fought for his Country,—the star of the brave, And died for its weal when to die was to save.

And Wisdom and Valor long over him wept, And Beauty, for ages, strewed flowers where he slept.

And the Bards of his people inwrought with their lays The light of his glory and truth of his praise.

But afar in the foreworld have faded their strains, And now of his being what record remains?

Within a lone valley a Tomb crumbles fast, The name of whose Sleeper is lost in the past.
