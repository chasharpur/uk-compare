[LOVE THE IDEALISER 3]

It is the happy truth I sing, When tracing Love’s diviner laws, I thus into my numbers bring The why and the because.

Yes: why for ever in my view So Muse-like looks the wild-fig tree?‘Twas 149 ‘Twas in its shade that first I knew To rhyme sweet things of thee.

And also in its shade it was That first I dared my love to tell— And thus is compassed all the cause Of its memorial spell.

Or o’er this fresh rose bending—why Awakes such sweetness at my heart? It gives to Love’s poetic eye Thy floral counterpart.

Nay, why to me seems any flower Itself with actual passion warm? Because some influence of thy power Hath pass’d into its form.

For Love is God’s flower, Mary mine! And Nature, tending up to God, Treads on the skirts of the divine Wherever Love hath trod.
