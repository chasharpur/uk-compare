II.

A Dream of the Orient.

With a splendid Eastern bride, Like a houri, at my side, And music round us swelling; ‘Mid odours of so rare a stream, That unembodied souls they seem, Dwell I, through a radiant Dream, In an Orient dwelling.

In the pleasure court we lie, By a fountain flashing high, 5B On a gorgeous pillow: The column’d waters mount the air, To hang in bright cascadence there, Like the sprays, ornate and rare, Of a christal willow.

And fair faces, darkly bright, Like the countenance of Night, In its starry glory; All ministrant, around us throng, And breathe their beauty into song, Or in tones, as rich, prolong Some wild rich-worded story.

‘Till through many voices, One Like a gush of gold doth run: “Why should kindred sever? Our life is a perpetual feast Of being, from all care released, Sunny souls are for the East— Dwell with us for ever!”
