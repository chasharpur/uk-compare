Companion Pieces

XXXI.

There is a trying spirit in the drift Of human life, apportioning the prize (In quality or color, shape or size) That each one seeketh to that seeker’s gift. Hence must he suffer many a perilous shift Who into fame by martial deeds shall rise; Hence look at Liberty with eagle eyes Must he who’d make the march of Right more swift: Hence Heaven’s best crown, more glorious than the sun, Is only gained by dying for mankind: And hence, too, True Love’s highest meed is won Only through agonies of heart and mind! Such, Nora, is the fate (and therefore ours) Of all whom Love would crown with love’s divinest flowers.
