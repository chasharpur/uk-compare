To a Black Swan.

(A pathetic Address that would be a Sonnet but for the last line being jerked out of it by Destructi[vene]ss getting upper most)

Beautiful Stranger! from afar that comest, Scared hither by the murderous musquet din Of Men much coveting thy downy skin. Soon as within their cruel ken thou loomest Destructi[vene]ss is up, and hanke[r]s greatly To lodge a pellet in thy form so stately. And when thy flight, with slow and heavy flops, Tracks nearingly the windings of the river, Some dweller on the banks intent to shiver Those laboring wings, with tube in hand, out pops! But here at length thou smooth’st each ruffled feather, Gliding serenely in thy new born hopes Of safety, like a ship in pleasant weather— —Egad! I’ll git the gun, and dress his leather. ※
