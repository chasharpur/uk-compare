The Meditated Declaration.

An unacknowledged hope lies in my breast Too deep for speech; and never pen could write How my pent spirit pineth, day and night, For one fair Being with whom I might be blest! Till with inscrutable jealousies possessed, I languish all the more that these may so Oppress Affection, that for very wo She longs at last to die and be at rest! O for a brimmer of the wine of Love, Or a deep draught of the Lethèan wave! The power a mutual passion to emove, Or the forgetfulness that seals the grave!Yet 48 Yet these my bonds are blameless: one more wise Had dreamt away his freedom, dreaming of her eyes.
