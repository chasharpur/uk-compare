Chorus of the Hours.

Is there no dawning of a better morrow? O Nature, even as a wronged mother starts Shouldst thou not shake with a maternal horror, Beholding chiefly in thy whole world, hearts Blood-ripe for joy, yet knowing only sorrow!

And Love, how vilely blotted is thy page! In the cold arms of gold-possessing age, Or worse embrace of fortune-crowned disease, Under costliest canopies, On beds of loveless and thenceless joyless state, Lo, loathing Beauty lies! Entrapped by interests beastial to the wise All by nature moulded good or great.

Yea, merely but to live,—though upon lies, 250 How many of Earth’s fairest dedicate Their glowing beings to whom they scorn or hate! While for each feminine flower thus blighted A manly heart yearns undelighted Or runs into those snares the base devise, Yea, into sensual sties, Lacking its natural mate!

And in the Future’s vision-lit pavilions Innumerable millions The icy misery of like woes await! Wait to be only in their generations, Through foregone pacts and old abominations, (For evil deeds project all evil fate) Unprofitted vessels of emotional natures! Yea, but there also shining in pure state Time-ripened Wisdom glories up, with great Goddess-like port and truth-illumined features! With Mercy and Justice,—lo! she hastes along To chase—down even to Hell, thron’d Mystery and her creatures Bad prudence, craft, and seeming-sacred wrong!
