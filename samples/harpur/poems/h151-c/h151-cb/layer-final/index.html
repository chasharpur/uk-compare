Chorus of the Hours.

Is there no dawning of a better morrow? O Nature, even as a wronged mother starts Shouldst thou not leap, filled with maternal rage, Beholding chiefly in Earth’s bosom, hearts Blood-ripe for joy, yet knowing only sorrow!

And Love, how vilely blotted is thy page! In the cold arms of gold-possessing age, Or worse embrace of fortune-crowned disease, Though Under costliest canopies, On beds of loveless and thence joyless state, Behold, how loathing Beauty lies! Entrapped by interests beastial to the wise And all by nature moulded good or great.

Yea, merely but to live,—though upon lies, 250 How many of Earth’s fairest dedicate Their glowing beings to whom they scorn or hate! While for each feminine flower thus blighted A manly heart yearns undelighted Or runs into those snares the base devise, And into feculent ways and sensual sties Lacking its natural mate!

And in the Future’s vision-lit pavilions Innumerable millions The icy misery of like woes await! Wait to be only in their generations, Through foregone pacts and old abominations, (For evil deeds project all evil fate) Unprofitted vessels of emotional natures! Yea, but there also shining in pure state Time-ripened Wisdom glories up, with great Goddess-like port and truth-illumined features! With Mercy and Justice,—lo! she hastes along To chase—down even to Hell, thron’d Mystery and her creatures Bad prudence, craft, and seeming-sacred wrong!

X Semi Chorus. Ah! that Death Should ever, like a drear untimely night, Descend upon the loved, in Love’s despite! Ah! that a little breath Expiring from the world, should leave each scene Where its warm influence before hath been, So empty to the heart in its despair Of all but misery—misery everywhere! But loving souls, Though sundered thus by Death, must tend again To union, since they die not, and no main Wide as the Eternal between them rolls. This is implied by suffering. What is pain But prophesy of pleasure? What is grief But preparation for some meet relief.
