Chorus.

(From the Sorrows of Chatterton.)

Ah, that death Should ever, like a drear untimely night, Descend upon the loved in Love’s despite! Ah, that a little breath Expiring from the world, should leave each scene Where its warm influence before hath been, So empty to the heart in its despair, So painful everywhere! But loving souls, Though sundered thus by death, must tend again To union, since they die not, and no main Wide as Eternity between them rolls! This is implied by suffering. What is pain But prophecy of pleasure? What is grief But preparation for some meet relief?
