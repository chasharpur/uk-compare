The Windy Subject Again.

———”his words The better reason, to perplex and dash Maturest counsels.”

M

I

’ve said, how Belial with the Patriot’s name Made merry: then, His Majesty, Old Nick, Retorted thus—Ha, of our servant Dick Whence cometh thy disrelish? Does his fame For indefatigable Wrongness Thy jealous indolence? Or does his quick And sneering hate of every thing but trick, A Rival’s envy even in Belial claim? To which the punning Feature answered—No: Yon barking fellow, though he had out sinn’d me, I could not envy, Sire; because I know He’s hollow, even in a Council-shindy; And hence, with reference to whate’er might blow, I held that Windy-er must mean more windy.
