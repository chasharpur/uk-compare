II.

EPIGRAM ON A LAW-MAD BUSH JUSTICE.

Poor * * * * * * ‘s rabidness of jaw Hath led him so to munch the law, That nothing but a quibbling fuss ’tis When he thinks he’s doing justice. A point is all that he can see— He hath no eyes for equity; And easier than one gnat of right, He’ll bolt ten legal camels quite.
