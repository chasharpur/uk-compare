II

The Awakening

Of many Influences held good and fair My soul had tasted; but in each at length Some cold infirmity, or sultry strength, Showed surely—showed to strip my nature bare Of ripening power, so that its branches ne’er Of their best fruit might the glad bearers be— Till, as if blinded in its tendency, My heart grew wild with passionate despair! At last one Influence with a soul-like sweetness O’er my lone spirit, measureful and free, Breathed, as breathes music o’er a moaning sea;— So pure, so rich too, in its charm’s completeness, That (as it seemed) from mere delight in meetness I made it my life’s crown—even Poësy.
