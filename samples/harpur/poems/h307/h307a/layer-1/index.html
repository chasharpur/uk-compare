[THE AWAKENING]

Of many an Influence held good and fair My soul had lasted: But in each at length Some cold infirmity or sultry strength Showed surely—showed to strip my nature bare Of budding power, so that my branches ne’er Of their best fruit might the glad bearers be: Till, as if blinded in its tendency, My heart grew wild with passionate despair. But one at last, so soul-sweet in its sweetness, Oer my lone spirit, measureful and free, Breathed, as breathes music o’er a moaning sea;— So pure, and rich too, in its charm’s completeness, That (as it seemed) from mere delight in meetness, I made it my life’s lot—even Poësy.

6th Jan 1867.
