XII.

The Confession.

She loves me! From her own sweet timid lips The dear confession came, like rich perfùme From rosy petals bursting into bloom! And still with the remembrance skips Like a young antelope, and my tongue trips, As drunk with joy; whilst every object seen In Life’s diurnal round, wears in its mien Smiles that all recollected smiles eclipse! And if the commonest things of Nature now Are like old faces flushed with new delight; Much more the consciousness of that dear vow Serenes the beautiful and refines the bright, While throned I seem on Love’s divinest hight, ‘Mid clouds of visioned bliss that diadem its brow.
