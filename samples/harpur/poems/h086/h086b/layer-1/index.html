II.

[DEAR HENRY, WHY SHOULD YOU COMPLAIN?]

Dear Henry, why should you complain, That I love to sing chiefly of Love, Since ever employed in like strain Are the fire-wingèd Seraphs above?

And what is there under the skies More worthy the voice of the Muse, When gracious, as beautiful eyes, And young cheeks, with their roseate hues;

103

And lips that as ripe cherries sp smile, Breathing balm like an Indian spring; And all these expressive the while Of the exquisite Spirit I sing?

And though faded were these, being crost, Then the more her adornment should claim, The love dreaming Soul that was lost In the measureless strength of her flame.

Then never again disapprove Of the passionate lay. Were it wrong, Would the stars sing together of Love, And the angels rejoice in their song?
