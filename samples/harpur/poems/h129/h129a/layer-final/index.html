FAREWEL.

We love, and we are blest in over measure: We give our hearts to friendship and rejoice In the peculiar fitness of our choice: Then blow the blasts of Fate, and all our pleasure Dies in a word—Farewel! And exquisite tears, Dimming the eyes of pining Beauty, still Follow its utterance, with such pangs of will As wither Manhood even more than years. What shapes of joys that faded in the past; Of dreams that in our better days we knew;— What memories of hopes too bright to last; Of absent Friends;—the dead—the changed—the true— Even while we muse of it, as to a spell, Throng shadowy round us at that sound—Farewel.
