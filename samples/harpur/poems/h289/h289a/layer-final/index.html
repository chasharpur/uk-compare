[THE MIDNIGHT MOON IS RIDING HIGH ALOFT]

The midnight Moon is right aloft, Yet only her dim outline may be seen, For the whole heaven is frosted with a soft And creamy cloud; through which her half quencht sheen But filters earthward, and, so filtering, seems Light’s wraith, not living light,—so ghostlywise it gleams

Two Bits that will be sure to tempt the Assaulter of hope and love into assorted breaches of the commandment.
