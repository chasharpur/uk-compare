Eva Gray.

Paler, paler, day by day, Waxeth wordless Eva Gray; Wasting—wasting, day by day, 126 Like a thin white cloud away.

And how shall those who wish her well, Lift the shadow erst that fell Around her from Love’s darkened spell?

Would they have her feast with Song? Its wild voices but prolong Through Memory’s echoes Passion’s wrong.

Would they, to beguile her leisure, Sweet reliefs in Music measure? Music but dreams of foregone pleasure.

Would they lure her where the Spring Gives the unhurt heart to wing Up heavenward, like a lark, and sing?

Still paler, paler, day by day, Waxeth gentle Eva Gray; Ever wasting, day by day, Like a thin white cloud away.
