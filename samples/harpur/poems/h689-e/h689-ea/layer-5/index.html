[Part V.]

Again I lived—again I felt. But now The winds of heaven seemed under me, and I Was sweeping, like the Spirit of the storm That bellowed round me, in the murky glooms That heaved and rolled with a wide manifold motion Tenfold more mighty than the darkling swells Of ocean, wrestling with a midnights gale! The wild winds tossed me! I was drenched throughout With heavy moister, and at intervals Amid the ragged gaps of moving cloud Methought I saw dim glimpses of the sun Hanging aloft, as if in drear eclipse. But gradually my senses cleared, and I Grew conscious that a new change was upon me! Than glancing at my limbs, I saw that they Were cothed with plumage; at the same time, too, I felt the tension of high taloned claws, As they worked closing, with a fierce desire And sudden eagernes for blood and prey! An impulse to pursue and to destroy Both on the earth and in the air, ran quick Out from my heart and shivered in my wings And as a thing more central yet, I felt Regnant within, throned o’er all, a lone And sullen, yet majestic sense of pride. Twas plain that I who had a pretence been Crushed out of human nature, into that Of a wild beast, had thence again been crushed Into the being of some mighty thing— That now swept sailing on van-like wings Amid the whirls of an aerial storm, 142a That out extending, in its murky mass, Hung heaving, like a black tent-roof,x o’er all The floor of Africa.

Still on I swept, And still as far as my keen vision went That now was gifted with a power that seemed To pierce all space, I saw the vapors roll, But soon in the remotest distance they Were different, as touched with sunny splendors And nearing still to these I saw them dash Like the wild surges of an uproused sea Of molten gold against the marble crags Of heaven-high mountains, which, though far below My flight, yet pierced up through them all, and stood Immovable as Fate. Beneath me, lo The grandam of the kingdom of the air Was circling in its magnitude immense! A dread magnificence of which before I might not even dream. I saw its quick And subtle interchange of forms and hues That more & more astonished: saw its black And solid-seeming reservoirs of rain— Its laboring forges of the awful thunder

At last as onward still I swept, above A murcky mass of vapour, that spread flat Like a vast floor of purest alabaster Behold, reflected in its quick gleam I saw a Shadow that swept on with me Reversed as was the Lion’s in the well: With van-like wings, with eyeballs seething fire 143 With taloned claws an cruel down-bent beak: The shadow of a monstrous Eagle—such As seemed indeed, the mightiest that had sailed The seas of space, since Adam named the first.

No doubt was left me as to what I was: My fated soul had passed into the form Of that hugh Eagle which was shadowed there! My first impression from the thing I saw Was a cold horror! I was since again Imprisoned in the being of a Brute, Albeit a noble on; and had again To live for ages,—centuries it might be, In the brute nature of a being, yet Inferior, by infinite descents, Even to that remnant of intelligent mind Which still went with me: had to live a life Of daily carnage, and to sail abroad A terror to all birds & gentle beasts That heard the rushing of my rapid wings! A royal bird indeed—and thence alone Whose inacessible pride of place was still The solemn solitude of the mightiest hills, And of the highest heavens

But in truth, Nor much, for many periods, had I need To search for prey, for countless hosts of men Were mustered forth o’er all the face of earth Casting a iron gleam o’er trampled leagues Of golden corn, or as thy onward marches, Leaving behind them raging seas of fire, In whose red surges cities thronged with men. And happy villages, and well-tilled fields That rang so late with rural thankfulness 144 Were whelmed in one wild doom, and from the earth All but erased. Or to then wish at last, Confronted upon some set field of fight Their sullen cloud-like masses, in their might, Rushed at each other with a dreadful roar That shrunk the furious yells of great beasts Into mere make-believe:—rushed on to wounds And sumless slaughter, with a madness such As could have issued only out of Hell! x Or else about fenced cities they would pitch Their crowded camp, and leaguer them for years Sowing the fields about then with a slime Of carnage, till their growths were plagues alone o And finally beating them so flat, that lo The very names of them were heard no more! What is the ravage made by beasts and birds Of Prey is man’s

With leisure hence derived, o (For ever when I hungered, close at hand, A fresh-slain man lay in his smoking gore) And even scandalised at what I saw: I said my Realm is large—and I will hold My state in all its regions; and though still The savage instincts of my Eagle life Were fierce within me—not the less I felt That how my lot was mollified and cast Into a mould even capable of joy Safe as I was from all pursuit—from thirst From famine, and from words; for what wild fool Would shoot an arrow at me as I soared Above the populations. It was plain That solaces, though few and far between Were added to me—and I thence derived Even in the musing of my Egle-heart A feeling, that was day by day conformed That not for ever was my soul condemned To suffer in the body of a Brute! For though the memory of the towering crimes And matchless lusts that filled my whole career While human, still was within evermore No longer did they shed about my life That scorpian venom whose so subtle fire Once burnt like blight. Nay I remembered well How often I had looked with longing eyes Up at the clear Egyptian heaven, and watched The wings that clove them, envying every bird: 145 That, soaring in the sunshine, seemed to be Exempt from all the grovelling cares of men! Oft I remememberd how, even in my most Triumphant moment I had wished to exchange My being with an ibis which was then Floating away, upon his speckled wings Tow’rds Memphis, at a height in heaven, secure From hurt and inaccessible to envy! I thought how oft, when with my hunting train In that rude region spread about the cataracts I had watched the eagles as they rose aloft Into the lovely blue, and saw them turn As through the colored clouds still up they went Against the sunshine, into feathered plays Of royal purple, edged with burning gold

These fields of space were now my kingdom! Why Should I then not enjoy them! Nay, the faculty Alone of motion in a world so rich Was somewhad noble—but to more at will With a rapidity through bondless space That nothing else might rival—and to have The majesty of the everlasting hills Perpetually beneath me, with the green Luxuriance of the people feeding plains Broad rivers, and the towering towns, that sat Beside their spacious mouths, and out beyond. The lonely strength of the dark-heaving seas This liberty began to move my pride Like somewhat godlike, and in moving, made A sure impression that kept graining more Into the texture of my brute estate Yea, graining in, through all its animal lusts —And savage wonts
