II

The Ancient Mariner.

Under a low sky of leaden hue A still sea glimmers in wide shoreless view, And midward—there, that sea and sky between, A sole Ship, idle as a cloud, is seen, On whose lone deck no living Wight doth stir, Save One—even he, the doom-struck Mariner! Fixed lies she there—but in her shade supine What slimy creatures writhe the rolling brine! While in the dim dead air that round her clings, Behold those vanishing visionary wings— Those vague-seen signs of a strange Yet-to-be, Through ghostly Powers at work on that mysterious Sea!
