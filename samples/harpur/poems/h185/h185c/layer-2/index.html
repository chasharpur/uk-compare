Ideality.

Written in 1840.

Spirit of Dreams! When many a wandered height Shut Paradise from banished Adam’s sight, Two wedded Powers were given thenceforth to stray On either hand, companions of his way: This Hope was named in heaven from whence he came, And that of Melancholy bore the name. Thy parents these—who wing’d thee with a ray Snatched from Perfection as she passed away, And inwardly endued thee with the grace That once suffused unfallen Nature’s face.

235

Queen-mother of Elysiums, and all fair Hesperian Gardens, hear thy suppliant’s prayer! Lend me the hues which from all splendors rich And rich On earth, in oceans, and in air—and which In God’s great world, thou dost so well compound:— From nameless flowers that wild in forests run, Up to the glories of the setting sun— From twinklings of the dew-besprinkled sward, Up to the banded glows of rainbows broad— From those white flickers that from fountains leap, To the phosphoric lightnings of the deep— From wave-wet sands that glisten on the shore, To blazing diamonds, and rich-beaming ore:— And thence from all their ultimata, combined And interblended with the light of Mind:— Those hues now lend thy suppliant, for he The brilliant steps would trace, of thy whole ministry!

O for a golden lyre, whilst first I sing How thou dost lovelier make the loveliest thing! Woman is beautiful!—no more: till, lo, She stands in thy regard’s esthetic glow! At once her being sweeter sweets invest Than breathe in gales of Araby the blest! A thousand bright particulars are given, And she outshines the very Moon of heaven! 236 This light how glorious—a God-woven robe Wrapping in living warmth the fruitful globe! Even so—and yet thy thaumaturgic ray Can pour an added brilliance o’er the day! Lovely this prospect! Yet thy presence here, Doubles each glory of the golden year! Breathe but thy influence o’er the common plain, And Nature flushes round with Eden-glows again!

O he whose wild heart leaps to thy wild call, Hath yet some joy whatever may befall! Hath yet some wealth where Destitution reigns— Where Rapine sits enthroned, and Slavery tugs her chains! Still his possessions compass whatsoe’er Of good and beauty nature treasures there! Still the lone vale, wild hill, and pathless grove To him belongeth—each a mine of love! For him what gold is in the morning’s hues! What unbought jewels are the lucent dews! What royal mansions are the brook-side bowers! What gay assemblies the balm-breathing flowers! Then every bird that pipes a matin lay, Doth unto him a subject’s tribute pay, 237 Doth unto him anthemic tribute pay, Yea! every grace of earth and air and sky— Are vassals of his sovereign ear and eye!

And when the busy hums of Mammon’s brood Fail like the voices of an ebbing flood, And from the scenes that glared with her array Pride-drunken Fashion rolls fatigued away; When from mild Evening’s shadowy robe the breeze Shakes cool delight and odorous messages; And when no longer the Sun’s beams invest The dusky hills, but round him, in the west, Are gathered, even like a summon’d host In gorgeous tents on the horizon’s coast, Ere to the regions of a separate day He rolls at length in purple pomp away;— Whilst yet—some moments yet, withdrawing gleams Travel the air and die along the streams, That symphonise in many-sounding moods To vespers shrilling through the gloaming woods: A sumptuous festival that hour shall prove, To bathe his royal soul with harmony and love— Yea, fill it, like a fountain, with immense 238 Content in the great God’s so great munificence!

When up the unclouded heaven, in starry sheen Walks Night, like a gen-spangled Ethiope Queen, And from her solemn curtains wide unfurled Shakes dense repose upon the drowsy world, No sordid appetite—no passion mean Chains down thy Votary to this damp Terrene; But, following thee, he freely soars afar From moon to planet and from star to star! Yea, outward still, if venturous, he may trace Thy sounding flight through dimmest depths of space, Till from the boundless gaining boundless might, He feel his being alike infinite!

And now, though mournful be the current theme— A tale of hapless love—or faded dream Of human worth, whose spirits, once so bright, Like halcyons gemm’d the rivers of delight, But fled Misfortune’s wintry floods before, Never to show their shining plumage more! Still those, thy hope-tired follower ever near, Sublim’st each sigh and hallow’st every tear, Till ev’n despair, in his so wild-wing’d and free, Expression, half ethereal grows to be, 237 And deepest anguish mounts to highest luxury! Nor rests with him thy influence alone, Men love him for the sorrows he hath known: And sung so sweelty—for the measured moan Of his complaint, unlike the worldling’s care, Is pleasing—yea, is like a plaintive air Heard mourning across waters wide and lone, When in them all heaven’s golden isles are shown— A time of starry peace, when even grief Becomes itself the means of ultimate relief!

Through all things thus—through pleasure and through pain— Thou stead’st thy follower with some special gain. Doth Danger threaten and doth Terror scream? O’er their wild fronts a reconciling gleam Thy wings reflect! The storm that rends the sky And ploughs the ocean deeps, if thou art nigh! Gives to the heart like strength, and lifts the soul on high! In Madness, ev’n, an eloquence canst thou find That fortifies while it appals the mind! Canst witch from lone Remorse and Murder fell, Such pleas as make even Vengeance placable! Throw a fierce loveliness o’er Ruin’s face! And over Death>’s calm front an enviable grace! Nay, be thy glance but turned where devils dwell, And a sad glory lightens out of Hell!

238

Spirit of Dreams! it yet remains to say How thou hast brightened my peculiar way.— When most beset by troubles stern and new— When foes most triumph’d—friends were most untrue, How much through thee of sweetness and of worth Was with me still, where’er I wandered forth? Then as I breasted the long-sounding gale On some lone hill—or, melancholy-pale, Hid me adown some seldom trodden vale, Brown with primeval trees that solemn grew By flowing waters struggling into view, My soul’s distractions thou hast all subdued Through dreams of Love and Liberty and Good!

Now travelled far—companionless, and bowed Wearily down on the rock-roughened road— Thee in that desert meeting, I have found Baronical halls in the old caverns round, And, wrapt in high chivalrie visions there Have bearded recreant knights and succored ladies fair.

Anon, slow wending over fertile plains, Where lay some worldling’s ill-deserved domains, At Fortune’s blindness have I much repined, 239 In heart defiant, and faith-hurt in mind: Till ‘neath thy brooding wings out o’er me spread, A new content, soul-born content, hath smiling said: What though the soil another’s right may be, Not less its vernal beauty lives for me! Yon bright stream mirrors me with heaven above,— The soft airs greet me with a kiss of love,— Fair flowers glow round me in their sweet delight,— Birds shower down gladness in their passing flight;— And while these shine abroad, and freshly grow, And purely breathe, and set their songs a-flow, Even thus, for me,—O say, what here of fine, Fresh, bright, and glad, hath he, that is not also mine?

Now, like a wizard, standing wild and lone On some great mountain’s cloud-frequented cone, Meet altar of a universal Fane, Where no fiend-rites had left their bloody stain! Thou gav’st my soul a prophet-power, to sieze High consolations out of mysteries!

O Spirit! thus attent me to the last; For though thou lay’st not, thou yet warm’st the blast Of bitter-cold contumely, when ’tis blown, 240 As always, ‘gainst all worth, if lowly and alone! And by thy parent, Hope, from hour to hour, Revested with a promissory power, Distill’st for Genius that neglected strays A draught of triumph from its future days, Or stretchest, as a rainbow strides the sea, Over all time,—all ages yet to be,—! The assurance of its immortality Yea, to the cast-away, unsought of all, Thou bring’st the heart-taste of a friendly call; Art to captivity as an eagle’s wing, Airs from the hills, by Freedom roamed, to bring; On the wild sea the green delight of earth; ‘Mid Winter’s wrath a sense of Summer’s mirth: Lap me in Visions! which may nought dispel Till the hard Real deserves the Muse’s praise as well!
