LITERARY REGISTER.

IDEALITY.

BY CHARLES HARPUR.

PART I.

Spirit of Dreams!—when many a wander’d height Shut Paradise from banish’d Adam’s sight, Two wedded powers were given thenceforth to stray On either hand, companions of his way; This Hope was nam’d in heaven, whence he came, And that of Melancholy bore the name: Thy parents these—who wing d thee with a ray Snatch’d from perfection as she pass’d away; And cloth d thee with a mem’ry of the grace And splendor that suffus’d, ere fallen, nature’s face.

Creator of Elysiums! and of dear Hesperian Gardens, thy fond suppliant hear! Lend me thy hues—which of all colours rare And rich on earth, in ocean, and in air— From nameless flow’rs that wild in forests run, To the effulgence of the setting sun— From the pale dew drops which impearl the sward, To rainbows vast, and Boreal meteors broad— From the white radiance that o’er fountains sleeps, To the phosphoric lightning of the deeps— From yellow sands that glitter on the shore, To blazy diamonds, and the flaming ore;— Thou dost compound—thine own hues lend! for thy Bright footsteps I would trace—describe thy ministry.

O for thy Milton’s voice! whilst first I sing How thou dost beautify the loveliest thing. Thrice exquisite the fair!—no more; till thou Turn’st on them thy regard’s seraphic glow; At once, their beings sweeter sweets invest, Than breathe in gales of Arabie the blest! A thousand bright particulars are given, And they outshine the very stars of heaven! Glorious this light!—yet shall thy secret ray Effuse an added brilliance o er the day! Lovely yon scene!—yet shall thy presence there, Furnish each glory of the golden year! Breathe but thy influence o er the common plain, And nature flushes round with Eden’s glow again.

Oh, he whose heart responds to thy wild strains, Is even rich where Destitution reigns Where Rapine sits enthron’d, and slavery hugs her chains! Still his possessions compass whatsoe’er Of good and lovely nature treasures there: Still each lone vale, wild hill, and shady grove, To him belongeth—each a mine of love! For him what wealth is in the morning’s hues! What diamonds flame in the pellucid dews! What costly mansions are the brook-side bowers! What gay assemblies are the nodding flowers! The various birds that pipe a matin song, Are then to him, a tributary throng! Yes, then, a vassal to his sov’reign eye Is every grace of earth,—each splendor of the sky.

And when the busy hums of Mammon’s brood Fail, like the voices of an ebbing flood; And from the scene he marr’d with stern array, Pride-drunken pomp hath shrunk fatigued away; When from mild evening’s shadowy robe, the breeze Shakes cool delight, and od’rous messages; And when no longer sunny beams invest The dusky hills, but gather in the west Around their regent, like a summon d host In gorgeous tents on the horizon[’]s coast; Into the regions of a sep’rate day With him, at length, to roll in purple pomp away: Whilst yet—some moments yet, withdrawing gleams Travel the air, and die along the streams, That symphonise in many-sounding moods To the shrill vespers of the dark’ning woods: A sumptuous feast that closing hour shall prove, His soul to fill with harmony and love! Flooding its brimming fountains with intense, Nay, with august delight in God s munificence!

PART II.

With stilly step, when from the eastern height Walks, like a spangled Ethiope Queen, the Night; And from her solemn banner, wide unfurl’d, Shakes dense repose upon the drowsy world; No sordid appetite,—no passion mean, Confines thy Vot[a]ry to this mere Terrene! Thee freely following, he soars afar From moon to planet, and from star to star! Thence onward still, if venturous, he may trace Thy sounding flight thro’ dimmer depth of space; Till, the apparent bounds of Order crost, He feel his Being in all vasty Wonder lost.

Now, in sad mood, if sorrow be the theme— The hopeless lover—or the faded dream Of Human glory, kindliness, and truth, Whose halcyons gemm’d Life limpid stream in youth, But fled the cold world s wintry floods before, Never to show their shining plumage more! Still thou, thy hope-tired Follower ever near, Sublim’st each sigh, and hallow’st every tear, Till e’en despair ethereal grows to be, And deepest anguish highest luxury! Nor rests with him thy influence alone, Men love him for the suff ring he hath known: For his complaint, unlike the worldling’s care, Is sadly-pleasing, as a plaintive air Is, nightly heard, o’er waters wide and lone, When all the golden eyes of Heav’n are looking down.

And now doth Danger threat, and Terror scream? O’er their wild fronts a reconciling gleam Thy wings reflect: The storm that rends the sky, And ploughs the deep, enchants if thou art nigh! An eloquence in Madness thou canst find, That ravishes while it appals the mind! Canst strike from lone Remorse, and Horror fell, Tones which, when heard, make vengeance placable! Throw a fierce loveliness o’er Ruin’s face! And o’er the cheek of Death an enviable grace! Nay, be thy glance but turn’d where devils dwell, And a sad splendor lightens over Hell!

Spouse of the Bard! to sing I next essay, How thou hast brighten’d my peculiar way.— When troubles most beset me, stern and new,— When foes most triumph’d friends were most untrue, How much of sweetness still twas mine to know, With Thee, unheeded, wand’ring to and fro; Then, as I breasted full the sounding gale, On some lone hill—or, melancholoy-pale, Hid me adown the seldom trodden vale, Brown with primeval trees, that solemn grew By flowing waters struggling into view,— Thou medicin’d my soul’s distractions rude, With dreams of Love, and Liberty, and Good.

Now trav’lling far,—companionless, and bow’d With weariness, on the rock-roughen’d road,— Thee meeting in that desert, I have found Baronial halls in the old caverns round; And, rapt in high chivalric vision, there Have bearded recreant knight, and succor’d lady fair.

Anon, slow wending over fertile plains, Enclos’d—some worldling’s ill-deserv’d domains, At Fortune’s blindness I have there repined, In heart defyant, and depress’d in mind; Till warming in thy rays, around me spread, Content, reviving in my heart, hath said— What tho’ the soil another’s right may be, Not less its vernal beauty smiles for thee! Yon bright stream mirrors the bright sun above! The gale breathes round thee with a voice of love! Gay flow’rs gaze upward in their sweet delight! Birds show r down gladness in their passing flight! And while these vital shine, and freshly flow, And purely breathe, and melodise, and glow, Even thus, for thee,—Oh, say, what here of fine, Fresh, bright, and pure, hath he, that is not also thine?

Now, like a brain-sick wizard, standing lone On some wild mountain’s cloud-frequented cone; Where its grey altar stone a pillary crowd Of pines enroof’d; Religion’s best abode! Thou gav’st my soul a prophet-pow’r, to sieze High marvels, and consoling mysteries.

O Spirit, thus attend me to the last; For, unpreventing, thou yet warm’st the blast Of cold contumely, by the bitter world Against the aims of lowly Merit hurl d; And by thy Parent, Hope, when worn and low Relumined aye with promissory glow, Distill’st for Genius, that neglected strays, A draught of triumph from its future days; Or stretchest, as a rainbow strides the sea, Over all time its immortality: Bring’st to the cast away, unloved and lone, A friendly voice—a far seraphic tone; Art to captivity an eagle[’]s wing, News from the hills, by Freedom roam’d, to bring; On the wild sea the green delight of earth; Mid Winter’s wrath a sense of Summer’s mirth; Lap me in Visions! and may naught destroy Thy spells—till what is real deserves the name of Joy.
