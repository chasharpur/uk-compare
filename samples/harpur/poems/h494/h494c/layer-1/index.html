XXXVII.

Self Dependence.

I who write this have practised what I preach— Have the hard road of Self-dependence trod; And all who would a growth heroic reach, Must in themselves trust only—under God! And what a sovereign thing is labor, which For many freedom manfully undergone, O’er all detraction (while it maketh rich) Will give us victory though we stand alone. Depend not upon friends. I once believed In many, and I yet believe in one: But why should we much risk the being deceived? Why trust until our social faith’s undone? Still, though thus self-dependent, tow’rds our kind Bear we at least a charitable mind.
