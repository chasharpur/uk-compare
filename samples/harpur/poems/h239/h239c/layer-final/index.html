NEW WORDS TO AN OLD AIR.

By Charles Harpur.

I wandered east, I wandered west, For long years, hoping yearly That Fortune at last would give me rest With the One I lov’d most dearly: Hoping ever it might be so, As the spirit of fancy inclined me, So purely my heart’s queen long, long ago, Was the Girl I left Behind Me.

To wander aye would seem my lot, To wander aye, though weary, Striving in vain to compass what My spirit hath prized most dearly. No longer I deem it may be so— For fancy no longer can blind me: Ah, well a-day, for the long, long ago, And the Girl I left Behind Me.

It may be, had we mated been, And I had never wandered, The Eden of Love ere this had seen Its beauty and bloom all squandered: But oh, in my heart, a vacant ache At thought of the past, will remind me How lonely it is for the sad sweet sake Of the Girl I left Behind Me!

September, 1859.
