5e

THE GIRL I LEFT BEHIND ME.

III.

I wandered east, I wandered west, For long years, hoping yearly That fortune at least would give me rest With the one I loved most dearly: Hoping ever it might be so, As the spirit of fancy inclined me, So purely my heart’s queen long ago, Was the girl I left behind me.

To wander aye would seem my lot, To wander aye, though weary; Striving in vain to compass what My spirit hath prized most dearly! No longer I deem it may be so— No longer can fancy blind me! Alas—alas, for the long ago And the girl I left behind me!

It may be, had we mated been, And I had never wandered, The Eden of love ere this had seen Its beauty and bloom all squandered. But oh! in my heart a vacant ache, When I think of the past, will remind me How lonely it is—and for the sake Of the girl I left behind me.
