[THE GIRL I LEFT BEHIND ME]

I wandered east, I wandered west, For long years, hoping yearly That Fortune at last might me rest With the one loved most dearly: Hoping ever, it might be so, As the spirit of Fancy inclined me— So meek the queen of my heart long ago Was the girl I left behind me!

To wander aye would seem my lot— To wander till I’m weary! Striving in vain to compass what My spirit hath prized most dearly! No longer I deem it can be so— No longer can Fancy blind me! Alas—Alas, for the Long Ago, And the girl I left behind me!

It may be, had we mated been And I had left her never, Our wretched love ere this had seen Its spells old snapt forever! But oh on my heart there’s a vacant ake That must evermore remind me How lonely it is—and for the sake Of the girl I left behind me.
