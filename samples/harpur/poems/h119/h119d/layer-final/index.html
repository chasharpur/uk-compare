XXXV.

EPITAPH ON A SAILOR.

In port at last. Around his bark no more The waves shall threaten or the tempest roar. None ever voyäged the wild sea of Life Less warped and rudened by its stormy strife. A man in strength, in intellect, in will; Full of resources; full of tact and skill; Brave; self-reliant;—yet of manners mild, And in simplicity of soul—a child. And I who mourn him, and who knew him well, Tell of him now but what the Angels tell.
