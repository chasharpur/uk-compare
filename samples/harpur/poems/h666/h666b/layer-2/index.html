The Voice of the Native Oak

Who hath laid him underneath An old oak by a lonely stream? He hath heard an utterance breathe Sadder than aught else may seem

Up in its dusk boughs, down tressing Like the hair of a giant’s head, Mournful things beyond our guessing Day and night are uttered.

Yea even when the waveless air May only stir the lightest leaf; A lowly voice keeps moaning there Wordless oracles of grief

23

So lonely spirit that hath dwelt For ages in one lonely tree Some dreary Spirit that hath felt The birth of Eternity

Till the listener surely deems That some lone spirit of the air Hath made that tree the lute of themes Wilder—lonelier than despair.
