The Voice of the Wild Oak

Who hath laid him underneath An wild oak by a lonely stream? He hath heard a Spirit breathe Sadder than aught else may seem.

Up in its dusk boughs, down tressing Like the hair of a giant’s head, Mournful things beyond our guessing Day and night are utterëd

Yea even when the waveless air Only stirreth the lightest leaf, That wierd voice still keeps moaning there Dreary oracles of grief

For surely, there doth ever dwell Some fallen Spirit Hath made that tree its wierd—and dreams Its [unclear] mysteries there
