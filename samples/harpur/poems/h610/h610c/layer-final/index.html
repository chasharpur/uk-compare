XXX. X

To Henry Parkes:

Before having seen him, but after the passage between us of several friendly tokens.

Dear Henry, though thy face I have not seen, Nor heard thy voice,—assured that one doth glow With goodness, and the other hath a flow Like a broad stream,—there dwelleth none between The waters of Port Jackson and the scene Where now I muse, that I so far would go To talk with, and more intimately know, In these so golden Autumn eves serene.And 262 And when we yet shall meet, say, shall not we Be as old friends at once, and sit and pour Our souls together? which, so mixed, shall be A flowing draught of thought-thick bardic lore, Such as Keats reeled with when he looked out o’er The ocean of old Homer’s minstrelsy.
