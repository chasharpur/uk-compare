V.

Dryden.

Even as French frippery anlaid his times, Art left small room for Nature in his rhymes. Of Waller’s school—but a far mightier flame Was his, illumining the skies of Fame.

Though scarce a genius, in his mind intense There wrought the alchymic power of native sense. A satirist—but chiefly therein great Because the slave of Jealousy and Hate. Well versed in selfish natures, he could hit The lurking purpose with the beam of wit; And finding in himself the colors still, Portray a sycophant with unerring skill.

None more than he accomplished to translate: And did he steal, adopt, or imitate, A subtle judgment to the work he brought, Repined the image and enlarged the thought.

Proud of the logic of his pointed rhyme, He nor affects, nor reaches, the sublime: 62a If we except the peerless festal song, Melting to pity and love the frantic heart of Wrong; And may not, sometimes, condescend to praise As such, the fury of his jingling Plays.

Not much I love him—yet, perforce, admire The mounting rage of his material fire! Though coarse of phrase and prodigal of verse, Sonorous his style and terse; His lines, like brooks, now warblingly assuage And now, like mountain floods, thunder across the page.+

+ It has sometimes struck me since this Criticism was written, that, from my being at that time too much under certain modern literary influences, I hardly did “glorious John” justice. Still I think that he was deficient in that fine breadth of eye and open relish of heart for the simplicities of Nature, which are the surest indications, as the fruits of them are the most lasting manifestations, of a thorough poetical genius. He was wanting in them, for in modernizing Chaucer, who is streaked all over with evidences of their fresh frank morning gladness, he conventionalised his pictures into mere stage scene-paintings. And wanting these, he was wanting in the most perpetuative qualities of the Poet; these bring such simply because they cannot find their objects in modes—nor in anything short of the immortalities of Nature. C.H.
