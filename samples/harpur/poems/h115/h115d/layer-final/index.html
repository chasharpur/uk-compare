Poems in early Life by Charles Harpur

English Beauty

Tell me not of maids Italian or the stately dames of Spain, Let their beauty’s burning semblance fire some other brain! But a gentle English Maiden—lily fair—a flower of light; She has all the dainty graces whereof I would write.

Smiles as rich and rare as rubies—utterance purely feminine Heaven blue eyes that orb affection as in spheres divine! And with these modest stillness, all the dearer that it seems And unwitting revelation of most loving dreams.

Sweet and lowly flowers that glare not [are] the latest to d[ec]ay; Stars that bring their blessing nearest have the mildest ray; He who visioned Desdemona was sweet Mary Ardens child; He who pictured Una loved an English maiden mild.
