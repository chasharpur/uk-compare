Out of the Wilds to Egypt I returned: Where it was now supposed that I had gone Down with the ship, and perished; and where none— Not even the dwarf Eunuck who of old Had worshipped me, and with a wizard heed Divined my every wish ere I myself Might fully feel it,—no, not even he 194 Now recognised me: for indeed my face And form were wholly changed,—from passing fair To fairer yet; from manly, to a pile So nobly built, that in all eyes I looked More beautiful than beseemed a mortal man— Yea, beautiful as Thammuz! Nor was changed My person only, but my heart and head. Ambition wilder than a leopard’s thirst For blood of roe, or flying hart, possessed My spirit, like the madness of a God! But which I yet, even in its fiercest strain, Could curb and guide with sovereign strength of will. And thus accomplished, on my new career I started.

And still I strode From bad to worse, corrupting as I went The lowly, making them more abject yet; Awing as with a thunder-bearing hand The high and affluent; while I bound the strong To basest service, though with chains of gold. Down to its death The old Greek dynasty was sinking fast, And with it all dominion—body and soul!
