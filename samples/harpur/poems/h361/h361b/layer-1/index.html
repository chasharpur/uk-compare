The Sonnet.

Why scorn the Sonnet? Though one-thoughted, it May show us,—as by the minutest star The world’s bright order is evinced most far,— Through how much truth may pierce and flit One subtle beam of wit Shot from a Poet’s brain! Besides the value of no Thing on earth Is in its bulk—but, in its grain: Lo man’s small body doth more soul engirth Than doth behemoth’s, big as it may be; Or As the tiniest gem’s of greater worth Than any boulder washed up by the sea. Nor can, with any reason, we Presume to lour with scorn Upon a lovely flower, Because it happend not to be tree.
