The Sonnet.

Why slight The Sonnet!: Though one-thoughted, it May show us,—as by the minutest star The world’s bright order is evinced most far,— Through how much truth may pierce and flit One subtle searching beam of wit Shot from a Poet’s regal brain! Besides the value of no Thing on earth 391 Is in its bulk—but, purely in its grain: As man’s small body doth more soul engirth Than doth behemoth’s, big as it may be; Or as the tiniest pearl’s of greater worth Than any boulder washed up by the sea. Wherefore, to slight a thing of mind (So long as it be happy in its kind) Because by verbal steps to climb it, Or with watch in hand to time it, May not ask at least an hour, Is just as tasteless, as it were if we Should look with scorn upon a flower Because ’twas not a Tree.
