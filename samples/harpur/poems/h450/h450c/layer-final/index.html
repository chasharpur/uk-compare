XXXIII.

III

The Inference

Loss follows gain, and sadness waits on mirth, And much is wasted where too much is given; We cannot fully have our joy on earth Without diminishing our joy in heaven. Envy dogs merit; madness neighbors wit; Stale is their gladness who were never sad; And Divès in this fleshly life, ’tis writ, Received his good things, Lazarus his bad. Thus, Nora, o’er the waves of many things My searching mind, even like a ship, is tossed, And from the quest this only inference brings: That True Love in its earthly course is crossed, Lest by dull worldly usage it should be Lowered in its final immortality.
