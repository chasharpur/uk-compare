XXXIII

The same Subject continued:

(In the manner of Shakspear’s Sonnets)

Loss follows gain, and sadness waits on mirth; And much is wasted where too much is given; We cannot fully have our joy on earth, Without diminishing our joy in heaven. Envy dogs merit; madness neighbours wit; And Stale is their gladness who were never sad; And Divès in this fleshly life, ’tis writ, Received his good things, Lazarus his bad. Thus, Rosa, o’er the waves of many things My searching mind, even like a ship, is tossed, 67 And all that Wisdom for my profit brings Is that True Love in this low stage is crossed, Lest by material uses it should be Much tarnished in its immortality.
