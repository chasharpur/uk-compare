[LUCK OUT OF SEASON]

A pilgrim of old who was hungering madly, Found a small bag quite full on his desert path lonely; “Thank God! ’tis a store of parched wheat!” he cried gladly— But he wept when he found it a bag of pearls only. Needs there more, then, to patent this truth to our reason, That the best things are only good things in their season?
