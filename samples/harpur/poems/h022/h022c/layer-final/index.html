An Epitaph.

Be his rest who sleeps below, Done to death by toil and woe, Sound and sweet: So much in fortune did he lack, So little meet Of kindness, as with bleeding feet He journeyed life’s most barren track, That only Hate in its deceit, Not Love, not Pity, would intreat To have him back! But he sleeps well where many a bloom That might not grace his living home Pranks the raised sod; Tokening perhaps, that one who here Missed Fortune’s smile, hath met elsewhere The smile of God.
