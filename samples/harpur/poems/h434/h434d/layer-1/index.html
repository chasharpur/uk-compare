XVII.

[RECORDS OF A POET’S LOVE XVII]

Night was new throned in heaven, and we did rove— Together in the cool and shadowless haze, At the wild lustre of the stars of gaze, Ere yet the Moon, whose rim just showed above A pine-crowned hill, should dim them. Soon did move In both our souls like fancies—that their rays Were richer for all eyes that from the ways Of Man’s long past had heavenward looked in love,— Still through their midst our eyes did roving run From orb to orb of such as seemed most fair, To fix at last, with mutual heed, on one That gloried in the West beyond compare, And thence we deemed, that when the day was done The Spirit of our joy glowed mansioned there.
