SONNET TO DR. LANG.

(written before his departure for England, and now republished)

Right onward! For the genius of the land— The spirit of this virgin soil, so meet For a new birth of Freedom, doth command The Muse, in loud heroic verse to greet All progress in the way which now thy feet Are treading. Be it thine, too, in thy grand Old age, to fashion with paternal hand A charter, that shall all our rights complete. Remit not! But now more than ever let ‘Right onward’ be thy motto; till thy name Become a household jewel richly set On that great heart of Freedom which shall flame Yet in Australia’s bosom?—to the shame Of Wentworth’s memory meshed in Mammon’s net.

CHARLES HARPUR.
