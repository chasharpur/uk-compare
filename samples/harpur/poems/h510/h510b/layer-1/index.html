Yet fear moved not Idomenëus—such As might have seizd upon some tender boy. But he stood rigid, like a mountain boar, That

in his well-tried strength confiding

, dares To wait the mighty din of many men Coming against him, out in the wild pastures: His bowed back bristles up; his red eyes glare With fiery anger, and he whets his tusks; Intent on keeping dogs and men at bay: Even so the spear-renowned Idomenëus Awaited the forthcoming there against him Of swift Eneas, nor retired.
