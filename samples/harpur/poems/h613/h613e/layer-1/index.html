44

XXX.

To James Norton Esq.

Think you I have not skill to gather gold, If I could love it as some others do? Or that I lack the spirit of a bold And resolute man in any cause that’s true, Because I do not juggle with some crew Of politic schemers? Let the truth be told: Whatever I can value I can mould Right deftly to my ends—and boldly too. “But Fame he sought not through a gainful hand,” (This of my being let the future tell) “Nor through the arts of popular command; But, in retirement, where the Muses dwell, That his life’s legacy might be a well Of living water to a thirsty land.”
