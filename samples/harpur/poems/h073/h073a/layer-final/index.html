5e

Compensation.

A sylphic form and angel face, A young life void of sin,— The outward lineaments and grace Of a white soul within: Such was my spirit’s visioned joy, When I was a dreaming Boy.

A figure rich in pleasant health, A warm heart and a good,— The summer attributes and wealth Of thorough womanhood: Such was my spirit’s yearned-for truth, When I was a lonely youth.

And in my manhood it may be I have not chanced to find One being shadowing faithfully The vision of my mind; Yet therefore feel I not as crost— Nothing but a dream is lost.

And in the power that dreamt there lives A love-creating ray, Worth all that fortune ever gives Or ever takes away; Inspiring oft a genial rhyme Even ’mid the snows of time.
