6d

V.

Point Taking.

Who takes a point beyond his right, Though legal, or though custom-tight, Would rob you of your purse, in fact, With equal safety in the act.

Note.—What a diadem of jewels in human character, are generous and lofty feelings, terminating (as they always do) in manly and honest principles of conduct? A man whose nature is thus elementally fraternal and elevated, will be just in all his dealings, because he is above all petty bias—because that which is fair and equal, not that which is gainful to himself alone, is his first and prevailing consideration. But these are so called men, who even glory in what is termed gaining a point—that is, a dishonest advantage in a bargain, or in any other worldly transaction. Note them: not for imitation, but for avoidance; they are unworthy of the high standing and privilege of the glorious creature, Man! They are of the “devil’s regiment,” as well as robbers, and thieves, and swindlers.
