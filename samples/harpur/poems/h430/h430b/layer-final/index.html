13.

[RECORDS OF A POET’S LOVE XIII]

There is one spot my grateful heart could deem A lingering vestage of old Paradise, Albeit not much to captivate the eyes Of ornament is there. And when I dream Of Love and Beauty, its dear objects gleam Amid the worldly troubles that arise To cloud me, like bright glimpses of the skies Smiling through storm-glooms that across them stream. I gaze upon it from the neighboring hills, And like a warbler of the forest, when The spirit of joy its artless being thrills Even to the pinions,—my wild fancy then Thitherward hurries on delighted plumes, All sweetened to return, as from a bower of blooms.
