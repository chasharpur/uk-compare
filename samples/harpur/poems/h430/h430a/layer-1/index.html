X XIII.

The Scene of the Confession.

There is one Spot in that my f grateful heart could deem 50 A lingering vestage of old Paradise, Albeit not much to captivate the eyes Of ornament is there. And when I dream Of Love and Beauty, its dear objects gleam Between the worldly troubles that arise To cloud me, like bright glimpses of the skies Smiling through vapours that across them stream. I gaze upon it from the neighbouring hills, And like a warbler of the forest, when The spirit of joy its artless being thrills Even to the pinions,—my glad fancy then Thitherward hurries on delighted plumes— All sweetened to return as from a bower of blooms.
