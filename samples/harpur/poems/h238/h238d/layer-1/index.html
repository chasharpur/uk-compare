Lyrics. By Charles Harpur.

Under the Wild-fig Tree.

Ah many and many a day is gone Since face to face were we, Gramachree! Under the shadowy Wild-fig Tree 62 While the suns of the past went cloudless down; And yet how my distant heart is thrilled Like a harp that is swept by the breeze alone, Whenever I think of thee, Gramachree! And my head with a dim wild stir is filled, Like the sound remote of a sooming sea.

‘Tis a sense of other [unclear] happier days, (When face to face were we, Gramachree! Under the shadowy Wild-fig Tree, Close hid from the low sun’s cloudless rays) That thrills and sweetens within my breast Like a breeze that afar from its Eden strays, Whenever I think of thee, Gramachree! And this dim brain-stir is the wild unrest Of a moaning and homeless memory.

Ah, that the beautiful Spirit of Love, That when a-met were we, Gramachree! Under the shadowy Wild-fig Tree, 63 Then sat as it were in the boughs above, Impassioning all our sympathies, Should be wandering now like the arkless dove— Or only have been, to be, Gramachree! Thus re-felt in the stir of Thought’s mystic breeze, And the shell-like murmurs of Memory!
