Many and many a day has flown Since standing together were we, Gramachree, Under the shadowy wild Fig-tree, While the suns of the Past in the red west shone; And yet how my distant heart is thrilled Like a harp that is swept by the breeze alone, Whenever I’m thinking of thee, Gramachree, And my head with a dim wild stir is filled, Like the sound remote of a sooming sea.

And so is it always with me, Gramachree, When I think of the Past, and of Thee, Gramachree, And so must it evermore be, Gramachree, When my soul dreameth back—dreameth back after thee!

‘Tis a fond feeling of happier days, (When standing together were we, Gramachree, Under the shadowy wild Fig-tree, Close hid from the low sun’s lengthening rays) That thrills and sweetens within my breast Like a breeze that afar from its Eden strays, Whenever I’m thinking if thee, Gramachree, And this dim brain-stir is the wild unrest Of a moaning and homeless memory.

And so is it always with me, Gramachree, 104 When I think of the Past, and of Thee, Gramachree, And so must it evermore be, Gramachree, When my soul dreameth back—dreameth back after thee.

Ah! that the beautiful Spirit of Love, That once, when together stood we, Gramachree, Under the shadowy wild Fig-tree, Sat homed as it were in the branches above Impassioning all our sympathies, Should be wandering now like the arkless dove, Or should then have been only to be, Gramachree, Thus re-felt in the stir of Thought’s mystic breeze, And the shell-like murmurs of Memory.

But so is it always with me, Gramachree, When I think of the Past, and of Thee, Gramachree, And so will it evermore be, Gramachree, When my soul dreameth back—dreameth back after thee.
