THE BANQUET OF LOVE.

‘TIS the hour of love, for fair is the night— In a mantle of brightness the cast is array’d: And though the moon lingers behind yon height, With the am’rous delay of a blushing fair maid, Leviathan like the stream ripples by, In its winding course afar, When like golden fruit in the boughs of the tree Seem to hang the broad planet and glittering star. The flowers sip in quiet the dews that are shed, Or expire in delight ‘neath the young lover’s tread; There is youth on his brow, there is hope in his eye, And he talks with his dream as he wandereth by, But a flash of desire spreads its light o’er his face— He starts from his trance, and he mendeth his pace; And now ‘neath yon lattice that looks on the grove, In the fullness of heart he invoketh his love.

e hath come forth in beauty’s hues, Floating in beauty’s beam; She’s brighter than the fairy muse Of the minstrel’s nightly dream. Her eyes are of an azure blue, Transparent as the silver dew, And each expression in them seem As tinctur’d with celestial mien. As fountains in the twylight seem All gilded by a nameless beam, Her dark locks falling from her head In clusters o’er her her shoulders spread, Or ripple as the breezes lave, Like sprayey curls that crown the wave; Her rounded cheeks are seats of bliss— Her lips seem only made to kiss. Whilst raving one would fain declare, That such a shape and such an air, An angel’s self could only wear; And [i]n the arms of truth and love She floats in brightness thro’ the grove.

d there is a bower in that leafy grove, That is sacred to lovers and pleasant to love. ‘Tis roof’d with the boughs of the myrtle and pine, And fenc’d all about with the rose tree and vine; And the bashful blue-bell and the violet fair, With all the sweet daughters of summer are there. The dew on the ripe rose, which hangs from its side, Glows like gems on the breast of a fair young bride, Whilst the shining leaves of embowering trees Seem to throng in wild dance to the notes of the breeze, And there they have entered, for fair is that bower, And true are their hearts and untroubled the hour.

Oh

, happy moments! None have power To paint the blisses of that hour, When lovers meet thus all alone, And every other thought alone, But those that soar all care above, And owe their origin to love: The heart is then at every kiss Bewilder’d in the round of bliss— A Paradise is then the grove, And all the soul is lost in love. ‘T‘T

then, O then, without alloy, We find there is on earth a joy: For the heart is full, yet the heart is light, And the soul is big with extatic might: For even pale memory’s sorrowful throng, Yea, even the thought of unmerited wrong, Within the soul is lull’d to rest, Whilst bosom is to bosom prest. In mute embrace so long, so clear, Two souls unmingled as it were, To dream you’re lov’d is more than sweet, But to feel the heart against heart beat, As billow’y raptures in them roll, Creates a heaven within the soul. And such are the joys that true lovers prove, In the feast of the heart—in the banquet of love. C. HAR HAR
