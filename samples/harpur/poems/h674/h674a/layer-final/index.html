The following is the Squib which was annexed to the above, with this note appended, in reference to that part which reflects upon Mr. Ewing’s countryship. “All scots of real sense and refinement, will perhaps excuse this ‘rub national’, when informed that there exists at present in Sydney, a conspiracy amongst a set of small Scotch wits, the sun and centre of which is or was Mr John Rae, against everything intellectual of such an order as may eclipse their own pygmý efforts.”

What Next!

‘Twas Ewing’s to ask t’other night at the Lecture, What’s Poetry, Ladies and Gents?—Let’s inspect her. Then straightway, by wholesale, from Johnson he picked, Sir, Such words!—each as long as a boa-constrictor. But his meaning hopped off, like an uncaughten ball, And the answer in sense was—Just nothing at all!

Then, Sir, his Classification how level!
