MISANTHROPICAL GOODNESS.

A good man may be sometimes misanthropical: Weighing himself with others, he hath made The shameful difference reflection’s topic all, Till he hath turned him from the world dismayed, And hating the rank vegetation tropical Of vice and folly through all climes displayed: And hence, that all are bad’s his general rule, Himself the exception—a poor scrupulous fool!
