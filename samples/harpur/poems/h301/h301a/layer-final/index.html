Original Poetry.

SONNET.

Most glorious is that firstling burst of light, Down streaming from the opening eye of morn; When like a Fairy Palace stands the thorn, Bough storying over bough, all dew bedight; And sweet the melodies that then are born In the deep copse, or wanton waving boughs, Of pine and myrtle. Dear the gradual close Of shadowy eve, along whose purple sky Elysiums float in dreamy pageantry; Making it bliss to Be. Then sequent night How grateful, with her silent-seeming Queen, Amidst the starry Flocks, far-coming seen. Yet even these molest my ear and sight, If thou, Idalia! sharest not each delight.

STEBII.
