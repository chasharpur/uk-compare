6c

A REPLY TO A CERTAIN ALTERED VERSION OF BYRON’S ODE.

(For such altered version see Empire of 22nd ultimo.)

“Will no one rise?” Wait ye till blood, In strife for land is seen to flow; Then look for tales of “field and flood,” And feasts of vengeance, blow for blow; And fire and flame, ‘till waste and wild, Proclaim the spoiler class despoiled.

“What, silent all?” Ah! ye shall hear, E’en tens of thousands shout “we come; The brave stand still, as dangers near, The deep resolved are proudly dumb; But be ye sure the sense of wrong, Burns now in tens of thousands strong.

A people’s thunder shout I heard, Borne on the midnight wind, And house and station, flock and herd, Left not that host behind; War with the spoiler class had come, “The people were no longer dumb.”

Take the first blow, the first blood yield, Then stand as stand the brave; Victorious long on battle field, Triumphant on the wave. “No Wentworth farce, no Thomson fraud,” “Can ever break your bucklers broad.”

1st January, 1854.
