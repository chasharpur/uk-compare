SONNETS DEDICATED TO AUSTRALIAN SENATORS.

NO. IX.

EXPOSTULATORY.

Most painful ’tis to see a man of merit Mixt up with creatures of such sorry grain, That Satire’s self almost disdains the strain Of which they are the theme! and hence my spirit Feels irked in its just indignation, when I mark thee, Boyd, thus batting the same ball As Wentworth and his scaramouches—all The meanest or most impudent of men! But thou hast a clear spirit, and a mind In its capacities of noble size; A heart, when not misled, that hateth lies, Private and public—brave and frank and kind; And gifted thus in spirit, mind, and heart, Think what thou might’st be, scorning what thou art!

NOT TOM CAMPBELL.
