VIII.

Expostulatory.

“When the wicked man turneth away from his wickedness &c”

(See the Text.)

‘Tis painful to behold a man of merit Mixt up with creatures of such sorry grain That Satire’s self almost disdains the strain Of which they are the Theme;—and hence my spirit Feels irked by its just indignation, when I mark thee, Boyd, thus batting the same ball As Wentworth and his scaramouches—all The meanest or most impudent of men! But thou hast a clear spirit, and a mind In its capacities of noble size; A heart, when not misled, that hateth lies Public and private—brave and frank and kind! But gifted thus in every manly part, Think what thou mightst be, scorning what thou art!
