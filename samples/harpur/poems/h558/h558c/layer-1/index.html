The Spouse of Infinitude.

Behold, the moon is in the midst of heaven, Serenely shining like a giant pearl Amongst the fierier jewelry of Night;— Of Night, the dark spouse of Infinitude! 408 With inconsumable and boundless beauty, And loveliness—the lovelier because Of its unfathomable mystery, Arrayed a[s] with a robe. O who can look Into her dusk but so resplendent face, As thus, unfailingly recurrent, she Comes over, in her fellowship with time, Nor feel that she was nourished into being Upon the bosom of Almighty Power, Illimitably present, and in strict Accordance with Eternal Verity,— And thence with perfect Love—for what indeed Can Truth itself be, but the fine test And crowning sanction of immaculate Love?
