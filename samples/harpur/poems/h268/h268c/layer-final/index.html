Gramachree

She said her heart was only mine, And so to hear her say, Would make mine own bound for joy Ev’n like a fawn at play; But now her scorn that once glad heart Is wounding all the more O Gramachree! O all to me! Core of my bosoms core!

And all the lyric feeling I In passion’s morning dream, Poured from my soul around her like An Eden-circling stream, Returned upon its source, o’erwhelms The life it brought before! O Gramachree! O all to me! Core of my bosoms core!

I know how sweet a spirit, Love With morning smile can start, To make earth’s rudest region seem A heaven for the heart! But why should aught so glad e’re make Life’s after woes the more! O Gramachree! O all to me! Core of my bosoms core!

The 40

The hunted roe, when reached his rock, May bid his anguish cease; The plundered dove can flee away And be again at peace; But I, where’er I fly, from greif Can shelter find no more! Oh Gramachree! O all to me! Core of my bosom’s core!
