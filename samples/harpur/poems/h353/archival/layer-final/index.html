ON THE DEATH OF A YOUNG LADY.

I saw thee late—a peerless maid, Nor dreamt that one so fresh and gay, Was given, alas! so soon to fade, And die away!

Unchronicled, thy moments sweet, Pass’d like a troop of zephyrs by. How sad a destiny to meet; Bloom—charm—then die!

I hear no more thy beauty’s fame, From those who late thy favor sought; Thy beauty!—nay, not e’en thy name; How soon forgot!

Thus I at morn a rose have seen, Admired within the gay parterre, Bur ere the noon-tide, it hath been Forgotten there.

What, all forgotten? I do sigh. Hence finding boasted Love the slave Of Time; and can, convenient, die On beauty’s grave.

Had I once vow’d a lover’s aim, In spirit, now ’twere all my care, To follow thee to heaven, and claim Thy promise, there.

And never more my steps might be, By earthward passions devious driven; But constant kept, by thought of thee, Direct for heaven.

STEBII.
