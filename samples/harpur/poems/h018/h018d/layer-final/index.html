AUSTRALIA, HUZZA!

Australia, how rapt can I stray by thy fountains, Or view thy dark forests wave far on my sight; And I rise in my love as I rise on thy mountains, Where the warrior eagle is proud to alight; And where’s the Australian so dead to all notion Of patriot pride, and of filial devotion, That feels not, thus wandering, a gush of emotion Constrain him to utter—Australia, huzza!

And when on thy headlands I muse, while the legions Of ocean, assaulting, in fragments are torn, In fancy I spread o’er thine evergreen regions The blue summer heavens of ages unborn; And, turning the leaves of thy national story, I see the effulgence of freedom stream o’er thee! I fight ‘neath thy banners the battle of glory! And sing as I wander-Australia, huzza!

My country! thy tall sons shall never betray thee, They’re faithful as friends, they are dreadful as foes. And thy daughters! do they not with graces array thee? The rose is the charm of the bough where it grows! Not a flower in thy garden of beauty is wanting, For all that can render dear woman enchanting— Ay, all that might crown love with joy in the granting, Is rich in thy fair ones-Australia, huzza!
