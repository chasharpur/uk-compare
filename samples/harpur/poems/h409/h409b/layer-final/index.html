PSALM CXIII.

Praise God, ye servants of his will, And magnify his holy name! From everlasting hill to hill Resounded be the same!

Ye nations, learn to praise him well Who robed the rising world with light! From sunrise to the sunset tell The wonders of his might.

Though with eternal power arrayed, Yet He, fulfilling mercy’s plan, Will stoop even to the earth to aid So weak a thing as man!

Or from the dust to lift his head Whose natal lot may lowliest be; And fill with gladness and with bread The child of poverty:

Or to exalt him till he stand O’er conquerors of imperial birth, And rule with a superior hand The mighty of the earth.

He wills it—and the barren womb Conceives and bears the child of grace, To fill the else unfruitful home With utterers of his praise!
