Odes and Lyrics.

Love to the Last.

Wrongly she loveth, doth true-hearted Carry, Loving a vagrant, her kindred aver; But all gone adrift must her faith be, ere Harry— Wild Harry the Rover’s a vagrant to her

Oft doth the wayward one make her feel lonely, Wilding away like a wave of the main; But she knows that he loves her, and loveth her only, And so she forgives him again and again.

Quick come the glad day—O were it to-morrow! When in Love’s haven her wild one shall rest: But living or dying, in joy, or in sorrow, She’ll love him, as now, and in loving do best.

So must it fortune, since tried love that’s blenched not, Meriteth heaven on heaven’s own plan; And so must it fortune, since tried faith that’s wrenched not, Is ever the germ of the Angel in man.
