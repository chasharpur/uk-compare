Impromptu.

Composed within the verge of a beautiful bit of woodland scenery at Granbelang

This lovely bit of Scenery Idealised in picture Might well become, as ‘twould, no doubt, A sweet æsthetic fixture:

With rainbow robe and snowy limb To Fancy’s vision glancing, From yon dim glade, where mythic Nymphs Were in the distance dancing,

To measures of celestial tone (For Picture does not lack sound x Poured forth by festal Gods unseen, All heaven’d in the background.

But here, in the reality, It gives the Muse a sad shock, To know that out beyond us there, We’ve only Bowman’s pad-dock!

His paddock, whose mere image turns To ice all genial dreaming, And from whose frightful prosiness All poetry flies screaming.

X At least it does not lack the suggestive power by which the ideas of sounds is excited in the beholder. One must be very precise and explanatory in these super-critical times—times that are absolute[l]y magoty with dogs to dunces.
