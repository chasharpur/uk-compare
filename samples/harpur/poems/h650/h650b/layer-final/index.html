XIII.

THE TRUTH OF NATURE.

The frame of the world to its Maker is true, And the foul but a means for ensuring the fair; The storms that submerge a whole navy renew, As by torment, the health of the ocean and air. A shower too abundant may give me to me, Yet whole lands are enriched by what’s hurtful to me. Nay even the serpent has something to do In nature that’s needful—whatever it be. And the fly?—let it go! for could’st thou divine The whole scheme of life, its right place is by thine.
