Impromptu

The Empire boasts that England’s ruled By what is now called ‘Journalism’: But may we never here be school’d In our state optics (to be fool’d) By looking through so mere a prism; But measure all things (to be wise) With naked individual eyes.

Note. I very much question whether a numerous (and especially a daily) Newspaper Press, conduces so much to the progress of the National mind as it is generally supposed to be. Talk is good; but the strongest individual intuitions and purposes, may be dwarfed and maimed and wasted by too much of it. In like manner, the mind of a nation may run too much to babble, and be over talked to. A Newspaper—a daily one especially—cannot have, and has not time to grapple effectively with its subjects, and give them the needful roundness & organism. It has time only to skim them, and it does no more—Sometimes, indeed of their cream, but as oftener, and oftener, of nothing but their froth.
