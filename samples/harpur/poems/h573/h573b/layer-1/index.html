A Summer House Treat.

Take a flawless porcelain dish Brightly clean as eye could wish, And place within it full a score Of strawberries, red-ripe to the core, And newly gathered,—where espied In the dewy morning tide, Through the leaves that sheltered them So meetly on their native stem: Then from a shining figured cup Just filled with cream foaming up, Like snow, yet with a buttery tint Gleaming through the bubbles in’t, Pour thou with unstinting hand Of that lymph so richly bland, As much as makes the fruit appear Like islets in a milky mere: And lo, before you there’s a Treat Which even the Gods might deign to eat, And, eating,———own as well They could spare Heaven for a spell, While Earth had fruit of such a kith, And cream as fresh to souse them with.
