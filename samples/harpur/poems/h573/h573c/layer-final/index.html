A SUMMER-HOUSE TREAT.

Take a flawless porcelain dish Brightly clean as eye could wish And place within it full a score Of strawberries, red-ripe to the core, And newly gathered,—where espied In the dewy morning tide, Through the leaves that sheltered them So meetly on their native stem; Then from a shining figured cup Just filled with fresh cream foaming up, Like snow, yet with a buttery tint Gleaming through the bubbles in ‘t, Pour thou with unstinting hand Of that lymph so richly bland As much as, dropt in clinging flakes, Subsiding floods the dish, and makes The nearly smothered fruit appear Like islets in a milky Mere: And lo, before you there’s a Treat Of which a god might fitly eat, And, having eaten, own as well He could spare Heaven for a spell, While Earth had fruit of such a kin, And cream as fresh to souse it in.
