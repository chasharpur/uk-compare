Personal and other Sonnets.

Beauty.

First in the human form and visage fair ‘Tis seen, and in all shapes of animal being, Also in things inanimate, if rare. In blending colors next, and sounds agreeing; Till Mind grows gifted with that inward seeing Which finds it in itself beyond compare, As through its knowledge working everywhere In infinite combinations—fixed or fleeing. The inmost spirit of all Truth, it hues The countenance of Science; throbs like thought In each broad star of heaven, and imbues All the moon looks on;—to the heart is brought Alive in every orient beam, if nought Inclines it such bright influx to refuse.
