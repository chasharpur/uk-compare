IV.

A Social Suggestion for the Future.

O, it is perilous to feel the weight Of unexerted powers: that fruitless state Which bares our branches to the rust of Fate! Besides, in the wide world there is not aught With after-misery so widely fraught. And what observer hence may fail to guess The fountain source of half our drunkenness? But is the remedy—Blood, Sway and Gold, The Social Trinity that served of old? Or that worse madness through which cunning Priests 78 Degrade us into herds of blatant beasts? If so, Man’s history is already told.
