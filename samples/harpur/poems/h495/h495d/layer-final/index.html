Self-Liberty.

I would not be dependent, even for love, On man or woman. Nay, I would—I will Be as the eagles through the heavens that move Boundlessly free, though separate. And as still A torrent, dashing from its native hill, Doth make its own best way; be’t mine to groove My individual world-path, and approve Its lonely fitness with a sovereign thrill! Thus large must be my freedom, for the need Is in my nature and defies dispute, Even as a bent peculiar to its breed Constrains yon tree to bear its proper fruit: And though the pliant deem me a strange brute, I care not, being thus myself indeed!
