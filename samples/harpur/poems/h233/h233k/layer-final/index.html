Odes and Lyrics.

Love and Song.

The dulcimer was sounding from within a mystic grove Where strown with blooms rose gleaming pure a sculptured shrine of Love, Round which tall youths and maidens fair went gliding to and fro, In the flowery dale of Sibma four thousand years ago.

And we, a group glad-dancing on this fair Australian earth, Have met for the same Spirit here to raise our rosy mirth; To know it as those mouldered hearts did its sweet influence know, In the flowery dale of Sibma four thousand years ago.

And in the days far coming upon destiny’s dim wing, Some loving band thus meeting all amid the blooming Spring, May muse of us as we of them whose happy hearts did glow In the flowery dale of Sibma four thousand years ago.

Thus Love, though old in worship, is in presence ever young; The same wherever prevalent—the same wherever sung: As now with us it moved with those who hand in hand did go In the flowery dale of Sibma four thousand years ago.

But thus too, of all passion, what is sung alone abides,— All else elapses, like a dream, on Being’s fatal tides: This Rhyme perhaps is all that now is flavored here below With the love that glowed in Sibma four thousand years ago.
