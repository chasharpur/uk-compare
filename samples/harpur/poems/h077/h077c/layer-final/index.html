Lyrics. By Charles Harpur.

X Cora.

Of Cora, once so dearly ours, Would mournful Memory sing: Of how she came when came the flowers, To leave us with the Spring.

That day (returned) which gave her breath Was that whereon she died, And o’er the pangs of Birth and Death Passed blooming as a bride.

The Spring it came, with never a storm, Nine times so came and went, Till its whole spirit with her form In budding beauty blent.

Yea, till its sentiment was wreathed About her ere it came, And all its foregone influence breathed At mention of her name.

14

And aye her soul—her inward worth Flushed out in subtle glows, As from its heart come mantling forth The ardors of a rose.

2.

Of Cora with the snowy brow Would mournful Memory sing: Of how with Spring she came, and how She left us with the Spring.

Her fairness only might be thought, Or told if hearts could speak, So subtle were the hues that wrought Within her lucid cheek;

And with a many-branching charm About her neck as well, Went like the veins of light that warm A lily of the dell.

But ’twas a glory from within That made her front so fair— 15 A radiant spirit void of sin, As working outward there.

Yea, like a veiling haze o’erflowed With splendors of the moon, Her soul’s lit vesture only showed That soul how bright and boon!

3.

Of Cora with the shining hair Would mournful Memory sing; Who came when flowers were everywhere, And went, too, with the Spring.

Her locks, as they were burnished, shone In many a massy fold, Or fell in their profusion down Like bursting sheaves of gold.

Bright garments of a Spirit bright, That even in the shroud, Were like the sunset’s aureate light Within a lifeless cloud:

16

When she, our Angel of the Sun, Had spread her wings in flight! Alas, that weary day foregone! That weary, weary night!

4.

Of Cora yet, our lost delight, Would mournful Memory sing; Whose eyes at last grew such as might Ne’er gladden out of Spring.

Those full orbed eyes of heaven-deep blue Befringed with glossy jet, So richly gentle—each a dew Illumined violet!

And for their fringes’ ample dower More sweetly rich they rayed, As light within a shady bower Is lovelier for the shade.

Though lovelier were they yet, in sooth, For manifesting still, 17 Deep in their gentle depths, the truth And beauty of her will.

5.

But ah! Thou of the azure eyes And shining golden hair, Mad’st what a void beneath the skies Though flowers were everywhere,

When with the Spring we saw depart Those eyes, those tresses curled; And Summer, dying in Love’s heart, To Winter left the world!

Alas, our best belovëd one! Our heart’s great lost delight! Alas, that weary day foregone! That weary, weary night!

6.

Ah! Soul that wore the snowy brow And gentle shining eyes, Our song hath aye this burthen now Beneath the vernal skies:

18

In vain the dews of heaven are shed Where blight hath been before: So vainly weep we o’er the Dead, Yet only weep the more.

And they are ever most undone Who best concieve delight, For ev’n the Angels of the Sun Leave after them—the night!

Or from the bright time of her birth And death, should Faith construe How like the Spring, though not on earth, Our joy shall bloom anew?
