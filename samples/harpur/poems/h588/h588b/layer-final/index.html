This Southern Land of Ours. X

With clowns to make our laws, and knaves To rule us as of old, In vain our soil is rich, in vain ‘Tis seamed with virgin gold! But the present only yields us nought, The future only lours, Till we have a braver Manhood In this Southern Land of ours.

What would pygmean statesmen but Our new-world prospects blast, 52 By chaining Enterprise and Thought To the misyielding past; With all its misery for the mass, And fraud-upholden powers: But we’ll have a braver system In this Southern Land of ours!

And lo, the unploughed Future, boys! May yet be all our own, If hearts that love their Native Soil Determine, this alone: To sow its years for crops of truth, And border these with flowers, Till we have a nobler Manhood In this Southern Land of ours.
