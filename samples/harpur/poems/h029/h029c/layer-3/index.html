The Bard of Paradise.

I.

Blind Seer with the soul’s all-piercing eyes! Son of Urania! Bard of Paradise! Milton! the triumph of whose epic fame Shall roll in ever-gathering pomp, and rise With distance louder on the world’s acclaim! When those usurping names now fondly dinn’d Abroad by Fashion’s folly-babbling brood, Shall have been swept by Truth’s prevailing wind, Like weeds, to where upon Time’s wasteful shore Oblivion watcheth for his mortal food— Shall have been syllabled by Repute no more For ages. Yea; and then his golden lore By kindred spirits wiselier understood, And meetlier meditated than before, The humbled Epic Muse to glory shall restore.

II.

Monarch of Song supreme! defeat and blame In the comparison shall surely dwell, For him who with a rival’s boast shall claim 4a To search the blissful fields of Heaven as well, And by the ghastly light of penal flame Venture so far into the wastes of Hell.※ For look upon his genius in its free And fearless voyäge through Infinity, Must the prim rhymers of more modern time, As emulous sparrows perched on brambles eye The noble eagle on his path sublime, Through the dim vistas of a clouded sky, Lone sailing,—like that ship wherein did fare Glorious Columbus, when first seen to trace The bays of a new world,—lone sailing where Long cape-like vapors bank the gleaming depths of Space.

III.

‘Taught by the heavenly Muse to venture down The dark descent,’ he saw the World of Dole, With all its rueful gulfs in heaps upthrown, Where the dread surges in their broken roll 5 Wedged up like crags of fire, in prospect shown Through blasting storms that deafened o’er the howl Of universal bale, and thence did wage Blind siege along Hell’s stedfast cope, or bowl In horrible conflict round, with undiminishable rage! See tower-like Demons crowding through the gloom, Clashing, indignant of their terrible doom, Their gleamy shields! that as they serry far Out into the dark, its lurid depths illume With mixing curves and intermittant rings, As lurking lightnings all the black clouds scar With sudden shapes of dragon-semblant things, When first the thunders of a tempest boom! Or where that earliest birth of Ruin’s womb The Pandemonium of their grisly kings, Upblazing, a malignant splendor flings, They mass in legions duskily descried Through that weird light’s inconstant flickerings! Others aloft in furious whirlwinds ride Practising battle; or in banded hordes Under the conduct of their several lords, On bold discovery wander far and wide, And with a pale astonishment admire Those dens of desolation where abide 6 Chimèra monstrous births, or list those dire And ghastly uproars ever heard beside The infernal rivers, whose drear tides reach wild Out through the Alps of Death, dim in the distance piled.

IV.

Next as from forth the Ark afar and fast The dove o’er the unbounded deluge swept, Seeking for Land,—so, o’er the limitless Vast That erst enwombed Creation, outward leapt His daring Muse; borne on the lightning wings Of prophecy, and which as on they past And threw broad splendors o’er all covert things, Revealed its Anarch legioned wide around With haggard Phantoms, such as madness brings Athward some mind that with distempered strength Outgrows the actual world. That Gulf profound Thus did she voyäge,—to return at length With tidings of the secrets drear and dread That in its loud and horrible riots dwell—. Of shoreless seas; of igneous skies that shed A hail of fire with many a writhing swell And inward knashing as aloft they hung; Of bedless bogs; of deserts drifting dead; 7a Of homeless mountains that still onward fled Like clouds, or, meeting in huge justle, rung With grinding thunders till perchance they sped Within some Void,—when, caught as by a spell, Together there a sullen space they swung In silence—hushed at once; then lumbering fell Downward for ever! These she saw outflung In one confusion ‘twixt the bounds of Hell, And where, back shrinking as in grim surprise, The skirts of Chaos reflect Creation’s coasting skies.※

V.

Through brightening spaces now Urania soars, Leaving the regions of phantasmal glooms: Hark! from above a wondrous music pours, 8 And circling over, wave on wave, out roams Far into Chaos; for the living doors Of Heaven, before her, are self-opening, hued With lamping glories of celestial gold, And turned on hinges inwardly endued With a melodious spirit. They unfold, And out on the wild front of Darkness old Flames a vast radiance! while beyond in view The measureless expanses are unrolled Of an immortalising World of light And sempiternal beauty! peopled through With Powers and with Virtues burning bright In the reflected strength of God, and who Legioned together in supernal state And blissful willinghood, day without night The sacred Throne encircling, celebrate In anthems thundrous through august delight, Him, the eternal, omnipresent One Who knowing all things yet is known of none: Him, the sole fountain of all excellent might, All stable good that beeth or can be— Sole God of gods—the whole-including Deity!VI.

VI.

And now from Heaven descended, to the Bower In Paradise we are led,—where Raphael lends At Adam’s board his presence till the hour Of evening warn him thence. Like that of friends Their converse: now we list the Angel tell Of that wild warfare which in Heaven befell, When his proud standard Satan there unfurled, Defying God! and listening, apprehend A rushing turmoil such as might attend The starry frame of all this nether world In ruin—system against system hurled! Then tells he how at God’s command arose, Burning in beauty through the darkness, out Of Chaos and Old Night, whatever glows In all this vast of worlds; and how the shout Of Angel myriads in wonder burst And filled all nature with triumphant throes, When they beheld the new-born glories first! And when the heavenly Historian’s voice Suspends awhile,—look we around, and lo All animals, immortal all, rejoice In pristine innocence: the wolf and roe, The lion and the lamb, the hound and hare, 10 The horse with proud mane peacefully a-flow, The sleek cat purring in the marmot’s lair, The pard, ox, elephant,—one happy crowd! With serpents, beautiful as threads of cloud When they hang burning o’er the horizon’s hoop, At sunset,—all in concord feed or play Under some broad-spread tree whose branches droop Loaded with globes of edible gold alway. And flowers and blossoms of divinest hues, And breathing all as sweetly as if they Were blissfully conscious, in star-clusters spring Beneath or bloom above; and warblers whose Bright plumes are painted by Perfection, sing Amid them, or, like wingëd Joys, effuse Their songs in flight, and, overflying, fling Swift splendors, that like rainbows span the clear Lit spaces ‘twixt the billowy leafiness shed Of neighboring groves; while insect myriads near, Buzzing or fan-borne (volant none the less), And glowing in such glory as to appear Incarnate ardors,—all in concert ring Murmurous peals of dizzy-mixing sound Such as intone the shining hours of Spring, Blithely monotonous; and lapsing round 11 With many an intermissive gleamy glance, Rivers of health amid the fruitful trees Keep cadence with them, as their branches dance To the invisible gladness of a breeze There weaving into one all Eden’s harmonies.

VII.

Now turn we to the Bower again, to hear Adam his happy history relate;— Into the affable Archangel’s ear He pours the blest experience of a state Only inferior to his own, and dear Even as a brother’s welfare to a brother: While busied near them, his so precious mate And dainty ultimate of all delight, The Garden’s gentle Empress—Eve our mother, Clothed only with her grace, in such sweet plight Is moving, and with manners so profuse Of lovely influence and magic might, As doth by royal right divine induce Palpable homage from whatever wings The air, and walks the ground,—and even beguile Tokens of heed from now-insensate things! Yea, quickened with a fresher spirit while 12 She looketh on them, are the flowers and trees! Yea, her all-felt perfection gives the voice Of the regarded waters to rejoice Gladlier, and passionates each passing breeze!

VIII.

Thus in the spirit of prophetic Song With fearless foot Hell’s dread profound along Trod Milton—where the lost for ever weep; And thence upon Uranian pinions strong Shot sheer—out into Chaos, whose hoar deep Its dismal secrets struggling still to keep— Even as torn clouds and surging vapours, all, Crushed up together, round a whirlwind heap— Upturned its embryon worlds against him in dismay! Emerging thence with a rejoicing leap And soaring—up, even to Heaven’s golden gates, He through them saw in the eternal clear Of central peace, all his great Muse relates! But to return within Creation’s sphere Tow’rds one fair Star, that grew, like Vesper, white, Then like the Moon—then vaster, and when near Became the Earth in her first green delight! 13 And thus replenished with creative power, The Paradise of his Vision did he dower With forms of perfect worth, divine content, And joys immaculately innocent.
