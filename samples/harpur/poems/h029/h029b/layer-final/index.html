ORIGINAL POETRY.

MILTON.

(BY AN Australian[ ])

Mightiest of Poets! Bard of Paradise! Milton, thy just and purely moral fame Shall roll in ever-gathering pomp, and rise With distance louder on the world’s acclaim, When many a light, and now usurping name, Sounded than thine more oft by Fashion’s brood, Hath known the silence of oblivion hour, Half the world’s age:—yea, rightly understood, Thy golden numbers and prophetic lore, The Epic Muse, disgraced, to glory would restore.

Who may compete with thee?—defeat and blame, In the comparison, shall ever dwell For him, the rival, who to tread shall aim, As thou, the heights of Heav’n and depths of Hell, Yes; look upon thy lofty track must they, The sickly rhymers of our modern day, As drawn upon the bramble, envying eye The noble eagle in his haughty way Lone-sailing—like a farseen argosy, Dim mid the isly clouds that scar the sunny sky.

‘Taught by the Heavenly Muse to venture down The dark descent, thou sought’st the world of dole; Beheld its vasty gulfs in heaps up thrown Of fiery Alps, in horrid prospect shown, Through blasting storms which there eternal roll. Here, tow’ring Demons scan the Stygian gloom, Clashing their flaming shields indignant of their doom; Where, blazing in a [gap]ay itself affords, Up swells the s[t]ructure vast that shrines their grisly lords: There direr shapes in whirlwind aimless ride, Each crude abortion of Chimera’s womb; Where the infernal rivers broad divide The further Universe as Death and Doom.

Next, as the dove from out the Ark, to seek For land, flew forth, on wings of thunder borne, Thy Muse far darting on the hoary deep Did laden (like the dove) to thee return With prophet-tidings of the secrets dread, Prodigious! which in its dire riots dwell, Of homeless, jarring continents; out-spread Between the flaming bounds of raging Hell, And where—confusion pausing in surprise— Its dunner skirts, at length, reflect Heav’n’s coasting skies.

Anon, imp’d for a bolder flight, she soars, Leaving the Vasts of Dolor and of Glooms. Hark! on the ear a turning cadence pours, Such as the old prophets heard, and, circling, roams Far into Chaos; for the living doors Of Heaven expand on hinge harmonious!—See, Flame out upon the dim immensity A ray as of ten thousand suns; while through The valves, in measureless discov’ry glow The depths of an immortalizing light; Peopled with burning Virtues, hymning loud, In stately troop, or mazy-moving crowd, Him, the Sole Fountain of all Life and Might; Of all that hath been, all that e’er shall be;— Sole God of gods! the unpartaken Deity.

And now from Heav’n descended, to the low’r She snatch’d us, where the Archangel lent At Adam’s board his presence, till the hour Of even warn’d him back to God. Attend, We list their colloquy; and witness fair Flow’rs, dreaming odours and immortal, spring Beneath, or bloom above; whit warblers sing Like sainted souls of love unmix’d, and near Fountains of liquid pearl are murmuring; And she, unfallen Nature’s choicest smile, The garden’s loved and loving Empress, there Is minist’ring with such grace as to beguile Apparent worship e’en from flowers and trees, The tuneful birds and brooks and fresh melodious breeze.

Thus, Milton, thou the deepest depth assay’d, And thence did’st soar above the highest height; And the bless’d Eden of thy song array’d In robes of deathless smiles—in gems.
