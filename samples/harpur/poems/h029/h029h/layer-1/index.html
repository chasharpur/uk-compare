The Bard of Paradise.

Blind Seer with the soul’s all-piercing eyes! Son of Urania! Bard of Paradise! Milton! the triumph of whose epic fame Shall roll in ever gathering pomp, and rise With distance louder on the world’s acclaim! When those usurping names now fondly dinn’d Abroad by Fashion’s folly-babbling brood, Shall have been swept by Truth’s prevailing wind, Like weeds, to where upon Time’s wasteful shore Oblivion watcheth for his mortal food— Shall have been syllabled by Repute no more For many an age: and then his golden lore, Ascendant still, and being as it should By kindred spirits wiselier understood And meetlier meditated than before, The humbled Epic Muse to glory shall restore.

Monarch of Song supreme! defeat and blame In the comparison shall ever dwell, For him who with a rival’s boast shall claim To search the blissful fields of Heaven as well, And by the ghastly light of penal flame Venture so far into the wastes of Hell.x For look upon his genius, in its free And fearless voyäge through Infinity, Must the prim rhymers of more modern time, 117 As emulous sparrows, perched on brambles, eye The noble Eagle on his path sublime, Through the dim vistas of a clouded sky, Lone sailing,—like that ship wherein did fare Glorious Columbus when first seen to trace The bays of a new world,—lone sailing where Long cape-like clouds embank the gleaming depths of Space.

“Taught by the heavenly Muse to venture down The dark descent”, he saw the World of Dole, With all its rueful gulfs in heaps upthrown, Where the dread surges in their broken roll Wedged up, like crags of fire,—in prospect shown Through blasting storms that deafened o’er the howl Of universal bale, and thence did wage Blind siege along Hell’s stedfast cope, or bowl In horrible conflict round, with undiminishable rage! —See tower-like Demons crowding through the gloom, Clashing, indignant of their terrible doom, Their gleamy shields! that as they serry far Out in the dark, its lurid depths illume With mixing curves and intermittent rings, As lurking lightnings all the black clouds scar With sudden shapes of dragon-semblant things, When first the thunders of a tempest boom! Or where that earliest birth of Ruin’s womb, The Pandemonium of their grisly kings, Malignant splendors wide around it flings, They mass in legions, duskily descried Through those weird lights’ inconstant flickerings. Others aloft in furious whirlwinds ride Practising battle; or in banded hordes Under the conduct of their several lords, On bold discovery wander far and wide, 118a And with a pale astonishment admire Those dens of desolation where abide Chimèra’s monstrous births, or list those dire And ghostly uproars ever heard beside The infernal rivers, whose drear tides reach wild Out through the Alps of Death, dim in the distance piled.

nd now from Heaven descended, to the bower In Paradise we are led,—where Raphael lends At Adam’s board his presence till the hour Of evening warn him thence. Like that of friends Their converse: now we list the Angel tell Of that wild warfare which in heaven befel When his proud standard Satan there unfurled, Defying God! and listening, apprehend A rushing turmoil such as might attend The starry frame of all this nether world In ruin—system against system hurled! Then tells he how at God’s command arose, Burning in beauty through the darkness, out Of Chaos and old Night, whatever glows In all this vast of worlds: and how the shout Of angel myriads in wonder burst And filled all nature with triumphant throes, When they beheld the new-born glories first! And when the heavenly historian’s voice Suspends awhile—look we around, and lo, All animals, immortal all, rejoice In pristine innocence: the wolf and roe, The lion and the lamb, the hound and hare, The horse with proud mane peacefully a-flow, The sleek cat purring in the marmot’s lair, The pard, ox, elephant,—one happy crowd! With serpents beautiful as threads of cloud When they hang burning o’er the horizon’s hoop At sunset—all in concord feed or play Under some broad-spread tree whose branches droop 121 Loaded with globes of edible gold alway And flowers and blossoms of divinest hues, And breathing all so sweetly as if they Were blisfully conscious, in star-clusters spring Beneath or bloom above; and warblers whose Bright plumes are painted by Perfection, sing Amid them, or like wingèd Joys effuse Their songs in flight;—and overflying, fling Swift splendors, that like rainbows span the clear Lit spaces twixt the billowy leafiness Of neighboring groves; while insect myriads near, Buzzing, or shard-borne (volant none the less), And glowing in such glory as to appear Incarnate ardors,—all in concert ring Murmurous peals of dizzy-mixing sound, Such as intone the shining hours of Spring In climes where yet some lingering glimps is found Of Earth’s first glow,—and to the heart doth bring A blithe monotony; and lapsing round, Rivers of health amid the fruitful trees With many an intermissive gleamy glance, Rivers of health amid the fruitful trees Keep cadence with them, as their branches dance To the invisible gladness of a breeze There weaving into one all Eden’s harmonies.

And thence upon Uranian pinions strong Shot sheer out into Chaos, whose hoar deep Its dismal secrets struggling still to keep, Opposed him as he sped, and, to appal, Upturned its direst dregs and from their sleep Called spectral worlds,—that gathered to its call Even as torn clouds and surging vapors, all Wildly involved, about a whirlwind heap! —Emerging thence with a rejoicing leap And soaring on, even to Heaven’s golden gates, He through them saw in the eternal clear Of central peace, all his great Muse relates! But to return within Creation’s sphere Tow’rds one fair Star, that grew, like Vesper, white; Then like the Moon—then vaster, and when near Became the Earth in her first green delight! And thus replenished with creative power, 123a The Paradise of his Vision did he dower With forms of perfect worth, divine content, And joys immaculately innocent.
