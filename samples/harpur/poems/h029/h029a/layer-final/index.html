POETRY.

MILTON.

Mightiest of Poets! Bard of Paradise! Milton! thy just, and purely moral fame Shall roll, in ever gathering pomp, and rise With distance, louder on the world’s acclaim, When many a light and now usurping name, Sounded more oft than thine by fashion’s brood, Shall know a long death period: thence no more For ever to be heard! Well understood, Thy varying period and thy kingly lore The English Muse, long drunk, to temperance shall restore.

Who may compete with thee?—Defeat and loss In the comparison shall ever dwell For him the rival, who shall dare to cross In song, the gate of Heaven—the hatch of Hell! Yea, look upon the lofty track; must they, The sickly rhymers of our modern day, As Jackdaws on the willow envying eye The noble Eagle in his haughty way, Dim ’mid the isly clouds of summer evening sky.

Even as the Dove from out the ark, to seek For land, on wings Heaven-strengthened borne, Thy Muse, far darting on the secret deep, Did laden, like the Dove, to thee return With prophet-tidings of the mysteries Prodigious, which in its va[gap]t chambers dwell; The hymns she heard while near Heaven’s coasting skies— The curses, surging from the vaults of Hell.

Yea, thou upon the deepest depth hast trod, Soaring from thence above the highest height; And the blest Eden of thy fancy clad In robes of deathless smiles—in gems of endless light.

C. HARPUR.
