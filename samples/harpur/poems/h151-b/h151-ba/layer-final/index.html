[GENIUS LOST. THE SORROWS PART ONE: THE FESTIVAL]

II. (make this the first)

And this has been a day of Festival To myriads! I alone, it seemed, of all The multitudes who thronged the public ways, Was sad, and utterly companionless, I only, too, of each mirth-noisy group, Did mark not by my air, or gait, or garb, A glad occasion. This methought, at times, Some noticed, with distasteful eye, as though Such apparition struck an instant chill Into their merriment. But what to me Were they? and why should I feel bitter now? Yet when with wandering glances I beheld (Like a Sultana’s zone lustrous with gems) Some public walk all studded with the forms Of rich-robed Women, whose expressive eyes 25 Were bright with joy or big with tenderness, Although not envious of others’ good, I saddened! for not one enchanting eye Was lighted up for me,—not one warm glance Approved me! And the sense of this came cold And venemous to my feelings, like a snake That wantons amid flowers; or as amid The warm hours of the Spring a wintry blast Breathes merciless blight:—so doubly cold to me It came, as unto one who even in My childhood, brooded with a burning cheek, And throbbing heart,—with lips apart, and breath Hushed to such silence as the matron dove Preserves, when warming into life her young, Over the secretly disclosing hope Of finding, in the fulness of my Youth, Some sweet congenial One to love,—to call My Own! my Beautiful! In the warm light Of mutual passion cultivating then Our sympathies so strictly, as to make 26 The thousand appetites, and tones, and all The uttered and unutterable love Of both our hearts, one System!

This were love Indeed! and loving thus,—with an untold And spiritual consent inscrutable, Our beings at once though separate might glow With the same hopes, and chill with the same fears; Enlarge with the same plenitude of joy, And wring with the same pang & wrench of grief, And not a dream pass unimparted! Hence, When bound at length in wedlock’s sacred bands, Each should to the other be throughout this life- Long pilgrimage, a cool Horebian rill Of blessëd water in the world’s wilderness; A palm tree on the sultry glebe of toil; An hospice on the wild and wintry alp Of outcast effort; a perpetual star Shining peculiar and apart, to guide In every enterprise; and on the coast 27 Of Ruin, a sole beacon, sending far Over the dread commotion of the deep Its tranquilising splendor: Finally, If shipwrecked there, even there unchanged, would each Yet be to each, a rainbow in the cloud That wrapped the unborn day, or in the hopes That flickered on Eternity’s dread front A promissory passport.
