TO MYSELF, JUNE 1855.

What is the Eastern War to thee, Its craft and folly, blame and blunder? Its aims are dodges plain to see, Its victories shams with all their thunder.

Heed not its proud but passing things, The royal mischiefs of their day; But give thou Thought’s immortal wings To glories of a purer ray:

To Freedom in her future prime, To Nature’s everlasting lore, To Science from her tower in Time Surveying the Eternal’s shore.

Be such the subjects of thy thought, Not Old World Kings and ruling sets, And liberties that flounder, caught Like fish in diplomatic nets.

For these, if pondered, can but hurt The straightness of thy moral view, And foul as with the Old Word s dirt The virgin nature of the New.

Charles Harpur.
