Coins & Roses

If, as sage Emerson supposes, Coins are as beautiful as roses, In this too must we all agree, That a joint stool is just as meet For picture as a garden seat, By dint of its utility! No doubt the doctrine’s somewhat funny, But what a bloom it gives to money! And how it clothes in Eden beauty Mere benches! doing kitchen duty!
