XI.

[THE FATE OF POETIC GENIUS IN A SORDID COMMUNITY]

30d * Hapless is he who meditates the Nine Where Greed is rampant, aiming there to build New bowers;—there where none deem ‘divine’ His eloquent Art, however he be skilled, For There the Ideal, as by the hectic killed, Fades early,—leaving him alone, to pine 30e O’er youth’s and hope’s and passion’s pale decline, Broken in means, and lastly—broken-willed! To Misery wedded then, as to a wife, 31 And bearing that great load—a noble mind Misvalued,—adown the outer ways of life, What has his genius gained him from his Kind? The worlding’s sneer;—a character maligned;— And thence, alas! with the ignoble,—strife!
