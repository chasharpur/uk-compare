VII

Wintry Results

Hapless is he who meditates the Nine Where Greed sits regnant, aiming there to build High domes of Thought;—there, where none deem “divine” His eloquent Art, however he be skilled; And where the Ideal, as by the hectic killed, Fades early,—leaving him alone to pine Oer youth’s and hope’s and passion’s pale decline, Broken in means, and lastly—broken-willed! To Misery wedded then, as to a wife, 548 And bearing that great load,—a noble mind Misvalued,—down the outer ways of life, What hath his genius gained him from his kind? The worldling’s sneer—a character maligned— And thence, alas! with the ignoble—strife!
