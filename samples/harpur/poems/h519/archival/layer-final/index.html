SONG,

Composed expressly for the benefit of Gentlemen

When woman, fickle minded creature, Seeks t’ensnare with winning smile, Ah! trust her not, but boldly teach her, That hearts can ne’er be won by guile.

Heed not her sighs, too sweet dissembler, Nor when she vows, her vows believe; Resist her tears, the little trembler, Sighs, vows, and weeps but to deceive.

And while you scorn affected passion, Fear not th’inconstant fair one’s frown; Nor love—because it is the fashion, But wisely keep your heart your own.

1836.
