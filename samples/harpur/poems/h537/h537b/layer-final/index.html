II.

A Windy Subject.

“Would not one think now, simply to hear this fellow blow his own trumpet, that there was some honesty in him? But I know him of old; he is like an adder-sucked egg—nothing but shell.”

The Devil astride on a “brickfielder”, drew Suddenly up, just as the dust had beat 182 Into the doors of half Macquarie Street, And turning with a grin to Belial, who Is ‘counted Hell’s best punster, asked—Can you Now tell me what’s more windy than the wind? Belial, who sat a little space behind Whistling, was mute at this; looked posed; then blew His nose, and from the question just was sliding With—Sire, I never joke while out a riding— When, luckily, he marked a furious stir Pass through our ”Patriots”, and with a sneer, Pointing to one of them, replied—’Tis clear The Thing more windy must be a Windy-er.
