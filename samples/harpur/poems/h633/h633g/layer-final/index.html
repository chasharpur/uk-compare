To the Rev: John Saunders: On his being about to return to England.

If a large love of the whole human race, With charity that hopeth a meet cure For life’s worst evils, indicates the grace Of Goodness, thine is such as must endure. And if pure prayers to stablish what is pure, Waste not away in the dim voids of space, But, Godward rising, pierce heaven’s starry face, Thine have been heard, and thy reward is sure. Farewell. This People might be well content To part with much besides, if so it might 116 Keep burning through its moral glooms, unblent With earthlier ardors perilous though bright, Thy steady flame of purpose in the right— Thy eloquent fervor, kindling wise intent.
