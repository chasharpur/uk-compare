51 Elsewhere

XLII.

To the Rev: John Saunders, On his returning to England.

If a large love of the whole human race, And charity that hopeth a meet cure For life’s worst evils, indicate the grace Of goodness, thine is such as will endure. And if pure prayers to stablish what is pure Waste not away in the dim voids of space, But, Godward rising, pierce heaven’s starry face, Thine have been heard, and thy reward is sure. —Farewel. This People might be well content To part with much besides, if so it might Keep burning through its moral gloom, unblent With earthlier ardors perilous though bright, Thy steady flame of purpose in the right— Thy eloquent ferver kindling wise intent.
