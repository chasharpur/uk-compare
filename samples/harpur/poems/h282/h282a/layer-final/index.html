Lyrics. By Charles Harpur.

Mary Arden.

When a simple English Maiden Nested warm in Wilmecote, Singing, like a sky-lark, trailing Heavenward up her morning note, Did no English ears that listened, Even then, fore-touched by Fame, Tremble to the prophet-music Fountaine-headed in that Name? Mary Arden! Tell me Shade of Mary Arden! Shade of Shakspere’s Mother, tell me!

And to thee, thyself, O tell me, Did no dazzling Vision come (Like a sun-burst over Stratford, Burning up its Boreal gloom) Of that bardic grandeurs waiting On thy matron fate, when he Who in time would call thee Mother Should all time’s Subjector be? Mary Arden! Tell me Shade of Mary Arden! 131 Shade of Shakspere’s Mother, tell me!

Then a Mother we behold thee, With that Babe upon thy breast— That great nascent Soul, so bird-like Babbling in its fragrant nest! Oh, what feelings, sweetly human! Oh, what instincts, mildly wise! Sucked he from those mother-fountains— Drew he from those mother-eyes! Mary Arden! Tell me Shade of Mary Arden Shade of Shakspere’s Mother, tell me!

But shall we, now spirit basking In the noon-blaze of his fame, Fail to read a sign prophetic In thy lovely Maiden name? No! it is the star that trembled O’er a royal Poet’s birth! And amongst immortal Maries Second to but one on earth! Mary Arden! Glory be to Mary Arden! Shakspere’s Mother! England’s Mary!
