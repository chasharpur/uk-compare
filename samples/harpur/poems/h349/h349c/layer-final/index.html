ORIGINAL POETRY.

A STRING OF EPIGRAMS. (From Charles Harpur’s Wild Bee of Australia.)

I.

[ON HEARING THAT CERTAIN LOW FELLOWS HAD DEFAMED ME]

I could not be so mere an ass, To heed the censures worldling pass Upon my nature[.] They, poor elves, Are nurtured in the scurvy trick, That prompts on other men to stick The dirt they find upon themselves.
