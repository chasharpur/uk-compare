197

Finis

Sonnet.

Self-vindicatory, and by way of Appendix to the foregoing Series.

Fat Fudge the Publican (and sinner) o’er His counter swears, I’m told, that I, the present Writer of these particularly pleasant Squib Sonnets, for some by-gone private sore Hate patriot Wentworth; and that therefore I, And therefore only, have in verse his public Character trounced so, that ’tis like a cub sick! And more I learn, that some believe his lie! To purge even these then of such “perilous stuff,” As Boys are disabused ‘mongst whom a fuss Is started about bug-bears, ghosts, and other lies, It is, or ought to be, methinks, enough For me to simply state the matter thus: I only hate all Rogues, political or otherwise.
