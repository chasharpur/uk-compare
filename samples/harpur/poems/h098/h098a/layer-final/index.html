Song.

It was (I well remember) The merry Springtime, when Young Dora in the eventide Came singing up the Glen: Come singing up the Glen, Till I felt her tuneful art, Like a subtle stream of vocal fire, Run glowing through my heart.

A fond resolve, long cherished, Till then I might control; Till then—but oh! that witching strain, It drew it from my soul: It drew it from my soul, And she did say me nay, And the world of Love was all the world To us that happy day.

I’m happy now in thinking How happy I was then, When tow’ards the glowing west, my love Went homeward down the Glen: Went homeward down the Glen, While my comfort surer grew, Till methoughts the old-faced hills all looked As they were happy too.
