VI.

TO AN OLD FRIEND.

How are you? It is long—’tis very long Since we have seen each other: yet I trust, That,—all unlike the fellowships which throng In the world’s ways about us, and like dust Powder the winds of every change, the strong Regard which knit our younger souls, no rust Hath gathered. But how seldom cometh now Thy friendly lines, to utter as of old The unadulterated and salubrious vow Of a believing kindness! Well tried gold Is therefore purer, and esteem’s best told When there’s a lour upon dame Fortune’s brow. Then reassure me, lest perforce I say Our Friendship has had (like a dog) its day.
