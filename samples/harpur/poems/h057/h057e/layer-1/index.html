The Bush Fire

[...] On all hands blazing! Though far off the dells Lay like dark gouts amid a general scene Of glimmering heights, which through the red gleams showed Like some imaginary waste of Hell Painted in blood. But nearer, all the veiw Was frightfully brilliant. From vast hollow trunks The wild Fire, with a sudden roar, would rush In quivering columns, chapitated with smoke Half turned into a lurid flame, as out Of funnelled craters; or with some great branch Full flaming, to shattered on the ground Into an upward rushing shower of sparks And upwards dust! Yea, mighty saplings gums &c Would, with a dire crash, from the hill side trees (There massed aloft against the gleamy dark) Falls flaming, like an unsphered astral sign Torn and desfeatured in its ruinous fall, And run together,—a swift-glowing mass With flaming angles and [unclear]luminous limbs, That shattered as it struck the blackened ground Into an upward rushing cloud of sparks And burning dust.
