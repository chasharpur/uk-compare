The Bush Fire

[...] On all hands blazing! Though far off the dells Lay like dark gouts amid the general scene Of glimmering heights, which through the red light showed Like some imaginary waste of Hell Painted in blood. But nearer, all the veiw Was frightfully brilliant. From vast hollow trunks The wild Fire, with a sudden roar, would burst In quivering columns chapitated with smoke Half turned itself into a lurid glow, As out of craters; or some monstrous bough Full flaming, to be shattered on the ground Into an upward rushing cloud of sparks And burning dust! Yea, mighty saplings gums &c Would, with a great crash, from the hill side trees (There massed aloft against the gleamy dark) Swoop flaming, like an unsphered group of stars Torn and desfeatured in their ruinous fall, And run together,—a swift-dropping mass Of luminous points and interblazing limbs, That shattered as it struck the blackened ground Into an upward rushing cloud of sparks And burning dust.
