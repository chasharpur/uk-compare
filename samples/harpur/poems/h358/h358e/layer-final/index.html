XL. X

On the New Land Orders.

I long did hope the soil of this bright Clime, Being unenfeoffed to oligarchal sway, Was a meet cradle for the birth sublime Of just equality at no distant day: And which when once, consùmmated, its ray 269 Had gloried out, should southward draw the prime Regard of th’ world, thence wiser for all time, And studeous more to keep the Godward way Of Perfect Freedom, here first rightly run! But now this Hope is shrunk into a Fear! Old-World feudality its worst hath done To sow the seed of splendid evil here! In Sheepshanks we behold a destined Peer, And Oxtail’s stockmen shall ‘my lord’ his son.

1843.
