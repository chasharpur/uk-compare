The Poet

Blissless am I, except in this That I delight to sing of Bliss; And loveless, save that oft and long I love to sing of Love—a loving Child of Song! Thus past, like roes that on their mountains skip, All my young Years—ay all! have I let trip Ungathering aught that men call gain! Wherefore the kind world thinks—did I myself Myself think sometimes, even while Yea, even while I sing, That I have lived in vain For letting Fortune give me so the slip! Most when some station-gilded thing That, like a gaudy butterfly Was yesterday a grub, comes flaunting, fluttering by.

But if a thought like this By so much stronger than my will Is this strong love of song—that still I am only happy coroling; For then joy-flashing eyes, and love-dewed lips, And things that company best with these, Joyous birds on splendid wing Clear streams, fresh flowers, and shady trees All starrd with golden pips, Drive from my thoughts all thoughts of pelf! Joy-flashing eys, and love-dewed lips! For ever still of these 240a My Fancy, like a honeysucker, sips; Or suns contentedly in those Her little lazy loving self, Even like some fairy Elf Pillowed the while upon a blushing rose! Still thus, with me, the world by goes, Whilst other—duller men enjoy Its real loaves and fishes— Each rosy substance of my rosiest wishes, Even till they cloy.

But if a thought like this Will mar at times my tunful bliss By so much stronger than my will Is still the love of Song—that still I turn me to the Muses’ Spring To steep my heart therein—and sing Of eloquent eyes That are of sunny thoughts the sunny skies; And gracious lips Dashd with such honey as young Passion sips, And what associate best with these, Birds and streams and flowers and trees All starred with golden pips.

But quitting, sometimes, themes like these:— Luminous fancies of luminous eyes When Love Dwells, as from sunset skies, Drea that swarm like hony bees Round hony-dropping lips Happy birds, glad plumed All starrd with golden pips:— 240b I clap to the meanings of my lyre Swift wings of fire! Or more to keep the sensual under Lap them in soul-shaking thunder! While for the noble and the wise, The valiant and the free, I sing of that mileneal sunrise Of hardy human progress yet to be When all true men shall seek, in foul and fair, 241 To breathe with lion lungs the difficult mountain air Of strenuous upclimbing Liberty.

And thus employd, my purpos is most clinging: The more, that, as unto the Present I Am nothing, I would be Yet unlike those who, Mammon’s torch enringing Like moths, live only in its blaze to die;— And Something for my sweet singing Unto the better—wiser times My Country yet shall grow to see: Yea, something for the silver pealing chimes, And for the beauty that fades not by, Of soul-enriching Rhymes! A Star of Poesy in the Future’s sky Throbbing with deathless thought, and to the praise That honeyd lips shall breathe, and eloquent eyes shall raise.

Thus with harmonious aims in lowliest station, And nameless to the passing generation, I labor—labor heartily and long, Even like the bards of old, To fix each rich dream in as rich a song As one shuld set some priceless gem In many-fingerd gold: The gold of a glorious diadem, While on the heds of a mighty line Of Prophet Poets, yet is doomd to sway The heart of a chosen People, grown divine From birth and nurture in the [unclear] holier day Shed over the broad Land from Truth’s desclouded shrine.
