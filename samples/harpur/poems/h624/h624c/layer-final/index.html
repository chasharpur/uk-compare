LIV.

To D. N. Stenhouse.

58

Kendall had written me: Alas! I fear Our dear good Friend is breaking—meaning you; And suddenly mine eyes grew dim and blear, So that I might not read his missive through Until another time.—Ah, misery! who Would bear to breathe in this Samaria here, When, with our Norton, every worthy-won frere He greatly prized were gone—the noble few! ‘Tis much but to have met you—But to say “He is in soul my very friend”, is what I would not be content to have decay Out of the values of my bardic lot, For aught besides my spirit hath begot Of kindred comfort, through my life’s whole way.

1863
