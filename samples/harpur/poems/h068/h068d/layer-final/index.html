A COAST VIEW.

High ’mid the shelves of a grey cliff, that yet Riseth in Babylonian mass above, In a benched cleft, as in the mouldered chair Of grey-beard Time himself, I sit alone, And gaze with a keen wondering happiness Out o’er the sea. Unto the circling bend That verges Heaven, a vast luminous plain It stretches, changeful as a lover’s dream— Into great spaces mapped by light and shade In constant interchange—either ‘neath clouds The billows darken, or they shimmer bright In sunny scopes of measureless expanse. ‘Tis Ocean dreamless of a stormy hour, Calm, or but gently heaving;—yet, O God! What a blind fate-like mightiness lies coiled In slumber, under that wide-shining face! While o’er the watery gleam—there where its edge Banks the dim vacancy, the topmost sails Of some tall ship, whose hull is yet unseen, Hang as if clinging to a cloud that still 204 Comes rising with them from the void beyond, Like to a heavenly net, drawn from the deep And carried upward by ethereal hands.
