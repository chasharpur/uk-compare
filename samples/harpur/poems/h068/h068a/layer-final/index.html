COAST SCENERY.

High ’mid the shelves of a grey cliff; that yet Hangeth in bluffs enormously above, In a benched cleft, as in the mouldered.chair Of greybeard Time himself, I sit alone, To gaze with a keen wondering happiness Over the sea. Immensely rolling out, See how it stretches to the circling bend That verges Heaven—a vast luminous plain Of waters, changeful as a lover’s dream! Into great spaces mapped by light and shade In constant interchange, or under clouds The billows darken, or they simmer bright In sunny scopes of measureless expanse! ‘Tis Ocean dreamless of a stormy hour, Calm, or but gently heaving;—yet, O God! What a blind fate-like mightiness lies coiled In slumber, under that wide-shining face. While o’er the watery gleam, the topmost sail Of some tall ship, whose hull is yet unseen, Hangs, as if clinging to a cloud, that still Bellies above it like a heavenly net, Drawn slowly upward by ethereal hands.

And if I look aloft, how deep the sky That arches Ocean!—deeper, richer far Than elsewhere. See, how delicately rare Are those sky colours that keep flickering up From the remote horizon! Beautiful Those lucid traceries of woven cloud That float about the sun; and lustrous too Are the white masses overhead, that move And gather inland tow’rds the verdant hills.

Withdrawing now the eye from heaven, behold The coast, how wonderful! Proportions strange, And unimaginable forms, more quaint, More wild and wayward, than were ever dreamt By a mad architect, keep crowding out As the eye runs along it. Columns such As old Egyptian trowels might have reared, Support fantastic arches. Rugged domes, And minarets in ruin, freak the sides Of chasms spanned by crude and haggard bridges. Or overhanging cliffs like this, whose shelf Sustains me, rest on buttresses of more Than Babylonian vastness:—while below, Against their bases everlastingly, Beats the white wrath of the relentless surge.

Yet even amid those rugged forms, the warm And gentle ministry of Spring hath wrought Its work of love. Most sparingly indeed, But thence most gratefully, a nameless shrub With a bright blossom, tufts some hollowed ledge That holds the scanty soil; and rarer still, Green runners, from some sheltering crevice, throw Their tendrils o’er the shelves, and trailing thence, Touch the stern faces of the rocks with beauty.

Nor wants the scene its meet inhabitants. Below the porpoise breaches, and the crab Waits for his prey among the wave-washed stones That glitter to the sun; or more remote, Amid the watery spaces may be seen Some solitary diver’s shining back. Sea gulls come clanging past, and overhead Sits the white-breasted hawk, with many a sleek And silver pinioned wanderer of the deep, Who hoard their mottled eggs, and rear their young Amid the jags and fissures of the cliffs.

How nourishing is Nature to the soul That loves her well! not only as she works In instant contact with its quickened powers, But as she tempers all its after-moods Through oldest memories and remotest tokens. And hence, when thus beloved, not only here By the great sea, or amid forests wild, Or pastures luminous with lakes, is she A genial ministress;—but everywhere! Whatever testifies of her is good, However common; fresh, however known. Dead city walls may pen us in, but still Her influence seeks, to find us,—even there, Through many a simple means. A vagrant mass Of sunshine falling into some void place, Shall warm me to the heart, and trade awhile With happiest instincts. Even a bird, though caged, Shall represent past freedom, and its notes Be spirited with memories that call The wild woods round. Nay, even a scanty vine Trailing along some backyard wall, shall speak Love’s first green language; and (so cheap is truth) A vessel of clear water from the well Be in its homely brightness beautiful!
