SONNETS. By Charles Harpur.

I.

WICKLIFFE.

One star in heaven, when the dank mists of earth Have hid all others from the asking eye, As they had forfeited their golden birth In the bright bowers of eternity, Doth keep our else-blanked vision lifted high;— A fountain in some desert’s mighty dearth Set newly flowing, hath a sumless worth To all who else had journeyed thirsting by: So, through the spiritual glooms that crept Round England’s altars, Wickliffe’s spirit shone; So faith’s pent wave, by him unscaled, leap’d Livingly forth; and, thereby overflown, Truths that were erewhile barren as a stone, Bloomed into beauty, and were fruitful kept.
