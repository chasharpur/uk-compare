XLI.

Wicliffe. X

One star in heaven, when the dank mists of earth Have hid all others from the asking eye, As they had forfeited their golden birth In the bright bowèrs of eternity, Doth keep our else-blanked vision lifted high;— A fountain in some desert’s mighty dearth Set newly flowing, is of sumless worth To all who else had journeyed thirsting by: So through the spiritual glooms that crept Round England’s altars, Wicliffe’s spirit shone; So faith’s pent wave, by him unsealëd, leapt Livingly forth; and thereby overflown, 270 Truths that were erewhile barren as a stone Bloomed into beauty and were fruitful kept.
