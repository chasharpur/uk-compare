XXVI.

Wicliffe.

One star in heaven, when the dark mists of earth Have hid all others from the asking eye, As they had forfeited their golden birth In the serene of the eternal sky, Doth keep our else blanked vision lifted high;— A fountain in some desert’s mighty dearth Set newly flowing, is of sumless worth To all who else had journeyed thirsting by: So through the ghostly glooms that idly slept Round England’s altars, Wicliff’s spirit shone; So Faith’s pent waves, by him unsealed, out leapt In living vigor and therby o’erflown, How Much that was erst was barren as a stone Bloomed into beauty and was fruitful kept.
