Wordsworth’s Poetry.

How much, O Wordsworth! in this world how much Has thy surpassing Book made rich for me, Of what was once unprized! A nameless tree— A wilding flower—the merest hues that touch The common clouds—lit summits—verdant leas— With every woodland murmur, every song Of bird or brook, as heard to gush along In each fresh on-’scape of some bough-caught breeze, Have thence a charm to thrill my bosom’s core, And gift me with a vast capacity For love and joys: such as were mine before Not even in boyhood, and that comes thus free From things on all hands now, because they store Some inspiration of thy Poetry.
