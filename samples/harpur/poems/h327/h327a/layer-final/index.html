Nature’s apparent Want of Benevolence

The man by indigestion smitten Finds poison even in his gruel; The man compelled to drown a kitten Must feel that Natures sometimes cruel.

Yet, in both cases, could we see Her final reason, we should find (Of this I feel assured) that she Is cruel only to be kind.

Unforced sheer abstinence to try The constipated man might die; Despite ing call The kitten might be spared—for what? To find in some too cat-full Cot A cuff’d, scratch’d, miserable lot, And famish after all
