To Mary.

Where Beauty is smiling with love undenied, And gladness is flowing from Pleasure’s hill side— Whatever of charming I elsewhere may see, I can turn from it Mary, to think upon thee.

When blasts of affliction blow cold on my rest, And the pang that will sleep not is loud in my breast; Still, however clinging these troubles may be, I can turn from them Mary, to think upon thee.

When Weariness sleepeth, and Care is at rest; When Happiness nestles with all it loves best: Then as the Moon gazes entranced on the sea, My Soul, o’er thy dwelling, dreams down upon thee.
