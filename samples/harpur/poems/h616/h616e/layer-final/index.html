Mary. X

Where Beauty is smiling With love undenied, And gladness is flowing From Pleasure’s hill side— Whatever of charming, Thus absent, I see, I can turn from it, Mary, To think upon thee.

When blasts of affliction Blow cold on my rest, 22 And the pang that will sleep not Is loud in my breast; Still, however clinging These troubles may be, I can turn from them, Mary, To think upon thee.

When Weariness sleepeth, And Care is at rest; When Happiness dreameth Of all it loves best; Then as the Moon gazes Entranced on the sea, My soul, o’er thy dwelling, Dreams down upon thee.
