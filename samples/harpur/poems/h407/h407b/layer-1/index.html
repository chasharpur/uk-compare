VERSIONS OF SOME OF THE PSALMS. BY CHARLES HARPUR.

PSALM XXIII.

My Shepherd is the Lord most high, Who giveth from my fold to fly The wolf of want and fox of shame, With aught that would assail the same: Who makes me, with unspotted fleece, Lie down in the green vales of Peace, Or leads me in the noontide near Palm-shaded waters, still and clear.

And if I faint, His care my soul Restores, and makes my being whole; Or feeds for my health’s redress Upon the hills of righteousness. Hence though I journey underneath The black impending crags of Death, No shape of evil will I fear While he, my Shepherd-God, is near.

For still in the Foe’s face for me He spreads a table plenteously, And makes my cup of joy to swell With boundy, like a flooding well! And thus his goodness and his grace Shall be my guards in every place; And thus my soul have its abode, For ever, in the power of God!
