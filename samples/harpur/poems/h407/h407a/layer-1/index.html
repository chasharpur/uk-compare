Paraphrase of the XXIII Psalm.

My Shepherd is the Lord of Heaven, Who from about my fold hath driven The wolf of want and fox of shame, With aught that might assail the same: Who makes me, with unspotted fleece, Lie down in the green vales of Peace, Or leads me, in the noontide, near Palm-shaded waters, still and clear.

And if I faint, his care my soul Restores, and makes my being whole; Or feeds me, for my health’s redress, Upon the hills of righteousness. Hence though I journey underneath The black impending crags of Death, No shape of evil will I fear, While He, my Shepherd-God, is near.

For still, in the Foe’s face, for me He spreads a table plenteously; 10 And makes my cup of joy to swell In bounty, like a reaming swell And thus his goodness, mercy, grace Shall be my guards in every place; And thus my sould have its abode Forever in the power of God.
