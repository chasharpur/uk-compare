Love in Vision.

Three Visions of Love I have wrought into numbers That lit with thy beauty shall evermore be, While they show that no dream could be born of my slumbers And reign for a moment unblended with thee.

Methought as I took in this Garden my pleasure, Broad roses of gold flourished forth on yon tree; But when I had hastened and siezed of the treasure— Behold they were splendid madallions of thee!

Next methought as alone ‘neath the moon I was roving, Through gossamer clouds she came stooping to me, And her orb, as she neared, looked more lovely and loving— When lo, ’twas a silver-set picture of thee!

Then it seemed the whole heaven was opened above me To music that flowed, wave on wave, like a sea; Disclosing the Angels in thousands—all lovely— All glory-wing’d sisters—God’s pictures of thee!
