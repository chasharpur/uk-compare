X TO A CHILD SLEEPING.

BY CHARLES HARPUR.

Do you dream of the future, my Boy? That future is dreaming of you! Summery seasons so heart-full of joy, Wintery periods too: All thronged with shadowy hopes and fears, All streaked with smiles and gleamed with tears, The sweet and bitter of coming years: Dream you of these? These of you In the spirit of fortune are all dreaming too.

Or comes to your slumber some thought Of your mystical life in the Past, 76 Like a far-wasting melody caught Out of a pause in Time’s blast 77b A thought of loving ones afar, Who blest you in some other star That rolleth through heaven its golden car? Love them again! Just as fast As Time flyeth, the present becometh the past.

For the Present, the Past, the To-come, Are nothing, sweet dreamer, to thee, But Time’s three-in-one, since Being home— Soul that for ever hath been! Whence thou,—I,—all,—our stand have each Where but in seeming ever reach The wild surge breaking on Death s dark beach! Dream we or die—are not we Even now and for evermore with them all three!
