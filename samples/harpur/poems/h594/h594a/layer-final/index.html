POEMS BY CHARLES HARPUR.

I.

TO A CHILD SLEEPING.

Do you dream of the future, my boy? That future is dreaming of you! Summery seasons so heart-full of joy— Wintery periods too; All thronged with shadowy hopes and fears, All streaked with smiles and gleamed with tears, The sweet and bitter of coming years— Dream you of these? they are all dreaming too, In the spirit of destiny dreaming of you.

Or comes to your slumber some thought Of your mystical life in the past? Like a musical utterance caught Out of Time’s on-going blast:— A thought of loving souls afar Who blest you in some other star, That rolleth through heaven its golden car? Love them again! while the present, as fast As the moments can hurry, becometh the past.

For the present, the past, the to-come, What are they, sweet dreamer, to thee, But Time’s three-in-one, since soul is their home— Soul that for ever must be? Whence we, my sleeping boy, have each Our part in them beyond the reach Of the wild surge breaking on Death’s dark beach! Sleep we or die—having been, is to be Even now and for evermore with them all three.
