TO A CHILD SLEEPING.

BY CHARLES HARPUR.

Do you dream of the Future, my boy? That Future is dreaming of you! Summery seasons so heart-full of joy, Wintery periods too: All thronged with shadowy hopes and fears, All streaked with smiles and gleamed with tears, The sweet and bitter of coming years: Dream you of these? They are all dreaming too,— In the spirit of destiny dreaming of you[.]

Or comes to your slumber some thought Of your mystical life in the Past, Like a musical utterance caught Out of Time’s on going blast,— A thought of loving ones afar, Who blest you in some other star That rolleth through heaven its shin- ing car? Love them again! while the Present as fast As the moments can hurry becometh the Past.

For the Present, the Past, the To-come, Are nothing, sweet dreamer, I ween, But Time’s three-in-one, since soul is their home— Soul that for ever hath been! Whence thou,—I,—all,—out stand have each Where but in seeming ever reach The wild surge breaking on Death’s dark beach! Sleep we or die—being once, is to be Even now and for evermore with them all three!
