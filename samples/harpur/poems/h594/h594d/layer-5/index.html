To a Child Sleeping. ᴓ

Do you dream of the future, my Boy? That future is dreaming of you! Summery seasons so heart-full of joy, Drear Wintery periods too: All thronged with shadowy hopes and fears, All streaked with smiles and gleamed with tears, The sweet and bitter of coming years: Dream you of these? These of you In the spirit of fortune are all dreaming too.

Or comes to your slumber some thought Of your mystical life in the Past, Like a far-reaching melody caught As Out of a pause in Time’s blast— A thought of loving ones afar Who blest you in some other Star, That rolleth through heaven its golden car? Love them again! Just as fast As Time flyeth, the present becometh the past.

Nay, the Present, the Past, the To-come, Are nothing, sweet dreamer, I see, But Time’s three-in-one, since Being’s their home— That Being which ever must be! Whence thou,—I,—all,—our stand have each Where never but in seeming reach The wild waves breaking on Death’s dark beach! Dreaming or dead—with all three, Through the fact our having been, ever are we!
