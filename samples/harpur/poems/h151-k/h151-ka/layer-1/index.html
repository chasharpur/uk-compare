Chorus of Spirits of the Earth.

O why is not this beauteous Earth The Eden men imagine? the fair seat Of fruitful Peace, pure Love, and sunny Mirth? And why are its prime Children, so complete In their conceptions of a Godlike state, The victims ever of fraternal hate, 22 Oppressing or oppressed? That so the portion is of all, deciet, And fear, and anger, sorrow and unrest.

There is not one enduring thing In Nature that appears, From shining star to river murmuring, That hath not glimmered through the mist of tears, Hot from some human brain That maddened with unpitied pain! There is not one green mound existent long, Nor ancient wayside stone, But upon these some child of bitter wrong Hath sate him down alone To bite his pallid lip, and groan.

And this has been the state of Man Since first the race began! And thus his piety is driven away From Earth, its proper home, 23 To seek vague Heavens above the source of day; Or beyond the gorgeous gloom Wherewith mild Evening curtains up the west, There, like the Psalmist’s dove, to rest In Gardens of unfading bloom, And Islands of the Blest.

Not but there is a place of bliss Beyond Mortality for the virtuous mind; But that State should not lure the soul’s desire from this Since one might be as perfect in its kind, Even as the other is. And yet awhile—a weary while! And some immortal Intellect shall plan A social scheme for Man, Whereby the face of Life shall smile For all throughout their days—days of delightful toil. Running like brooks through green and flowery ways, Leaving theGiving 24 Giving them then contentedly to die, Full to Enjoyment’s best capacity.
