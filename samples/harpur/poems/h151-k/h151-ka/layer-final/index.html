Chorus of Spirits of the Earth.

O why is not this beauteous Earth The Eden men imagine? the fair seat Of fruitful Peace, pure Love, and sunny Mirth? And why are its prime Children, so complete In their conceptions of a Godlike state, The victims ever of fraternal hate, 22 Oppressing or oppressed? That so the portion is of all, deciet, And fear, and anger, sorrow and unrest.

There’s not one bright enduring thing In this great round of Nature that appears, From shining star to river murmuring, That hath not glimmered through the mist of tears, Hot from some human brain That maddened with unpitied pain! There is not one green mound existent long, In any region Nor old wayside stone, On which some weary child of shame and wrong Hath sate not there alone To bite his pallid lip, and hear the unheeded groan.

And such has been the state of Man Since first his recreancy began! And thus his Piety is scared away From Earth, its proper home, 23 To seek vague Heavens above the source of day; Or out beyond the gorgeous gloom Wherewith mild Evening curtains up the west;— There flying, like the Psalmist’s dove, to rest In innocent Gardens of unfading bloom, And Islands of the Blest.

Not but there is a place of bliss Beyond Mortality for the virtuous mind; But that State should not lure from this The soul’s desire (to high[es]t good inclined) Since one might be as perfect in its kind, Even as the other is. And yet awhile—a weary weary while! And some immortal Intellect shall plan A social scheme for Man, Whereby the face of Life shall smile For all throughout their lengthened days— Days running like glad brooks through green and flowery ways, 24 And leaving them at last content to die, Full to Enjoyment’s best capacity.
