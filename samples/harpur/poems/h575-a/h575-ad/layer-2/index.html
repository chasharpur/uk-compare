A Supernaturalised Battle-Piece from the XVIII Iliad.

I.

As Paraphrased in dactylic hexameters.

Up in the might of his soul, rose Achilles, the favored of heaven, Pallas then slung the broad shield of Jove o’er his shoulders; And she put round about his bold head a golden mist, lightning with splendor— Like a cloud of the morning inflamed with the mountain-top glory of sunrise. As when a great smoke goeth up from a town in some outlying island, Whose foes, from their own stronger hold pouring out, all the day through assault it, So soon as the set sun hath drawn down with him the grey strip of twilight, How thickly the nimble-tongues fires are seen to rush up in the darkness, Making a glow in the sky, and pathing with light the dim waters, Where haply some help-bringing Chief comes ploughing the deep with his galleys— So up from the head of Achilles went glowing aloft an effulgence.

On the trench-mound stood he forth, beyond the camp-wall, though still keeping Aloof from the host of the Greeks, being held by the hest of his mother; And there, standing straight up like a tower, in his terrible ardour he shouted! And as his shout, rang in wrath along the gored fronts of the battle, Pallas enforced with a cry, that fell as if shrieked out of heaven! And, as that clear-ringing voice tore out like the blare of a trumpet, Threatening the gates of a town which implacable foemen beleaguer, loud in the host of the Trojans rose a wild thunder-like tumult! When too her cry in their midst, fell shrieking,—fell with it confusion, Fell with it fear, and horror that suddenly shuddered within them, Rending their legions, as clouds are torn by the winds of a tempest! Even the proud maned steeds,—swerved, huddled, and backed on their haunches, 90 full well foreseeing, (it seemed,) calamity clothed in the omen! While all the charioteers were smitten with panic, beholding That fire on the terrible head of that God-like great-minded One burning,— Flame as divine—raying forth with an active and ominous glory!

Thrice, o’er the trench, did he shout, and as oft, with their helps, were the Trojans Whirled into dust-clouded heaps, and rolled back in utter disorder: Till of their valliantest chiefs twelve in the huddlement perished, Crushed by the jambing of wheels and under the wrecks of their chariots.
