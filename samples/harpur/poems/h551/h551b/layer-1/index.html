XVI.

A Batch.

“Cressida. Here comes more. Pandarus. Asses, fools, dolts! chaff and bran, chaff and bran! porridge after meat!”

Shakespear.

Macarthur!—(whereto must descend my Theme?) D’Arcy and Lawson!—(which the greater spooney?) Wild, Lord, et cetera! each and all as moony In mind and spirit as a drunkard’s dream! What, into the unlucky hands of such, My Country, should thy destinies be given Aught longer? Crudities that satire even Bemuds her fingers having thus to touch! And now, though at some trouble here to rake them Into one Batch—again I let them drift Notelessly off upon the wind that’s whift From o’erblown Folly. Let grim Wentworth make them Just what he can, since only at a gift, The Devil himself, methinks, will care to take them.
