Odes.

Life and Death.

A little light, heat, motion, breath— Then silence, darkness, and decay, Are all that difference life and death In him that weareth clay: But Time’s one drop ’twixt that and this, Ah! what a gulf of doom it is!

The cheek is fair, the eye is bold, The ripe lip like a berry red: Then the shroud clothes them;—thus behold The Living and the Dead! And how Time’s last cold drop serene Swells to eternity between!

Yet not for horror, nor to weep: But through the solemn dark to see That Life, though swift, is wonder-deep, And Death the only key Which lets to that mysterious height Where Earth and Heaven in God unite.
