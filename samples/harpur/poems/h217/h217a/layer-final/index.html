LIFE AND DEATH.

By Charles Harpur.

A little light, heat, motion, breath— Then silence, darkness, and decay, Are all that difference Life and Death In him who weareth clay: But Time’s one drop ’twixt that and this, Ah! what a gulph of doom it is!

The cheek is fair, the eye is bold, The ripe lip like a berry red: Then the shroud clothes them:—thus behold The Living and the Dead! And how Time’s last cold drop serene Swells to Eternity between!
