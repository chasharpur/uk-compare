54

To. Twank.

Ah! Daniel mine, some Muse malign Hath skimm’d thy judgments cream away But take a slice of “good advice”— Even that I proffer thee today. 55 Again read Shakespear by the hour,— Read Milton more—McDonald less— And Wordsworth for his simple power, Not for his namby-pamby-ness.

And know,—’twere better to esteem What’s best in Byron’s godless “Don” Than with crude Browning much to dream, Or wire-draw through with Tennyson.

And better at the “woes of Moore” To shed the artifical tear, Than doat with eunuch passion o’er The feeble beauties of De Vere.
