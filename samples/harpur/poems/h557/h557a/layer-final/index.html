POETRY.

THE SPIRIT OF THE BOWL.

A Teetotal Lay.

(BY CHARLES HARPUR.)

A strong man and a boy, With a damsel young and fair. Went forth in eager quest of joy, Defying sober care; So a draught to each was brought By one who stoutly swore, On earth was nothing to be bought Might glad their spirits more.

The strong man drank it up, And I beheld at length, A poison lurking in the cup Had burned away his strength: Hark! fearfully he moans, Because of horrid pain— Disease is gnawing at his bones, And madness at his brain.

The boy then tasted too, And in a little space, I saw that health’s celestial hue, Had withered from his face: Hark! reeling to and fro, Thus doth the urchin rave— I would my father’s head were low, My mother in the grave!

The damsel coyly eyed The bowl, but sipped at last; And soon the robe of decent pride From her fair neck was cast: Hark! ruined in an hour, She boasts with gesture wild, That Sin shall be her paramour, And Infamy her child.

Oh, if unto the soul And to the body such Be found the spirit of the bowl, Should any taste or touch? Dash—dash it to the earth! It sparkles to destroy! Let’s have no riot in our mirth, No ruin in our joy.
