Chorus of the Hours.

Ah, that swoll’n luxury with its masked uncleanness And arrogant lack of human brotherhood! Ah, that gaunt poverty with its dog-like meanness And fox-like cunning, or yet direr brood Of wolfish tempers! should for ever so Deform the whole earth and on all hands grow And widen into such a monstrous woe, As gives, even in the hopeful, all belief In Man’s original aptitude For rightious wisdom and fraternal good, To perish through excess of honest grief!

He is Truth’s enemy who but endures! Children of light and prophecy, the deeds That make the world (with all its jarring creeds) Alone Endurable to God, are yours! 310 And are not all the rest of men, a heap Of sensual wallowers, in their various styes Bentishly wakeful, wrapped in fraud and lies Or sottishly asleep? Powerful to suffer, are ye weak to do? No: and then wherefore are ye slow to be Supremely, nobly, wisely, boldly free From such vile burthens as now most ye rue? To be what through all time ye might have been, Had ye been only true To one another and the light within.

Nor were there aught to fear From a supremacy, though world-wide, of mind, When highest in its kind: For Genius, if its daily path be free From that low need and vulgar misery Which irk it most, and make it wild and vain, Is always sane, Is always wise in its simplicity, Always fraternal,—and should therefore reign.

Semi Chorus.

Crowned heads and mitred dignities— The courtly slave and ministerial lord, And Governments composed of these And them who serve them with the sword, Backed by the unproducing moneyed great— What rational hope—what pledge can such afford Of better human destinies? For such, skilled only in state schemes of state, And sleights of war and trade, though therein sage, Lag aye behind the virtue of their age Where pomp and spoil and interest allures!

Chorus—

Then fail ye not to heed, Children of light and genius, that the deed Which gives the harvest unto Truth, is yours! Hold ye to her eternal creed; 283 And with a spirit that not alone endures The evil by a foregone lapse upthrown, But shapes as well the destiny that cures Earth’s ailment, and her day of health ensures, Arise at once from zone to zone And claim all sovereignty for Mind alone! Arise to the great hour of moral need; In with the plough, and sow the better seed— And reap your own!
