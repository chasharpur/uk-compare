Chorus.

Ah, that swoll’n Luxury with its masked uncleanness, And arrogant lack of human brotherhood! Ah, that gaunt Poverty with its dog-like meanness And fox-like cunning—or yet direr brood Of wolfish greed!—alas that these should so Cumber the whole Earth, and on all hands grow And widen into such a world-like woe, As even in the best gives all belief In human aptitude For righteous wisdom and fraternal good, To perish through excess of honest grief!

He is Truth’s enemy who but endures! Children of Light and Intellect, the deeds Which make the Earth (with all its jarring creeds) Endurable to God, are yours! 90 Powerful to suffer, are ye weak to do? No: and then wherefore are ye slow to be Supremely—nobly—wisely boldly free From such vile burthens as now most ye rue? To be what through all time ye might have been, Had ye been only true To one another and the light within!

X

Crown’d Heads and mitered Dignities, The courtly Slave and ministerial Lord, And Earth’s mere Governments composed of these, And they who serve them with the sword, Backed by her Merchants and her worldly Great— What rational hope can such afford Of better human destinies? For such, skill’d only in state schemes of state And sleights of war and trade, though therein sage, Lag aye behind the virtue of their age 91 Where pride and spoil and interest allures! Then, fail ye not to heed, Children of Light and Genius, that the deed Which gives the harvest unto Truth, is yours! Hold ye to her eternal creed, And with a spirit that not alone endures The evil by a foregone fate upthrown, But shapes as well the destiny that cures Earth’s ailment, and her day of health ensures, Arise at once from zone to zone, Claiming all sovereignty for Mind alone! Arise to the great hour of moral need; and sow the better seed,— In with the plough And reap your own!
