A Vision of an Angel. X

I had a vision of an Angel, who Within the glimmering circle of my half Awakened consciousness, arising, stood In Where its dim verge just met the dark of Sleep; Even as the Moon, on the horizon, stands Within the dream-gaze of the slumbering Earth— Stood shadowing his snowy beauty so As but the more confirmed its glorious grace 245a Of lineament, and utter loveliness, supreme, 245b With ample-folding wings, whose p[a]radise-plumes, Glowed with such hues, as, still intenseleer, mix In the bright borders, of an evening cloud that hang, 245c So stood he, a resplendent Apparition! And could I now revoice to human ears The wondrous things he spake,—spake with a tongue That seemed itself all spirit, and from which His words came raying, each word like a star, Though fusing all into one luminous wave That flowed with such a golden cadence forth Such golden miracles of melody melodious sound As Beings attune whose breath of life is light, Not air impure, the vital draught of mortals! Could I revoice those oracles—O then Should all who heard, in utterance jubilant, Conjoin a symphony in words like these: “Nations no more shall trade in war; no more Shall banish charity from the homes of earth: But all mankind, together knit in love, Shall henceforth move in concord with all truth, All goodness; while, as from a well, they drink Eternal strength and arch-angelic beauty From one sole heavenly all-redeeming Faith!”
