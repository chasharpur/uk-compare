Part IV.

“Awaking as from sleep, I bounded up, Strung with a feeling of enormous strength, Though yet half wild with horror. Onward then Ramping I went,—out through the palace gates— Down through the streets, and into the highways, Astonished at my own miraculous speed! And now afar, in long-drawn line appeared A Caravan upon its outward way Over the desert of Pentapolis. And strange the instinct seemed that urged me then To rush amongst them—and devour: for I Was fierce with hunger and inflamed with thirst.

“Still bounding on, I suddenly sprang amidst A laggard company that yet beside A clear spring rested, as for the last time;— One of those clear springs that, like giant pearls Inlay the burning borders of the grey Enormous Desert. All at once they rose At sight of me, and made a startling outcry! Some fled—some threw themselves amongst the brakes— Some siezed their swords and lances, and so stood As if on their defence; all which to see Filled me at once with unaccountable rage, And savage joy! The sternness of their looks— The very gleam of their protending spears 203 Seemed to insult me, and I rushed amongst them! But, as I did so, sudden spasms of pain Searched deep my side—a lance was quivering in it! I roared a roar that startled even myself, So loud and hoarse and terrible was its tone! Then bounding, irresistible it seemed As some huge fragment from a crag dislodged, Against the puny wretch that had assailed me, Instantly tore him, as he were a kid, All into gory shreds! The rest all fled At sight of this, nor cared I then to chase them: So parched was I with thirst, so faint with heat, And wearied by my rampant flight that day Under the burning sun. Besides, the well Was gleaming in its coolness there before me.

“At once forgetful of all else, behold, I rushed to it—I stooped to drink. Ha, see! Stooping reversed within the lucid lymph The semblance of a monstrous Lion stood! I saw his shaggy mane—I saw his red And glaring eyeballs, rolling in amaze! His rough and grinning lips! his long sharp fangs, That like discolored rows of ivory spikes, Were foul with gore, and hung with dripping strings— The muscular integuments of the slain! I shrank away in horrible dismay.

“But as my thirst each moment fiercer grew, I soon returned, to stoop at once and slake Its insupportable heat!—Alas! alas! Again was that tremendous Presence there Standing reversed, as erewhile, in the clear And gleaming mirror of the smiling well! 204 The horrid truth smote like a rush of fire Upon my brain! The terrible Thing I saw Was my own Shadow! I was a Wild Beast!

“‘They did not fable then, who held that oft The guilty dead are punished in the shapes Of beasts, if brutal were their lives as men; Each in the form of that particular beast His nature most was kin to!’ Through my mind Thus flashed that doctrine. At the fatal well A final glance now swept all shadow of doubt From the dread Verity—I was a Wild Beast! A Presence terrible in face and form As that grim Semblance! Down I stooped and drank.

“Long lapped I the cool lymph, while still my tongue Made drip for drip with that so monstrous one Which, as in ugly mockery, from below Seemed to lap up against it. But though thirst Was quenched at length, what might appease the fierce Dissatisfaction of my fated soul? The thought that I no more was Human, ran Like scorpian venom through my mighty frame! And off I bounded, tearing up the sands, That like a drab mist coursed me as I went Out on my homeless track. I made my fangs Meet in my own pierced flesh, that so I might In the sharp violence of bodily pain, Find respite from the anguish of regret! From morn to night, from night to morn, I fled Along the limitless wastes, that so I might In the exhaustion of my giant strength, Find haply some remmission from the keen And searching memory of my lost estate! 205 Worn out and bleeding, in the burning sands I lay down, as to die! In vain!—in vain! The savage vigor of my Lion life Might yield alone to the long tract of time.

“From hill to valley rushing after prey With whirlwind speed, was now my daily wont. For all things fled before me—all things shrunk In mortal terror at my shaggy front And ponderous ramp. Or sometimes, I would seek The perlieus of those close-fenced villages, Wherein the desert dwellers hide their swart And naked bodies from the scorching heats, Hoping that I might perish by their shafts. And often was I wounded—often bore Their poisoned arrows in my burning flesh. But still I lived.

“The tenor of my life Was always this—the solitary state Of a Wild Beast of Prey, who hunted down The antelope, the boar, the goat, and gorged Their quivering flesh, and lapped their steaming blood: Then slept, till hunger, or the hunter’s cry, Roused him again to battle or to slay, To flight, pursuit, blood, strategem and wounds! And to make this rude life more hideous yet, I still retained a consciousness of all The nobler habits of my earlier life, And had a keen sense of what most had moved My nature as a Man: and knew besides That this my punishment was fixed by One Too mighty to be questioned—and too just One tittle of its measure of remit.

“How long this haggard course of life went on I might not even guess: for I had lost The human faculty that measures time. But still from night to night, I found myself Roaming the desert, howling at the moon, Whose cold light always, as she poured it down, Awoke a drear distemper in my brain! But much I shunned the sunblaze, which at once Inflamed me, and revealed my dread approach.

“Homelessly roaming thus for evermore, The tempests beat on my unsheltered bulk, In those bleak seasons, when the drenching rains Drove into covert all those gentler beasts That were my natural prey. I swinkt beneath The furnace heats of the midsummer sun, When even the palm of the oasis stood All withered, like a weed: and for how long Yet knew not.

“Thus the sun and moon arose Through an interminable tract of time, And yet in that dim remnant that remained Within me, of my former understanding, I strove in vain even to guess the term Of their prolonged sucessions. But though thus So dim in this regard, the view of all My human life was ever at my beck— Nay, opened out before me of itself! Plain as the pictures in a Wizard’s glass I saw again the trains that round my car Streamed countless, as I rolled in state through Egypt! I saw the wondrous beauty of my form As it strode smiling through the courts of Ptolomy! 207 With them who there adored it,—saw their fair And passionate faces, such as dreams alone Might limn as extant elsewhere;—saw again The pomps and pageants that around me beamed Unparagoned brightness! And, in seeing, felt Again the temper and the taste of all The godlike loftiness of such a life— Its passions, pleasures,—even its noble pangs! Then in the rage of my thus-goaded heart Would I roll howling, in the burning sand!

“At length this life of horror seemed to near Its fated bourn. The slow but sure approach Of old decay was felt in every limb And every function of my Lion frame. My massive strength seemed spent; my muscular springs That once were as express as are the bolts Of heaven, were ponderous still, but quick no more. The antelope escaped me! In disgust I sought a mountain cavern, shut from day By savage draperies of tangled briers, And only dragged my tardy bulk abroad When pressed by hunger.

“On a day, thus urged Abroad by the dull cravings of my maw, Behold, I sprang upon a buffalo herd That passed across the Desert, tearing soon Its leader down, loud bellowing as he fell. When lo, the Chief of those that drove them came Against me, and I turned my rage on him! But though the long lapse of so many years Of ever-grinding wrechedness, had dulled My memory, yet, I felt that I had seen 208 His withered visage twice before: and straight A shuddering awe subdued me, and I cowered Beneath him in the dust. My appetite For carnage; my unpitying delight In mortal anguish—these had died within me! And could I then have uttered as I lay, In human speech, the wild desire that now Inspired me, I had prayed him, once for all, To crush me out of being, and consign My misery to the pit of final death! But when, thus wishing, I again looked up, The tawny presence of the Afric Chief Was gone! and I beheld the shining Son Of Paradise instead,—arrayed with power And majesty immense, but from whose brow There flashed as well the lightnings of a wrath Inexorably just, because divine! Yea, ’twas the Angel that with Sammael Had fought for me in Egypt; and once more He laid his crushing hand upon my front, And earth and sky, with all that in them is, Became to me a Darkness, swimming blank In the Eternal, round that point where now Stretched out in body, I again lay—dead.
