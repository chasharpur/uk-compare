SQUATTER SONGS.

NO. V.

(To be Sung at the next General Meeting of the Pastoral Association.)

THE GREAT GUNS OF SQUATOCRACY.

PRELUDE.

Squatter gentlemen; every one Bellow—bellow your applause, While I sing of each Great Gun Now firing in “The Cause”! And ever so, to crown your revels, Till your gladness Mount to madness, Roar like very—very Devils!

I.

First on our Ordnance List I’ve placed The darling of the “Brandy-faced”! (Their darling, just because he once Rubbed noses with them for the nonce!) For backed by those, who fails to see In him the Tell of Squattery? Not that he cares a d———n for us (And them he scorns) for all the fuss He has made—makes—and yet shall make! No! his own Interest is the stake He fights for! ’tis his only sense Politically speaking;—hence, His country ruin, to his fear, Means but the loss of Windermere! And by the same self-centring rule, Australia’s growth, the growth of wool! Her liberty,—for Number One A universal Right of Run!— Her morals—mind—and happiness— All that her humbler homes should bless! Are elbowed from his care by what- Soever may be boiled for fat, And tunn’d for exportation, then Be turned to wine for Gentlemen! Such is our Man,—how fit! for thus Bound to himself, he’s bound to us! Yes; for our first Gun, say, Where in Australia may We find a fitter than This “Billy Windford”?— Chorus. He’s the man! For all of our Great Guns, you know, To go “the whole hog” heartily, In principle, of course, should be Thorough Number Ones, you know.
