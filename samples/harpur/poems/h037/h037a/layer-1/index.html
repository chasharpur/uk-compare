3.

A Lady by Moonlight.

A splendid Creature of delight, How she swims upon the night, And in her virgin beauty glows More rosy than the virgin rose.

Her dew-gemm’d feet, on twinkling, seem Two faeries of the Lunar beam, For there were almost room for them On lilies of the slenderest stem.

Her happy eyes, befringed with jet, Glow,—each a sapphire fitly set, And in them softly blends with mind A splendor not to be defined, As fountains in the twilight ray And radiate with a nameless ray.

Her dark locks, parting from her brow, Redundant down her shoulders flow, Which through them shine, like alpine snow Between the splitting volumes gleaming Of a dark cataract downward streaming. Or as more bounding is her tread, Those glossy glories round her head Float rippling, as the sprays in motion 700 Crown-curl a billow of the ocean.

O Earth, with so much beauty clad, Look up to heaven, and be glad! Gaze earthward from your golden cars, And be yet happier, O ye stars! Out shining from thy heavenly noon, Smile yet more, thou smiling Moon!
