A MID-WINTER MORNING.

‘Tis Morn—and the frost-bleachëd hills are all white, Like the bones of a summer-world dead, And the ice-crusted waters blink blind in the light, Like the eyes in a sightless man’s head. And the birds but complain, like sweet feelings undone, When Love wanders forth an outcast; And yon willow’s locks hang like the thin hair of one Whose happiness died with the past. And the trees in the damp dells—ah! how they must ache With the cold of a morning like this, Encoiled by the blast, as, so chill, like a snake It strains through the wood with a hiss.
