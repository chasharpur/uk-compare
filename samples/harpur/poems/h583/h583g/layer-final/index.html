Miscellaneous Poems.

Theodic Optimism.

Man’s life is but a boastful weakness, Pale with terrors unconfessed; Yet Virtue may not lose her treasure, And God is power exceeding measure— Wherefore all is for the best In the end— In that certain Godward end Tow’rds which all things, together working, tend.

The world is mountainous with danger, And with failure, and unrest; Yet is it isled in the supernal, And God is peace—yea, peace eternal— Wherefore all is for the best In the end.

The world is inter-pathed with errors, And with follies unredressed; Yet even wise men are forecasting, And God is wisdom everlasting— Wherefore all is for the best In the end.

The world is sowed with lies for lucre— Lies that every heart infest; Yet fraud is overcome by duty, And God is truth, and truth is beauty— Wherefore all is for the best In the end.

Power, peace, wisdom, truth, and beauty, These are God—and here we rest Secure though weak: for these attest That Evil must be self-consuming, A bale-fire Final Good illuming— All then—all is for the best In the end— In that certain Godward end Tow’rds which all things, together working, tend.

But no man should less active be, Believing thus,—as not less free To use his strength, skill, wit, to reach The things attractive unto each: And somewhat good will ever run From all that’s well-designed and done, Though out beyond our range it go, (Because by God’s hand prospered so) Or wide of what we aimed at—lest All should not be for the best.
