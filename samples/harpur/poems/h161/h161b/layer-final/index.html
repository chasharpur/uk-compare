Your papist, in the Pope’s infallibility, Can see the only barrier of utility ‘Gainst anarchy, and heresy, and schism; Whilst your grave churchman of the English nation Beholds the source of popular salvation, Not in the Bible—but the Catechism; And thus for the same spiritual trout Both angle, though with different tackle out: A huge monopoly in the cure of souls, High places, and blind worshippers in shoals.

Note. Certainly one of the best first steps towards a rational riddance of popery, and that proud ape of it, Anglican prelacy, is to exclude all Church Catechisms 580 from our Systems of national education.

But I object to dogmatical catechisms having an elementary place in our educational systems upon other and higher ground. We have but to simplify any question, to render it the more obviously truthful, or the more evidently fallacious: and applying this principle to the one in hand, I object to their having any such elementary place in popular education, for the following simple and straightforward reason:

Because the truth of any and every thing which the mind in its infancy has to be broken into the unconditional reception of, is the very thing, beyond all others, that it has the greatest right to reserve for future investigation. Or to invert the principle,—that which it is feared the mind would not freely subscribe to in its maturity, and when perfectly unshackled,—is that, of all things, which should not be unconditionally im= 581 imposed upon it in its infancy,—is that indeed which cannot be so imposed upon it without hideously dwarfing its moral stature, and doing cowardly dishonor to its rational liberty.

Still, I admit, that it is good to believe. It gives bent and purpose to individual character; and did these effects terminate in the individual, it were little matter (humanly speaking) what he believed. But inasmuch as these effects influence our conduct with regard to other individuals, to their benefit or detriment, and to our own reciprocally, it becomes vitally necessary that we should only believe in what is true, and therefore universally fit, feasible, and fruitful of good.
