The After Meeting.

We met again! and oh, how sweet that meeting! Her lustrous eyes, with fond tears only dimm’d, 59 And turned to mine as erewhile, overbrimm’d With all that even Love’s large heart in beating With tenderness, could ask of tender greeting And passionate gladness! Then soul-treasured words Flowed, or were stilted, like interupted birds, By the long kisses mine would keep repeating On her rich lips as by their own wild will— Her lips yet richer for such dear constraint! And when at length our joy grew mute, the faint Perfumery of an orange grove did fill The air around and make our breath:—and thus The world seemed Paradise, and to hold but us.
