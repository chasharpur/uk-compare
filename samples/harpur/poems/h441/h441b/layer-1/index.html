[RECORDS OF A POET’S LOVE XXIV]

We met again! and precious was that meeting! Her lustrous eyes, with fond tears only dimm’d, And turned to mine, as erewhile, overbrimm’d With all that even Love’s large heart, in beating With tenderness, could ask of tender greeting, And passionate gladness! Then soul-treasured words Flowed, or were stilled, like interrupted birds, By the long kiss which mine would keep repeating On her rich lips as by their own wild will— Her lips yet richer for such dear constraint! And when at length our joy grew mute, the faint Perfumery of an orange grove did fill The air around, and make our breath:—and thus The world seemed Paradise, and to hold but us.
