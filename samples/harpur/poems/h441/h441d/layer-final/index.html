Re-Meeting.

We met yet once again: at which last meeting Her lustrous eyes with fond tears only dimm’d, And turned to mine as erewhile, overbrimm’d With all that even Love’s large heart in beating With tenderness, could ask of tender greeting, And passionate gladness. Then soul-treasured words Flowed, or were stilled, like interrupted birds, By the long kiss my lips would keep repeating On her dear hand as by their own wild will! And when at length, from serious self-constraint, 195 Our joy grew mutually mute, the faint Perfumery of an orange grove did fill The air around, and make our breath;—and thus The world seemed Paradise, and to hold but us.
