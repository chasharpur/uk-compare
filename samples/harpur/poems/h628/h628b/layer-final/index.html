To the Hon. James Martin.

Be prudent, yet not crafty. Evermore Be doubtful of that seeming wisdom which From sleek Expediency draws all its lore, In reasons poor, in pretexts only, rich. Nor ever held the heroic Fane a niche For such as palteringly bend before Mob interests, mostly beastial at the core, Or those of traders, with their Mammon itch. 234 Who lives but to the Present is a dunce; Who lives but to the Future may at last Turn out a dreamer, and forgotten be: But true power mints the Present, and at once It is the sweet child of a bitter Past, And mother of a raised Futurity.
