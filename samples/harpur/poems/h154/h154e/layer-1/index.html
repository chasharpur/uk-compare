V.

True and False

How long shall Man in splendid wrong believe? 244 See furious Conqueror after Conqueror sweep Over the past! ambitious each to achieve Time’s most unrightious victory, and so reap Unrivalled sway, and make their age one heap Of kindless triumphs for slave-bards to weave Into loud verse; and finally, so leave To Glory names that Infamy should keep! How greater they, who Tyrants did debel, Putting all personal dominion down, Save as a master-means to serving well? Compare an Alexander’s wild renown With those immaculate memories that crown The lives of Hampden, Washington, and Tell!
