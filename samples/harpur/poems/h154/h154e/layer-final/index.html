V.

Glory. Tested.

How long shall Man in splendid wrong believe? 244 See furious Conqueror after Conqueror sweep Over the past! ambitious each to achieve Time’s most unrightious triumph, and so leap To Empire!—making all their age a heap Of bloody marvels for slave-bards to weave Into loud sounding verse, and thereby leave To Glory names that Infamy should keep! How greater they, who Tyrants did debel, Putting all personal dominion down, Save as a master-means to serving well? Compare the Macedonian’s wild renown With those rare memories that like amaranths crown The deeds of Hampden, Washington, and Tell!
