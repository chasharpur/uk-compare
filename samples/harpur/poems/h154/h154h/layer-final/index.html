Personal and other Sonnets.

Glory Tested.

How pronely men in splendid wrong believe! See furious Conqueror after Conqueror sweep Across the Past! ambitious each to achieve Time’s most unrighteous triumph, and so leap To Empire!—making every age a heap Of bloody marvels for slave-bards to weave Into loud-sounding verse, and thereby leave To Glory names that Infamy should keep! How greater they, who Tyrant’s did repel, Putting all personal dominion down, Save such as was a means to serving well? Compare the Macedonian’s wild renown With those pure memories that like amaranths crown The patriot lives of Washington and Tell!
