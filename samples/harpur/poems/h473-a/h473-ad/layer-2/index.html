The Runnel: or a Poem on a Poem.

Mark you Runnel how ’tis flowing, Like a silvan spirit dreaming Of the spring-blooms near it blowing, And the sunlight in it gleaming. Broad,—or with a checker streaming Where the some denser shade hangs bend[ing] O’er it, in green mingled masses Lights and shades and blossoms glowing All for greater beauty blending In its vision, as it passes

Where a shelving rock is spied, There, with a smooth warbling slide, It lapses down into a cool And brimming,—not o’erflowing, pool: Then between its narrowed banks, Playing mellow gurgling pranks, It gushes,—till some channel’d stone Gives it a more strenuous tone.

Or away it wendeth thence With an even profluence: (Beaming to sun afar Like a mighty scimitar) Or betwixt thick-reeded beaches It whispers wild mysterious speeches: Or with an underswirling spread Over a wide pebbled bed, It bubbles with a gentle pleasure, Ere some new mood change the measure.

Such a Runnel typeth well 608 The sweet wild Verse of Christabel. And if, all suddenly, at length In the fulness of its strength, Sinking, it an end should make In some subterranean Lake, A further type we might behold Of the Story half untold.

But what might fitly figure too The wonder-world it warbles through?
