Rising and setting suns of Liberty; Mountainous èxploits, and the wrecks thick strewn By stormy Passion o’er life’s treacherous sea, Relieved with shores of green delight, and boon And starry dreams, and the serene pale moon Of pathos;—these,—thus figured, sensuously, The visual Temple are of Poetry, When into beautiful proportion sho hewn. And for its essence:—all that Mind can sieze Of character, spirited with the living might Of household loves and social sympathies, And that religion of the heart, whose flight 139 Is strongest, and most heavenward, ’mid the blight Of mortal woe;—its inmost soul are these.
