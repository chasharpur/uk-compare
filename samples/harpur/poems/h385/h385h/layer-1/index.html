Poems: By Charles Harpur.

I.

[POETRY]

Rising and setting suns of Liberty; Mountainous èxploits, and the wrecks thick strewn By stormy Passion o’er life’s treacherous sea, Relieved with shores of green delight, and boon And starry dreams, and the serene pale moon Of pathos;—these, arrayed thus, sensuously, As in these emblems are of Poetry, The visual Fare to fit proportions hewn. And for its essence:—all that Mind can seize Of character, spirited with the living might Of household loves and social sympathies, And that religion of the heart, whose flight Is strongest, and most heavenward, ’mid the blight Of mortal woe;—its inmost source are these.
