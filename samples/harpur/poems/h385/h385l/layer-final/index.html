Poetry.

Rising and setting suns of Liberty; Mountainous ventures, and the wrecks thick strewn By stormy Passion o’er life’s treacherous sea, Relieved with shores of green delight, and boon And starry aims, and the serene pale moon Of pathos; these must ever form & be The sensuous world-dome of Poetry, Self-built and fashioned to some Orphic tune. And for its spirit:—all that Thought can seize Of worth and character, worked on by the might Of public needs and household sympathies, With that religion of the heart, whose flight Is strongest, and most heavenward, ’mid the blight Of mortal woe;—its human spirit are these.
