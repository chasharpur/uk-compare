Relief at Last

Love not in over measure, However safe it seem Lest the heart spend all its treasure On a dream-devouring Dream.

And in loving aught that’s human What dote we on but clay? And what charmeth most in Woman Is what fadeth first away.

But if a whole adorer Of somewhat thou must be, Then turn thou Truth’s explorer, And Right Reason’s devotee.

Or if thou wouldst not ever But love unto thy loss, Love thou the God-endeavour Of the Christ upon the cross.
