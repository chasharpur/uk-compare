X IV.

The First Great Australian Poet.

His lot” how glorious” whom the Muse shall name” Her fìrst Hìgh Prìest in this so sunny clime,” Clòthing his life” as with a robe of flame!” With hèr creations of the olden time” Much conversànt”, and by their bulk sublime” Moulding new matter,” let him build to Fame;” Qùarrying from Nature’s everlasting frame” The solid substance of his lofty rhyme.” Then”, meetly mirrored”, o’er his polished page” Shall glow his Countrywomen’s lustrous eyes,” And future Heroes thence a righteous rage” Derive or stimulate”;—all bràve and wìse And beàutĭfŭl sòuls, ùnder his native skies,” Brèathing his influence from age to age.
