XVII.

John Heki. (1845.)

Should Switzerland’s rude rocks be held the throne Of Liberty (God-sanctioned there to quell All proud Invaders, Gesler-like oerthrone!) Because the echoes in their caves that dwell Held converse once with the bold voice of Tell? And thine, New Zealand, still be kept unknown To glory and fame now thy dark Chief hath thrown Broad o’er them a like patriotic spell? Should Wallace be word time ne’er may lose; And Hofer—(him, who for his mountains died, Rather than see the Spoiler there abuse Freedom’s rock-altar!)—be a sound of pride; Yet Heki‘s name remain unglorified In the grand lore of the Heroic Muse?
