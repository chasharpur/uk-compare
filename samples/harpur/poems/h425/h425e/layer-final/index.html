The Crown of Nature

‘Tis beautiful to mark the Morning rise Over the hills, as from her bower a bride Comes brightened—blushing with the shame-faced pride Of love that now consùmmated, supplies All her full heart hath wished; and to the eyes Dear are the flowers then, in their green haunts spied, Glist’ning with dew: pleasant at noon the side Of shadowy mountains ridging to the skies: 7a At eve, ’tis sweet to hear the breeze advance Through the responding forest dense and tall; And sweeter, in the moonlight, is the dance And natural music of the waterfall: 7b Yet to complete & fix the charm of all Love must be near us with its magic glance.
