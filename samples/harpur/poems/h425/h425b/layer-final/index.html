The Crowning Charm of Nature.

‘Tis beautiful to mark the Morning rise Over the eastern summits, like a bride Blushing with the fond secret of the pride She dares to feel in him whose love supplies All her full soul desired; and to the eyes 46 Dear then the flowers in green concealment spied, And wet with dew: pleasant the shadowy side, At Noon, of mountain towering to the skies: At Eve ’tis sweet to hear the breeze advance Through the responding forest, dense and tall; And sweeter in the moonlight is the dance And natural music of the waterfall: But oh, ’mid these, from her we prize, a glance Of tender love’s the Crowning Charm of all!
