Social Charity.

‘Tis life’s prime household wisdom not to scorn An erring friend too fiercely. For if tried 131 Severely in some weakness, all will turn The wrong road sometimes, and make Friendship’s pride Blush with such shames as for the fallen burn: So much hath Charity in the best to mourn. And knowing this we should be sadly kind When we reprove, not cruel,—lest we churn Folly to madness.—Though in One mind Those gems of worth, high motives, feelings pure, Set in the find gold of a constant mind, Yet even he shall sometimes prove insure! As in the clearest well a sediment lies Ready, alas! if much disturbed, to rise.
