XXXI.

Social Charity.

45 Not copied Finally copied

‘Tis life’s prime household wisdom not to scorn An erring Friend too grimly. If but tried Severely in some weakness, all will turn To evil sometimes—and love’s cheek be dyed With blushes such as for the fallèn burn, For Love hath much even in the best to mourn. And knowing this, we should be sadly kind, Not cruel, in reproof, nor sourly churn Folly to madness!—Though in One we find Those gems of worth, high motives, feelings pure, Set in the fine gold of a constant mind? Yet even he shall sometimes prove insure! As in the clearest fountain ever lies, A sediment—disturb it, and behold, ‘twill rise!
