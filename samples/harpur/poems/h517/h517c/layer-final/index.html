XXXI.

Social Charity.

45 Not copied Finally copied

‘Tis life’s prime household wisdom not to scorn An erring Friend too fiercely. For if tried Severely in some weakness, all will turn The wrong road sometimes—and make Friendships pride Blush with such shames as for the fallèn burn: So much hath Charity in the best to mourn. And knowing this, we should be sadly kind, When we reprove, not cruel, lest we churn Folly to madness!—Though in One we find Those gems of worth, high motives, feelings pure, Set in the fine gold of a constant mind? Yet even he shall sometimes prove insure! As in the clearest well a sediment lies, The which, alas! if much disturbed, will rise.
