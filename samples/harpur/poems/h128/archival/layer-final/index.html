[FANCY NEVER DREW A FAIRER]

Fancy never drew a fairer, Rapture never clasped a rarer; And were I thy Chosen, oh! Nought above and nought below Could make me such a Heaven forego! Every feeling fond and and true, Such my passion is for you.

Justice to your own rich merit, And the beauty you inherit Never may admit you mine; Ever, yet, my soul is thine.

Do not think these lines are writ Only to display my wit, You, of all the world most dear! Love like mine, so deeply clear, Even if scorned, were still sincere.
