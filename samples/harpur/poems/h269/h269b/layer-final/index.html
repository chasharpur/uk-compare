Wilding Away

Ye woods where I’ve sung like a bird— Ye homes where old schoolfellows dwell— Ye streams that so often have heard My heart’s gladness—for ever farewell!

Ye once formed an Eden for me, Thus aglow in your summer away: It is terrible from you to flee, But alas! it were madness to stay!

For she—Helouise, the false fair, Whom I erringly chose for your Eve Still frequents these shades—to ensnare! Still strays by yon streams—to deceive!

She promised me love—but at last The chalice ran over with pain, And was e’en such as Hate might have passed For its veriest victim to drain!

Ah! Love’s morning promises, why, After toning the heart like a lyre, Should they ever thus waste to a sigh With all the fond hopes they inspire?

Like racks on the wings of the wind— Like ships o’er the waves of the main, They are borne from the gaze of our mind, And we never may know them again!

But instead, dark distrust and dispair Like thunder-glooms over us brood, Eclipsing whatever is fair, And doubt, dimming all that is good!

Mid the scenes of my youth, thus by Love Made an outcast, twere madness to stay! And oh! for the wings of a dove To rush forth on my desolate way!
