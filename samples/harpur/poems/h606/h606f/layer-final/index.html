Personal and other Sonnets.

To Doctor Lang.

Little perhaps thou valuest verse of mine— Little hast read of what my hand has wrought, Yet I would with thy brave memory would entwine The Muse’s amaranths. For thou well hast fought For Freedom; well her sacred lessons taught; Well baffled wrong; and delved with far design Into those elements where treasures shine Excelling those wherewith our hills are fraught. And when thy glorious grey head shall make One spot all-hallowed for the coming days,— Tombed in the Golden Land for whose sole sake With labor thou hast furrowed all thy ways,— Well a young Nation shall thy worth app[r]aise Even through the grief which then shall o’er thee break.
