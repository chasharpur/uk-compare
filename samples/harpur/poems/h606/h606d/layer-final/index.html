XXXVIII.

To Doctor Lang.

Little perhaps thou valuest verse of mine— Little hast read of aught my hand has wrought, Yet I with thy brave memory would entwine 49 The Muse’s amaranths. For thou well hast fought For Freedom; well her sacred lessons taught; Well baffled wrong; and delved with far design Into those elements where treasures shine Transcending those wherewith our hills are fraught. And when thy glorious grey head shall make One spot all-hallowed for the coming days, Tombed in the Golden Land for whose sole sake With labor thou hast furrowed all thy ways, Well a young Nation shall thy worth appraise Through a grand grief which then shall heavenward break.
