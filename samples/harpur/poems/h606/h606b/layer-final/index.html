SONNET TO DR. LANG.

by Charles Harpur.

Little perhaps, thou valuest verse of mine— Little hast read of aught my hand has wrought, Yet I with thy brave memory would entwine Immortal amaranth. For thou well hast fought For Freedom; well her sacred lesson taught; Well baffled Wrong: well delved with far design Into those elements where Truth’s treasures shine Richlier than those wherewith our hills are fraught. And when thy glorious grey head shall make One spot all hallowed for the coming days, Tombed in the Golden Land for whose sole sake With labor thou hast furrowed all thy ways Well a young Nation shall thy worth appraise Through the great grief that from its heart shall break.
