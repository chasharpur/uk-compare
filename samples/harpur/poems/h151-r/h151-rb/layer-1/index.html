Chorus of the Hours.

So, legal Privilege securely squanders 299 What Nature would not should be bought or sold, Even when her mother-motive most meanders! Lo, Selfishness feeds fat his beastial lust Through the world’s glittering pestilence—gold! While Skill and Talent, without scruple, thrust Into their service and become their Pandars; And lowly Genius, ever, as of old, Between them, penniless, an outcast, wanders, Bowed to the dust!

How long, O Earth, is such to be thy fruit? How long shall thy most pleasant dwelling places Be but the lairs of what is mainly brute In Man? the nurseries of his disgraces?

How long shall Customs whose bad growth appeared First round the blood-fed altars of old time, Be by thy Rulers sedulously reared To choke each vernal promise of thy prime? For human Power hath aye been Wrong’s handmaiden, And still each national code hath chiefly been A statist-woven screne For throned Prerogative, and those who trade in The honors, privileges, rights and creeds— All that for which each cheated people bleeds! But to the poor a yoke instead, Yea, to starved millions an interdict on bread, An added burthen to the heavy laden; Making long life but more of toilsome breath— But more of a stretched out curse! And finally, Leaving the shackled soul that would be free No hope but from the liberty of death!

Yet cheer thee, Earth, this may not always be! Much of thy dole is past! 300 Though lying lips have said that misery And evil are to be thy dower for ever, The day of Intellect is dawning fast, Bright with the fire of God, and as the wide world vast! Bringing, with light, full sanction to deliver! Bringing salvation, like a clensing river Whose nearer voice comes louder, and yet louder, in the blast!
