CHORUS.

Lo, pampered Privilege securely squanders What Nature meant not to be bought or sold! Lo, Selfishness feeds fat his lust Through the world’s glittering pestilence—Gold! While penniless between them Genius wanders Bowed even to the dust!

How long, O Earth, is such to be thy fruit? How long shall thy most pleasant dwelling places Be but the dens of what most savours of the brute In Man? the nurseries of his disgraces?

53

How long shall Customs whose bad growth appeared First round the blood-fed altars of Old Time, Be by thy Rulers sedulously reared To choke each vernal promise of thy prime? Thus human Power hath still been Wrong’s handmaiden, And still each national code hath chiefly been A statist woven screen For arm’d Prerogative, and such as trade in The honors, rights and creeds— All that for which the cheated People bleeds; But to starved millions an interdict on bread, An added burthen to the heavy laden: Leaving the noble heart that would be free No prospect but the freedom of the dead!

Yet cheer the Earth! this may not ever be! Much of thy dole is past! Though lying lips have said that misery That Evil is to be thy birth for ever, The day of Intellect is dawning fast, 54 Bringing full Power to deliver, Bright and gloriously vast! Bringing Salvation like a cleansing river Whose voice comes loud in every blast!
