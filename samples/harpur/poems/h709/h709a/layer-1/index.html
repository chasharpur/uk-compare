Morning Love.

How dear is Love to young beginners, How sweet his voice, how bright his wings! Little the unreflecting sinners Yet dream how through regret he strings The heart in after-wanderings. For, as the Spring is not for aye, So every earliest joy Love brings Must, like Spring-roses, fade away; And with them from the day and night An Eden glory take its flight! Yet is it better to have loved The freshness of delight away— Yet is it better to have proved How early passion knows decay, Than bear about this weary scene— This weary, weary, weary scene! That misbelieving stone-like load— A heart where love hath never glowed! When passion never glowed.
