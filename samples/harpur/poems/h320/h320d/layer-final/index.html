IV.

My Friend Jaques.

A dilettante, deep in law and politics, Up to all sorts of Journalistic jolly tricks; Profoundly far seen in æsthetic latitudes, And awfully sage in philosophic platitudes: One of his tastes is (all his tastes are healthy ones) To toady great men who are also wealthy ones; Thinking them rich, to hug them till they glow he’s fain, But if he finds them poor—Jaques lets them go again, And looking like a victim, wonders mightily How his sage self could have behaved so flightily.
