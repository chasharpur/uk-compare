XXVIII.

The Resignation.

We must forget! Though harsh the sentence be ‘Tis born of truth—truth wedded to despair! Had I one lingering hope of good, I’d share Still loved and loving that one hope with thee! But the vile load of life-long misery I’ll henceforth bear alone;—for selfish ‘twere Longer to dream—even dream, that One so rare Should waste her smiles on a doomed wretch like me! Then Rosa turn thee from the Past away, And give the music of thy tuneful heart To songs of loftier love; that so the ray Which Fortune ever willeth to impart To excellence in Woman, may be thine: Thy life’s bright current must not darken into mine.
