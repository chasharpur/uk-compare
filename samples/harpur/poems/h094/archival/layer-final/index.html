THE DEVIL’S OWN GLEE:

OR, THE MIDNIGHT VOICES OF THE REVENUE HELLS.

Hark, hark! in the dead of the night, How the Revenue Hells in glee, Are raging amain with infernal delight, And boasting each one with the Devil’s own spite, What glorious Hells they be!

“Ho, ho!”—thus their voices amain Arise like a bacchanal roar: “Ho, ho!”—they arise from street, alley and lane— “There’s not one of us all but his victim hath slain, Not one but has ruined its score!

“See, see! how some desolate wretch Lies coiled by each cold dead wall! And yonder they prowl—the minions of Ketch! And hark to the plundered what curses they fetch, While we are the causes of all!

“Ho, ho! ‘tis a mother that groans Her famishing children to see! And hark! ‘tis a beautiful Bride that bemoans, While the Father and Bridegroom lie drunk on the stones! What glorious Hells are we!

“On the night-blackening horse of Despair, In the van of all horrible Powers, Hark, Satan comes riding aloft in the air, While the down-shouting Voices of Evil declare How dear to their Monarch are ours!”

CHARLES HARPUR.
