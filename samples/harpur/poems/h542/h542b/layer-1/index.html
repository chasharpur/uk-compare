VII.

A Thorough Woolgatherer.

“Let me play the lion too.”

Bully Bottom.

“A clerk foredoomed all common sense to cross, Who draws a Bill while he should engross.

Adapted from Pope.

Sure Cumberland, at length, must feel ashamed Of Thee—her Folly’s representative! 188 A conscious dunce, yet not content to give Thy vote in silence—no, thou wouldst be famed For Something, and so calvedst a Bill; conceived In the true spirit of “It sounds more clever, To us and to our heirs, my lord, forever:” And eke a Speech of mental tapeworms weaved. I doubt not thou canst tell when wool is good, Or count correctly in a shearing pen; Yes, and when flies shall have sufficient ken To comprehend a mountain’s magnitude, Thou shalt be fit to legislate for men As well as sheep—but Cowper, not till then.
