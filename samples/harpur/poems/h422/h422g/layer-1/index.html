To ————.

As one who o’er the Arabian wilderness Hath toiled a long, long, spirit-wearying space, Desireth in his thirst the gleaming face Of one known Spring;—and ’mid the shadowless And fiery sand doth then in memory bless The sun-proof shelter of its storied race Of palms, that with their plume-like arms embrace 121 Each one the other in green gracefulness: So ’mid the barren cares it proudly brooks, And the dull daily tedium that it knows, Despite consolatories found in books; My nature panteth for the week’s set close, When it may drink clear welcome from thy looks, And in the shadow of grace repose.
