V. ☞ ☞

[RECORDS OF A POET’S LOVE V]

As one who o’er the Arabian wilderness Hath toiled a long, long, spirit-wearying space, And now athirst, longs for the gleaming face Of one known spring,—and ’mid the shadowless And fiery sand, doth then in memory bless The sun-proof shelter of its storied race Of palm trees, that with plume-like arms embrace Each one the other, in green gracefulness: So ’mid the barren cares it proudly brooks, And the dull daily tedium that it knows, (Despite consolatories found in books) My nature panteth for the evening’s close, When it may drink clear welcome from her looks, And in the shadow of her grace repose.
