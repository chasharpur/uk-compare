Calming Down.

Here would I solitary range To try the Visions I have nurst, Albeit, at length, o’er feeling’s change, I sigh as if my heart would burst.

The long calm day how richly bright; And warblings load the evening breeze:— Why hath my soul no kindred light? My heart no answering melodies?

And now the folding star is near— Bright witness of my happiest day! ‘Tis hanging in its pristine sphere, And burning with its olden ray.

But where are now the beauteous eyes That ‘neath it held me then in thrall? The hopes that hailed its evening rise— The joys that blest its twilight fall?

All left me—and in memory Alone to leave a trace behind, More than spent billows of the sea— Than fragments of a desert wind!

And yet I would not be again The fond one of my happier years, Since Love could sting, and Hope was vain, And Rapture but the sire of tears!

Rather that earlier Being I’d trace Through memory unencarnate shown; Unweighed upon by time and place, And living unto thought alone.
