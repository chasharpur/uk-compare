A Huntress born to Nature through the might of olden lore, Lithely on bounding, lightens right past me, Full as the morning of hurry and vigor, When roam I the mountains o’er—: A seer in soul, like a Poet of yore: See her bow! I see it plain! And shafted quiver balanced nice! 92 See her robes, blown back amain Though looped in front with a sure device, Sweeping behind her, like the train Of a bird of Paradise!

Or when the forest shadows are dew-silvered by the morn, Lonely forth roaming, not long do I listen, Till suddenly sounding up, merry and mellow, There hear I her mystic horn—: As of old ’mid the echoes of Latmos ’twas born: Then her buskined limbs how white They glance, as past she boundeth there! See her bow—a curve of light Across her arm upraised and bare! And see how backward streameth bright The torrent of her hair!

Or when the vales are sleeping in a silent silver sleep, Musefully strolling, not long am I lonely, Till mystical melody, billow on billow, Wells from each echoey steep—: And thence o’er my soul with a confluent sweep: Then an arc of rainbow light Sudden spans some viny glade; And there, behold her, boon and bright, Disclouding from the deeper shade, All girt with dancing Nymphs, in white And festal robes arrayed!
