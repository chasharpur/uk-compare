III.

DIAN.

A Huntress, born of Fancy, Through a dream of olden lore, Lithely bounding, lightens past me Morning full of hurry and vigor, When I roam the mountains o’er:

See her bow!—I see it plain! And shafted quiver balanced nice! See her robe, blown back amain, Though looped in front with a sure device, How it sweeps behind her, like the train Of a Bird of Paradise!

Or when the forest shadows Are dew-silvered by the Morn, Lonely roaming there, I listen, Till,—upsounding merry and mellow, Hark! I hear her mystic horn:

Then her buskined limbs how white They glance, as past she boundeth there! See her bow,—a curve of light Across her arm upraised and bare! And see how backward streameth bright The torrent of her hair!
