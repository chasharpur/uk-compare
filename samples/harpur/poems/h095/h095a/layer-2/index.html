A Sylvan Vision

A Huntress, like Diana, In a dream of old delight, Flies for ever past me, when When I roam the mountain height: I see her robe, blown back blown back amain, Yet pinnd with a sure device, Sweep behind her, like the train Of a Bird of Paradise!

Or when the forest shadows Are dew-silvered by the Morn, Roaming there, in thought I heer her wind her golden horn: Then her buskined limbs how white They glance, as past she boundeth there! See!! how backward shineth bright The torrent of her hair.
