Lyrics. By Charles Harpur.

Dian.

A Huntress born to Nature Through the might of olden lore, Lithely on bounding, Ere long lightens past me, all Full like the morning of Hurry and vigor, When I roam the mountains o’er— A Seer in soul, like a Poet of yore:

See her bow!—I see it plain, And shafted quiver balanced nice! See her robes, blown back amain, Though looped in front with a sure device, Sweeping behind her, like the train Of a Bird of Paradise.

2.

Or when the forest shadows Are dew-silvered by the morn, Lonely forth roaming, Not long do I listen, till 9 Suddenly sounding up, Merry and mellow, Hark! I hear her mystic horn— New borne to my soul, as from Latmos ‘twere borne:

Then her buskined limbs how white They glance, as past she boundeth there! See her bow—a curve of light Across her arm upraised and bare! And see how backward streameth bright The torrent of her hair!

3.

Or when the vales are sleeping In a silent silver sleep, Musefully strolling, Not long am I lonely, for Mystical harmony, Billow on billow, Wells from every echoey steep— And thence o’er my soul, with a confluent sweep:

Then an arc of rainbow light Sudden spans some viny glade, 10 And there, behold her, boon and bright, Disclouding from the deeper shade, All girt with dancing Nymphs, in white And festal robes arrayed.
