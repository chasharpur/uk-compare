Personal and other Sonnets.

Charity.

Man, even in his youth, at heart were hoar, A grinding niggard—the mere slave of Need, Didst thou no influence through his dealings pour. Unmoved by thee, who would, though eloquent, plead Against that scorn which maketh poor indeed The children of distress? Thou art the door Of Wisdom’s social fold, who still, the more The kindless flout thee, holdeth to that creed. On thy maternal bosom many a time I lay my head, and dream that yet thy reign In wide effusion every earthly clime Shall sweeten; and, as o’er some sultry plain Fresh airs breathe vigor, quicken men to gain Capacity for Love millennial prime.
