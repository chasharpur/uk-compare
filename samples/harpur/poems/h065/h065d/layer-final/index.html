8.

Charity.

Man, even in his youth, at heart were hoar, The help-denying niggard of his need, Didst thou ne’er bring thy blessing to his door. Unmoved by thee, who would, though eloquent, plead Against that scorn which maketh poor indeed The children of distress? Thou art the core Of Wisdom’s social aims, who, all the more The kindless flout thee, first holdith to thy creed. On thy maternal bosom many a time I lay my head, & dream that yet thy reign In its faith-widened influence, every clime Shall sweeten; and, as o’er some torrid plain Fresh airs breathe vigor, quicken Man to attain Capacity for Love’s millennial prime.
