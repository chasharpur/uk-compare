The Death of Shilock.

By Charles Harpur.

Scene.—A Closet in Shilock’s House: in which he is discovered in the midst of his treasures, shortly after the Trial, and before the arrival of the Duke’s officers, sent to confiscate them.

He speaks.

The Officers of Justice dogged me home! Justice? ha, ha!—such justice as the wolf Once showed the lamb that in its thirst dared drink At the same runnel. X x though, unless the stream Ran backward towards its source, the alleged offence Had no foundation. But they’ll soon be here, To rob me of my gains—my care-won thrift! My sealéd bags of gold! my precious jewels! Diamonds all light, and rubies red as blood— Yea, red and precious as the drops which now Seem breaking into flames about my heart, And fevering all my frame! For life to me, Uncomforted by their orient glow, were cold, And stark, and joyless even, as death itself— Death conscious of its coldness.

[A Pause.

Here I stand A shattered cedar that was once the crown And glory of Lebanon, in my old age even, For I was wealthiest of all my tribe, And many sought my shade,—but here I stand, My pleasant boughs and golden leaves all stript By one fell storm-blast from my hoary trunk, And scattered in a vagrant litter round! Out! out upon it! Fool! boy-baffled fool! So to intend the swoop of thy revenge, That even the cunning of a beardless scribe Could baulk its course, and on thy own grey head Roll it in ruin back! And what remains me? 480 To slink about as hand in hand with Want Where once I prospered, and my conscious pride Derived its richest and most pungent sauce From that compelled and outward humbleness, Which was to my well-being, but the rind Of an old custom,—merely semblance-deep? O it were viler even than was of old The self-abasement of the Gibeonites, For me, thus ruinously foiled, to live A hemm’d in slave where I was wont to rule The great and powerful, through their lordliest needs, By my more potent wealth. But now, of this Stript to the skin, how vile it were in me To skulk about in dark and narrow ways, Not only mocked by every Christian hound That winds me as I walk, but shunn’d by all, As one most dangerous—an example—nay, A grizzled effigy of evil luck! Mine only child too—gone! O God, canst thou Be still the Mighty One of Israel! The Rock of Jacob! and yet give the Gentiles So critical a triumph? No, no, no! Moses, and ye, miscalled prophetic, who Dreamed dreams of old upon the hills of Judah, Away with all your rants! And come, instead, Thou ready handle to a heathenish mode Of jumping desolation!

[Takes up a Daggar.

This I had thought To sheathe in Jessica’s heart if e’er again She fell within my power. False Jessica! A trait’ress to her sire, and to her race A memorable scandal,—shame be with her! And may she, like the outcast Hagar, be 481 The vagabond mother of a houseless line! When the smooth chamberer that hath witched her eyes, Shall weary of her, and then thrust her forth Amongst the Christian scorners of her blood! She whom I one time in my foolish pride Would call the Rose of Sharon—she! deflowered And trod into the mire of infamous places!

[A pause with agitation.

Now for it.—But first let me, standing here, Baffled, and desolate, and alone with Death, Heave all this maddened heart up into one Dread curse on Venice—one dread dying curse! Henceforth, proud harlot wedded to the waves, Mayst thou be spirited (like thy mariner’s stone, Though in another and an evil sense) With such a seated traction as shall draw, Not riches any longer, nor the fat Of other lands into thy marble lofts, But thunder-laden navies waged against thee, From all the ends of Earth! May hideous Famine, With every Plague that in the olden time Blasted the breadth of Egypt, pitch their camp For ever in thy desolated marts! May a Venetian and a Parricide Be terms of a like meaning; and thy daughters, In every household, be what Jessica Has been in mine;—a creature scheming plans Of deep domestic treason in the home Whose conscious floors, in sounding to her own, Could hardly have seemed to have forgot the trick Of her dead mother’s footsteps! Shame be with her! Shame—shame be with her!

[Walks to and fro in great agitation.

482

But enough of her. And now to my last work.—As here I stand, Hounded by Ruin, hard upon my heels, And faced in front by all that nature most Doth dread, and shrink from with a shivering frame, Hear, Venice, how I heap my curse upon thee! May every tribulation which the heart Of unremitting hate, when scorpean-like Thus dying self-stung, in its burning woe Can vision through the wideness of its wish, Vex all thy future! May they blacken down Thick as a flight of carrion crows about thee! Thick as, when summoned by the Levite’s rod, Locusts came looming, like a hunger-cloud, To waste the fields of Pharoah: till they make Thy lot so monstrous, that the wretch may be Counted a standing miracle who attains My sum of years within thy fatal gates! And then, to scale the very top of horror, May that one wretch, impelled by such despair, Imitate Shilock in his final act.

[Stabs himself and dies.
