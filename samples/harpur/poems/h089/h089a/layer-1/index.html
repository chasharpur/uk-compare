The Death of Shilock.

Scene.——A Closet in Shilock’s House: in which he is discovered in the midst of his treasures, shortly after the Trial, and before the arrival of the Duke’s officers, sent to confiscate them.

He speaks.

The officers of justice dogged me home! Justice? ha, ha!—such justice as the wolf Once showed the lamb that in its thirst dared drink At the same fountain. But they’ll soon be here, To rob me of my gains—my care-won thrift! My seated bags of gold! my precious jewels! Diamonds all light, and rubies red as blood— Yea, red and precious even as that which now Seems flaming at my heart! For life to me Unquickened by their orient glow, were cold, And stark, and comfortless, as death itself— Death conscious of its penury!

[A Pause.

Here I stand, A blasted cedar that was once the crown And glory of Lebanon, in my old age even, For I was wealthiest of all my tribe, And many sought my shade;—but here I stand, My pleasant boughs and golden leaves by th’ storm Stript from my hoary trunk, and scattered round! Out! out upon it! Fool! fool! baffled fool! So to intend the swoop of my revenge, That even the cunning of a beardless scribe Could baulk its course, and on thy own grey head Roll back its ruin! Can I live, thus foiled? 108 Not only mocked by every Christian hound That winds me as I walk, but shunn’d by all, Even the friendliest, of my Jewish peers, As one most dangerous—an example—nay, A grizzled effigy of evil luck! Forsaken of mine only child!—O God! Canst thou be still the Mighty One of Israel! The Rock of Jacob! and yet give the Gentiles So critical a triumph? No, no, no! Moses, and ye, miscalled prophetic, who Dreamed dreams of old upon the hills of Judah, Away, with all your psalmody! and come Thou ready handle to a heathenish mode Of jumping desolation!

[Takes up a Daggar.

This I thought To sheathe in Jessica’s heart if e’er again She fell within power. False Jessica! A trait’ress to her sire, and to her race A memorable scandal,—shame be with her! And may she, like the outcast Hagar, be The vagabond mother of a houseless line! When the smooth chamberer who hath witched her eyes Shall weary of her, and then thrust her forth Amongst the christian scorners of her blood! She whom I one time in my foolish pride, Would call the Rose of Sharon,—she! deflowered And trod into the mire of infamous places!

[A Pause, with agitation.

Now for it.—But first let me, standing here, Baffled, and desolate, and alone with Death, 109 Heave all this maddened heart into one dread Anathema! —my dying curse on Venice! Henceforth, proud harlot wedded to the waves, Mayst thou be spirited (like thy mariner’s stone, Though in another and an evil sense) With such a seated traction as shall draw, Not riches any longer, nor the fat Of other lands into thy marble lofts, But thunder-laden navies waged against thee! Conquering armies and marauding hordes From all the ends of the earth! May hideous Famine, And every Plague that in the olden time Blasted the breadth of Egypt, pitch their camp For ever in thy desolated marts! May a Venetian and a Parricide Be terms of equal import; and thy daughters In every household be what Jessica Has been in mine;—a creature scheeming plans Of deep domestic treason in the home Whose conscious floors, in sounding to her own, Could hardly have seemed to have forgot the trick Of her dead mother’s footsteps! Shame be with her! Shame—shame be with her!

[Walks to and fro in great agitation.

But enough of her! And now to my last work.—As here I stand, Hounded by Ruin, hard upon my heels, And faced in front with all that nature dreads, Hear, Venice, how I heap my curse upon thee! May every tribulation that the heart Of unremitting hate, when scorpian like Thus dying, self-stung, in its burning woe 110 Can vision through the wideness of its wish, Vex all thy future! May they blacken down Thick as a flight of carrion crows about thee! Thick as, when summoned by the Aaronic rod, Locusts came looming, like a hunger-cloud, To waste the fields of Pharoah: till they make Thy lot so monstrous, that the wretch may be Esteemed a standing miracle, who attains My sum of years within thy fatal gates! And then, to scale the very top of horror, May that one wretch, impelled by such despair, Imitate Shilock in his final act!

[Stabs Himself, and Dies.
