XX.

[MY LOVE OF POESY]

O Poetry! what image may I start 36 That best shall figure all the loving pride My spirit hath had in thee? In truth thou art A Joy that keeps for ever next my heart, Even as a fawn is constant to the side Of its own proper dam. And as the tide Of morn hath in the sun its central part, Thou wert the Eye of that Utopean chart My earliest hopes unrolled (in quest of fame) In the wide future. Rivers to the sea More surely run not, than each wish I frame Of personal glory hath its end in thee, And in thy Queenship: For thy very name Is to my soul as hony to the bee.
