Third

II

[MY LOVE OF POESY]

O Poetry! what image may I start That best shall figure all the loving pride My spirit hath had in thee? In truth thou art A Joy that keeps for ever next my heart, 138 Even as a fawn is constant to the side Of its own dam. And as the morning-tide Hath in the risen sun its central part, Thou wert the eye of that Utopean chart My earliest hopes unroll’d (in the so wide) And wondrous future. Rivers to the sea More surely run not, than each wish I frame Of personal glory hath its end in Thee, And in thy Queenship: for thy very name Is to my soul as honey to the bee.
