A Regret.

There’s a Regret that showers about my heart Memorial sweetness—a mellifluous rain Of [foregone] love; that also in my brain Murmurs rich melody, like a foregone part Of all the primal harmonies that start Into their morning life, when the green plain And woody hills unveil themselves again To meet the sun—the great world’s fiery heart. ‘Tis that though living in his tuneful day, My childhood never wore the gentle smile And beautiful gaze of Shelley; that away His soul had journeyed, ere I might beguile, In my warm youth, by some fraternal lay, One thought of his tow’ards the great Austral Isle.
