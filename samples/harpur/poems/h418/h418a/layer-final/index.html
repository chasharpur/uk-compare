PETRARCHIAN MUSINGS; OR, THE HUNDRED SONNETS OF LOVE. BY CHARLES HARPUR.

ON FIRST SEEING ROSA.

Why tower my spirits thus? what means this wild Disturbance at my heart?—this dreary chase Of ideal joys that glow like stars in space? Now to sweet death I were reconciled, For all seems spiritual!—Hills up-piled And rude are cheated into beauty—grace Suffuses the dull forest’s fruitless face! The river that, like a pure mind beguiled, Grows purer for its errors, and the trees That fringe its margin with a dusty shade, Seem robed in mystic wonder! and are these Thus transcendentalized because surveyed By one whose presence sweetens this same breeze, In simple maiden majesty arrayed?
