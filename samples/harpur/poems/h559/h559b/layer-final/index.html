XXVII.

EARLY SUMMER.

Chasing cold Winter’s dull sadness, Summer is come in her gladness! The sky how serene! and the hills how green! And look you, my beautiful Mary, ‘Neath the vigorous beams Of the Sun, how the streams Dazzle abroad, e’en as Spencer’s dreams Wandering blaze through the realms of Faery.

The echoing valleys and rocks How they ring with the life of the Flocks! And how lusty and sportful the rout and the noise Of yon gambolling Herds! And hark to those little Boys Gleeing like birds, As forward and backward they dodge and they run, And tumble and roll o’er the grass in their fun.

And the flowers,—so many they puzzle one’s choice, How they star the green Landscape, and all but rejoice Aloud in their blooming delight! And seem not the sprout-tender trees, And the shrubs all in blossom, red, purple, and white, With the vines amid these That tress down on the sight, As their features dilate in the glad-coming breeze, A laughing with all their might.

And the birds, as they’d never have done, Are carolling over their happy employ That in the first flush of the morning begun! And the bees are abroad in their murmurous mirth Wherever a balm-breathing blossom has birth, To plunder but not to destroy! And the beautiful lizards how deftly they run Over the pathway! and see, in the sun, How the grasshoppers beat their bright wings for joy!
