Springtime Gladness.

Chasing cold Winter’s dull sadness, The Springtime has come in its gladness! The sky how serene! And the mountains how green! And look you, my Sister—my Mary, ‘Neath the quickening beams Of the sun, how the streams Go dazzling on all hands like luminous dreams Flowing wide out through the Queendom of Fairy!

And the echoing valleys and rocks How they beat, like a pulse, with the life of the flocks! And how lusty and sportful the rout and the noise Of the everywhere gamboling herds! And hark, in the morning excess of their joys, How that rabblement yonder of holiday-boys Are all gleeing together like birds; As backward and forward they run, And roll the green grass in their fun!

And the wild-flowers, so many and choice, How they people the meadows and all but rejoice Aloud in their blooming delight! And the trees—the sprout-beautified trees, With the shrubs, all in blossom, red, purple, and white, As their features convulse in the glad-coming breeze, Seem they not—lo! to a Fancy that sees, As if laughing with all their might!

And the birds, as they’d never have done, Are caroling all at their happy employ That in the first flush of the morning begun! And to rifle, but not to destroy, The bees are abroad in their murmurous mirth Wherever a balm-breathing blossom has birth! And the sleek-shining lizards how deftly they run Over the pathways. And, as in the sun They start up, see the grasshoppers, every one, How they clap their bright wings for joy! Even as yonder some holiday boy Clappeth his hands in the height of his fun!
