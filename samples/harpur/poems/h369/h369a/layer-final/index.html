THE MASTER MARINER’S SONG.

(OUTWARD BOUND.)

Away—away She plunges With her white sails o’er her spread, Like the summer clouds that gather On some hill’s piny head: Still away She plunges, rampant, Like a Lion roused to wrath, And the proud wave lies humbled In the track of her path.

Ye ho! my gallant Sailors, Wear her head from off the Land! As his steed obeys the Arab, How she gives to the hand! Like a Soul the world forsaking, Now she leaves the coast behind— And the Main’s her wide dwelling, And her spouse is the Wind.

Then pledge we a full measure To the Friends we left to day, Whose kind thoughts shall hover o’er us On our watery way: Where diurnally remind us Shall the same bright-brimming rite, Of the eyes that yearned blessings When we last knew their light.
