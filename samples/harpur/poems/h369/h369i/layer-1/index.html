XI.

The Master Mariner’s Song (Outward Bound.)

Away—away she plunges With her white sails o’er her spread, Like the sheety clouds that gather On some hill’s piny head! Still away she plunges rampant, Like a lion roused to wrath, And the late-proud wave lies humbled In the foam-track of her path.

Ye ho! my gallant sailors, Wear her head from off the land: As his steed obeys the Arab, How she gives to the hand! Like a soul from earth departing Now she leaves the coast behind, And the Deep is her wide dwelling, Her wild spouse—the gipsy Wind.

Then fill we a full measure To the friends we left to day, Whose kind thoughts shall hover o’er us On our wave-foaming way; Where diurnally remind us Shall the same bright brimming rite, Of the fond eyes that yearned blessings When last we knew their light
