THE HARP OF THE EMPIRE.

THE MASTER MARINER’S SONG.

(OUTWARD BOUND.)

Away—away she plunges With the white sails o’er her spread, Like the sheety clouds that gather On some hill’s piny head. Still away she plunges rampant, Like a lion roused to wrath; And the proud wave lies humbled In the track of her path.

Ye ho! my gallant sailó[?]rs, Wear her head from off the land: As his steed obeys the Arab, How she yields to the hand! Like a soul the world forsaking, Now she leaves the coast behind; And the main’s her wide dwelling, And her spouse is the wind.

Then pledge we a full measure To the friends we left to-day, Whose kind thoughts shall hover o’er us On our watery way; Where diurnally remind us, Shall the same bright-brimming rite, Of the eyes that yearned blessings When we last knew their light.

CHARLES HARPUR.
