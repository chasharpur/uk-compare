III.

Poetry and Prose.

What is the true difference ‘Twixt Prose and Rhyme, Since both may be beautiful, Both be sublime? Nor in subject, nor treatment, Nor passion it ‘bides— But breathes through a certain Rich something besides.

Note.—The true difference between Poetry and Prose is more marked by intensity of tone than by anything in form and matter; and has its perfect analogy in that of the human voice, when singing, compared with the same voice, when in ordinary discourse, or speaking merely. When the tone of this voice is excellent, both in song and speech, it exactly countertypes the best Poetry and the best Prose; while its recitative has its analogy in a good mixture of both, like the Arcadia of Sidney’s or Gesner’s; death of Abel. And even when it is displeasing under all of these conditions, it may be still taken as a similitude of the indifferent, or the execrable, in these several kinds of composition.
