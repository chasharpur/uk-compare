The Infinite.

As it is revealed in the Chambers of Space. (From the German.)

Up from his dreams, as slumbering soft he lay, Into the vestibule of Heaven itself Did God once call a Man—a pure, though far Enquiring spirit, full of faithful thought, And deep devotion to all lofty lore;— Saying “Come hither, and behold thou all The glory of my house”. And then to those Who ministered about his throne, the high And holy servants of his instant will, In fatherly benignity, he said: “Take him, and strip him of his robes of flesh, Cleanse ye his vision also, and inbreathe A new and strengthening life into his nostrils; Nor fail to plume him with angelic wings: But touch ye not with any change his heart— The human heart that trembles and that weeps. Then with a meet companion—thou—or thou— Let him go forth, that he may see indeed That glory, whereof, in his fleshly state, His searching spirit hath but guessed, though well.” So was it done. And the Man stood equipt, With a majestic Angel for his guide, To make his awful voyäge.
