[A LYRICAL LOVE STORY 3:4]

The past is flowing through my thoughts— Flowing like a sea; With all its billows dancing bright, Over What?—an under-might Of darkling loss and destiny.

Still it floweth through my thoughts— Floweth like a sea; While of worn Hope I ask alway, Like an unsought castaway, What can earth’s future be to me?

And Hope herself admits: “To thee But a dark’ning scene: Or, at the very best, sans doubt, But a stale, stale lengthening out Of what the gainless Past hath been.”
