VI.

[RECORDS OF A POET’S LOVE IV]

Dressed all in snowy white I saw her so, And thought her garb the emblem of her mind. “Pure—spotless—innocently unconfined— What chastity of style (I said) can be Yet flowing as the breeze when it is free, And with sweet fancies of the angelic kind, A Virgin so arrayed, and so inclined In spirit, doth most witchingly agree! Oh that I were that graceful garb, enfolding 3b All I most covet in this world so wide, How vainly have I in my cunning tried To stop at admiration? since beholding Her form thus garmented, my will at length Is drowned in the wild waves of Love’s arousing strength!”

Euroma, September, 1866.
