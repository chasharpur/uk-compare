VIII.

As when upon a leafy forest falls A wide-devouring fire, which still the wind, Wild whirling, carries upon all hands out, Till the great branches, all with raging flames O’erwhelmed, fall blazing round the blasted boles: So at the hand of Agamemnon now The heads of many flying Trojans fell, And eke before him, many proud-necked steeds With emptied chariots rattled through the rout, Longing in vain for them, whose master-hands No more might guide them.
