POETRY.

WOMAN,

BY C. HARPUR.

Vain are the promptings of the Nine If woman aid them not. Inspire They may the perfect sounding line, Beauty alone can lend it fire. Once loves the Bard—the sounding shore, And all he coldly sang before, The moon-light stream—the flower—the grove— Start to Elysian being, and star a Heaven for Love!

Yes,—true Love’s pleasureable alarms Magnific over earth and sky Triumphant reign; sounds with its charms Even thy depths Immensity! Now looks our Mistress on a flower, And straight it breath’s a magic power; Anon towards Heaven she lifts her eye And the grand Orb himself rolls gladlier thro’ the sky.

There’s not a bird seen flying far, Nor filmy rack that dots his jaunt, There’s not a meteor, not a star, But thus is ever ministrant To love of woman! Its pleasing thrall With morning’s rise, and evening’s fall Claims kindred,—and throughout the night— As the wild moon walks over burnishes her light.

Behold yon wretch with fervid eye, Whose hope hath dwindled to a spark, Go, teach to him philosophy: He lists—but yet his soul is dark, Each Gospel-promise to his needs He lists—but still his bosom bleeds; Go—bring the Aurora of his heart Upon the scene, and bid her play the soother’s part,— A A
