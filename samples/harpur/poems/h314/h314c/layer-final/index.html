XV.

Speak the Truth.

Let the truth be always told, Despite of loss, or bonds, or death! It fits not that the bold Should breathe dishonest breath.

Truth in word is first the way For all who would in deed be true: And none should fear to say, What he would will to do.

‘Tis the sole heroic plan— The one strict line ‘twixt low and high: For it unmans a Man Only to think a lie.

Not that we are bound to tell To every prattler all we think: But what explains him well From that let no man shrink!

For that, once said indeed, ‘Twere utter baseness to unsay! ‘Tis then his manhood‘s creed, Imperil what it may.
