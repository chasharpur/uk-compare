[THE “NEVERS” OF POETRY]

[...] Or with him think, the musical in style Is best achieved through piggled metres vile, And that a line is racy to the core, And bold, and truly eloquent, the more It striving seems to tear itself asunder,— Like this one, “Down i’ the deep heart o’ the thunder”! But for which surely (out of Chaos) none Would seek a motive sanction—save in fun.
