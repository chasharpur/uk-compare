195c

Original Poetry.

SONG.

No more from thee Alen!—the spell now is broken That bound me so firmly—so dearly to thee, Take back your fond sonnet, and golden love token, For you have dealt cruelly, falsely with me.

How oft, when they told me your faith would prove fleeting, And my father and mother have trembled for me, Have I stole to the scene that last witnessed our meeting, To weep, for their thinking so lightly of thee.

But, away to your new love—away! and be given To her the gay flattery fondness approves; Go—swear that the light of her being’s a Heaven, Whose cherubs, are graces—whose seraphs, are loves!

Oh! I’m bow’d down with woe and amazement together, And as from a fowler I’ve seen on the lea, A dove sorely wounded fly heedless of whither, So my heart turns affrighted and bleeding from thee.

We weep ourselves wiser! hence sisters beware ye, That ye study to judge not mankind as they seem, Lest the star of your love, that but once rises clearly, Waste—waste all its brightest—best beams on a dream.

STEBII.
