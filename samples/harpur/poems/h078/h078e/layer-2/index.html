Lyrics. By Charles Harpur.

Sir Gilbert. X

Sir Gilbert is a happy Knight, And happier is he aye, To think his sole Child’s lovesome eyes Grow lovelier day by day: Two violets bathed in morning dew,

Where two are seen alone

; Two well-springs filled with heaven, as through Their willowy fringes shown! And so it hath been ever forth Since first his heart could say: A Lily of Love’s green Valley Is born to me this day.

Sir Gilbert is a gladsome man— A gladsome and a free! 25 But why hath he provoked to day This storm of minstrelsy? It is as though his heart had run Stag-wild with mirth and wit, And he would have old Time’s grey one Go bounding on with it! But did you ask the reason why, The happy man would say: My Lily of Love’s green Valley Is seven years old to day.

2.

Sir Gilbert was a gladsome man— A gladsome and a free; But silent in his hall is now The voice of minstrelsy: It may be, he hath felt his prime A-wearing from him fast And so would use the coming time More staidly than the past. But did you ask the reason why, The altered man would say: My Lily of Love’s green Valley Is withering fast away.

26

Sir Gilbert was a stalwart Knight, And sturdy as a tree, Though genial as the warmth of wine In pleasant company; And can it be, that he hath so Misused a year of time, That few his face again would know Who saw him in his prime? Ah! that is not the reason why His head is turned so grey: His Lily of Love’s dimm’d Valley Has witherëd all away.
