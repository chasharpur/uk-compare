The Song of the Poet Shepherd’s Wife.

O weary sometimes seems the day As I look forth alone, And think what might have happened In yon dark Bush unknown: And then my heart is lonely As any heart can be, Till Charley, with his thousand, Comes home at eve to me.

Like lines of mist, I see his flock Far looming through the trees, Until its myriad bleatings Come thickening in the breeze; And then my heart is happy As any heart can be— For Charley and his thousand Are coming home to me.

My Charley! he hath sung of love Till even his native Land Is hued with rarer colors By his so cunning hand!And 4 And things of light are round he And spirits eagle-free— Yet he must tend his thousand For his little ones and me.
