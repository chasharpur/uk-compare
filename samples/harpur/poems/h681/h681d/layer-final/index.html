7.

The Poverty of Genius.

Haply the reason we in vain would know, Why the most rarely mental of mankind Are seldom world-wise. Yet ‘twould seem as though High Heaven, to rigid verity inclined, Doth abnegate to majesty of mind Much outward blazonry, the more to show That lasting light but from itself can flow, And that the favor of the world is blind. The Seers of old were poor in all but style; Nay, even Jesus for a price was sold, And lived and died a Man of Sorrows; while A brutal Herod could buy realms with gold! A dotard Rome’s almighty empire hold! And even Barabbas, liberated, smile!
