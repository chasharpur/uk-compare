I will conclude this section, by annexing, as a sort of com- panion piece to the portraiture immediately above, some fugitive lines on

ANIMAL HUMAN BEAUTY.

I knew a Youth, in feature and in form Shaped like a God; yet in the air of these There was a somewhat so all-animal— So fleshly, fierce, and froward, that they seemed Instinct with sensual venom as it were; And which, whenever I beheld him, made His beauty frightful even while it took The vision,—like a bold and splendid Snake! With a predominating intellect Forth shining from his front, and ordering all His attitudes, how different the effect Of lineaments so perfect, and a form So toweringly and beautifully built! Then such a Youth were a sustaining tone, Not a strange discord, in the March of Man.
