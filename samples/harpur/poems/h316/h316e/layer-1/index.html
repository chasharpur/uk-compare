x x x The Muse’s Ethics. ᴓ

Hold to the right, and be loyal all things: This is of manhood the only true plan! Hold to thy faith, or in great or in small things: This is the statute God layeth on man.

Build not thy structures upon the world’s notion That deemeth all good may compassed through pelf: Nothing in fortune is worth our devotion That any can buy, as not owning itself.

Nothing in fortune, and nothing in nature, That time can impair or base interest mould! But whatso hath part in the spirit’s God-stature Is absolute value, and cannot be sold.Perish

Perish the wisdom that makes us time-servers! Perish the virtue that’s gain in disguise! Leave them to comrades, to weaklings, to swervers— Yea, make them thy scorn, as one valiantly wise.

Fear not, when wrong Power contriveth undue laws, That right shall be fountained in manhood no more; Blench not, when traitors abandon a true cause— The truth of it liveth with God as before!

Nations benighted by failure, believe not; But wait for the morning do thou—and believe: Mammon’s high castes ban the poor, and relieve not; But make God thy debtor do thou—and relieve.

Yea, to their cause, in God’s name, and the devil‘s, Hold, if thou must, when contending with Pride! Led by that wisdom whose rule only levels The lofty, by raising the worth it has tride.

Live—die for thy own, be they many or few men— Following Love even into the grave! Thus, and thus only, can any be true men, Faithful in all things—fraternal and brave!
