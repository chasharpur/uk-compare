4d

POEMS BY CHARLES HARPUR.

I.

A SONG FOR THE TIMES.

Hold to the right, and be loyal in all things! This is of Manhood the only true plan: Hold to thy faith, or in great or in small things!— This is the statute God layeth on Man.

Build not thy structures upon the world’s notion, Deeming all good may be compassed with gold! Nothing in fortune is worth thy devotion— Nothing worth loving can ever be sold.

Perish the wisdom that makes us time-servers! Perish the virtue that’s gain in disguise! Leave them to cowards and shamites and swervers— Yea, make them the scorn of the valiant and wise.

Nations benighted by failure, believe not! But wait for the morning, do thou,—and’believe; Mammon’s high castes ban the poor, and relieve not! But make God thy debtor, do thou,—and relieve.

Live—die for thy own (be they many or few men!) Following Love even into the grave! Thus, and thus only, can any be true men, Faithful in all things, and pious, and brave.
