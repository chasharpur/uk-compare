The True Poise of Manhood.

Trust not too much in him whose cold clear light Comes sharply shining only through his reason, Nor him whose mental rampires are so slight That he misdeems all self reliance treason; Though one appear to penetrate the core Of nature, with the shaft of thought severe, And though the other like an angel soar Through the fine element of feeling’s sphere; For one might buy or sell you, did it fit Some theory to do so; and the other, To purge the earth of all unholy wit, Might burn you at a stake, were you his brother! Then choose we for our Sage, him only, who In thinking widely feeleth deeply too.
