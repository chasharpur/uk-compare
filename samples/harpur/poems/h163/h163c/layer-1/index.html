The Great Gun of the Australian-Crimean-War-Fund, brought to a queer Test.

Squire Sham, with many duties lying Under his very feet unfound, After a deal of internal sighing To think of other Shams outvying So great a Sham as he—(’twas trying!) Thumped down Fifty Pound! Several more, with Mort the thrifty, Gave five times Fifty! One Bank down thundered Twice that—Five Hundred! But to its swelling store Sir Daniel Cooper flung a Thousand! And should a beggar fling a Louse and Nothing more! I wonder which might prove to be The greater patriot—Sir Dan or he: Since by the test of numbers nice, The one is possibly a man Yet richer even in Thousands than The other were in Lice?
