189

To Samuel Prout Hill Esq.

Author of Tarquin the Proud and other Poems.

Like Cowper, when he first read Milton’s verses, 190 I danced for joy, thine reading. Lines so wonderful, That Homer even, with all the Nine for nurses, Did forge so early nothing half so thunderful! What though the critics daily cut thee sharper? (I wonder where the villains think they’ll go to!) Preferring Halloran, and even Harpur— Nay, that mere sketcher in rhymed moonshine—Lowe, too? Alas! not therefore our Parnassian Hill Should give his Castaly to flow no more! Original indeed must be the quill, And blest as goose-quill never was before, Through which a heightened miracle’s disclosed: For Balaam’s Ass, dear Samuel, only prosed.
