From the crude records which mysterious Time Hath printed on the mountains and the shores, And in the rocky bowels of the Earth, We learn that she, at dateless intervals, Hath been the plastic and predestined mould Of awfullest changes. Beings, unto which The monstrous serpent of the Indian waste Were but a minim, in her miry lap Have wallowed snorting in the unwieldy joy Of life enormous: but to perish, when Her hardening surface grew the meet abode 482 For things of greater beauty. Lastly, Man, And all the world of Creatures over which He holds dominion, with absolute forms Fairer, and faculties to nicer ends adapted, gave her Orb, That heightened loveliness we now [unclear] behold.

The hunter’s voice went forth then on the hills, And in the valley’s the glad chaunt of birds Mixed sweetly with the gurgle of pleasant streams And sway of bloomy boughs; till Nature taught Art music, and bright Poesie arose Out of the dayspring, like a morning star, And Form idealised, in hues or graven, Adorned the abodes of Wisdom;—sister Arts, Three sister Arts in fellowship divine, An intereffluent trinity of Mind.

But dare we say the awful Law of Change, From good to better still, hath here an end? That Earth is Man’s forever?—his and theirs, The Creatures over which he reigns predominant, As King, his only Kingship? No: the Hours Which shall behold it the prepared abode Of new successions in the scheme of Life, May even now, (like a long flight of storks Seen seaward yet, be winging from Eternity Their dim but destined course.

But whence, ’tis asked, Might spring these novel orders? Whence, but from Our mother Earth’s prolific womb, wherein, Even from the first, their causes shall have slept In pregnant preparation, waiting but The evoking word of God: or rather, they Were but progressive changes in the form And garniture of that divine idea, whereof Is, with its bodily populations all, The outward shape in manifold reflexion: Just as a human thought (if finite may With infinite compare),—as knowledge grows Before it, and combines all relative things, A necessary progress undergoes
