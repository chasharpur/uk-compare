The Lost Voice.

A Voice for ever sweet and low Like the flute-tones of a brook— Richly familiar, Lik a loved song given to flow From some heart-valued book; Day by day was at my side Amid the hills where I was born, 91 And if it went at eventide, It came again at morn: And ’twas the last that blest me, when I wandered from my native glen.

Again returned—again I hear Brooks in flow that then I heard— Wildly familiar, Youth’s glad dreams seem warbling near In every happy bird. Yet the more I feel alone That round me these known things rejoice, Because I miss ’mid every tone That still-present Voice, Which I may listen for again In all the weary world—in vain!
