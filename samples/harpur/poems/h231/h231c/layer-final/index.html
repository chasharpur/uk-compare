V.

THE LOST VOICE.

A Voice forever sweet and low, Like the fluting of a brook— Richly familiar, Like a loved song given to flow From some loved book; Day by day was at my side Amid the hills where I was born, And if it went at eventide, It came again with morn; And ’twas the last that blest me when I parted from my native glen.

A wanderer long,—again I hear Brooks in flow that erst I heard— Wildly familiar, Youth’s glad dreams seem warbling near In each glad bird: Yet the more I feel alone That round me these known things rejoice, Because I miss in every tone The one according Voice, Which I may listen for again In all the weary world—in vain!
