Original Poetry.

TEETOTALISM.

A power is shed abroad—a light has shone Upon the nations—in the wilderness Of the world’s social horror and distress, Heralding Temperance, as the Baptist, John, Announced the Christ.———Amazed, upon her throne, Built of Time’s rarest skulls, in life not less Than temples of immortal intellect—Excess Blinks in its rays and feels her empire gone; And Ignorance and Crime—each brutal vice That brands the brow with shame and steels the heart— Are starting from their lairs in human sties, Like felons scared, and gathering to depart— Even as the monstrous idols of the earth Trooped hellward at the Babe of Bethlehem’s birth!

Charles Harpur.
