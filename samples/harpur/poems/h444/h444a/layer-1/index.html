XXVII.

The Retrospective Broodings of Hopeless Love.

My hope is cast behind! and now my heart Hath but one well of joy, delved in the Past, Whereat to slake its thirst; and this, at last, Hath made my very life seem but the part Of a deep mystery of exceeding love! The Future’s beingless—but from the haze Of by-gone time, two warm eyes ever gaze In distant kindness on me, to emove Some recollection, ‘trancingly intense, Of rich vows granted for the bliss of granting! The Present too is but a blank—and hence 62 Still dearer grows the Past; until, thus haunting My memory, it seemeth to its sns A living Spirit with destined passion panting.
