XXVII.

The Retrospective Broodings of Hopeless Love.

My hope hath lagged behind me and my heart Hath but one well of faith, delved in the Past, Whereat to slake its thirst; and this at last Hath made my very life seem but a part Of the deep mystery of a refluent love! The Future’s beingless—but from the haze Of by-gone time two warm eyes ever gaze In distant kindness on me, to emove Some recollection ‘trancingly intense Of rich vows granted for the bliss of granting! The Present too is cold and blank—and hence 62 Still dearer grows the Past; until, thus haunting My memory, it seemeth to its sense A living Spirit with destined passion panting.
