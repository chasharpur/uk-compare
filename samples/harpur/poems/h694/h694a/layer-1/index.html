Words and Deeds also suggested the following counter-effusion, from which it will appear at least appear, even to Mr. H., that there are two sides to every question, and two ways of looking at every thing.

[WORDS ARE DEEDS]

Words are Deeds. The words we hear May revolutionise or rear A mighty state. The words we read May orb a spiritual deed, Excelling any fleshly one, As much as the celestial sun Transcends a bonfire, made to throw A light on some mere Raree-show. A simple proverb, tagged with rhyme, May color half the course of time; The pregnant saying of a sage, May influence every coming age; A song in its effects may be More glorious than Thermopylæ; While a great Book is in my view A greater deed than Waterloo,And 182 And many a lay that schoolboys scan A nobler feat than Inkermann.
