CHORUS.

Ah, that a happy Childhood’s happiest days The more enjoyed should be the deadlier food Therefore for bitter-voiced Regret In Misery’s after mood; That all the more their innocent displays May bloom in memory’s Eden yet, 38 The harder still should seem the present’s barren ways!

And why, with soul so full of faith & free, Should young Humanity thus straitened be? Although pursuing its appropriate bliss, It can but live to the rue the road! Ah! in a world so excellent as this, Why should a loving heart become lifes heaviest load?

To still these awful questionings of Pain The Sages of the Earth have striven in vain, Since Love first wailed beneath the skies, And men, to their most vital interest blind, Grew careless of their kind; Although a voice in every soul replies— A voice mere logic never deigns to heed, How movingly soever it may plead, Renewing all the foregone wisdom of the wise, 39 Who were the wise indeed, While it declares that absolute Brotherhood And that equality it would devise, Is the one only social creed Whence simple and co good And life-long pleasantness can flow indeed.

And ever until Man To such large charity shall conform his plan His soul shall be a wanderer from the Earth; Unsettled as a cloud, that from its birth Keeps roaming whither the wind wills; Or as a hart amid the desert’s dearth, Still panteth on in search of visionary rills Running in gladness down from visionary hills.
