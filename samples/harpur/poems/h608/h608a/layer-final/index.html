To the same

I sometimes wish my Muse a Monthly Nurse, Or something else—or even something worse! For here I’m still “Poor Jack”—without ”the shiners”, And thence unwelcome to all sorts of diners! Whilst others clip their fleecy flocks, and store Bright gold, I’ve only so much verse the more! Gainless, however gainful be the times; Even you turn ivory—I nought but rhymes! A vagrant—and the cause still, all along, This damn’d unconquerable love of song!

I’ll cut it yet, though! and, the mans devising, Can’t we get up an Act ‘gainst poetising? I’ll cut it—yes! as all my advisers bid me, And get as rich as any Jew in Sydney.

That is, I mean to turn an Auctioneer, And take to buying cheap and selling dear. Gods! how I’ll puff the “lots” I’ll have to class— Even Stubbs shall stand astonished at my brass! Stale cheese shall be ambrosia, and sour Cape The blessèd vintage of any heaven-grown grape Filched by Prometheus, and for which Jove’s ire Was thundered at him—not for filching fire: Nay, even Gin half yellow to the eyes Shall be the very cream of Paradise! And when I think of what I’ll do for Rum However hell-fire-ish—but all that’s to come. Then lands beneath my hammer, which a rat Would starve, shall wax than Lombardy more fat; Shall utter forth a murmurous song of streams, And glow with verdure!

Are not those the dreams To foster? Is not that the one design For which ‘twere well to cut the coinless Nine? Being the sole one that ‘mongst Australia’s sons can E’er make me great: they’re all such Number Ones, man.

I’m serious—Henceforth In a son of pelf! But honest: hark, how I advis myself. “Let no low trick of Trade, Charles, be thy trick; Nor cut out aught that makes men “cut their stick”, And then, “their lucky”—if they’ve luck to do it: If not, their sure unluckily to rue it: Though quite the dealer, always cut the sharper; Or Parkes, thy friend, will have to cut Charles Harpur.”
