What’s Poetic?

What’s Poetic? Think whether the day-god is not, As rolling through measurless space, He dilates with that life-glow, on all hands out-shot, Which the planet-orbs drink from his face: 190 And we prize him, as pictured by Poësy, more Than we do ‘neath his actual power, Because we are then by her art shaded o’er As it were with a Paradise bower.

What’s Poetic? Think whether the moon’s orb is not, And the clustering glories of night; Hanging all, she and they, in their heavenly lot, Like isles of immortal delight: And we prize them, as pictured by Poësy, more Than we do while familiarly seen, Because on her dream-wings our spirits then soar As it were in their deathless demesne.

What’s Poetic? Think whether a green wood is not,— A green wood by balmy winds stirred, And a runnel that slides with a flash from its grot, And a trill like the song of a bird: And we prize them, as pictured by Poësy, more Than we did when old outlying things; Because they are brought as it were to our door Through the spells that around us she flings.

What’s Poetic? Think whether a sunset is not, When it turns all the depths of the west, Into jasper seas rolled round some golden-banked spot, Or purple cloud-isle of the blest: And we prize it, as pictured by Poësy, more Than we did when to Nature confined, Because it is then ever fixed, through her lore, In the scenic world-dome of the Mind.

What’s Poetic? Think whether a tempest is not, With its dragon-winds bellowing by, 191 And its thunder-flames jagging out hissingly hot As from fractures and flaws in the sky: And we prize it, as pictured by Poësy, more Than we did when its terror was known, Because its dread face then looks in at our door In the strength of its grandeur alone.

What’s Poetic? Think whether some Hecla is not, Standing grim in a wild winter-world, With its lava-floods seething from chasm and grot, And its fire-rocks at heaven uphurled: And we prize it, as pictured by Poësy, more Than we should in the outward alone, Because ever thence it shall riot and roar As a wonder-nurst birth of her own.

What’s Poetic? Think whether the ocean is not, Or in storm, or when plain-like it sleeps, Lying wide as the heaven whose face is begot Again in the womb of its deeps: And we prize it, as pictured by Poësy, more Than we did while around us it wrought, Because it is then, not a waste we stare o’er, But a mighty possession of thought.

What’s Poetic? Think whether all gladness is not, From that of the bird to the bee;— Yea, all happy motion—kids skipping—a knot Of revellers dancing in glee: And we prize them, as pictured by Poësy, more Than we do in Time’s jading displays, Because in her light they live not,—as before,— At the mercy of wearisome days.

What’s Poetic? Think whether all passion is not, That is lovely, terrific, or grand, As awakened by Beauty, Hate, Glory, or what Ambition hath wrought in the land: And we prize it, as pictured by Poësy, more Than if ‘twere our burthen indeed, Because it is then, through the balm of her lore, From the ache of its earthliness freed.

Thus the realm of the Muse is as wide as the world, And as high as the heaven of night, While the wings of her power day by day are unfurled In the orient banners of light: Till tending through all that is meet in its kind, In the guise of a mystical dove She maketh her nest in some beautiful Mind, And the brood she discloseth—is Love.
