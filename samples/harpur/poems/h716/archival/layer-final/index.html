THE FLIGHT OF FAITH.

As through an ocean of uprolling mist Amid the morning Alps, a morning bird Keeps soaring, trustful of the risen sun, That then is turning all the mountain tops To diamond islands washed by waves of gold That shatter as they surge,—keeps soaring, till It bursts at length into the cloudless light, And gleams a bird of fire: so faith upmounts, Through the earth’s misty tribulations, up Into the clear of the eternal world; Unfainting, fervent,—till, with happy wings Dispreading full amid the rays of God, It glories, gleaming like the Alpine bird. But wearying in its flight, even Faith returns, As does the bird,—returns into the mist That shutteth down all less adventurous life But happier for the glorious vision left, And livelier for the warmth upon its wings.

Charles Harpur.
