The Chilly Man’s Lament

A mock Sonnet

To be copied

I dont like cold—I never did. Perhaps This skin of mine, from over-tender breeding, Warm clothes, snug seats, soft lying, and nice feeding Is grown all too delicate —at all events it chaps Whenever the sharp wing of Boreas flaps Against it; till I feel a sense exceeding Of frosty misery., as if pricked and bleeding At every pore, or smarting after slaps. Behold how near the bright and rousing fire I use to sit me, huddled all together: In vain—for though in front I may perspire, Behind I’m bitten by the cursëd weather! Ye cruel Fates! how could ye all conspire To lap Pil Garlic in such chilly leather.
