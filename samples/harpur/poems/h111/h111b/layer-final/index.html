5c

III.

Emerson.

The Old World, sectioned by its dull dead walls Of custom and convention, caste and creed, That interclude (were there no other thralls) The liberty of thought and word and deed, Had dwarfed or perished the original seed Of that large fruit which from thy Genius falls In this thy priceless Book, to still the calls Of the Mind’s ancient hunger while we read. And never was there Volume wherein shone More wisdom, nuggetted* like virgin gold; Or one through which more streams of Nature run, Or grander thunder peals of thought are rolled. Thou Bard of Self Reliance, strong and bold, Thou giant-minded Mystic, Emerson.

* The root of this word, which came up with the gold discovery, may now be reckoned, I suppose, a part of the 6a language. It is certainly no beauty; but used thus adjectively, it expresses more exactly my meaning than any other word would, and I therefore adopt it.
