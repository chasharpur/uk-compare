219

To the American, Emerson.

The Old World, with straitened dull dead walls Of custom & convention, cash & creed, That interclude (were there no other thralls) The liberty of thought and word and deed, Had dwarfed or perished the original seed Of that large fruit which from thy genius falls In this thy priceless Book, to still the calls Of the mind’s ancient hunger while we read. And never was there Volume wherein shone More wisdom, nuggetted x like virgin gold; Nor one through which more streams of nature run, And grander thunder-peals of thought are rotted: Thou Band of self reliance, strong & bold! Thou giant minded mystic, Emerson.

x The root of this word, which came up with the gold discovery, may now be reckoned, I suppose, a part of the 219 language. It is certainly no beauty; but used thus adjectively, it expresses more exactly my meaning, than any other word would, and I therefore adopt it.
