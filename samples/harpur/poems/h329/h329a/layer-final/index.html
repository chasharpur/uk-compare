Charles Harpur’s Wild Bee of Australia, A Series of Poems, With Prose Notes.

Winter & Summer, or Neither will suit.

Winter I hate thee! In thy frigid lot there is Little that’s pleasant and less that is cherishing! Give me the Summer! no matter how hot it is; Heat may distress one; but cold!—it is perishing!

Sumer I hate thee! thy sun is so fiery like; Drought too’s a thing worse than language can tell it is! Give me the Winter! no matter how miry like; Cold may be pinching; but heat!—what a hell it is!

Note. Are these squib verses good? They ought to be so, at all events; seeing that, to improve the joke, and make it the more wilful, they are constructed upon a principle of strict me- -trical quantity:—a versification-feat quit practicable in our said-to-be-unpliant English, notwithstanding the several failures of Bob Southey.
