XLVII.

NEITHER WILL DO.

1.

Winter, I hate thee! in thy chilly lot it is To have almost nothing that’s pleasant and cherishing. Give me the Summer!—no matter how hot it is: Heat may distress one; but cold!—it is perishing.

2.

Summer, I hate thee! thy reign is so fiery-rayed. And drouth is a thing worse than language can tell, it is. Give me the Winter!—no matter how miry-wayed: Cold may be pinching; but heat!—what a hell it is!
