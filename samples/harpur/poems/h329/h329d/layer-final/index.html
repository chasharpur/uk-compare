1.

Neither Will Do.

Winter I hate thee! In thy chilly lot it is To have almost nothing that’s pleasant and cherishing: Give me the Summer, no matter how hot it is; Heat may distress one—but cold! it is perishing.

Summer I hate thee! thy reign is so fiery ray’d, And drouth is a thing worse than language can tell it is: Give me the Winter, no matter how miry way’d; Cold may be pinching—but heat! what a hell it is.
