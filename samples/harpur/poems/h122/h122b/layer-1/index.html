VI.

[EVENING IN THE COUNTRY]

Hale to the shadowy Hour! whose gleamy wings Fan into bloom Love’s rosiest visionings! That hour delicious, from whose glowing skies Drops visual balm for beauty-loving eyes! And when, or hurrying over in a throng, Or pairing still, from tree to tree, along, The wild birds warble their last,—sweetest song. While the mute symphony of Flora breathes From cedarn dingles which the woodbine wreathes, And where, slow-arowsing into homeward flight, The bees seem faint, as sated with delight.

Now sinks the Sun cloud-curtained to his rest Beyond the mountain forests of the West: And while the crimson of the dying day Deepens, to fade into a dusky grey, A silvery fringe of many-pointed light 620 Rays, like a halo, from yon eastern hight, Though yet the Moon, with an expected bride’s Amorous delay, beyond its cone abides: Whence, like a vast cerulean snake, the stream Its liquid length draws winding with a twilight gleam. The beasts are still; the birds are flown; and lo, The moist flowers, ere they fold their petals, glow More richly in the mitigated light, Embroidering so the border of the Night: Or where young Linus, hurrying past, now sees Idalea’s dwelling top yon rounding trees, Shaken, they shed the dews like christal fire, Or crushed, in odorous ecstacies expire.
