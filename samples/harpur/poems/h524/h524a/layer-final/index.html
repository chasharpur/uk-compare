O Love it is a pleasant song That wakens in the Spring, When days are waxing bright and long And bowers are blossoming; To strengthen all the powers that bless, And gladden all employ, To give a joy to tenderness And tenderness to joy.

In the bright blue of the warm skies It anthems beauty’s birth, Or raineth thence, to fertilize The bridals of the earth: And then, if measureful and strong It cometh to our door, O Love it is a pleasant song, To cheer us evermore.
