II.

Their bossy shields met clashing, and full loud The din and clangor of the battle rose. At once were heard the shouts and groans of men Slaying and being slain,—till all the field Flowed with their blood. As when loud wintry streams Down from the mountains rushing, mix at last Their wild impetuous waters all in one Deep scooped receptacle of ample springs Oozing for ever from every hollow, And the hill-shepherd hears the distant roar— So rose together, in one hubbub mixed, The shouting and the panic.
