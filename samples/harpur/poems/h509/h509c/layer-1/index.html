IX.

As when a stubborn ass, caught trespassing, Will case not from devouring the tall corn, Though many a sturdy stick on his gaunt sides Already hath been smashed, and still the boys Keep spending all their feeble strength, in still Repeated thwacks upon his sounding hide; Nor can they, till the brute is cramm’d with food, Expel him, and with difficulty then: So the great-hearted Trojans, with their far- Derived auxiliaries, still following, throng The mighty Ajax,—sorely taxing all His stubborn vallor, and from every side Striking his broad shield with their flying spears; While, often wheeling, mindful of his own Impetuous vigor,—now he holds in check The wedging fronts of the horse-taming Trojans; Then, turning warily, again retreats.
