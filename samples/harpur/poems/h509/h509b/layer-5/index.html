As when a stubborn ass, caught trespassing, Will cease not from devouring the tall corn Though many a sturdy stick on his gaunt sides Already hath been smashed, and still the boys Keep spending all their feeble strength, in still Repeated thwacks upon his sounding hide; Nor can they; till the brute is cramed with food, Expel him, and with difficulty then: So the great-hearted Trojans & with their far Derived auxilliaries, still following, throng The mighty Ajax,—sorely taxing all His stubborn valor, and from every side Stricking his broad shield with the flying d While often wheeling, in the consious pride Impetuous vigor, now he holds in check The wedging fronts of the horse-taming Trojans; Then, turning warily, again retreats
