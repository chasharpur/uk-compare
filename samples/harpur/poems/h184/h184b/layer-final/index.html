Lyrics. By Charles Harpur.

I would get me a New Love.

I would get me a new love, The old fire is out! I would get me a new love Ere Spring comes about! That as in her warm arms The fields all re-bloom, My lone heart may awaken From coldness and gloom.

O there’s nothing in nature Like love when ’tis new; And there’s nothing in fortune Like love when ’tis true! But my passion for Meg, was A fire that’s gone out; And I’d get me a new love Ere Spring comes about.

I would get me a new love, Less Custom’s cold slave; 115 And I’d get me a new love More earnest and brave! Too pure to mistrust me, Too noble to doubt! I would get such a new love Ere Spring comes about.

But if such a new love I no where may get, I’d as lief be thus lonely— Nay, lonelier yet! Than have one like the old love, Just playing a part, With a flare in her fancy, But ice at her heart.
