525

Simony

How horribly Satan must grin, When two Bishops, both worn out and old, Yet feel it no scandal, and deem it no sin, To trust less in God than in Gold!

I’m past work, says Durham, and yet I will not retire from my billet, Unless on a pension as great As my salary is while I fill it.

I’m worn out, says London, I know, And Christ-ward no longer win some: Well, my See to a younger may go If only they leave me the income.

As if an old Bishop should be Fattened into uneatable bacon, And paid but the higher when he Is most with infirmity shaken!

As if, when quite palsied with age, His christianly digits should mostly Keep clutching with miserly rage At all matters & things—but the ghostly!

Drawn ever was Mammon of old (That prompter to the most of the crime here) As a devil-king throned upon gold In a place that won’t come into rhyme here.

But 526

But in those sermonical days, Full certain I am it were righter To have Mammon figured always As an Old Man wearing a mitre!

1853.
