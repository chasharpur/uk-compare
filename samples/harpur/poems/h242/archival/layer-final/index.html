33

Love in the Springtime.

(From Solomon’s Song.)

With the light of the morning, a Voice, like a ray, Came saying My Fair One, arise: come away: For the spirit of Winter hath past from the plain, And over and gone is the cold-dropping rain.

New blooms are adorning the face of the ground, And the green woods with tokens of gladness resound: For the air by the wing of the song-bird is fann’d, And the voice of the turtle is heard in our land.

And thickly the fig tree puts forth her green store, And the vine’s tender clusters smell pleasant once more. Arise then, my Love, with the light of the day; My Fair One, arise, and come quickly away.
