Accusations.

Helouise, when faith is breaking, Then first urged are injury’s pleas; And when love the heart’s forsaking, Then are written words like these: ‘Come no more! The spell is broken That hath mastered me till now! Take back every once-prized token— Light and faithless both art thou!’

Showed it light, when all the daughters Evermore conspired to praise, My fond heart’s so genial waters Showered for thee in living lays; Then to turn away uncaring— Then, thus flattered, but to be 293 In its ruddy depths preparing Some new gush of love for thee?

Light? some fountain, shade, or meadow, Till the wintry sun wore low, Still to haunt, if but thy shadow There had fall’n, though long ago! Or when all the youths were near me, Grouped in sport on every lea, Lonely still, the Muse to weary For some sweeter sense of thee!

Faithless also?—that is stranger? I?—who oft, upon my bed, Grieve, though but to dream of danger Hanging cloud-like o’er thy head! Yea, with darkness aching round me, I have yearned on bended knee, Till the Morn pried in, and found me Storing heaven with prayers for thee!

Faithless wilt thou yet declare me? Nay, if thou our troth wouldst break, 294 E’en break it!—but reproaches spare me, If alone for memory’s sake. For there cometh a To-morrow, When with aching heart and brow, O’er this faith-breach thou shalt sorrow Bitterly—as I do now!
