KOSSUTH.

Thou man of moral majesty and might, Now that thy country, bound and bleeding, cries To thee in vain, and regnant Austria’s spite Can cloud thy starry fame with dastard lies, Not to despair alone bespeaks thee wise— Bespeaks thee true, thou pleader for the Right!— For Liberty, all vital like the light, May swoon—may slumber—but she never dies! Thy sacred purpose evermore shall swell The Magyar’s heart, till through his war-trump given In final triumph;—purpose clothed so well With eloquence that stormeth up to heaven! Even as the thunder cloud is pierced and riven By some great mountain in the Land of Tell.

Charles Harpur.
