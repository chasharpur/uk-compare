To Kossuth.

Thou Man of moral majesty and might! Now that thy Country, bound and bleeding, cries 118 For help—in vain! and regnant Austria’s spite Can wound thy truth with diplomatic lies! Not to dispair alone bespeaks thee wise— Bespeaks thee true, thou pleader for the right! For Liberty, an essence born of light, May swoon—may slumber;—but she never dies! Thy sacred aims the Magyar’s heart shall swell, Till through his war-trump in full triumph given; Thy vast-ascending eloquence so well, In mighty appeal, projects them up to heaven! Storming God’s peace—as the still sky is riven By some great mountain in the Land of Tell.
